Tip 01 — Why Mitochondria Matter for Your 50 lb Goal
You Can't Lose 50 lbs Without Healing the Engine First

Most fat-loss protocols fail at the same place: months 2–3 of a sustained deficit, when the body's energy production starts to crash. The classic story: weight loss is great for 4–6 weeks, then progress stalls, energy drops, motivation evaporates, and within a few months the weight comes back. The reason isn't willpower or food choices. It's mitochondria.

Mitochondria are where your body burns fat for energy. When you cut calories, the body is asking the mitochondria to work harder — to oxidize stored fat instead of incoming food. If your mitochondria are already stressed (chronic inflammation, sleep debt, alcohol, aging) they can't keep up. The body senses this and adapts by lowering metabolism, conserving energy, and making you feel tired. The deficit is no longer producing the same fat loss it did in Week 1.

This is why we built the stack the way we did. Reta drives the deficit. SS-31 + MOTS-C + SLU-PP-332 keep the engine healthy under the load. You don't just lose 50 lbs — you do it while preserving energy and metabolic capacity. That's the difference between a transient loss and a sustainable one.

Tip 02 — First-Time Injecting
Day 1, Step by Step

Every shot in this protocol is SubQ — just under the skin of your abdomen, not into muscle. The needle is short and very thin (29–31G). Most people describe it as a quick pinch or barely noticeable.

Day 1 plan: SS-31 only. Phase 1 is intentionally one compound, one shot — the cleanest possible start. Reconstitute SS-31 (1 vial with 2 mL bac water), draw 10 units, inject in the upper-left abdomen quadrant AM fasted (wait 30–45 min before eating). That's the entire Day 1 routine. Repeat M–F for 4 weeks. SLU-PP-332 starts whenever your first training day is — 30–60 min before workout, 10u (500 mcg), separate routine from the AM SS-31 shot. Reta + MOTS-C don't start until Week 5 — you'll get a second walkthrough then.

The injection technique: wipe the vial stopper with an alcohol pad. Pull air into the syringe equal to your dose. Inject air into the vial. Invert the vial, draw your dose. Wipe the abdomen injection spot with alcohol. Pinch a fold of skin, insert the needle at a 90° angle (or 45° if you're slim), push the plunger steadily, withdraw, dispose in sharps container.

Mild redness or a tiny bruise at the injection site is normal and resolves within a day. If a site stays red/hot/swollen past 24 hrs, message us.

Tip 03 — Blood Pressure Medication Interaction
Monitor Your BP, Especially Weeks 1–2

You're on blood pressure medication. Retatrutide and other GLP-1 class compounds can lower blood pressure modestly — usually a small drop in systolic pressure as weight comes off and insulin sensitivity improves. Combined with BP meds, this can occasionally cause hypotension (low BP) symptoms.

What to watch for: lightheadedness when standing up, dizziness, unusual fatigue, headache. These are signals your BP may be dropping more than your meds are set for.

What to do: take your BP at home twice a week during Weeks 1–4 (drug store BP cuff works fine). If readings are consistently lower than your normal baseline, or if you have hypotensive symptoms, contact your prescribing doctor about possibly adjusting your BP medication dose. Do not stop your BP meds on your own.

Tip 04 — Alcohol on Reta Days
Skip or Minimize on Dose Days

You mentioned 3–7 drinks/week. Reta + alcohol increases the risk of nausea, GI discomfort, and dehydration. Most people who get sick on Reta did so after drinking the night before a dose.

Practical rule: skip alcohol the night before any Reta day (Sun, Tue, Thu nights since Reta is M/W/F). Save drinks for Fri/Sat nights when you have Saturday off from all shots. This also helps with sleep quality — alcohol within 4 hrs of bed cuts deep sleep, which directly hits the mitochondrial recovery you're paying for.

Tip 05 — Sleep & Stress (Both 3/5)
Why This Matters More Than Most People Realize

You rated sleep and stress at 3/5. Both directly impact mitochondrial function and fat loss. The most under-discussed reason fat-loss protocols fail isn't dosing — it's that the body in a chronically stressed, under-slept state preferentially stores fat and resists losing it.

Two things to prioritize during this cycle:

1. Sleep 7+ hrs. Same bedtime ±30 min. Phone out of the bedroom. Room cool. The mitochondrial repair this protocol depends on happens during sleep.
2. Stress: one daily "decompression" anchor. 10 min walk after a meal, 5 min of slow breathing before bed, anything that signals to your nervous system "the day is over." Cortisol is the fat-loss saboteur most people forget about.

Tip 06 — Reta Titration Ladder
When & How to Bump the Dose Up

Starting at 1 mg M/W/F is the conservative entry — let your GI system adapt. After Week 4, if response is stalling and tolerance is solid (no persistent nausea, no GI issues), you can titrate up:

Wks 1–4: 1 mg / 10 units M/W/F
Wks 5–6: 1.5 mg / 15 units M/W/F (if tolerated)
Wks 7–8: 2 mg / 20 units M/W/F (cap — only if 1.5 plateaued)

Don't bump if: nausea is still happening, fat loss is on track (1–1.5 lbs/wk sustained), energy is dropping. Hold the dose that's working.

Tip 08 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild nausea / appetite suppression first 1–2 weeks of Reta — subsides
· Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites
· Constipation on Reta — hydration + fiber addresses it
· Slight fatigue Week 1 as body adapts to deficit

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours
· Severe nausea or vomiting past Week 2 of Reta — drop to 0.5 mg or pause
· Lightheadedness or dizziness (possible BP drop — see Tip 03)
· Fat loss faster than ~2 lbs/wk sustained — drop Reta dose
· Persistent fatigue beyond Week 2 — usually under-eating; add cals around training
