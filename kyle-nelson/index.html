Protocol Overview — How This Stack Works Together
HGH (Somatropin) 2 IU
The foundation of the stack, dosed at a conservative anti-aging / recovery range. Drives protein synthesis, preferentially burns stored fat as fuel, and rebuilds lean tissue. Runs M–F for 12 weeks, then walks down across a 4-week taper (1.5 → 1 → 1 M/W/F) so your pituitary wakes back up gradually instead of crashing.
CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin
A dual GH secretagogue blend that amplifies endogenous GH release via two complementary mechanisms (GHRH + ghrelin-receptor). Runs pre-bed through all 12 weeks — stacked on top of HGH during the cycle, and keeping your own GH axis awake as exogenous HGH walks down.
IGF-1 LR3
The primary downstream anabolic mediator of GH signaling. LR3's 20–30 hour half-life keeps IGF-1 receptor activity elevated through the critical post-workout protein synthesis window. Dosed M/W/F within 30 minutes of finishing training, paired with a carb meal for insulin-IGF amplification.
5-Amino-1MQ
An NNMT inhibitor that restores intracellular NAD+ in adipose tissue and muscle. HGH pulls fat out of storage at a high rate — if NAD+ is low, those liberated fatty acids recirculate instead of being oxidized. 5-Amino-1MQ keeps adipose tissue receptive to burning what HGH has mobilized, and activates dormant muscle satellite cells as reserve for rebuilding.
L-Carnitine (IM)
The transport molecule that physically shuttles fatty acids across the mitochondrial membrane. Without sufficient carnitine at the site, mobilized fat gets recirculated. Injectable delivers near-complete bioavailability versus ~15% oral — so every fat molecule HGH liberates actually reaches the mitochondria and gets burned.
Stack Synergy: HGH + CJC/Ipa create layered GH signaling that simultaneously drives protein synthesis and fat mobilization. IGF-1 LR3 amplifies the downstream anabolic response directly at the muscle cell, timed to the training window. 5-Amino-1MQ restores the NAD+ fuel needed to oxidize liberated fat at the mitochondrial level. L-Carnitine is the essential transport linking fat mobilization to fat oxidation. Each compound addresses a distinct step in the cascade — none are redundant, and each one enables the next.

⚠ Mixed HGH Supply — Read This Carefully

Your HGH comes in two different concentrations. The bottles look alike but require different syringe marks. This is by far the easiest thing in the program to get wrong.

  • Weeks 1–12 (Main Phase): 24 IU bottles → 20 units per shot = 2 IU. Use all 6 of these bottles first.
  • Weeks 13–16 (Taper): 36 IU bottle → 13 units or 8 units depending on the week. Different concentration, different unit marks.
  • When you start Week 13, physically separate the bottles. Move empty 24 IU vials to a ziplock in a different part of the fridge so there's no way to grab the wrong one at 6am.

Why it's the foundation
HGH sits at the top of the stack. At 2 IU on weekdays, the dose is deliberately in the anti-aging / recovery range — well below the supraphysiological doses used in bodybuilding protocols. What this produces over 8–12 weeks is deeper sleep, improved skin quality, faster recovery between training sessions, and a gradual body composition shift driven by GH's preferential use of stored fat as fuel. Everything else in the program either amplifies what HGH starts (CJC/Ipa, IGF-1 LR3) or makes sure its byproducts are fully used (5-Amino-1MQ, L-Carnitine).
The 4-week taper is deliberate. After 12 weeks of daily HGH, your pituitary has been reading elevated IGF-1 and has dialed its own GH output down via negative feedback. Stopping cold leaves endogenous GH suppressed for weeks while exogenous HGH clears — that's the "rebound crash." The taper walks the dose down (2 IU → 1.5 → 1 M–F → 1 M/W/F) so the axis reactivates gradually.
Why Two Different Bottles
Your supply came as 6 bottles at 24 IU + 1 bottle at 36 IU — a small stocking quirk, not a clinical decision. Rather than leave taper doses short, we reconstitute the 36 IU bottle at a slightly higher concentration (12 IU/mL vs 10 IU/mL) and use it exclusively for the 4-week taper. The unit marks change because the concentration changes. Delivered IU per shot lands within ±4% of the target dose at each taper step, which is well inside clinical tolerance.

5-Amino-1MQ
NNMT Inhibitor · SubQ · M/W/F → M–F Ramp
$200.00
2 × 50 mg
Why it's in the stack
5-Amino-1MQ (5-Amino-1-methylquinolinium) selectively inhibits NNMT — an enzyme that consumes NAD+ and methyl groups in adipose tissue and muscle. NNMT activity rises with age and metabolic stress, and when it's high, NAD+ gets siphoned off before the mitochondria can use it to oxidize fat.
In the Gorilla Stack 2.0 context this matters because HGH is pulling fat out of storage at an elevated rate. If NAD+ is low, those liberated fatty acids don't get burned in the mitochondria — they simply recirculate and re-deposit. Blocking NNMT raises intracellular NAD+ without needing to supplement NAD+ directly, keeping adipose tissue receptive to burning what HGH mobilizes.
Secondary mechanism: 5-Amino-1MQ activates dormant muscle satellite cells — the reserve pool of muscle-building cells that HGH and IGF-1 then recruit. Amplifies both sides of the stack (fat oxidation + anabolic reserve).

L-Carnitine
Mitochondrial Transport · IM (not SubQ) · Mon + Fri
$225.00
5 × 600 mg / 10 mL
Why it's in the stack
L-Carnitine isn't a peptide — it's the amino-acid derivative that physically shuttles fatty acids across the mitochondrial membrane. Without sufficient carnitine at the injection site, free fatty acids mobilized by HGH sit at the mitochondrial doorstep and get recirculated back into storage rather than burned.
Oral L-Carnitine is limited by roughly 15% bioavailability and gut conversion to TMAO. Injectable L-Carnitine at 120 mg IM delivers near-complete bioavailability. Twice-weekly (Mon + Fri) is enough to maintain elevated muscle carnitine — saturation is slow-building and persistent. Timed pre-workout, you hit peak muscle carnitine concentration when mitochondrial fat oxidation demand is highest.
Paired with HGH's upstream fat mobilization, the full chain — liberation → transport → oxidation — is covered.

Hypoglycemia Watch: IGF-1 LR3 can cause mild post-injection hypoglycemia — slight dizziness, shakiness, or sudden hunger — particularly if dosed on an empty stomach. This is why the post-workout + carb-meal timing is specified, not optional. If you ever feel dizzy, eat fast-acting carbs (juice, fruit) and sit down. Over 12 weeks, IGF-1 LR3 drives recovery speed, protein synthesis, and muscle fullness well beyond what HGH alone produces.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Clean · Rotate Sites
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

⚠ Mixed HGH supply — keep bottles separated. Store the 24 IU bottles (Main Phase) in one clearly labeled ziplock and the 36 IU bottle (Taper) in a different part of the fridge. At Week 13 you'll switch to the 36 IU bottle and the unit marks change — do not let them end up next to each other in the same tray.

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze. HGH, CJC/Ipa, IGF-1 LR3, and 5-Amino-1MQ are temperature-sensitive — the fridge is non-negotiable.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days of reconstitution. Reconstitute one vial of each compound at a time — don't start the 30-day clock on a second vial until the first is close to empty.

L-Carnitine: pre-mixed in solution, store refrigerated once opened. Protect from light. Does not require reconstitution.

Syringe discipline: fresh insulin syringe for every SubQ shot. Fresh 3 mL Luer-lock + 25G × 1.5″ needle for every IM shot. Swap to a fresh needle between drawing and injecting when possible — piercing a rubber stopper dulls the needle tip and increases site reactions.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants (upper left/right, lower left/right), never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. IM rotates between glute left, glute right, vastus lateralis left, vastus lateralis right on a 4-site rotation.
