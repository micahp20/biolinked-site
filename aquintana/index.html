The frame. 193 to 180 is a clean ~13 lb cut over a run-till-empty timeline — not aggressive, not extreme. You should expect the diabetes markers to improve alongside the scale.

Four compounds, three different mechanisms working in parallel. Reta (triple agonist GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon) is the headline — appetite suppression + visceral fat burn + direct glucose improvement, all from one weekly shot. CJC/Ipa pre-bed amplifies your overnight growth hormone pulse, which is the lever for protecting (and building) muscle while you're in a calorie deficit. 5-Amino-1MQ blocks the NNMT enzyme that progressively locks fat into adipose storage as we age past 50 — the over-50 fat-accelerator. Coordinate any med adjustments with your diabetes provider; don't change anything on your own without telling them. Track fasting glucose daily for the first 3 weeks.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry vials (before reconstitution): refrigerate at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Stable for many months sealed.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Reconstitute one vial at a time — on Day 1, mix Reta, CJC/Ipa vial #1, 5-Amino, Wolverine vial #1. Around week 4: reconstitute CJC/Ipa vial #2 + Wolverine vial #2 as the first ones run dry. Wolverine vial #3 and #4 follow as needed.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. With 2 shots in some blocks, use different quadrants for each. Never the same 1″ spot twice the same day.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Day 1
Tip 01 — Blood Sugar Watch (First 2–4 Weeks)
The Diabetes-Aware Starting Protocol

Most important rule for the first month: Reta is going to improve your glucose handling. If you're on metformin, insulin, or any glucose-lowering medication, your fasting sugar will likely start running lower than your usual baseline. This is a "your med dose may need adjusting" signal, not a problem with the protocol.

Watch for hypoglycemia symptoms:

· Shakiness, trembling
· Sweating (especially cold sweats)
· Lightheadedness, dizziness
· Confusion, trouble concentrating
· Sudden, intense hunger
· Heart racing
· Headache that comes on quickly

If you get any of these, especially in the morning before food or 2–3 hours after a meal, check your glucose immediately. If it's <70, eat a fast-acting carb (juice, glucose tabs, honey, regular soda — not diet). Recheck in 15 min. If still low, repeat.

Three things to do this first week:

1. Log fasting glucose every morning (already do this? Great. If not, start). Track the trend, not just one number.
2. Tell your diabetes provider you're starting Retatrutide. Send them this protocol if helpful. Do not adjust diabetes meds on your own — they need to decide when/how to step you down based on your numbers.
3. Keep glucose tabs / fast carbs at hand the first month (in your bag, bedside, gym bag).

What's a "good problem": if your fasting sugar drops from 130 to 110 over the first few weeks, that's exactly what we want — AND it's the signal to talk to your provider about whether your current diabetes med dose still fits.

Tip 02 — The Daily Rhythm
AM Block + PM Block, 5 Days a Week

AM (fasted, first thing after waking): 5-Amino every M–F. Add Reta on Monday only. Wait 30–45 min before eating — the fasted state is what makes 5-Amino work best (low insulin = best NNMT inhibition).

PM (pre-bed, 2+ hrs after dinner): CJC/Ipa + Wolverine, both M–F, into different abdominal quadrants. The 2-hour fasted gap is non-negotiable for CJC/Ipa — food spikes insulin and blunts the GH pulse. Routine: dinner → wait 2 hrs → brush teeth → CJC/Ipa + Wolverine (different quadrants) → lights out within 30–60 min.

Saturday + Sunday: full off. 5-day-on / 2-day-off pattern across the entire stack — tissue gets a rest cycle, schedule stays predictable.

Time budget: Monday AM is 2 shots, ~2 min. Tue–Fri AM = 1 shot. M–F PM = 2 shots, ~2 min. Sat/Sun = off. Total ~5 min/day on weekdays only.

Tip 03 — Week-by-Week Honest Timeline
What to Expect on the Scale + Glucose

Weeks 1–2 (acclimation): appetite drops noticeably from Reta. Some mild nausea on Mondays (Reta day) is normal — eat smaller, lower-fat meals that day, hydrate aggressively. Fasting glucose may already start trending DOWN by end of week 1 — flag to your diabetes provider. Wolverine + 5-Amino + CJC/Ipa are loading; no dramatic effects yet from those.

Weeks 3–5 (the engine kicks in): fat-loss curve takes hold. Realistic target is 1–2 lbs/week. CJC/Ipa-driven deeper sleep is established. 5-Amino's energy lift compounds. Glucose should be running noticeably lower — this is when most clients have the diabetes-med dose review with their provider.

Weeks 6–8 (consolidation): 5-Amino runs out around week 6. CJC/Ipa + Wolverine finish together around week 8. Reta continues for many weeks more. Scale should be at or near 180 by this window. Take stock at week 8: photos, measurements (waist especially), strength numbers, fasting glucose trend.

Reta titration check-in at week 3: message Micah with: how appetite feels, what the scale is doing, fasting glucose trend. We adjust based on data:
· 15u working cleanly — stay there.
· Scale stalled, appetite easy, glucose stable — titrate to 20u (~2.67 mg).
· GI side effects persistent — drop to 10u (~1.33 mg).

Tip 04 — Protein + Hydration
The Two Numbers That Keep Muscle On

Protein target: ~180 g/day (1 g per lb of goal bodyweight). Non-negotiable for keeping muscle through the cut at 59. Reta suppresses appetite so front-load protein early in the day — a protein shake within an hour of waking is the easy lever. With your 3–4×/wk training base + CJC/Ipa-restored GH pulse, hitting this floor is what guarantees the 180 lb shows up as lean 180 lb.

Hydration target: 3–4 L/day. Reta suppresses thirst alongside appetite — you won't feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people get headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor workout performance and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit.

Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day, ideally pre-training. The cramping and fatigue people blame on the peptide is almost always low sodium or magnesium — especially on a cut.

For the diabetes layer: consistent meal timing helps. Reta works best when you eat your daily calories in a relatively consistent window (don't skip meals on Reta days — eating small + light is better than eating nothing).

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Reta (Monday once-weekly): miss Monday, take it within 24–48 hours (Tuesday or Wednesday). More than 48 hours: push your anchor day to whichever day you took it, then resume that day the following week. Don't double up.

5-Amino (M–F AM): miss one morning, take it later that day if you can. If the whole day passes, skip and resume the next day. Not strictly time-locked, but earlier is better.

CJC/Ipa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it. Don't shift to AM the next day — the pre-sleep window is what makes it work. If you ate too close to bed and the shot would be on a fed stomach, skip rather than dose — a fed CJC shot is wasted.

Wolverine (M–F PM): miss one, take it the next day if you can. Tissue exposure compounds steadily; missing 1–2 doses across a week doesn't break the trend.

Missing 2–3 days in a row: not a problem. Pick up on the next planned day. Don't try to "make up" missed doses.

Tip 06 — Red Flags · When to Pause
Signals to Watch For (Text Micah First)

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain (right-upper-quadrant), especially after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit too aggressive, eat more
· Hypoglycemic episode that doesn't respond to fast carbs within 15 min — get medical help

Hold CJC/Ipa if: persistent morning hand/face puffiness past week 3, joint pain that's new, sustained tingling/numbness in hands.

Diabetes-specific: if fasting glucose drops below 70 consistently or you have any unexplained hypoglycemic symptoms, contact your diabetes provider immediately — your meds need adjustment. Don't stop the protocol on your own without checking in.

Other yellow flags — reassess: strength dropping noticeably in the gym (means muscle is going — lift protein, may need to drop Reta to 0.75 mg), sleep getting worse instead of deeper, persistent fatigue past week 3.

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own — we adjust based on what's actually happening.
