Humberto's Metabolic Core + HCG
Fat loss + cellular energy + testosterone support — engineered around Tacrolimus, Mycophenolate, and Carvedilol.
⚠️ Post-Kidney Transplant · Physician Coordination Required

This protocol is engineered around your transplant. Tacrolimus + Mycophenolate (immunosuppression) + Carvedilol (cardiovascular) all have narrow therapeutic windows. No HGH, no hormonal-axis modulators, no peptides that stress the kidney directly. The four compounds chosen are the ones with the cleanest safety profile for a transplant patient on your med stack — specifically including MOTS-C, which has documented renal-protective effects in research.

The four jobs the stack does. Retatrutide drives the visceral fat off (triple agonist — GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon, the most powerful fat-loss compound available). MOTS-C resets mitochondrial efficiency via AMPK activation — the metabolic substrate your cells need to actually rebuild while Reta drives the deficit. 5-Amino-1MQ blocks NNMT, restoring cellular NAD+ levels — both age and chronic immunosuppression compound NAD+ depletion, and this is the direct fix. HCG addresses the Tacrolimus-induced testosterone suppression by stimulating Leydig cells directly (not the HPG axis), restoring drive, muscle preservation, and recovery without modulating pituitary signaling.

End-state at Week 8: visible fat loss (especially visceral / abdominal), restored cellular energy, recovered testosterone signaling, and a metabolic baseline that holds. Critically: requires physician coordination. Tacrolimus trough levels should be checked at Week 2 and Week 6 to confirm the protocol isn't shifting the immunosuppression window. Your nephrologist's call — not ours.

Storage & Handling
Refrigerated · HCG + MOTS-C Especially Temperature-Sensitive
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately. Use within 30 days. Fresh insulin syringe for each draw — never reuse. HCG and MOTS-C are the most temperature-sensitive — keep them in the main fridge body, not the door.

HCG reconstitution sequencing: Don't reconstitute all 3 HCG vials at once. Mix one at a time as you finish the previous — HCG potency drops noticeably after ~30 days reconstituted.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. With heavy days hitting 4 shots in one window, plan rotation so each shot lands in a different quadrant.

Critical Tips for Your Situation
Tip 01 — Critical · Physician Coordination
Tacrolimus Trough Labs at Week 2 + Week 6

This is non-negotiable. Tacrolimus has a narrow therapeutic window (typically 5–15 ng/mL trough). The metabolic shift this protocol drives — particularly the AMPK activation and significant fat loss — can subtly affect Tacrolimus pharmacokinetics. Catching any shift early avoids any rejection-risk window.

Action items:

  • Before Day 1: notify your nephrologist that you're starting this stack. Share the protocol if asked.
  • Week 2: Tacrolimus trough lab draw (morning, before dose). Report results to nephrologist.
  • Week 6: Tacrolimus trough lab draw again. If shifted, adjustment may be needed.
  • Anytime: if you notice unusual fatigue, edema, urinary changes — pause and call your nephrologist before resuming.
Tip 02 — Why This Stack Is Transplant-Safe
What's IN the Protocol — and Specifically What's OUT

In this protocol (by design):

  • MOTS-C — documented renal-protective effects in research, one of the safest peptides for transplant patients
  • 5-Amino-1MQ — works on NNMT (cellular NAD+ pathway), no interaction with immunosuppression
  • Retatrutide — GLP-1 class compounds are increasingly accepted in transplant populations for weight management
  • HCG — testicular-level action, not HPG axis, cleanest testosterone-support choice for your med stack

OUT of this protocol (deliberately):

  • No HGH — insulin resistance risk + kidney load both incompatible with your med profile
  • No CJC-1295 / Ipa or other HPG-axis peptides — interaction risk with immunosuppression
  • No GHRH analogs — same axis-modulation concern
  • No anti-aging peptides that stress filtration (some Khavinson peptides, certain NAD+ IV protocols)

The point: this isn't a watered-down protocol. It's a focused 4-compound stack chosen because each one is appropriate for your specific medical context. The "no HGH" decision in particular often gets pushback from peptide-enthusiasts — for a transplant patient on Tacrolimus, it's the correct call.

Tip 03 — AM Cluster Logistics
4 Shots Sounds Like A Lot — It's a 5-Minute Routine

Heavy days (Mon + Fri) hit 4 shots in the morning. In practice this is one ~5-minute routine:

  • Wake, water/black coffee. No food yet.
  • MOTS-C first — 10u abdomen upper-right. Fasted-dependent.
  • Reta next — 10u abdomen lower-right.
  • 5-Amino-1MQ — 20u abdomen upper-left.
  • HCG (Mon/Wed/Fri only) — 20u abdomen lower-left.
  • Wait 30–45 min, then breakfast.

By Week 2 it's habit. Rotate the right/left and upper/lower assignments each week so no single quadrant takes the same shot day after day.

Week-by-Week
What to Expect Across 8 Weeks
Weeks 1–2
Foundation · Tacrolimus Trough Check

First 2 weeks build the rhythm. Subtle effects possible: mild appetite suppression from Reta within first week, gentle energy lift from NAD+ restoration, possibly slightly more vivid dreams. Week 2 lab draw is non-negotiable — Tacrolimus trough.

Reta GI side effects (mild nausea, occasional fullness) are most likely in this window. Stay hydrated, eat smaller meals.

Weeks 3–4
First Visible Fat Loss + Testosterone Lift

This is the noticeable-shift window. Expect 1.5–2.5 lbs/wk fat loss (heavier early, settling by Wk 4). HCG effects start to land — improved morning energy, libido, mood. MOTS-C + 5-Amino are working underneath — you'll feel the difference in afternoon energy more than anything else.

Weeks 5–6
Peak Effect · Second Tacrolimus Check

Body comp shift becomes obvious in the mirror. Visceral / abdominal fat distribution begins to visibly tighten. Recovery from physical activity is meaningfully faster. Week 6 lab draw — second Tacrolimus trough. If still in range, the rest of the cycle is smooth sailing.

Weeks 7–8
Cycle Close + Plan Maintenance

Final stretch. Vials run dry around end of Wk 8 (with small buffers on Reta and HCG). Reta buffer = ~7 weeks if you want to extend; HCG buffer = ~2 weeks extra.

End-of-cycle decisions: body comp result vs Wk 1? Energy + testosterone lift? Lab values stable? These shape what the next cycle looks like — could be a repeat in 6–8 weeks (after off-cycle), or a maintenance dose of HCG continuation (M/W/F at lower frequency).

Tip 04 — Normal Effects
No Action Needed
  • Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites and clean technique
  • Mild GI effects first 1–2 weeks of Reta — nausea, early fullness, occasional constipation. Subsides as tolerance builds.
  • Slightly more vivid dreams — common from NAD+ restoration + HCG hormonal shift
  • Improved morning energy / libido by Wk 2–3 from HCG — the compound working
  • Mild flushing 5–10 min after MOTS-C — normal AMPK-related vascular response
Tip 05 — Watch For · Pause & Call
Transplant-Specific Red Flags
  • Any change in urination pattern or urine color — pause and call nephrologist same day
  • Unusual fatigue or swelling (especially lower legs / ankles) — possible Tacrolimus shift, pause and lab
  • Tacrolimus trough OUT of range at Wk 2 or Wk 6 — protocol pauses pending nephrologist guidance
  • Severe Reta GI effects past Wk 2 — drop to 0.5 mg (5u) or pause and contact us
  • Injection site hot / swollen / red >24 hrs — technique or contamination issue
  • Anything that feels wrong — immunosuppressed patients can mask symptoms. Default to calling early.
