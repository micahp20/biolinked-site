Why it's in your protocol
5-Amino-1MQ inhibits NNMT (nicotinamide N-methyltransferase), the enzyme that depletes NAD+ and drives fat accumulation in aged cells. By blocking NNMT, cellular NAD+ levels climb back up — which is the substrate that MOTS-C, AMPK, and sirtuins all need to actually do their job. The effect is a direct amplifier: both Reta and MOTS-C work noticeably better in a NAD+-replete cellular environment. For a 50-year-old post-transplant, restoring NAD+ is particularly load-bearing since both age and chronic immunosuppression compound NAD+ depletion.
