First-Time Injector — Start Here

You noted you've never injected before — that's completely fine. All of these are subcutaneous (SubQ) injections — shallow injections just under the skin, not into muscle. Use a 29–31 gauge insulin syringe, pinch the skin on your abdomen or outer thigh, insert at a 45° angle, and inject slowly. It should be nearly painless. If it stings, slow down the injection speed. Rotate sites daily. You'll be a pro by week 2.

Storage & Handling

Dry powder vials (before reconstitution)

Store all lyophilized (powder) vials refrigerated at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep away from light and moisture. Do not freeze. Shelf life is 12–24 months unopened. L-Carnitine is a liquid — refrigerate from day one.

After reconstitution

All reconstituted vials must be refrigerated immediately. Use within 30 days — except NAD+ (14 days for best potency) and HGH (21 days). Never freeze a reconstituted peptide. Always use a fresh insulin syringe for every draw.

Bacteriostatic water (BAC water)

Use sterile BAC water for all reconstitution except L-Carnitine (pre-mixed). BAC water's benzyl alcohol extends shelf life. Do not substitute plain sterile water — it lacks the preservative needed for multi-draw vials.

Syringes & injection technique

Use 29–31 gauge insulin syringes (0.5 mL or 1 mL). Never reuse. Dispose in a sharps container. Rotate injection sites — abdomen (preferred SubQ), outer thigh, or glute. Pinch 1 inch of skin, insert at 45°, inject slowly.

What to Monitor
Normal: Mild flushing or warmth during NAD+ injection — slow your injection to 30–60 seconds. Resolves after week 1–2.
