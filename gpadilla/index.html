Storage & Handling
Refrigerated · Protected from Light
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials — before reconstitution: refrigerate at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep away from light and moisture. Do not freeze. Shelf life is 12–24 months unopened. L-Carnitine arrives as a liquid — refrigerate from day one.

After reconstitution: refrigerate immediately. Use within 30 days — with two exceptions: NAD+ within 14 days for peak potency, and HGH within 21 days. Never freeze a reconstituted peptide. Always use a fresh insulin syringe for every draw.

Bacteriostatic water: required for all reconstitution except L-Carnitine (pre-mixed). The benzyl alcohol preservative extends shelf life. Do not substitute plain sterile water — it lacks the preservative needed for multi-draw vials.

Injection technique: 29–31 gauge insulin syringes (0.5 mL or 1 mL). Never reuse. Dispose in a sharps container. Rotate injection sites daily — abdomen (preferred SubQ), outer thigh, or glute. Pinch 1" of skin, insert at 45°, inject slowly.

Featured Tips
Tip 01 — First-Time Injector
SubQ Is Shallow, Painless, and Forgiving

You noted you've never injected before — that's completely fine. All of these are subcutaneous (SubQ) injections — shallow injections just under the skin, not into muscle.

  • Use a 29–31 gauge insulin syringe (the thin, short needle).
  • Pinch 1" of skin on your abdomen or outer thigh.
  • Insert at a 45° angle, slowly.
  • Inject the contents slowly — if it stings, slow down further.
  • Pull out, dispose in sharps container. Done.

Rotate sites every day. With this many AM shots, you'll be moving between abdomen quadrants and thighs constantly — that's the right pattern. You'll be a pro by week 2.

Tip 02 — Why Injectable Beats Oral
Why You Didn't Feel Oral BPC-157

You tried BPC-157 orally and felt nothing. That makes sense: oral peptides have extremely low bioavailability — most of the molecule is destroyed in the stomach before it ever reaches the bloodstream. What's left is a fraction of the intended dose, often not enough to produce noticeable effects.

Injectable BPC-157 (inside the KLOW blend) goes directly into your bloodstream at full potency. Same compound, completely different experience. Most clients notice meaningful joint and tissue changes within 2–3 weeks of starting injectable KLOW — the kind of shift oral BPC simply can't produce.

The same logic applies across the rest of the stack: L-Carnitine is ~15% absorbed orally vs. near-100% injectable; HGH and IGF-1 don't survive oral delivery at all. The injectable route isn't a preference — it's what makes the protocol work.

Tip 03 — NAD+ Half-Dose Start
Start at 30 Units (50 mg) for First 1–2 Sessions

NAD+ is the one compound in this stack with a notable acute side effect: mild flushing, warmth, or a slightly intense feeling during injection. It's harmless and resolves within minutes, but it's surprising the first time.

The fix: start at 30 units (50 mg) for your first 1–2 injections, then titrate up to the full 60 units (100 mg). And inject slowly — 30 to 60 seconds, not 5 seconds. Fast injection = stronger flush. Slow injection = barely any flush.

By week 2 you'll be doing the full dose with no issue.

Tip 04 — What to Monitor
Normal Signals vs. Watch Signals

Normal — no action needed:

  • Mild flushing or warmth during NAD+ injection — slow your injection to 30–60 seconds. Resolves after week 1–2.
  • Small redness, minor bruising, or mild soreness at injection sites — rotate daily to prevent buildup.
  • Increased hunger during week 1–2 as metabolism activates — especially with the GH stack and L-Carnitine.
  • Mild nausea with first 1–2 PT-141 doses — reduce to 250 mcg (5u) and titrate up.

Monitor — pause and reassess:

  • Dizziness or shakiness after IGF-1 LR3 — low blood glucose. Eat a small meal or glucose source after the morning injection. Do not inject IGF-1 LR3 and fast all day.
  • Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — stop using that site. May indicate technique issue. Contact BioLinked.
  • Water retention or tingling in extremities with HGH — if significant, reduce HGH from 1 IU to 0.5 IU (5u) and contact BioLinked.

Daily Rhythm
Tip 05 — AM Stack Logistics
Group Your Shots, Rotate Sites

M/W/F mornings carry the heaviest load — up to 7 shots fasted on waking (MOTS-C, SS-31, KLOW, CJC/Ipa AM, HGH, L-Carnitine, IGF-1 LR3). That sounds like a lot; in practice it's a 5-minute routine once you get the pattern down.

Practical pattern:

  • Lay all reconstituted vials and syringes out on a clean surface before starting.
  • Wipe each vial stopper with an alcohol pad.
  • Draw and inject one compound at a time — right abdomen quadrant, then left, then right thigh, then left, rotating across the 4 main sites.
  • Each shot takes ~30–45 seconds. Total time: 5–7 minutes.

Tip: keep a Sharpie note on the storage container with the day's compound order so you don't have to think about it half-awake.

Tip 06 — Pre-Bed Routine
CJC/Ipa PM + Kisspeptin for Overnight Recovery

The pre-bed window is when the anabolic signaling lands during your body's natural overnight repair phase. On M/W/F you'll have CJC/Ipa PM + Kisspeptin; Tue/Thu and weekends are just Kisspeptin.

Timing matters: CJC/Ipa pre-bed should be 30–60 minutes before sleep, on an empty stomach (no food 2 hours before). Kisspeptin should be ~1 hour before bed, same time nightly for hormonal entrainment.

Most clients report the deepest sleep of the cycle on these nights — the GH pulse plus the upstream hormonal signal compound into a particularly restorative window.

Tip 07 — PT-141 Use
As-Needed, Not Daily

PT-141 is the one as-needed compound in your stack. Inject SubQ 30–60 minutes before activity — not part of the daily schedule. Max 8 uses per month.

If you experience nausea on the first 1–2 uses (common), drop to 250 mcg (5u) and titrate up over the next couple of uses. Most clients find the full 500 mcg dose well-tolerated once their body has acclimated.
