✨ Why M/W/F Instead of Once-Weekly

Reta has a half-life of about six days, so once-weekly dosing is the standard clinical pattern — but that pattern creates a sharp peak in the 24–48 hours after the shot, which is when most people feel nausea and the post-shot fatigue you're already familiar with from tirz. Splitting the same weekly total into three smaller M/W/F doses smooths the curve. Lower peaks, fewer side effects, more even daytime energy, and steadier appetite suppression across the whole week instead of front-loaded into days 1–3.

This matters for you specifically because the reason you're switching off tirz is the energy crash. Putting you on weekly Reta would replicate that exact problem with a stronger drug. Micro-dosing breaks the loop: same therapeutic effect, no shot-day crash.

Design Philosophy

🧭 One Vial. Six Weeks. A Gentle Ramp.

This protocol is intentionally minimal. One compound, one schedule, one ramp. The job of this round is to cleanly transition you off tirzepatide and let your body acclimate to a different — and gentler-feeling — GLP-1/GIP/glucagon agonist before we consider layering anything else on top. Six weeks is enough to know how Reta sits with your physiology, hit a maintenance dose, and decide whether to extend, taper, or stack with one of the recommendations at the bottom of this page.

The ramp is conservative on purpose: 3 mg in Week 1, climbing 1 mg per week for three weeks, then steady at 6 mg/week through Week 6. That's a fraction of a typical Reta target (clinical maximums climb to 12 mg+), but the goal here is not to chase a top-end dose — it's to retain the weight-loss benefit you already had on tirz while restoring your daytime energy. If 6 mg gets you there, we don't need more.

Reconstitution
  1. On Day 1, swab the rubber stopper of the Reta vial and the 3 mL BAC water vial with an alcohol pad.
  2. Slowly draw 3.0 mL of bacteriostatic water into a syringe and inject down the side of the Reta vial — not directly onto the powder.

📈 What to Expect

Weeks 1–2 (3 mg/wk): appetite suppression starts subtly, similar in feel but milder than your tirz baseline. Some mild nausea on injection days is possible but uncommon at this dose. Energy should already feel cleaner than tirz weekly dosing — no shot-day crash.

Weeks 3–4 (4.5 mg/wk): "food noise" reduction becomes obvious. You'll notice you forget to eat or stop earlier than normal. Mild GI changes (constipation more often than nausea at this dose pattern) — hydrate aggressively and add fiber.

Weeks 5–6 (6 mg/wk): this is the maintenance window. Weight loss should be steady, energy stable, sleep often improves. By the end of Week 6, we'll have enough data to decide: hold here, taper down, extend at 6 mg, or layer in one of the recommendations below.

Recommended Stack to Consider

🧬 The Synergy Story — How These Layers Stack on Top of Reta

Reta drives the deficit. It does the appetite suppression, glucose control, and total weight loss work. What it doesn't directly do is target the specific physiology layers behind your low energy and visceral fat concerns. Each peptide below addresses one of those layers. None are required — they are recommendations to consider for the next round once Reta is running cleanly.

Read these as a menu, not a prescription. Pick what matches what you're actually feeling: low energy → MOTS-c, NAD+, or Semax. Visceral belly fat → Tesamorelin. Slow fat oxidation → 5-Amino-1MQ. Bad sleep → DSIP. Training and recovering hard → CJC-1295/Ipa.

Tesamorelin
Visceral Fat · Body Composition
A GHRH analog that's specifically been validated to reduce visceral adipose tissue — the deep belly fat around your organs, the kind that's metabolically dangerous and resistant to general weight loss. Reta drops total weight; Tesa shifts the *composition* of what you lose toward the visceral compartment. Mechanism: pulsatile GH release → IGF-1 → preferential lipolysis of visceral stores.
Synergy with Reta: Reta gives you the caloric deficit; Tesa makes sure the deficit is being paid out of the right fat compartment. Most useful if your goal includes "flatten the belly," not just "lower the scale."
5-Amino-1MQ
Fat Oxidation Accelerator
An NNMT inhibitor — it blocks the enzyme that drains the methyl-donor and NAD+ pool inside fat cells, which keeps adipocytes in their "store fat" mode. Inhibiting NNMT flips them toward burning stored lipid and resists fat-cell expansion. As a bonus side effect, it raises your endogenous NAD+ levels.
Synergy with Reta: Reta creates the deficit; 5-Amino-1MQ accelerates the metabolic consequence. Together: bigger deficit + faster oxidation = faster scale movement, particularly during the maintenance phase when Reta plateaus tend to appear.
MOTS-c
Mitochondrial Energy
A mitochondrial-derived peptide that signals the body to build more mitochondria and run them more efficiently. This is the cellular root of the "low energy" complaint — when mitochondria are tired, you're tired, no matter how much sleep or caffeine you throw at it. MOTS-c addresses the source instead of stimulating around it.
Synergy with Reta: Tirz fatigue is partly metabolic (less food in, less ATP out). MOTS-c counters that by improving how efficiently your cells use what they're getting. Pairs especially well with NAD+, which is the actual coenzyme mitochondria need to do their work.
NAD+
Cellular Coenzyme · Energy Floor
The coenzyme behind nearly every energy-producing reaction in the body. Levels decline with age, with metabolic stress, and with weight loss (which is itself a metabolic stress). Direct supplementation rebuilds the floor that mitochondria run on, sirtuins use to enforce healthy aging, and DNA-repair enzymes draw from.
Synergy with Reta: Caloric deficit drains NAD+ pools. Adding it back lets your cells keep up with everything else the protocol is asking them to do. Pairs naturally with MOTS-c (same axis) and with 5-Amino-1MQ (which raises endogenous NAD+ via NNMT inhibition).
Semax
Cognitive Energy · Focus · Mood
A short peptide originally developed for stroke recovery, now used for cognitive lift, mental focus, and motivation. Acts on BDNF and dopamine signaling — different mechanism from the mitochondrial peptides. If your "low energy" includes brain fog, low motivation, or mood drag (not just physical fatigue), Semax is the one to add.
Synergy with Reta: Reta + caloric deficit can flatten cognition for some people in early weeks. Semax is the counterweight — it's not about more physical energy, it's about feeling clear and engaged while the body adapts.
DSIP
Deep Sleep · Recovery Foundation
Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide — supports deep slow-wave sleep, the stage where most physical recovery and metabolic regulation happens. Sleep deprivation alone can blunt GLP-1 weight loss by 20–30% in published data. Use as-needed on rough nights rather than daily.
Synergy with Reta: Sleep is the foundation everything else sits on. If your sleep is ragged, Reta's appetite suppression weakens, energy drops further, and recovery from anything (including training) collapses. DSIP is the cheapest insurance policy in this entire stack.
CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin
Recovery · Lean Mass · GH Pulse
A GHRH (CJC-1295) and GHRP (Ipamorelin) combo that drives natural pulsatile growth hormone release. Benefits: improved recovery from training, lean tissue preservation during caloric deficit, deeper sleep, modest fat loss synergy. This one is conditional — only worth adding if you're training 3+ days a week.
Synergy with Reta: GLP-1 weight loss can include some lean-mass loss, particularly if protein and resistance training are inadequate. CJC/Ipa preserves muscle through the deficit so what you lose is fat, not metabolically active tissue. Most useful in the maintenance phase (Weeks 5+) once Reta is steady.
Safety & Holds

⚠️ When to Hold a Dose

Hold the next dose if any of the following: persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to back — rule out pancreatitis), unexplained tachycardia or palpitations, severe injection-site reaction beyond mild redness, or any new symptom that feels meaningfully off.

Tirz washout: if you're still inside ~10 days of your last tirz dose when starting Reta, ease in — start with the 1 mg M/W/F as planned and watch for cumulative GLP-1 effect (extra nausea, extra appetite suppression). The tirz tail will fade across Week 1 and Reta will fully take over by Week 2.

Hydration + fiber: low-grade nausea and constipation are the two most common Reta side effects. Both respond well to aggressive hydration (~3L/day) and adding fiber (psyllium husk or whole foods). Address before they become a reason to hold a dose.

Storage & Handling

❄️ Refrigerated, Protected, Single-Use

Dry vial (before reconstitution): store refrigerated at 36–46 °F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Reconstituted vial: refrigerate immediately after mixing, always.

Syringe hygiene: fresh single-use syringe for every draw, no exceptions. Needle reuse is the most common cause of injection site reactions and infections.

Site rotation: rotate between lower abdomen (a fingerbreadth from the navel), outer thigh, and love handles. Never the same 1-inch spot two consecutive injections — sites need 48+ hours to recover. With M/W/F dosing, alternating sides each shot works cleanly.
