Nic's Vitality + Strength Stack
Feel alive. Stay strong. Build muscle. Get back in the weight room.

This is the comeback stack — lean version. Goal you said it best: feel alive, stay strong, have energy, build muscle, get back in the weight room. Four compounds running this cycle, all AM, focused on the metabolic + cellular foundation: body comp leaning out, daytime energy floor lifted, fat oxidation amplified, muscle satellite cells re-activated. Two more (KLOW + CJC/Ipa) are queued up as later additions — we layer them in when you're ready to push training harder and want the recovery + GH pulse working alongside the metabolic base.

Five layers, five independent levers:

Retatrutide (20u / ~2.67 mg once weekly Mon AM fasted) — triple-agonist GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon. The fat-loss + body-comp anchor. Once-weekly cadence matches Reta's long half-life cleanly. Tier up or down as needed — we'll watch how the body responds and adjust the dose without changing anything else in the stack.
MOTS-c (1 mg M–F AM fasted) — mitochondrial-encoded peptide that activates AMPK, drives fat oxidation, protects lean mass during the cut, and lifts cellular energy. The "feel alive" lever.
5-Amino-1MQ (~1.67 mg M–F AM fasted) — NNMT inhibitor that frees up NAD+ in adipose + muscle tissue. Accelerates the Reta-driven fat-loss curve AND activates dormant muscle satellite cells (more raw material for building back).
NAD+ (50 mg M/W/F AM, push slow) — cellular fuel for mitochondrial ATP, sirtuin activation, DNA repair. The energy floor underneath everything. After 30, NAD drops every year — restoring it noticeably lifts daytime energy and recovery capacity.
Semax (500 mcg / 10u M/W/F AM) — synthetic ACTH(4-10) heptapeptide. Drives BDNF + NGF upregulation, modulates dopamine + serotonin signaling. The cognitive enhancement layer — while NAD lifts the mitochondrial floor and MOTS lifts the cellular energy floor, Semax lifts the mental floor. Same M/W/F days as Reta + NAD.

How the cycle runs. MOTS + 5-Amino M–F (all five days). NAD + Semax M/W/F (three days/wk). Reta Mon only (once weekly — long half-life, doesn't need M/W/F splits). Sat/Sun off everything. Heaviest day (Mon): 5 AM shots. Wed/Fri: 4 AM shots. Tue/Thu: 2 AM shots. No PM shots this cycle (CJC/Ipa is on the "also considered" list below). Under-5-minute morning routine.

Why 5-Amino-1MQ on your stack: NNMT inhibitor. NNMT (nicotinamide N-methyltransferase) is an enzyme that accumulates in adipose and muscle tissue with age — it sequesters NAD+ precursors into a methylated dead-end, suppressing fat oxidation AND keeping muscle satellite cells dormant. 5-Amino-1MQ blocks NNMT, which does two things at once: (1) frees up NAD+ to fuel fat oxidation (compounds with Reta and MOTS), and (2) reactivates dormant muscle satellite cells — the precursor cells that have to come online for new muscle to be built. This is the "build muscle" lever specifically for someone getting back to training: it gives the body more raw material to lay down lean tissue when training stimulus is applied.
Stack synergy: Reta creates the deficit. 5-Amino keeps the fat Reta liberates actually getting oxidized (instead of recirculating). MOTS activates AMPK. NAD raises the cellular substrate floor. Tip 01 — The Morning Routine
Mon = 5 Shots, Wed/Fri = 4, Tue/Thu = 2

Wait ~30–45 minutes after the AM stack before food. Coffee (black) is fine in the gap.

Tip 02 — Reta Tier Up or Down
How We Iterate the Dose

Starting dose: 20u (~2.67 mg) once weekly Mon AM fasted. Conservative-mid range for a triple agonist — appetite suppression real but not crushing, GI side effects manageable, body comp shift starts cleanly. Once-weekly cadence matches Reta's ~6-day half-life, so blood levels stay steady without needing a split-dose schedule.

When to tier UP:

· Appetite suppression is barely noticeable past Wk 2
· Fat loss has stalled (less than 0.5 lb/wk for 2+ weeks) and protein intake is on point
· You're at 20u without any GI side effects whatsoever — the body's adapted, room to go up

Tier up path: 20u (~2.67 mg) → 25u (~3.33 mg) → 30u (~4 mg). Step up one notch, hold for 2 weeks, reassess.

When to tier DOWN:

· Persistent nausea past Wk 2
· Fat loss faster than 2 lbs/wk sustained (deficit too aggressive — eat more or drop dose)
· Energy crashing or training suffering

Tier down: 20u (~2.67 mg) → 15u (~2 mg) → 10u (~1.33 mg). Pull back one notch, hold for 2 weeks, reassess.

The point: Reta isn't a fixed dose. We start at 20u, watch how the body responds for 2–3 weeks, then adjust. Message me when you have data — how the appetite feels, what the scale is doing, how energy and training are holding up.

Tip 03 — NAD Slow-Push Technique
The Whole Trick Is the Plunger Speed

NAD SubQ can cause warmth, flushing, or a brief "hot" sensation in the chest, neck, or face during or right after the shot. It's harmless — the body clearing the bolus — and usually passes in 1–5 minutes. The fix is pure technique:

Push the plunger slowly. Aim for 15–30 seconds to deliver the full 25 units. Not 3 seconds, not "as fast as the other shots." Slow push gives the tissue time to absorb the bolus gradually — less flushing, less sting.

If flushing is still uncomfortable: slow the push even more next time (30–45 seconds), or split it — deliver half, pause 10 seconds, deliver the rest. After 3–4 shots the body usually acclimates and the flushing fades almost entirely.

The 50 mg dose math: at 5 mL recon (200 mg/mL), 50 mg lands at a clean 25-unit pull (0.25 mL). Easy mark to hit on the syringe.

Tip 04 — Training + Protein for the "Build Muscle" Goal
The Peptides Multiply What You Bring — Bring Real Inputs

The peptides don't build muscle on their own. They amplify the signals your body already has when you put real training stress on muscle + give it real protein to rebuild with. Without those two inputs, the stack drives recovery and energy but leaves the "build muscle" goal unfunded.

Protein floor: aim for 1 g per lb of bodyweight per day, every day. Front-load earlier in the day (breakfast, mid-morning shake) since Reta will suppress appetite later. A whey or casein shake within an hour of waking is the most reliable way to hit the floor when appetite is dialed down.

Training: 3–4 lifting sessions/week is the right intensity for getting back in. Compound lifts (squat, deadlift, press, row) drive the systemic anabolic signal. Start lower than you think you should — coming back to lifting hard from time off is when people get hurt. The metabolic base (Reta + MOTS + 5-Amino + NAD) supports recovery, but the dedicated connective-tissue layer (KLOW) and the overnight GH pulse (CJC/Ipa) are on the "also considered" list — if joints start barking or recovery between sessions feels slow, those are the obvious layers to add.

Hydration: 2.5–3 L/day. Reta suppresses thirst signaling — you won't feel thirsty even when dehydrated. Headaches, fatigue, poor training performance — usually water deficit, not the peptide. Add electrolytes once daily (LMNT, Liquid IV, similar).

Sleep: 7–8 hrs target every night. Sleep depth is the lever everything else compounds on top of — mitochondrial repair, glucose regulation, lean tissue rebuild. No CJC/Ipa this cycle yet, so you don't have the GH-pulse sleep deepening assist; that's exactly the gap that compound closes when we add it.

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

Reta (Mon, 1×/wk): miss Mon morning, take it Tue AM if within 24 hours; otherwise push to next Mon. Don't double up. Once a week means the dose isn't in a hurry — better to delay 1–2 days than skip a week.

MOTS / 5-Amino (M–F): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across the cycle doesn't move the needle.

NAD (M/W/F): miss a scheduled day, skip it. Resume the next M/W/F day. One missed dose doesn't move the cellular energy floor.

Tip 06 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain or right-upper-quadrant pain after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit is too aggressive, eat more
· Lightheadedness or dizziness on standing — could be blood sugar or dehydration; hydrate, eat, monitor

Hold MOTS / 5-Amino if: significant injection-site reaction (redness, heat, swelling past 24 hrs), or any allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes). Both are well-tolerated; individual reactions are rare but possible.

Hold NAD if: severe flushing that doesn't resolve in 5–10 minutes despite slowing the plunger, chest tightness, racing heart, or anxiety-type symptoms post-injection, persistent injection-site reaction past 24 hours.

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own.
