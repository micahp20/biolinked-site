Run it together. Eat well, drink the wine, feel + look great doing it.

The "have it all" stack. Deno and Laura love pasta. They love red wine. They love eating well together — and they want to feel + look great while doing it. That's not a contradiction; it's the entire point of this protocol. Six compounds engineered to give the body the metabolic machinery it needs to enjoy the lifestyle without paying for it: appetite that knows when to stop, insulin sensitivity that routes calories toward muscle and away from fat, cellular energy that holds steady through long dinners and slow mornings, overnight repair that compounds while you sleep. Same morning routine, same kitchen counter, run it together as a couple — the habit is dramatically easier to keep when both partners are on the same wave.

What this means in practice over 8 weeks: the pasta nights stay on the table. The Barolo stays in the glass. Energy goes up, not down, because NAD lifts the cellular floor and MOTS keeps mitochondrial output high through any deficit. Joints and connective tissue stay quiet because KLOW is repairing in the background. You're not fighting the lifestyle — you're upgrading the system that runs underneath it.

Six layers, six independent levers:

Retatrutide (1 mg M/W/F AM fasted) — triple-agonist GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon. The fat-loss + body-comp anchor. Tier up or down as needed — we'll watch how the body responds and adjust the dose without changing anything else in the stack.

NAD+ (50 mg M/W/F AM, push slow) — cellular fuel for mitochondrial ATP, sirtuin activation, DNA repair. The energy floor underneath everything. After 30, NAD drops every year — restoring it noticeably lifts daytime energy and recovery capacity.
CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin (500 mcg PM pre-bed fasted M–F) — amplifies your overnight growth-hormone pulse. This is the muscle-building lever — GH drives IGF-1 which drives protein synthesis and lean tissue rebuild during sleep. Without this, the work in the gym doesn't compound nearly as efficiently.

How the cycle runs. KLOW + MOTS + 5-Amino M–F (all five days). Reta M/W/F (three days/wk, anchored on the same days). NAD M/W/F (three days/wk, anchored same as Reta). CJC/Ipa PM M–F. Sounds like a lot — in practice it's a 5-minute morning routine and one shot before bed.

Tip 02 — Reta Tier Up or Down
How We Iterate the Dose

Starting dose: 1 mg M/W/F (3 mg/wk total). This is the conservative range — appetite suppression real but not crushing, GI side effects manageable, body comp shift starts cleanly. Most people land here.

When to tier UP:

· Appetite suppression is barely noticeable past Wk 2
· Fat loss has stalled (less than 0.5 lb/wk for 2+ weeks) and protein intake is on point
· You're at 1 mg without any GI side effects whatsoever — the body's adapted, room to go up

Tier up path: 1 mg → 1.5 mg M/W/F (15u pull) → 2 mg M/W/F (20u pull). Step up one notch, hold for 2 weeks, reassess.

When to tier DOWN:

· Persistent nausea past Wk 2
· Fat loss faster than 2 lbs/wk sustained (deficit too aggressive — eat more or drop dose)
· Energy crashing or training suffering

Tier down: 1 mg → 0.75 mg (7.5u) → 0.5 mg (5u). Pull back one notch, hold for 2 weeks, reassess.

The point: Reta isn't a fixed dose. We start at 1 mg, watch how the body responds for 2–3 weeks, then adjust. Message me when you have data — how the appetite feels, what the scale is doing, how energy and training are holding up.

Tip 03 — NAD Slow-Push Technique
The Whole Trick Is the Plunger Speed

NAD SubQ can cause warmth, flushing, or a brief "hot" sensation in the chest, neck, or face during or right after the shot. It's harmless — the body clearing the bolus — and usually passes in 1–5 minutes. The fix is pure technique:

Push the plunger slowly. Aim for 15–30 seconds to deliver the full 25 units. Not 3 seconds, not "as fast as the other shots." Slow push gives the tissue time to absorb the bolus gradually — less flushing, less sting.

If flushing is still uncomfortable: slow the push even more next time (30–45 seconds), or split it — deliver half, pause 10 seconds, deliver the rest. After 3–4 shots the body usually acclimates and the flushing fades almost entirely.

The 50 mg dose math: at 5 mL recon (200 mg/mL), 50 mg lands at a clean 25-unit pull (0.25 mL). Easy mark to hit on the syringe.

Tip 04 — CJC/Ipa Fasted PM — The Non-Negotiable
Why the Empty Stomach Window Is the Whole Point

Food — especially carbs and protein — spikes insulin. Insulin shuts down the pituitary's GH release. A fed CJC/Ipa shot is essentially wasted — the peptide will fire the signal, but the pituitary won't release GH while insulin is elevated.

The exact pre-bed routine: dinner → wait 1.5–2 hours → brush teeth → inject CJC/Ipa → lights out within ~30 minutes. The GH pulse needs to fire into the slow-wave-sleep window, not into a still-awake brain.

Why this matters for your "build muscle" goal specifically. CJC/Ipa drives the overnight GH pulse, which drives IGF-1, which drives muscle protein synthesis during sleep. This is the actual muscle-building lever in the stack. If you waste the shot by injecting on a fed stomach, you've still spent the dose but gotten ~10% of the benefit. Better to skip a night entirely than dose it fed.

What to expect: sleep deepens noticeably within the first 4–7 nights — this is the most consistent early signal. Vivid dreams the first 1–2 weeks (deeper sleep showing up). Mild morning hand puffiness may appear briefly as the GH effect ramps; it settles by Wk 2–3. Muscle recovery between training sessions noticeably better by Wk 3–4.

Tip 05 — Training + Protein for the "Build Muscle" Goal
The Peptides Multiply What You Bring — Bring Real Inputs

The peptides don't build muscle on their own. They amplify the signals your body already has when you put real training stress on muscle + give it real protein to rebuild with. Without those two inputs, the stack drives recovery and energy but leaves the "build muscle" goal unfunded.

Protein floor: aim for 1 g per lb of bodyweight per day, every day. Front-load earlier in the day (breakfast, mid-morning shake) since Reta will suppress appetite later. A whey or casein shake within an hour of waking is the most reliable way to hit the floor when appetite is dialed down.

Training: 3–4 lifting sessions/week is the right intensity for getting back in. Compound lifts (squat, deadlift, press, row) drive the systemic anabolic signal CJC/Ipa is amplifying. Start lower than you think you should — coming back to lifting hard from time off is when people get hurt. KLOW + CJC/Ipa keep tendons + connective tissue catching up to the muscle, but they're not magic — respect the ramp.

Hydration: 2.5–3 L/day. Reta suppresses thirst signaling — you won't feel thirsty even when dehydrated. Headaches, fatigue, poor training performance — usually water deficit, not the peptide. Add electrolytes once daily (LMNT, Liquid IV, similar).

Sleep: 7–8 hrs target. CJC/Ipa is going to deepen sleep dramatically — don't waste it by staying up scrolling. Lights out within 30 min of the PM shot, every night.

Tip 06 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

Reta (M/W/F): miss a scheduled day, take it the next morning if within 24 hours; otherwise skip and resume the next M/W/F day. Don't double up.

MOTS / 5-Amino / KLOW (M–F): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across the cycle doesn't move the needle.

NAD (M/W/F): miss a scheduled day, skip it. Resume the next M/W/F day. One missed dose doesn't move the cellular energy floor.

CJC/Ipa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it — do NOT try to take it AM the next day (the pulse only works in the pre-sleep window). Resume the next pre-bed window. If you ate too close to bed and the shot would be on a fed stomach, skip rather than dose — a fed CJC/Ipa shot is wasted.

Tip 07 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain or right-upper-quadrant pain after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit is too aggressive, eat more
· Lightheadedness or dizziness on standing — could be blood sugar or dehydration; hydrate, eat, monitor

Hold MOTS / 5-Amino / KLOW if: significant injection-site reaction (redness, heat, swelling past 24 hrs), or any allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes). All three are well-tolerated; individual reactions are rare but possible.

Hold NAD if: severe flushing that doesn't resolve in 5–10 minutes despite slowing the plunger, chest tightness, racing heart, or anxiety-type symptoms post-injection, persistent injection-site reaction past 24 hours.

Hold CJC/Ipa if: persistent morning hand/face puffiness past Wk 3, joint pain that's new, sustained tingling/numbness in hands — drop dose to 250 mcg (5u) or pause. Sleep getting worse instead of deeper — flag.
