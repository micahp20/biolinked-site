Week-by-Week
What to Expect Across the 12 Weeks
Tip 01 — The Recomp Logic
Why This Stack for 260 → 230 While Holding Muscle

The trap at 54 trying to drop 30 lbs is dropping muscle along with the fat. A standard caloric deficit alone — without the right peptide support — loses about 25–30% of weight as lean tissue. That's how people end up smaller and softer instead of leaner and cut. The stack is built specifically against that failure mode.

Reta does the fat side (appetite + GLP-1/GIP/glucagon-driven oxidation). Tesa does the muscle side (GH/IGF-1 amplification — the single biggest lever for preserving lean tissue in a deficit, especially at 54 when natural GH output has dropped). 5-Amino removes a metabolic brake (NNMT inhibition — the enzyme that gets worse with age and actively suppresses fat oxidation). MOTS-C amplifies your training (AMPK activation makes every workout do more metabolic work).

What you bring: protein intake at 1 g/lb bodyweight (~230–260 g/day) — non-negotiable for muscle preservation; heavy compound lifting 3×/wk minimum (mechanical tension is what tells the body to keep the muscle); and the 5+ days of activity you're already doing. The peptides amplify what you do — they don't replace it.

Tip 02 — First Time Injecting
Start Slow on Day 1, Be Comfortable by Day 5

All four compounds are SubQ — just under the skin, short thin insulin needle (29–31G). Most people describe it as barely noticeable. Day 1 isn't the day to shoot all four.

Day 1: reconstitute and inject ONE compound. Tesa is a good first choice — small dose, daily use, builds the habit. Day 2: add Reta (Mon is the first M/W/F day if you're starting Monday). Day 3: add MOTS-C (this is the fasted one — inject before any food). Day 4: add 5-Amino. You'll be running the full stack by end of Wk 1 with no GI shock.

Mild redness or a small bruise at the injection site is normal and resolves quickly. Always use a fresh needle. Wipe both the vial top and the injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Weeks 1–2
Foundation · Reta GI Tolerance Window

Don't expect dramatic scale changes — the compounds are building. The thing to manage is Reta's first 1–2 weeks of GI signaling: mild nausea and appetite suppression are normal, usually clears by end of Wk 2. If nausea is severe, drop to 0.5 mg (5 units) for a few doses before resuming the 1 mg schedule. Don't power through — let the gut adapt.

Tesa contribution shows up first as deeper sleep within 7–10 days — the nocturnal GH pulse amplification. 5-Amino + MOTS-C are quiet right now; both work on cellular signaling that takes a couple weeks to compound.

Log baseline: weight, waist circumference, how you feel in workouts. These matter for evaluating Wk 4 vs. Wk 1 changes.

Weeks 3–4
First Visible Shifts

Where most clients notice the first real change. Appetite control is firm — you're naturally eating less and not white-knuckling it. Fat loss starts to register on the scale and in the mirror. Energy steadier through the day from the MOTS-C / Tesa GH layer.

Recovery between training sessions should be noticeably faster — the GH amplification + AMPK activation are working synergistically with your active 5-day schedule. Soreness clears in 24 hrs instead of 48.

Don't push the deficit aggressively. Reta does the appetite work for you — let it. Hit protein at 1 g/lb daily. This is where preserving the muscle starts paying off.

Weeks 5–6
Peak Fat Loss · 5-Amino Wraps

The steepest part of the fat loss curve. 5-Amino vial finishes end of Wk 6 (exact 30-dose fit). The NNMT-block contribution carries forward in cellular metabolism for several weeks even after the compound stops — it's not a switch that flips off the moment dosing ends.

Weight should be tracking close to 2–2.5 lbs/wk down at this point. If you're seeing less than ~1.5 lbs/wk over a rolling 2-week average, that's the signal to revisit the deficit (probably under-eating around training, which is more common than over-eating) or consider the Reta titration up to 1.5 mg using the vial 2 buffer.

End of Wk 6: 5-Amino done. Reta + Tesa + MOTS-C carry into Phase 2.

Weeks 7–8
Mid-Cycle · MOTS-C Wraps End of Wk 8

Stack is now Reta + Tesa + MOTS-C. The fat loss curve continues but the slope softens a bit without 5-Amino. Stay patient — this is normal in a phased protocol and Tesa is still building toward its payoff window.

MOTS-C vials finish end of Wk 8 (exact 40-dose fit). The AMPK activation contribution to training stays in the cellular machinery for ~1–2 weeks after dosing ends.

Mid-cycle reassessment: if you're tracking ~2 lbs/wk and feeling good, hold the plan. If progress has slowed, consider the Reta titration up using vial 2 buffer (1.5 mg = 15 units instead of 10).

Weeks 9–10
Reta + Tesa Alone · The GH Payoff Begins

Two compounds now — Reta carrying the fat-loss side, Tesa carrying the lean-tissue + visceral-fat side. This is the window where the Tesa-specific payoff starts compounding: visceral fat reduction becomes visible (midsection composition shifts), skin quality improves, muscle definition starts showing through.

Sleep should still be deep — Tesa carries the GH pulse on its own without MOTS-C support. If sleep dips for a week, that's the body adapting; usually self-corrects.

Don't add cardio aggressively this late. The compounds are doing the metabolic work — let them. Heavy lifting stays the priority for preserving muscle definition.

Weeks 11–12
Final Stretch · Lock in 230 (or Below)

Steepest part of the body comp curve in the Tesa clinical data. Whatever was set up in Wks 4–10 visibly consolidates here. Visceral fat, skin quality, muscle definition all compound.

End-of-cycle questions: Where did the scale land? How does the mirror look vs. Wk 1? Strength in the gym holding? These answers determine whether to (a) run a maintenance cycle to lock in body comp, (b) repeat with adjustments, or (c) take 4–6 weeks off and reassess.

If the goal of 230 has been hit and you want to push to 220, you have the framework now — a similar 8–12 week stack run at lower body weight will hit different targets. But cycle off first.

Execution & Monitoring
Protein · Training · Sleep · Hydration
Tip 03 — Protein at 1 g/lb
The Non-Negotiable for Holding Muscle

At Steven's weight (260 starting, 230 target), protein target is 230–260 g/day. This is the single most important lifestyle variable for the "lean while cut" outcome. Tesa amplifies GH/IGF-1 but it needs amino acid substrate to actually build/preserve muscle. Underfeed protein and the GH signal hits a wall.

Practical split: 40–50 g protein at each of 4–5 meals across the day. Whey or protein powder for 1–2 of them is fine. Reta will suppress appetite — if you're not careful you'll hit calories low and protein lower. Eat protein even when you're not hungry. Especially around training (pre and post).

Tip 04 — Lift Heavy 3×/Wk Minimum
Mechanical Tension Preserves Muscle in a Deficit

Your 5+ active days a week is great for total energy expenditure. But the muscle preservation signal comes specifically from heavy compound lifting — squat, deadlift, bench, row, overhead press patterns with progressive load. Not steady-state cardio. Not just yoga or hiking.

Three lifting sessions per week (e.g. M/W/F or Tu/Th/Sat), full body or upper/lower split, working sets in the 4–8 rep range with progressive overload. The other 2–3 active days can be anything — walking, biking, sport. The lifting is the muscle insurance policy.

Tip 05 — Sleep is Compound
Tesa Amplifies the Nocturnal GH Pulse — It Needs Sleep to Work

Sleep self-reported at 3/5 going in. Tesa works almost entirely through amplifying the natural GH surge that happens during deep sleep stages. If sleep is the bottleneck, you're leaving the biggest Tesa benefit on the table.

Targets: 7–8 hours consistently; same bedtime / wake time within 30 min on weekdays; no alcohol within 3 hrs of bed (the 1–2 drinks/wk is fine spacing-wise — just not the last thing before sleep). If sleep is still bad by Wk 3–4, that's the moment to add a sleep peptide (DSIP PRN, etc.) — flag for follow-up.

Tip 06 — Hydration + Reta GI
Constipation is the Most Common Reta Side Effect

GLP-1 agonists slow gut motility. Combined with a deficit (less food volume), constipation is the most common Reta complaint after the first 1–2 weeks of nausea pass. Hydration + fiber address it.

Targets: 100 oz water per day minimum (more if you sweat heavily training); fiber from vegetables + a daily psyllium husk or similar if veggie intake is inconsistent. If constipation persists past Wk 3 despite hydration, magnesium citrate 400 mg before bed helps.

Tip 07 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild nausea / reduced appetite the first 1–2 weeks of Reta — subsides
· Constipation on Reta — hydration + fiber addresses it
· Mild injection-site redness — rotate sites and use proper SubQ technique
· Slight water retention first 1–2 weeks of Tesa — normal GH effect, resolves
· Mild tingling in hands occasionally on Tesa — normal GH effect, fades

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique issue or contamination
· Severe nausea or vomiting past Wk 2 of Reta — pause and drop to 0.5 mg
· Fat loss faster than ~3 lbs/wk sustained — back off Reta dose or add calories around training; lean tissue is at risk
· Persistent fatigue or workouts feeling flat — usually under-eating; add cals around training
· Numbness/tingling in hands beyond Wk 4 — can happen at higher GHRH exposure; flag
