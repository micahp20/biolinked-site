Execution Tips
Fasted Window · Pairing · Ramp Logic
Tip 01 — Critical
Tirzepatide Ramp · Start Light

Always start at 10u (0.5 mg) for 2–3 weeks regardless of how it feels in Week 1. Nausea is dose-dependent — the longer the body has at 0.5 mg, the smoother the step up.

If nausea appears at any dose, drop back to the previous tolerated dose and hold there for the cycle. The "lowest effective dose" isn't a hedge — it's the working dose for someone who responds well.

Tip 02 — Fasted Discipline
KLOW & Tesamorelin Both Need Empty Stomach

KLOW AM — fasted morning shot. Wait 30–45 min before eating. Coffee and water are fine.

Tesamorelin pre-bed — fasted 2+ hours after last meal. If dinner was at 7 PM, inject at 9 PM or later. Food — especially carbs — spikes blood glucose and blunts the natural GH pulse Tesa is designed to amplify. Skip the dose if you snacked within 2 hours; the shot won't do what it's supposed to do.

Tip 03 — The GHK-Cu Weekend Bridge
Why Standalone GHK-Cu Sat / Sun

KLOW gives you GHK-Cu Monday through Friday as part of the blend. The weekend standalone GHK-Cu fills the 2-day gap — continuous exposure across all 7 days maximizes collagen synthesis, fibroblast activity, and the skin payoff.

Skipping the weekend shots cuts the collagen results by ~30% over the cycle. Set a Saturday + Sunday morning alarm and treat it like any other shot day.

Tip 04 — Tirzepatide on Mon + Fri
Why 2×/Week, Not Daily

Tirzepatide has a long half-life (~5 days), so 2×/week spacing keeps steady blood levels without overdoing it. Mon + Fri is the cleanest spacing — 3 days between doses, similar at the back end before the next week.

Don't double-dose if you miss one — just skip and resume the next scheduled day. Mon + Fri also lines up with the natural eating-pattern rhythm (heavier meals on weekends), where the appetite suppression is most useful.

Tip 05 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild appetite drop from Tirzepatide starting Day 3–5
· Vivid dreams or slightly disrupted sleep in Week 1 as the GH axis recalibrates
· Small injection-site redness or bruising — rotate sites and use proper technique
· Mild flush or skin tingle from KLOW — resolves within 30 min

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Persistent nausea or vomiting past Week 2 on Tirzepatide — drop dose
· Severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to the back — pancreatitis risk)
· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique issue or contamination

Tip 01 — Why SS-31 + Humanin for Migraines
The Mitochondrial Hypothesis of Migraine

Conventional migraine treatment focuses on blood vessels and neurotransmitters (the triptans, the CGRP inhibitors). But the leading cause theory in current research is that migraine sufferers have measurably impaired mitochondrial function — the energy production inside brain cells isn't keeping up with demand. When the energy supply falls short, the brain becomes hypersensitive, neurons fire abnormally, and the cascade that produces a migraine kicks off.

SS-31 and Humanin are the two most direct mitochondrial-repair peptides we have access to:

  • SS-31 binds cardiolipin on the inner mitochondrial membrane. Cardiolipin keeps the electron transport chain (the engine that produces ATP, your cellular energy) running cleanly. When cardiolipin degrades, energy production drops. SS-31 stabilizes it.
  • Humanin is a peptide encoded inside mitochondrial DNA itself. It protects cells from oxidative stress and prevents apoptosis in stressed neurons. It's been studied for Alzheimer's, stroke, and ALS — all conditions with mitochondrial dysfunction at their core.

The two together address both the membrane (SS-31) and the cell (Humanin). For someone with chronic migraines, this is the foundation. Don't expect overnight changes — mitochondrial repair compounds over weeks. Track migraine frequency + severity in a simple journal. Most people who respond start noticing a difference around Week 3–4.

Tip 02 — Phase Sequencing
Why MOTS-C Comes AFTER, Not Alongside

This is the single most important design choice in your protocol. Most people who buy MOTS-C take it right away because it's an "exercise mimetic" that drives fat oxidation. We're not doing that for you.

The metaphor: MOTS-C is the gas pedal of the mitochondrial engine. SS-31 + Humanin are the tune-up. If you floor the gas pedal on an engine that needs servicing, two things happen: (1) the engine runs poorly, (2) you stress the damaged parts further. That's a particularly bad combination if your mitochondria are already stressed enough to trigger migraines.

The sequence:

  • Weeks 1–4: SS-31 + Humanin repair and protect the mitochondrial machinery. No MOTS-C yet.
  • Weeks 5–8: Engine is now in good shape. MOTS-C steps in and activates it — AMPK signaling drives fat oxidation, insulin sensitivity, exercise-mimetic effects.

The body composition + metabolic outcomes of Phase 2 are compounded by the foundation Phase 1 built. Running MOTS-C without the repair phase first would still produce some result, but with more side effects and less durability. Trust the sequence.

Tip 03 — Why CJC/Ipa Is Mandatory for Muscle
GLP-1s Took Muscle. CJC/Ipa Builds It Back.

Real talk: Tirzepatide and the GLP-1 class are famous for muscle loss alongside fat loss. Across the major trials, ~25–40% of total weight lost on Tirz comes from lean tissue (not fat). For women specifically — with naturally lower testosterone and more difficulty building muscle from baseline — this hits harder.

You're now at a good weight but probably with less muscle than you started Phase 1 with. Just lifting weights won't fix this without help. Your body's signaling for muscle growth (GH, IGF-1, satellite cell activation) is suppressed compared to pre-Tirz baseline.

CJC + Ipamorelin amplifies your natural GH pulse during deep sleep — the time when most muscle protein synthesis happens. The combo doesn't add exogenous GH; it tells your pituitary to do its job more strongly. After 8 weeks at M–F pre-bed dosing, you'll have noticeably better recovery between workouts, deeper sleep, and visible muscle gain in the areas you train.

Stack with training: 3–4 lifting sessions per week is plenty. You don't need to live in the gym. Focus on compound movements (squats, deadlifts, presses, rows), train with intent, and let CJC/Ipa + protein + sleep do the rest.

Tip 04 — 5-Amino-1MQ for Stubborn Fat
Pre-Workout Timing Is the Whole Game

Stubborn fat — the lower-abdomen and visceral fat that diet alone won't shift — doesn't respond to caloric deficit the same way other fat does. It's metabolically slower, blood-flow restricted, and resistant to standard fat oxidation. The way to attack it is to combine a metabolic enhancer with active muscle demand — that's what 5-Amino-1MQ + a workout does.

How it works: 5-Amino-1MQ inhibits the NNMT enzyme, which protects your cellular NAD+ pool. More NAD+ = more energy for working muscles + more efficient fat oxidation in the exact tissues you're using. Pre-workout dosing means the compound is peaking when your muscles are demanding fuel — and the body preferentially pulls from fat stores under that combined signal.

How to dose it:

  • 20 units (5 mg) injected 30–45 minutes before your hardest training session
  • 3–4 sessions per week (leg days + harder lift days benefit most)
  • Subcutaneous abdomen — rotate sites

Tip 05 — AM Cluster Logistics
Phase 1 vs. Phase 2 Morning Routine

Phase 1 mornings (Wks 1–4): SS-31 + Humanin together, 5 min. Same window, rotate to different abdomen quadrants. Doesn't have to be fasted — can be before or after breakfast.

Phase 2 mornings (Wks 5–8): MOTS-C is fasted only — first thing AM, water/black coffee, wait 30–45 min before eating.

Pre-workout 5-Amino: 30–45 min before training. If you train AM fasted, this works alongside MOTS-C (Phase 2). If you train PM, dose 5-Amino separately.

Pre-bed CJC/Ipa: 30–60 min before lights out. Empty stomach (2+ hrs after last meal). Skip if you ate late.

Tip 06 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites and use clean technique
· Slight warmth / "fullness" feeling 20–30 min after CJC/Ipa — normal GH response
· Improved sleep depth + vivid dreams Week 1–2 — CJC/Ipa kicking in
· Mild flushing 5–10 min after MOTS-C — normal AMPK-related vascular response

Migraine-specific:

· Slightly increased migraine in Week 1 — uncommon but possible as mito metabolism shifts. Usually settles by Week 2.
· If migraines worsen significantly past Week 2 — flag to BioLinked, consider pausing SS-31 specifically (rare but documented response in some sensitive individuals)

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique or contamination
· Persistent fatigue or workouts feeling flat — usually under-eating; add protein/carbs around training
· Numbness/tingling in hands beyond Week 4 — can happen with GH compounds, flag to reassess CJC/Ipa dose
