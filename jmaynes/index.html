Storage & Handling

Before reconstitution (dry powder)

Store all vials in the refrigerator at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep away from light and moisture. Do not freeze. Shelf life is typically 12–24 months unopened. Store KLOW, GHK-Cu, Tesamorelin, and Tirzepatide separately labeled.

After reconstitution

All reconstituted vials must be refrigerated. KLOW and GHK-Cu — use within 30 days. Tesamorelin — use within 30 days. Tirzepatide — use within 30 days. Never freeze any reconstituted peptide. Use a fresh syringe for every draw.

Bacteriostatic (BAC) water

Use sterile BAC water for all four compounds. BAC water's benzyl alcohol preservative extends shelf life of reconstituted peptides to 30 days. Do not substitute plain sterile water — it does not preserve reconstituted compounds.

Syringes & injection sites

Use 29–31 gauge insulin syringes (0.5 mL or 1 mL barrel). Never reuse a syringe. Dispose in a sharps container. Rotate injection sites every day — abdomen, outer thigh, and upper arm all work. Keep morning (KLOW/GHK) and evening (Tesamorelin) sites separate.

What to Monitor
Normal: Mild nausea with Tirzepatide in weeks 1–2 — take in the morning, start at 10 units only, and titrate up slowly once your body adjusts. Eating a small meal after injection helps.
Normal: Slight redness or minor bruising at injection sites — rotate daily and use proper SubQ technique (pinch skin, 45° angle, slow injection).
Normal: Increased energy in the morning from KLOW's BPC-157/TB-500 component — this is the healing response activating. Sleep may deepen in week 1 from Tesamorelin.
Normal: Slight golden tint to reconstituted GHK-Cu solution — this is the copper component and completely normal. Clear to faint gold is correct.
Monitor: Persistent nausea or vomiting beyond week 2 with Tirzepatide — reduce to 10 units and hold there for an additional week before attempting to increase.
Monitor: Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — possible technique issue or contamination. Do not reuse that syringe or vial. Contact BioLinked.
Monitor: Significant water retention or joint discomfort — may indicate GH response to Tesamorelin. Reduce Tesamorelin to every other day temporarily and reach out to your consultant.
