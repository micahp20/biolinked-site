Featured Tips for Your Reset
Tip 01 — Why M/W/F (Not Once-Weekly) Is the Whole Point
The Smoother Curve Is Why You'll Feel Different on Reta

Reta has a half-life of about six days, so the once-weekly clinical pattern is mathematically defensible — but that pattern creates a sharp peak in the 24–48 hours after each shot. That peak is when most people experience nausea, GI upset, and the post-shot fatigue you're already familiar with from tirz.

Splitting the same weekly total into three smaller M/W/F doses smooths the curve. Lower peaks, fewer side effects, more even daytime energy across the whole week instead of front-loaded crash days.

For you specifically: the reason you're switching off tirz is the energy crash. Putting you on weekly Reta would replicate that exact problem with a stronger drug. Micro-dosing breaks the loop — same therapeutic effect, no shot-day crash.

Tip 02 — Tirz → Reta Transition Taper (1 Week)
Clear the Receptors Before You Start Reta

You don't taper Reta into Tirz — you clear tirz first, then start Reta clean. Tirz half-life is ~5 days, so the effect carries through naturally for almost a week after your last shot.

  • Step 1: take your last regular tirz shot on your normal day.
  • Step 2: the following week, skip the tirz shot entirely. Don't replace it with anything. The tirz effect is still on board across most of that week (declining).
  • Step 3: the Monday after that skip-week, start Reta at Tier 1 (1 mg / 10u). Wednesday + Friday repeat.

Why not overlap: tirz and Reta both bind GLP-1 (and GIP) receptors. Running both at full doses simultaneously double-occupies the receptors and can amplify side effects (nausea, fatigue) without amplifying benefit. The 1-week gap lets tirz clear so Reta lands on uncrowded receptors.

Tip 03 — The Ramp Logic (3 → 6 mg/wk Over 4 Weeks)
Conservative on Purpose · Maintenance, Not Max-Dose

The ramp is intentionally gentle: 3 mg/wk for 2 weeks → 4.5 mg/wk for 2 weeks → 6 mg/wk for 2 weeks. By Wk 5 you're at maintenance and you hold it through end of cycle.

Clinical Reta dose-finding studies have climbed to 12 mg+/wk — but those are weight-loss-max studies. Your goal isn't max weight loss — you already had tirz delivering that. Your goal is retaining the body-comp benefit while restoring daytime energy. 6 mg/wk is plenty for that target. If 6 mg gets you to a place where you feel good and your weight is stable, you don't need more.

If a tier feels hard: hold it an extra week before climbing. The ramp isn't a deadline — it's a path. Holding Tier 1 for 3 weeks instead of 2 doesn't break anything; it just shifts your maintenance window by a week.

What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For
Tip 05 — Normal Effects (No Action Needed)
Expected on Reta Micro-Dose
  • Mild appetite suppression starting Wk 1 — the signal you want.
  • Mild nausea or mild GI upset first 2–3 days of each tier climb — settles within 48–72 hrs as the body acclimates to the new dose.
  • Cleaner daytime energy starting around Wk 2 (vs the tirz crash you're leaving behind) — the glucagon-arm effect landing.
  • Slight injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites, clean technique.
  • Mild flushing / warmth in the 30 min after the shot — vascular response, normal, fades.
Tip 06 — Watch For (Message BioLinked)
Anything Below Warrants a Quick Check-In
  • Severe nausea, vomiting, or appetite loss that doesn't settle within 72 hrs of a tier climb — hold the current tier or step back to the previous tier, then flag.
  • Injection site hot, swollen, or red >24 hrs — possible technique or contamination issue.
  • Energy got worse on Reta than it was on tirz — should be improving across Wks 2–3; flag if reversed.
  • Sustained abdominal pain (especially upper-right or upper-mid abdomen) — rare with Reta but warrants ruling out pancreatitis / gallbladder; pause and contact your physician.
  • Any allergic reaction symptoms (rash, itching, throat tightness) — very rare but stop and seek medical attention immediately.
  • Heart rate consistently elevated at rest — uncommon at micro-doses but worth flagging.
