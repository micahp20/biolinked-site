Storage
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected

Dry vials: refrigerate at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Stable for many months sealed.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Mix one Tirz vial at a time and finish before opening the next.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Tirz Tips
Read Before Day 1
Tip 01 — Tirz Onboarding
What the First Few Weeks Look Like

Week 1: appetite suppression starts within 2–3 days. Some mild nausea on shot day (Sunday) and the day after is common — manageable with smaller meals and lower fat content for 24–48 hrs after the shot. Skip greasy / fried food the day after dose day. Hydrate aggressively.

Weeks 2–3: initial GI side effects settle. Appetite control feels like baseline. Food noise quiets noticeably. Some mild fatigue is normal as the body acclimates.

Weeks 4–6: fat loss curve takes hold. Aim for 1–1.5 lbs/week — faster than 2 lbs/week sustained means the deficit is too aggressive.

Weeks 7–8: decision point. Extend with another vial run, taper to maintenance (e.g. 0.5 mg every other week), or wrap and reassess.

Tip 02 — Hydration on Tirz
The Thing Most People Underestimate

Target: 2.5–3 L of water per day. Tirz suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite — you won't feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people end up with headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor energy and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit.

Add electrolytes once daily. Sodium, potassium, magnesium. LMNT, Liquid IV, or similar work fine.

Coffee doesn't count. Drink it on top of your water target, not instead of.

Tip 03 — Missed Tirz Dose
What to Do If You Skip

If you miss your scheduled day, take it within 4 days of the missed day and reset the schedule to that new day. If more than 4 days have passed, skip that week's dose entirely and resume next week on your original day — don't double up.

Tip 04 — Red Flags
When to Pause Tirz

Hold the next Tirz dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain or RUQ pain after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit is too aggressive, eat more

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own.
