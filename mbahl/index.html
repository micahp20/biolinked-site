Who this is built for. You run the biggest club basketball program in Colorado, you coach kids year-round with basically no off-season, and you're a former D-II All-American who still trains hard in your own gym. The goal here is simple: hold onto (and add to) the muscle, get leaner, and get your energy back up — without adding one more thing you have to think about all day. Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Peptide vials (Reta, MOTS, CJC, 5-Amino, KLOW): keep dry vials in the fridge (36–46°F). Once reconstituted, refrigerate and use within ~30 days. Reconstitute one vial at a time — open the next only when the current one runs dry.

Testosterone (oil): store at room temperature, out of direct light — it does not need refrigeration and can get thick/cloudy if too cold (warm it in your hand before drawing if so). Never add water to it.

Reconstitution amounts (peptides): Reta 3 mL, MOTS 3 mL, CJC 2 mL, 5-Amino 1 mL, KLOW 3 mL. Add the water slowly down the inside wall of the vial — don't blast the powder — then swirl gently to dissolve. Never shake hard.

Every shot: fresh single-use syringe, wipe the stopper and your skin with alcohol, rotate injection sites across abdomen quadrants (and delt/quad if you run testosterone IM).

Tip 02 — Injection Walkthrough (You're New to This)
SubQ Is Easy — Here's Every Step

Almost everything here is a SubQ shot — just under the skin in the fatty layer of your abdomen, with the smallest needle in clinical use. It's genuinely painless once you've done it twice.

Step-by-step (every shot):

1. Wash hands. Take the vial out of the fridge (testosterone lives at room temp).
2. Wipe the rubber stopper with an alcohol pad; let it dry.
3. Pull the needle cap, push through the stopper, and draw to the right unit mark: Test 35u, Reta 30u, 5-Amino 20u, KLOW 15u, CJC 10u, MOTS 7.5u. Glance at the line every time — don't auto-pilot.
4. Wipe a spot ~2" from your navel; let it dry.
5. Pinch an inch of skin, insert at 45° (90° if you have more padding), push the plunger slowly.
6. Pull the needle straight out, drop the whole syringe in the sharps container. Don't massage the site.

Testosterone is thicker (it's oil): it draws and pushes slower than the peptides — be patient. Warming the vial in your hand helps. You can run it SubQ with the same insulin syringe, or IM into the delt/quad with a slightly longer needle if you prefer.

Multiple AM shots: use a different abdomen quadrant for each (upper-right, upper-left, lower-right, lower-left) so nothing stacks in one spot.

Tip 03 — Sleep, Protein, Alcohol
The Three Levers That Decide Your Results

Sleep is where the magic happens. Testosterone and the CJC/Ipamorelin GH pulse do their building overnight. You rated sleep 3/5 — get the room cold and dark, screens off 30 min before bed, and take the magnesium glycinate from the Supplies tab. Better sleep alone will amplify every compound in this stack.

Protein feeds the anabolic signal. Testosterone + GH tell your body to build — give it material. Aim for ~1 g protein per pound of target bodyweight, spread across the day. Without it, you get the signal but not the muscle.

Alcohol is the one working against you. At 3–7/week it's showing up on your labs (MCV, ferritin) and it directly blunts overnight GH and testosterone signaling — the exact processes this stack is trying to amplify. You don't have to quit; trimming toward the low end and keeping it off pre-training and pre-injection nights will speed up every result.

Tip 04 — Week-by-Week Timeline
What to Expect Over the Cycle

Weeks 1–2: loading phase. Reta at the 15u starter dose — some appetite drop, maybe mild nausea the first couple of Mondays (eat lighter that day, hydrate). CJC/Ipa deepens sleep almost immediately — this is often the first thing you notice. Don't judge testosterone yet; it builds over weeks. Start logging in the tracker now so the habit sticks.

Weeks 3–5: testosterone starts to register — steadier energy, better gym drive, libido up, mood more level. Reta climbs to 22u then 30u; the metabolic/fat-loss effect from Reta + MOTS + 5-Amino kicks in — you'll notice the waistline and how clothes fit before the scale moves much. Energy from MOTS/5-Amino is well established by now.

Weeks 6–10: the recomposition window — leaner and holding/adding muscle at the same time. KLOW runs out around week 8; if the joints feel good (they usually do), reorder to carry it. 5-Amino runs out around week 10 — reorder if the fat loss is rolling. Book your 8–12 week recheck here (free T, E2, hematocrit, A1c, lipids).

Weeks 12–16+: steady state. MOTS and CJC run ~16 weeks, testosterone ~14, Reta ~20. Reorder testosterone and Reta ahead of time so there's no gap. This is an ongoing, run-till-empty protocol — we adjust doses based on the recheck and how you feel, and keep what's working.

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Testosterone (Mon/Thu): if you miss a day, take it within a day or two and resume the Mon/Thu rhythm. Missing one shot barely moves your levels — don't double up.

Reta (Monday): take it within 24–48 hrs if you miss Monday, then resume the following Monday. More than that, skip and pick up the next Monday. Never double up.

MOTS / KLOW / 5-Amino (AM): take later that day if you can; if the day passes, skip and resume the next scheduled day.

CJC/Ipa (PM): miss a night, skip it — don't shift it to the morning (the pre-sleep timing is the point). If you ate too close to bed, skip rather than dose fed.

A few missed days won't derail anything. Pick up the schedule on the next planned day — consistency over weeks matters far more than any single shot.

Tip 06 — Red Flags · When to Message First
Signals to Watch For

On testosterone, message me if you notice: persistent puffiness/facial bloating or moodiness (possible high estradiol — we check E2 on recheck), or if the follow-up hematocrit is trending toward 50% (we lower the dose or you donate blood). These are routine adjustments, not emergencies.

On Reta, hold the next dose if: nausea or vomiting that won't settle within 24 hrs, or severe abdominal pain radiating to the back. These are uncommon at your dose but worth knowing.

Injection sites: a little redness at the pinhole is normal. Warmth, spreading redness, swelling, or pus that's getting worse is a possible infection — stop that site and message me.

General rule: anything that feels off, message me before pushing through or stopping on your own. We adjust based on what's actually happening — that's what the check-ins and the recheck panel are for.
