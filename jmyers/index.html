Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry vials (before reconstitution): refrigerate at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Stable for many months sealed.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Reconstitute one vial at a time — on Day 1, mix Reta vial #1, CJC/Ipa vial #1, and the 5-Amino vial. Around week 4, mix Reta vial #2 + CJC/Ipa vial #2 when the first ones run dry.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. With 2 shots in the AM cluster on Reta days, use different quadrants for each.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Day 1
Tip 01 — The Daily Rhythm
AM Block + PM Shot, 5 Days a Week

AM (fasted, first thing after waking): 5-Amino every M–F. Add Reta on M / W / F only. Wait 30–60 min before eating — the fasted state is what makes 5-Amino work best (low insulin = best NNMT inhibition).

PM (pre-sleep, 2+ hrs after dinner): CJC/Ipa every M–F. The 2+ hour fasted gap is non-negotiable — food spikes insulin and blunts the GH pulse the peptide exists to amplify. Routine: dinner → wait 2 hrs → brush teeth → CJC injection → lights out within 30–60 min.

Saturday + Sunday: nothing. The 5-on / 2-off rhythm is intentional — gives receptors a reset window so the compounds don't lose sensitivity. Prep syringes for Monday's AM cluster on Sunday night to make the week-start frictionless.

Time budget: AM block on a Reta day is 2 shots, ~2–3 min once routine. PM is 1 shot, <1 min. Total ~5 min/day of actual injection time across the cycle.

What to Expect Across the 8 Weeks

Weeks 1–2 (acclimation): appetite shifts as Sema clears and Reta takes over. Some mild GI adjustment days 3–7 is normal — eat smaller, lower-fat meals on Reta days, hydrate aggressively. The very first noticeable effect is usually CJC/Ipa — deeper sleep within 3–5 days. Don't expect dramatic scale movement yet; your body is recalibrating.

Weeks 3–5 (the engine kicks in): this is where the math vs. Sema becomes obvious. Glucagon-receptor activation is now fully online; 5-Amino-1MQ is unlocking adipose storage. Most clients in your position report 2–4 lbs of clean fat loss across these two weeks. Critically, your gym sessions feel better, not worse — that's the GH/IGF-1 axis from CJC/Ipa preserving muscle even as you cut.

Weeks 6–8 (consolidation): the fat loss accumulated through weeks 3–5 consolidates, and you're likely approaching or hitting 138. Take stock at week 8: photos, measurements, strength numbers, energy. The goal isn't just the scale — it's the composition. 138 with more lean tissue is the real win.

Tip 03 — Why an Off-Cycle Matters
8 Weeks On → 4–6 Off → Reassess

After 8–12 weeks on any GLP-1 (Sema, Tirz, Reta), your body adapts. Receptors downregulate, appetite suppression dulls, and the same dose stops doing what it did week 1. Cycling off for 4–6 weeks lets receptor sensitivity reset. When you cycle back on, often at a lower dose than where you left off, the appetite suppression returns at full strength.

A pattern that keeps showing up in the GLP-1 community: people's second and third cycles produce cleaner results than their first — because they're working with fresh receptor sensitivity instead of fighting adaptation. The 8-on / 4–6-off rhythm has become the de-facto sustainable approach.

What to lean on during the off-cycle: training (4–5×/wk you're already doing), protein floor (~110–120 g/day), sleep, and your existing HRT base. The lean tissue protected by CJC/Ipa stays. That's the whole point of running it alongside Reta — when Reta pauses, the muscle you built doesn't go anywhere.

Long-term pattern many people land on: 8 weeks on → 4–6 weeks off → reassess. Some maintain their happy weight indefinitely with a 0.25–0.5 mg microdose every 10–14 days during off-cycles. The protocol becomes a tool you use intermittently, not a crutch you stay on forever.

Tip 04 — Injection Refresher
You've Done This Before — Quick Refresh

You have prior injection experience — this is just a quick refresh. The injection is short, thin, SubQ — just under the skin in the fatty layer of your abdomen. Insulin syringe, smallest needle in clinical use.

Step-by-step:

1. Wash hands. Pull vial out of fridge.
2. Wipe rubber stopper with alcohol pad. Let dry.
3. Pull needle cap. Push needle through stopper, pull plunger to 10 units (0.10 mL) for all three compounds. Glance at the mark every shot — don't auto-pilot.
4. Wipe a spot on your abdomen (~2″ from navel) with alcohol pad. Let dry.
5. Pinch about an inch of skin between fingers. Push needle in at 45° (or 90° if more padding). Push plunger slowly until empty.
6. Pull needle straight out. Don't massage. Drop the whole syringe in the sharps container.

Reta days (2 shots in the AM cluster): use different quadrants of your abdomen for each shot. Upper-right, upper-left, lower-right, lower-left — rotate. Never stack 2 shots in the same 1″ spot.

Pro tip: on Sunday night, lay out Monday's AM syringes + label them with sharpie ("Reta", "5-Amino"). Turns the morning from rushed to routine.

Tip 05 — Protein + Hydration
The Two Numbers That Keep the Muscle On

Protein target: ~110–120 g/day. This is non-negotiable for keeping muscle through the cut. At your goal weight (138 lb), aim for roughly 0.8–0.9 g per lb of bodyweight. Reta suppresses appetite, so front-load protein early in the day before suppression peaks — a protein shake within an hour of waking is the easy lever. With your existing HRT-T base + 4–5×/wk training, hitting this floor is what guarantees the 138 number is fat loss, not muscle loss.

Hydration target: 3–4 L/day. Reta suppresses thirst alongside appetite — you won't feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people get headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor workout performance and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit. Add electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day, ideally pre-training.

Creatine stays. Your existing 5 g/day creatine is perfectly aligned with this protocol — keeps muscle hydration + ATP regeneration high during the deficit. Don't drop it.

Tip 06 — Alcohol on the Stack
3–7 Drinks/Week Is Fine — One Small Adjustment

Your 3–7 drinks/wk is moderate and well-controlled — not a meaningful issue for the protocol overall. Nothing in the stack has a dangerous alcohol interaction.

The one small adjustment worth knowing: alcohol modestly blunts overnight GH pulse amplitude. That works against CJC/Ipa specifically. When possible, time drinks to non-training days, or earlier in the evening — not within 2–3 hours of your PM CJC injection. At your current intake you don't need to change anything; just be aware of timing.

Don't drink the night before a Reta shot. Compounds the nausea risk meaningfully, especially in weeks 1–2 of the transition off Sema.

Tip 07 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Reta (M/W/F): if you miss one, take it within 24 hours and resume the schedule. More than 24 hours: skip and pick up at the next scheduled day. Don't double up.

CJC/Ipa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it. Don't shift to AM the next day — the pre-sleep window is what makes it work. Resume the next pre-bed window. If you ate too close to bed and the shot would be on a fed stomach, skip rather than dose — a fed CJC shot is wasted.

5-Amino (M–F AM): miss one morning, take it later that day if you can. If the whole day passes, skip and resume the next day. Not strictly time-locked, but earlier is better.

Missing 2–3 days in a row: not a problem. Pick up the schedule on the next planned day. Don't try to "make up" missed doses.

Tip 08 — Red Flags · When to Pause
Signals to Watch For (Text Micah First)

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain (right-upper-quadrant), especially after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit too aggressive, eat more

Hold CJC/Ipa if: persistent morning hand/face puffiness that doesn't resolve past week 3, joint pain that's new, sustained tingling/numbness in hands.

Other yellow flags — reassess: strength dropping noticeably in the gym (means lean tissue is going — lift protein, may need to drop Reta to 2×/wk), sleep getting worse instead of deeper, persistent fatigue past week 3.

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own — we adjust based on what's actually happening.
