Jessie's Cycle 2 Rotation
Reta continues · CJC/Ipa → Tesa · 5-Amino → SLU-PP-332 · KLOW joins.

Cycle 1 did its job. Sema plateau broken, lean tissue stayed, sleep deepened — the stack worked exactly the way it was supposed to. This is Cycle 2. Reta carries through (you still have ~12 weeks of supply on the schedule), the inflammation / skin / gut layer joins via KLOW, and two compounds get rotated for the most important reason of all: so your body keeps responding instead of adapting around them.

Why we rotate off CJC/Ipa + 5-Amino — even though they worked. Both peptide families produce receptor-level adaptation when run continuously. CJC/Ipa amplifies a GH pulse via the GHRH + ghrelin receptors; after 8–12 weeks of nightly stimulation, those receptors progressively downregulate and the same dose stops producing the same pulse. 5-Amino-1MQ blocks the NNMT enzyme, but at the cellular level NNMT activity adapts to the inhibition over weeks. Continuing either past Cycle 1 would mean diminishing returns at full price. The smarter move is a clean rotation off — same mechanisms, different molecules — so the response comes back fresh.

What replaces them — same job, different door:

Tesamorelin replaces CJC/Ipa. Both work in the GHRH family — but Tesa is a stabilized GHRH analog that hits the GHRH receptor cleanly without engaging the ghrelin (GHS-R) pathway CJC/Ipa shares with Ipamorelin. The body responds to it differently, which is the whole point of rotating. Bonus: Tesa is FDA-approved specifically for reducing visceral + hepatic fat — which becomes the new edge for the back half of your recomp.

SLU-PP-332 replaces 5-Amino-1MQ. 5-Amino blocks fat storage (NNMT pathway). SLU-PP-332 is a different mechanism entirely: it's an ERRβ/γ agonist — it tells your mitochondria to burn fat and increase mitochondrial biogenesis. It's been called the closest thing to "exercise-in-a-vial" in the research literature. Where 5-Amino reduces storage, SLU drives oxidation. Stacked with Reta, it specifically targets the back-half fat that's hardest to release.

KLOW Blend joins for inflammation, gut, and skin. KLOW is GHK-Cu + BPC-157 + TB-500 + KPV in one blend — the systemic anti-inflammatory + tissue-repair + skin-tone layer. With Cycle 1's CJC/Ipa gone (it was your sleep + recovery layer), KLOW picks up the recovery side. The HRT base + 4–5×/wk training already builds; KLOW makes sure inflammation doesn't drag against the gains.

Reta is the through-line. You still have ~12 weeks of supply at your current 1 mg M/W/F dose. Carry it through Cycle 2. The one thing to watch: if you hit 138 (or your "this is enough" weight) before Reta runs out, drop to 0.5 mg every 10–14 days for maintenance instead of running the bottle to zero. The cycle doesn't have to finish for the protocol to have done its job.

Tip 02 — Why Rotating Compounds Matters
The Receptor-Reset Logic Behind Cycle 2

You might wonder — if CJC/Ipa and 5-Amino worked in Cycle 1, why not just run more of them? The answer is in how the peptides interact with your body at the receptor and enzyme level.

CJC/Ipa hits GHRH + ghrelin receptors. Both receptors downregulate (lose sensitivity) under continuous nightly stimulation. Around the 8–12 week mark, the GH pulse from the same dose is meaningfully smaller than it was in week 1. Tesamorelin hits the GHRH receptor only, with a stabilized molecular structure that's read as a fresh signal. Same family, different door. The body responds to Tesa as new input even though your receptors have seen "GHRH-class" molecules recently.

5-Amino-1MQ blocks the NNMT enzyme. The cell adapts to enzymatic inhibition over time by adjusting the upstream production of NNMT — same as how chronic NSAID use slowly attenuates anti-inflammatory effects. SLU-PP-332 is a completely different mechanism — it agonizes ERRβ/γ receptors (estrogen-related, not estrogen itself). It's hitting an entirely separate pathway that hasn't been stimulated in Cycle 1, so the response is brand new.

The pattern that consistently shows up in peptide cycling literature: rotations between same-family compounds produce stronger Cycle-2 results than running the same compound longer would. The trade-off — we get a fresh effect window in exchange for a small learning curve on the new molecules (timing, side-effect profile). Worth it.

Reta is the only exception because GLP-1 / GIP / glucagon receptors don't downregulate the same way GH-axis receptors do — particularly at the conservative 1 mg dose you've been running. Reta carries through Cycle 2 cleanly.

Tip 05 — Pre-Workout Fasted Cardio Protocol (AOD-9604)
How to Get the Most Out of AOD on Training Days

AOD-9604 is the only compound in the stack with a strict pre-workout fasted protocol. The mechanism makes the timing rules unusually rigid — here's exactly how to do it:

Step 1 — Wake up. No food. No coffee with cream or sugar. Black coffee or water is fine (helps mobilize fat).

Step 2 — AOD-9604 shot. 250 mcg (10u, 0.10 mL) SubQ abdomen. First thing in the AM block — before SLU + KLOW + Reta.

Step 3 — Wait 15–20 min, then do 20–30 min of low-intensity cardio. Treadmill walk on incline, easy bike ride, walk outside, elliptical at conversation pace. Zone 2 — you should be able to hold a conversation. Not HIIT. Not sprints. The point is to burn the released fatty acids for fuel, which happens best at zone 2 heart rate (where fat is the dominant substrate).

Step 4 — Finish cardio. Come back. Do the rest of the AM block (SLU + KLOW + Reta on M/W/F). Wait 30–60 min after this second block, then eat.

Why low-intensity matters: at HIIT or strength-training intensities, your body shifts to glucose/glycogen as the primary fuel. The fatty acids AOD released into the bloodstream get sent back to storage if not used in the cardio window. Zone 2 keeps fat oxidation as the dominant fuel source.

If you can't do cardio that morning (busy day, traveling, etc.): skip AOD that day. The compound only works paired with the fasted-cardio window. Saving the dose for a day when you can do the protocol gets you more value than burning a shot without the cardio.

Tip 08 — Missed Doses (Cycle 2 Compounds)
What to Do If You Skip One

Reta (M/W/F): miss one, take within 24 hours and resume schedule. More than 24 hours: skip and pick up next scheduled day. Don't double up.

Tesa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it. Don't shift to AM the next day — the pre-sleep window is what makes it work. If you ate too close to bed and the shot would be on a fed stomach, skip rather than dose — a fed Tesa shot is wasted.

SLU + KLOW (M–F AM): miss one morning, take it later that day if you can. If the whole day passes, skip and resume next day. Not strictly time-locked, but earlier is better for AM-block synergy.

AOD (training days only): if you miss a training-day morning, just skip AOD that day — it's tied to the cardio window. Don't try to use AOD without fasted cardio "to make up for it."

Missing 2–3 days in a row: not a problem. Pick up on the next planned day. Don't try to "make up" missed doses.
