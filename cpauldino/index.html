MamaP's Full Stack
Seven compounds. A focused 8-week cycle layered on a year-round baseline.

Two layers, one routine. Your stack splits into a focused 8-week cycle — P21, NAD+, Testagen, Ipamorelin, and Oxytocin — running on top of a year-round baseline layer of Retatrutide and HGH that continues past the 8-week mark. The cycle compounds finish when their bottles run out; the year-round pair keeps going.

How the days are built. Most shots land in the AM. Ipamorelin is the one PM shot — taken in the evening on an empty stomach (fasted) so it clears the strongest GH pulse. Oxytocin is dosed twice — once in the AM and once around midday. HGH runs every single day; Retatrutide runs Mon / Wed / Fri year-round; the rest of the cycle is Monday–Friday with weekends off.

Reta, HGH, and Oxytocin are already reconstituted — those three are mixed and ready to draw. P21, NAD+, Testagen, and Ipamorelin are the ones you'll reconstitute before Day 1 (see the Supplies tab for the exact BAC-water volumes and the bottle math). Every spec is confirmed — nothing outstanding.

Protocol Overview — 8-Week Cycle + Year-Round Layer
8-Week Cycle — P21 · NAD+ · Testagen · Ipamorelin · Oxytocin
P21 (1 mg, M–F AM) for cognition; NAD+ (100 mg, M/W/F AM) for cellular energy; Testagen (1 mg, M/W/F AM) for endocrine-axis balance; Ipamorelin (0.5 mg / 10u, M–F PM fasted) for the overnight GH pulse; Oxytocin (0.05 mg / 5u, M–F twice daily AM + midday) for mood + calm. Each cycle compound finishes when its bottles run out. Sat/Sun off (except the daily HGH baseline and M/W/F Reta).
Year-Round Layer — Retatrutide + HGH
Retatrutide (10u, Mon / Wed / Fri AM) for the metabolic baseline and HGH (~1.2 iu / 10u, every day) for the daily recovery / body-composition signal. Both continue past the 8-week mark — they're the ongoing foundation the cycle rides on top of, not part of the 8-week block. Both are already reconstituted.
Already Reconstituted — Reta · HGH · Oxytocin
These three are mixed and ready to draw — no reconstitution step before Day 1. The four you reconstitute yourself are P21 (0.5 mL BAC), NAD+ (5 mL BAC), Testagen (2 mL BAC), and Ipamorelin (2 mL BAC). See the Supplies tab for the full bottle math and BAC-water volumes.
How the routine flows: most shots cluster in one short AM window (HGH daily + Oxytocin + P21 M–F, plus NAD+, Testagen, and Reta on M/W/F). Oxytocin repeats once around midday. Ipamorelin is the single evening shot, taken fasted. Weekends drop to just the daily HGH baseline. It's a lot of compounds but a simple rhythm — one main morning window, a quick midday oxytocin, and one evening shot on weekdays.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately. Use within 30 days. Fresh insulin syringe for each draw — never reuse. This applies to the three that arrive already reconstituted (Reta, HGH, Oxytocin) as well as the four you mix (P21, NAD+, Testagen, Ipamorelin).

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. With several shots in one window, spread them across different quadrants (e.g., HGH upper-right, Oxytocin upper-left, P21 lower-right).

Tip 02 — Daily Rhythm With Seven Compounds
One AM Window · A Midday Oxytocin · One PM Shot

Seven compounds sounds like a lot, but the rhythm is simple — three touchpoints on weekdays:

  • AM (after waking): HGH 10u (~1.2 iu) + Oxytocin 5u + P21 10u every weekday. Add NAD+ 50u, Testagen 10u, and Reta 10u on Mon / Wed / Fri. Spread across abdomen quadrants. Push NAD+ slowly.
  • Midday (~lunch): the second Oxytocin 5u. One quick shot.
  • PM (evening, fasted): Ipamorelin 10u on an empty stomach — ~2+ hrs after eating, ideally before bed.

Weekends: only the daily HGH 10u (~1.2 iu). Everything M–F — including the M/W/F Reta — is off Sat/Sun.

Tip 03 — Why Ipamorelin Is Taken PM Fasted
Empty Stomach = Cleaner GH Pulse

Ipamorelin triggers a growth-hormone pulse. Food — especially carbs — raises insulin, which blunts that pulse. Taking it on an empty stomach (~2+ hrs after your last meal) lets the pulse land clean. An evening dose also pairs the GH release with overnight recovery, when the body does most of its repair.

Practical: if you snack late, just push the shot later so you're genuinely fasted. Don't eat for ~30–60 min after the shot to keep the window clean.

Tip 04 — NAD+ Push-Slow Reminder
Take 30–60 Seconds on the Plunger

NAD+ can cause a transient flush, warmth, or chest-tightness if pushed too fast. It's harmless but uncomfortable. The fix is simple: push the plunger slowly — 30–60 seconds for the full 50-unit dose. If a dose still feels intense, slow down further next time. At 100 mg (50u, 0.50 mL) on M/W/F this is a slightly larger volume than the other shots, so the slow push matters more here.

Tip 05 — SubQ Injection 101
The 60-Second Routine
  • Wash hands. Pull a fresh syringe + alcohol pad.
  • Wipe the vial top with alcohol. Let it dry 5 sec.
  • Draw your dose to the right unit mark.
  • Pick an abdomen spot, ~2″ from the belly button. Wipe with alcohol.
  • Pinch a small fold, insert the needle at 45–90°.
  • Push slowly, pull out, light pressure if any bleeding.
  • Drop the syringe in the sharps container. Done.

With multiple shots in one window, use a different quadrant for each so no single spot gets repeated.

Tip 06 — Normal Effects
No Action Needed
  • Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites, clean technique
  • A brief NAD+ flush / warmth if pushed a touch fast — slow the plunger next time
  • Slightly more vivid dreams or deeper sleep once Ipamorelin + HGH settle in — the GH-axis signal
  • A calm / mood lift from Oxytocin in the AM + midday windows
  • Mild hunger changes as Retatrutide runs in the background — expected with a GLP-1 axis compound
Tip 07 — Watch For (Message BioLinked)
Anything Below Warrants a Quick Check-In
  • Injection site hot, swollen, or red >24 hrs — possible technique or contamination issue
  • A strong or persistent NAD+ reaction even with a slow push — drop the dose and flag it
  • Persistent nausea or GI upset from the Retatrutide layer — may need a cadence / dose tweak
  • Numbness, tingling, or fluid retention that lingers — can relate to the GH-axis compounds; flag it
  • Allergic reaction symptoms (rash, itching, throat tightness) — very rare, but stop and flag immediately
