🆕 First-Time Injector — What You Need to Know

You've never injected before, and that's completely fine — thousands of people learn SubQ technique every year and get comfortable within the first 2–3 injections. SubQ (subcutaneous) means the needle goes into the fat layer just below the skin, not into muscle. The abdomen (2–3 inches from the belly button) is the easiest site. Pinch an inch of skin, insert the needle at a 45° angle, inject slowly, withdraw. The needles are tiny — 29 to 31 gauge, about half an inch long — and the injections are nearly painless. Start with one compound on Day 1 (KLOW is a good first injection), add the others from Day 2 onward. Your supplies page has everything you need. You'll feel confident by Week 2.

Storage & Handling

Dry powder vials (before reconstitution)

Store all lyophilized vials refrigerated at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep away from light. Do not freeze. Shelf life is 12–24 months unopened. Only reconstitute what you plan to use within 30 days. HGH is the most sensitive — treat it with extra care.

After reconstitution

All reconstituted vials must be refrigerated immediately and used within 30 days. Never freeze a reconstituted peptide — this destroys the structure. Use a fresh syringe for every draw. HGH reconstituted vials should always be stored horizontally to minimize protein aggregation.

Bacteriostatic water (BAC water)

Use sterile BAC water for all 9 compounds. The benzyl alcohol preservative allows safe multi-draw use over 30 days. Do not substitute plain sterile water. With 9 compounds you'll need at least 10 BAC water vials — see your supplies checklist.

Syringes & injection technique

29–31 gauge insulin syringes (0.5 mL or 1 mL barrel). Never reuse — one syringe per injection, always. Dispose in sharps container immediately. Rotate injection sites: abdomen is preferred for all SubQ injections. Pinch skin, 45° angle, slow injection. Wipe vial stopper and skin with alcohol prep pad before every use.

Normal: Noticeably deeper and more restorative sleep from days 3–7 — Epitalon and Pinealon acting on pineal melatonin regulation. This is the intended effect.

Monitor: Persistent nausea after NAD+ beyond week 2 — reduce to 50 mg (30 units) and notify BioLinked. NAD+ tolerance builds but some people need a longer ramp on their first cycle.
Monitor: Water retention or joint swelling from HGH — at 2 IU this is uncommon but possible. If you notice significant puffiness in fingers or joints, consider spacing HGH to every other day for 1–2 weeks.
Monitor: Injection site infection (not just redness — look for heat, swelling, spreading redness, or pus). If any of these occur: stop injecting that site, clean it, and contact BioLinked. Sterile technique prevents this almost entirely.
