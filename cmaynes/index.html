Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Reconstitute one vial at a time per compound — don't start the 30-day clock on the second vial until the first is close to empty.

Syringe discipline: fresh U-100 insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe stopper + injection site with alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. Local-to-knee injections are fine but stay away from the surgical incision itself until cleared by his surgeon.

Protocol Notes
Execution · Timing · What to Watch For
Tip 01 — The TB-500 Question
Why You Don't Need TB-500 Separately

The most common follow-up question on this protocol is: "Where's the TB-500?" Short answer: it's already inside GLOW.

GLOW is a multi-peptide blend containing BPC-157 + GHK-Cu + TB-500 in one vial. Adding a separate TB-500 vial on top of that would be:

· Redundant. Same compound, twice the spend.
· Unnecessary. The TB-500 fraction inside GLOW — combined with the BPC-157 + GHK-Cu in the same shot, plus the Phase 1 BPC and Cartalax that already ran — covers the full healing cascade Cruz's recovery requires.
· Not the limiting factor. What ACL recovery needs at the 5–12 week stretch is coordinated, multi-axis signaling (inflammation, collagen, vascular, tendon), not more of any single mechanism.

If Cruz's recovery is significantly slower than expected by week 8–10, that's the moment to talk about adding a dedicated TB-500 layer. Until then: GLOW handles it.

Tip 02 — AM / PM Timing
Why Cartalax AM and BPC-157 PM in Phase 1

Cartalax in the AM — keeps cartilage and connective-tissue bioregulator signaling active through the day, when the knee is being loaded by rehab exercises, walking, and any standing time. Joint-level signal coverage during the working window.

BPC-157 in the PM — gut-lining repair and soft-tissue healing are most active overnight when the body is in its natural repair cycle. A pre-bed (or post-dinner) shot rides along with the overnight window.

You can swap if life requires it — consistency matters more than the exact slot. Just don't stack both shots in the same hour; spreading them across the day amplifies each one's window.

Tip 03 — Phase 1 Structure
20 Days Straight · No Breaks

Phase 1's two compounds run M–F straight for 20 weekdays. The supply matches the dosing exactly — 2 vials of BPC at 20 doses, 2 vials of Cartalax at 20 doses. Don't skip days; if you miss one, you lose a dose at the back end.

Sat / Sun off is for site-rotation recovery and to give the receptors a small two-day signaling break each week.

Tip 04 — Signs Phase 1 Is Working
What to Watch For Weeks 1–4

Week 1 (immediately post-op): the surgical insult dominates everything else. Don't expect to feel BPC or Cartalax. They're working in the background; the surgical recovery is what's visible.

Week 2: swelling resolving slightly faster than expected. Less generalized inflammation. Sleep deeper. Gut feels normal earlier than typical post-op recovery (BPC working on the gut lining).

Weeks 3–4: by end of Phase 1, recovery markers should be ahead of where post-op week 4 usually lands — rehab milestones hitting on time or early, less daily pain, range of motion progressing.

Yellow flags — tell your surgeon, not us: increasing pain, redness, swelling, fever, or any sign of infection or graft trouble. Those are surgical issues, not peptide protocol issues.

Tip 05 — Phase 2 Transition
Starting GLOW in Week 5

BPC-157 and Cartalax finish at the end of Week 4. Start GLOW the following Monday (Week 5). No gap, no overlap.

One shot per day M–F at 10u (2.5 mg). Pick a slot (AM or PM) and stay consistent. PM works well — especially if Cruz is doing PT or rehab earlier in the day, GLOW post-session catches the repair window with a peptide load right when the tissue's signaling for it.

Don't worry about fasted state for GLOW — the blend isn't insulin-sensitive in the way mitochondrial peptides are. Eat normally around it.

Tip 06 — Signs Phase 2 Is Working
What to Watch For Weeks 5–12

Weeks 5–6: rehab progressions feel cleaner. Less day-after soreness from PT sessions. Knee feels less "fresh" and more "settled."

Weeks 7–9: the rebuild signal from GHK-Cu + TB-500 starts to show up. Collagen and connective tissue stability noticeably better. Skin around the surgical site looks healed. Tendons and surrounding muscle responding to load.

Weeks 10–12: the long-tail healing window. Strength returning. Surgical knee feels measurably closer to the contralateral side. By end of Week 12, the peptide work has done its job — rehab and time take it the rest of the way.

Tip 07 — Coordinate with Rehab
Peptides Don't Replace PT, They Amplify It

The peptides do the cellular healing. The structural rebuild only happens with load. Stick to the rehab progression Cruz's surgeon and PT set out — the peptides make every session more productive by reducing the inflammatory cost of progression and accelerating the repair window between sessions.

Don't push the rehab harder just because the peptides feel like they're working. The protocol earns its value by letting Cruz hit the scheduled milestones cleanly, not by accelerating past them.
