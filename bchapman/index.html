What Retatrutide is — the educational piece. Reta is a triple-receptor agonist. One molecule activates three different gut/metabolic receptors at once:

· GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) — suppresses appetite, slows gastric emptying so you feel full longer, improves insulin sensitivity.
· GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) — reduces post-meal glucose spikes, modulates fat-cell sensitivity.
· Glucagon receptor — this is the differentiator. Glucagon at the right dose drives direct fat oxidation, preferentially burns visceral and hepatic (liver) fat, and raises basal energy expenditure.

The net behavior: appetite drops noticeably within the first 1–2 weeks. Scale movement typically week 2–3 (1–2 lbs/week sustainable). The glucagon arm means you keep burning even between meals, not just when you're under-eating.
What to expect. Mild nausea on Mondays (Reta day) the first 2–3 weeks is normal — eat smaller, lower-fat meals that day, hydrate aggressively. Appetite suppression makes hitting protein the active job (1g/lb goal bodyweight); front-load it early in the day before appetite drops off. Reta also suppresses thirst alongside appetite, so 3–4 L/day water + daily electrolytes (sodium/potassium/magnesium) matter more than they normally would.

What CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin is — the educational piece. This is a two-peptide blend that works on the growth-hormone axis. Critical distinction up front: it does not inject GH. It amplifies your own natural GH release from the pituitary so your body stays in control of the dose.

· CJC-1295 (no DAC) — a GHRH analog (growth-hormone releasing hormone). It extends the natural GH-release window so the pulse lasts longer instead of shutting down fast.
· Ipamorelin — a selective ghrelin-receptor agonist. Triggers a clean GH pulse without the cortisol or prolactin spikes that older GH-releasers (GHRP-6, hexarelin) cause — meaning no hunger surge, no mood/sleep disruption, no estrogen-related side effects in men.

Stacked together, they create a stronger, longer pulse than either one alone — mimicking the kind of pulses a healthy 20-year-old's pituitary makes naturally.
Why it matters for body comp. Natural GH output declines steadily after age 30. The downstream effects of restoring that signal: deeper sleep (especially slow-wave / stage 3), faster muscle recovery between training sessions, lean tissue preservation in a calorie deficit, improved skin/connective-tissue quality, modest reduction in visceral adiposity. Pairing with Reta is the synergy story — Reta drives the fat loss, CJC/Ipa keeps the lean tissue underneath. Cut without GH support past mid-30s and you tend to lose muscle alongside fat. With CJC/Ipa in the stack, you keep what you've built.
Fasted PM timing matters. Food — especially carbs — spikes insulin and blunts the GH pulse CJC/Ipa is designed to amplify. Routine: dinner → wait 2 hrs → brush teeth → inject → lights out within 30–60 min. If you ate too close to bed, skip rather than dose.

The simple version: Reta makes the fat come off. CJC/Ipa makes sure what's underneath when it does come off is muscle and not loose skin + lost lean tissue. Two compounds, two complementary mechanisms, one outcome: you end the cycle visibly leaner with the strength + muscle base intact.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry vials (before reconstitution): refrigerate at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Stable for many months sealed.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Reconstitute one vial at a time — on Day 1, mix Reta vial #1 + CJC/Ipa vial #1. Reconstitute the next CJC/Ipa vial around the 4-week mark as the first one runs dry. Reta vial #2 comes out at the ~20-week mark.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never the same 1″ spot twice the same day.

Execution & Tips
Quick-Read Reference
Tip 01 — Reconstitution Walk-Through
First Time Mixing? Read This.

Step 1. Pull the plastic flip-tops off the peptide vial + bacteriostatic water bottle. Wipe both rubber stoppers with an alcohol pad.

Step 2. Use an insulin syringe to draw the correct amount of bacteriostatic water:

· Retatrutide 20 mg vial: draw 2 mL (200 units on the syringe), inject slowly into the side of the vial — let it run down the glass, don't blast the powder.
· CJC/Ipa 5+5 mg vial: draw 2 mL (200 units), same technique.

Step 3. Gently swirl — do not shake. The powder dissolves in about 30 seconds. Should look clear.

Step 4. Label the cap with the recon date (sharpie). Put it back in the fridge.

Step 5. To dose: wipe stopper, draw to the units listed in your compound row (10u for both), inject SubQ into abdomen pinched skin, pull needle out, light pressure with alcohol pad. Done.

Tip 02 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Reta (Monday once-weekly): miss Monday, take it within 24–48 hours (Tuesday or Wednesday). More than 48 hours: push your anchor day to whichever day you took it, then resume that day the following week. Don't double up.

CJC/Ipa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it. Don't shift to AM the next day — the pre-sleep window is what makes it work. If you ate too close to bed and the shot would be on a fed stomach, skip rather than dose — a fed CJC shot is wasted.

Missing 2–3 days in a row: not a problem. Pick up on the next planned day. Don't try to "make up" missed doses.

Tip 03 — What to Expect
Honest Week-by-Week Timeline

Weeks 1–2: appetite noticeably down from Reta. Some mild nausea on Mondays (Reta day) is normal — eat smaller, lower-fat meals that day, hydrate aggressively. CJC/Ipa is loading; sleep may already feel deeper by end of week 2.

Weeks 3–5: fat-loss curve takes hold. 1–2 lbs/wk is the clean sustainable range. CJC/Ipa-driven deeper sleep + faster recovery is established.

Weeks 6–12: trend continues. Message Micah at the 3-week mark with how appetite + scale are tracking; we'll decide whether to hold at 10u Reta or tier up.

Hydration + protein: Reta suppresses thirst alongside appetite — aim for 3–4 L/day and 1 g protein per lb goal bodyweight. Front-load protein early in the day since appetite drops as the day goes on.

Tip 04 — Red Flags
When to Hold + Text Micah

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain (right-upper-quadrant), especially after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit too aggressive, eat more

Hold CJC/Ipa if: persistent morning hand/face puffiness past week 3, joint pain that's new, sustained tingling/numbness in hands.

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own — we adjust based on what's actually happening.
