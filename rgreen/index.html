Ryan's Selected Stack — Weight Loss + Energy
Five compounds, each pointed at one goal. Built for 260→200 while energy, sleep, and muscle build in parallel.

Goal: 260 → 200, with energy back in the tank. At 44 carrying 60 lbs of extra weight, the #1 lever is removing the appetite + metabolic friction that's keeping the deficit unsustainable. The second lever is getting energy and recovery up to where the day actually feels good while the loss is happening. This stack is built around both at once — weight loss without the "running on empty" feeling that wrecks most cuts.

Each compound is pointed at one specific goal:

· Fat loss + energy — Retatrutide (the appetite + fat oxidation anchor, M/W/F AM) + MOTS-c (mitochondrial energy + insulin sensitivity, M–F AM fasted).
· Cognitive enhancement — Semax (BDNF + focus + mood, M/W/F AM).
· Muscle building & hormonal balance — CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin blend (GH/IGF-1 pulse for lean mass + sleep + recovery, M–F PM fasted pre-bed).
· Sexual health & libido — PT-141 (libido + arousal, as needed).

Cycle length: until the bottles finish. No fixed end date. Semax empties first around Week 6–7. MOTS and CJC/Ipa finish around Week 8. Reta runs the longest — ~10 weeks at M/W/F. PT-141 is PRN (as-needed) and has 20 doses in the vial — outlasts the rest of the cycle.

You've never injected before. That's fine — the protocol is built for it. Don't start all seven compounds on Day 1. The Notes tab walks through a 4-day ramp-in: start with one shot Day 1, add one more each day until you're running the full stack by end of Week 1. Same total weekly load; just a gentler start to dial in the routine.

Storage
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Labeled

Dry vials: refrigerate at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Mix each vial only when you're ready to start using it — staggered timing keeps every vial inside its 30-day stability window.

Label everything. With this many vials going, write the compound name and reconstitution date on each top with a fine-tip marker. Five seconds of labeling saves a lot of "wait, which one is which" moments.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your skin with an alcohol pad every time.

Tip 01 — First-Time Injector 4-Day Ramp
Don't Start All 7 on Day 1 — Here's the Onboarding

All 7 compounds are SubQ — just under the skin, short thin insulin needle (29–31G). Most people describe injection as barely noticeable. But Day 1 isn't the day to learn the technique AND run all seven at once. Stagger the ramp:

Day 1 (Monday Wk 1) — MOTS only. Smallest dose, simplest pull, fasted AM. Get the technique locked in: alcohol pad on vial top, alcohol pad on skin (upper-left abdomen, 4-quadrant rotation), pinch a small fold, insert needle at 45°, push steady, withdraw, dispose in sharps container. That's the whole skill — same exact process for every other compound.

Day 2 (Tuesday) — MOTS + CJC/Ipa. Add the evening shot. CJC/Ipa pre-bed, fasted (no food 2+ hrs prior), different site than MOTS.

Day 3 (Wednesday) — MOTS + Reta + Semax + CJC/Ipa. Now add the M/W/F additions: Reta and Semax in the AM (three AM shots total). CJC/Ipa continues in the PM.

Day 4–5 — full rhythm. Thursday + Friday are the same Tue/Thu pattern: MOTS AM, CJC/Ipa PM. By end of Week 1 you've done every shot the protocol calls for at least once. From there, just repeat the weekly rhythm.

PT-141: save it for later. Don't add it to onboarding. After Week 1–2 when the daily routine is locked in, PT-141 becomes a "when you want it" night addition.

Tip 02 — The 8-Week Timeline
What Each Week Will Look Like

Week 1: learning curve. Appetite suppression from Reta starts within 3–5 days. MOTS energy lift may show up subtly by end of week. Semax effect is gradual — expect Week 2–3 to feel it. CJC/Ipa effects build in the background. Mild nausea or "full quickly" Monday/Tuesday after first Reta shot is normal — your existing omeprazole helps.

Week 2–3: appetite control is solid. MOTS energy clearly noticeable — "running on more fuel" feeling. Semax sharpening kicks in: focus + mood baseline lift. Sleep starts improving from CJC/Ipa (3/5 → trending up). Weight loss curve starts moving — aim for 1–2 lbs/wk steady, not faster.

Weeks 4–6: meaningful weight is off (~5–10 lbs). Energy + cognitive at sustained baseline. This is when most clients say "the protocol is actually working." Sleep quality should be visibly improved from the CJC/Ipa pulse.

Weeks 7–8: sustained loss continues. CJC/Ipa and MOTS finish around end of Week 8. If you've added the 3×/wk movement component, lean mass is holding well through the loss — not the typical "smaller and softer" outcome.

Weeks 9–10: Reta solo tail at M/W/F. Loss continues at a gentler pace. End of cycle decision: reorder another round, taper Reta to 2×/week for maintenance, or stop. We talk through that based on where the body comp is at Week 10.

Peptides aren't penicillin-class compounds. They share zero structural similarity. Penicillin allergy doesn't translate to peptide cross-reactivity.

Tree nut allergy is also a non-issue here. Lyophilized peptide vials are pure peptide powder; bacteriostatic water is sterile water + benzyl alcohol preservative. No nuts, nut oils, or nut-derived ingredients anywhere in the supply chain.

You're already on cetirizine 10 mg. That's a clean Type-I allergic-response buffer if anything unexpected ever happens at an injection site. Keep it running.

If you ever see an unusual injection-site reaction (more than mild redness for an hour or two, persistent itching, swelling past 24 hours, any breathing symptoms): pause that compound, text Micah, get checked. The likelihood is very low but the response plan should be clear.

Tip 05 — Hydration on Reta
The Thing Most People Underestimate

Target: 3 L of water per day. Reta suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite — you won't feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people end up with headaches, constipation, fatigue, and lightheadedness (especially on losartan) and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit.

Add electrolytes once daily. Sodium, potassium, magnesium. LMNT, Liquid IV, or similar work fine.

Coffee doesn't count. Drink it on top of your water target, not instead of.

Tip 06 — Sedentary → Movement Ramp
Muscle Building Goal Requires Movement (Honest Note)

You flagged sedentary, and muscle building is one of your goals. CJC/Ipa is in the stack specifically for the muscle + sleep + hormonal axis — but it can't build muscle without mechanical signal. Honest: without movement, CJC/Ipa improves sleep depth and recovery, but doesn't add muscle. The peptide amplifies the body's response to lifting; it doesn't replace lifting.

Minimum effective dose: 3×/wk basic resistance work. Whatever you'll actually do. Options ranked by what's most likely to stick:

· Bodyweight at home (15–20 min): push-ups (or wall push-ups to start), squats (or chair-assisted), dumbbell rows if you have any. Done in your living room. Lowest barrier to start.
· Dumbbells at home: a single 25–40 lb adjustable dumbbell pair covers everything. Goblet squats, dumbbell presses, rows, deadlifts. Solid full-body work in 25 minutes.
· Gym 3×/wk: if you'd actually go. Compound movements (squat, bench, row, overhead press, deadlift) with light weights to learn the patterns.

Walking counts for fat loss, not muscle. 7,000–10,000 steps/day adds to the Reta-driven deficit and helps the cardiovascular adaptation, but it doesn't trigger muscle protein synthesis. The lifting is separate.

The honest version: if you don't add movement, you'll still hit 200 lbs and feel great from Reta + MOTS + Semax. You just won't see the "leaner + more muscle" body comp outcome — the loss will be mostly weight, not recomp. Worth deciding which version you want before Week 4.

Tip 07 — PT-141 Date Night Use
How to Run It

Inject 30–45 min before the evening starts. PT-141 handles the desire and arousal axis — central nervous system, melanocortin receptors. Works on the brain's pathway, not the vascular one like Viagra/Cialis. Effect lasts 4–6 hours.

Where to inject: SubQ abdomen, same technique as the AM/PM routine.

What to expect first time: PT-141 may cause a brief "warm" or flushing feeling right after injection — passes in a few minutes. Some mild nausea is possible on the first one; goes away with subsequent doses.

Don't combine with Viagra/Cialis on the same evening — both can drop BP further alongside your losartan. PT-141 alone is plenty.

Tip 08 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain or right-upper-quadrant pain after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit is too aggressive, eat more
· Severe lightheadedness on standing — could be BP dropping too low with losartan; hydrate, eat, talk to your doctor

Hold MOTS, Semax, or CJC/Ipa if: significant injection-site reaction (redness, heat, swelling past 24 hrs), or any allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes). All are well-tolerated; individual reactions are rare but possible.

Hold PT-141 if: severe flushing or sustained BP drop with the shot (your losartan is the main consideration here — PT-141 can lower BP transiently and shouldn't stack with Viagra/Cialis).

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own.
