Julie's 8-Week Recomp & Recovery Stack
Fat loss, lean tissue preservation, and deep recovery — titrate up, then run through your supply

Four-compound recomp stack built around the same logic that runs through every BioLinked protocol: one compound drives the deficit, the others protect what shouldn't be lost. Reta does the fat loss heavy lifting. MOTS-C runs metabolic protection in parallel — better glucose disposal, preserved insulin sensitivity, exercise-mimetic AMPK activation. 5-Amino-1MQ keeps the cellular machinery primed to oxidize what Reta releases. CJC/Ipa rebuilds at night through amplified GH pulses — deeper sleep, faster recovery, lean mass held intact.

~8 weeks is approximate. The real cycle is titrate up over the first 2 weeks, then run at maintenance dose until your bottles are used up. Some people move faster, some slower — let your body and your supply set the pace, not the calendar.

Protocol Overview — How This Stack Works Together
Retatrutide
The engine of fat loss. Triple agonist (GLP-1 / GIP / glucagon) suppresses appetite, accelerates fat oxidation, and improves insulin sensitivity. Titrates from 0.5 mg up to 1 mg on M / W / F — the slow ramp is what keeps GI side effects manageable.
5-Amino-1MQ
An NNMT inhibitor that restores intracellular NAD+ in fat and muscle. Reta aggressively mobilizes fat — if NAD+ is low, those liberated fatty acids recirculate instead of being oxidized. 5-Amino keeps the cellular fuel machinery primed so what Reta releases actually gets burned, and activates dormant muscle satellite cells as reserve for rebuilding.
MOTS-C
A mitochondrial-derived peptide that mimics exercise at the cellular level — activates AMPK, improves glucose disposal, and increases insulin sensitivity. Stacked alongside Reta, it amplifies metabolic efficiency and helps preserve lean tissue through an aggressive calorie deficit.
CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin
A dual GH secretagogue blend (GHRH analog + ghrelin receptor agonist) that amplifies endogenous growth hormone release. Drives recovery, deeper sleep, and lean tissue preservation — the counterweight to Reta's catabolic pressure. Pre-bed on an empty stomach is non-negotiable.
Stack Synergy: Reta does the heavy lifting on fat loss, but used alone in an aggressive deficit it can erode lean tissue and leave mobilized fat un-oxidized. 5-Amino-1MQ restores the NAD+ fuel needed to actually burn what Reta releases. MOTS-C runs metabolic protection in parallel — better glucose disposal, preserved insulin sensitivity, exercise-mimetic AMPK activation. CJC/Ipa rebuilds at night through amplified GH pulses: deeper sleep, faster recovery, lean mass held intact. Each compound addresses a distinct part of the recomp cascade — none redundant, each one enabling the next.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Reconstitute one vial at a time — don't start the 30-day clock on a second until the first is close to empty.

Syringe discipline: fresh U-100 insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Protocol Notes
Execution · Side Effects · What to Watch For
Tip 01 — Critical
Don't Skip the Reta Titration

The first ~2 weeks at 0.5 mg (5 units) aren't optional — they exist so your GI tract can adapt to GLP-1/GIP/glucagon signaling before you step up to the working dose. People who jump straight to 1 mg are the ones who throw up on day three, blame the peptide, and quit.

If the end of week 2 still feels rough (persistent nausea, low energy, food aversion), stay at 0.5 mg for an extra week before stepping up. The protocol is a guideline — your body sets the pace.

Tip 02 — Hydration
Drink More Than You Think You Need

Reta suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite. You will not feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated — that's how people end up with constipation, headaches, and fatigue blamed on "the peptide" when it's really just water deficit.

Target: 80–100 oz / day, minimum. Add electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day, especially on training days. Coffee and tea don't count.

Tip 03 — GI Side Effects
Manage Nausea, Reflux & Constipation Proactively

Nausea: usually shows up 4–24 hrs post-shot, peaks in week 1–2, fades after that. Eat smaller meals, slower, lower-fat. Ginger tea or 25 mg Vitamin B6 morning-of-injection blunts it significantly.

Reflux / early fullness: Reta delays gastric emptying — eat half-portions and stop at the first sign of fullness. Don't lie down for 2 hours after eating.

Constipation: almost universal at first. Counter with hydration (see Tip 02), 25–30 g fiber/day, and magnesium glycinate 400 mg at bedtime. Add a fiber supplement (psyllium) if needed.

Red flags — call a clinician: persistent vomiting, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to the back — pancreatitis risk), or signs of gallbladder issues.

Tip 04 — GH Peptide Timing
CJC / Ipamorelin Needs an Empty Stomach

CJC/Ipa works by amplifying your natural pre-sleep GH pulse. That pulse is suppressed by elevated blood glucose and insulin. Eating — especially carbs or fats — within 2 hours of injection blunts the effect dramatically.

The rule: finish your last meal at least 2 hours before the bedtime shot. If you eat at 8 pm, inject at 10 pm or later. If you snacked at 10 pm, you've burned the dose — skip and resume tomorrow.

Water and black coffee/tea are fine in the fasting window. The same 2-hour rule applies on the front end — ideally don't eat for 30–60 minutes after the shot either, to let the pulse complete.

Tip 05 — Training & Protein
Lift Heavy & Hit Protein Targets

Reta is going to drop body fat fast. Without resistance training and adequate protein, a meaningful chunk of that loss will come from lean tissue — not what you want for "recomp." The stack is designed so 5-Amino, MOTS, and CJC/Ipa protect lean mass, but they can only do their job if you're actually loading the muscle and feeding it.

Targets: resistance training 3–4 days/week. Protein intake: 0.8–1 g per lb of target body weight, daily. Lower appetite makes this hard — lean toward protein-dense foods first and fill in the rest with what you can stomach.

Tip 06 — Signals
Signs the Protocol Is Working

Weeks 1–2 (titration): appetite drops noticeably (Reta GLP-1 kicks in fast). Some GI noise. Energy slightly inconsistent — this resolves as the dose acclimates.

Weeks 3–4 (maintenance starts): steady weekly weight drop of 1–2 lbs. Recovery from training noticeably faster (5-Amino + MOTS layering NAD+ and mitochondrial output). Less afternoon crash.

Weeks 5–6: sleep deeper (CJC/Ipa pre-bed paying off). Skin clearer. Workout output and endurance lift. Body composition starts shifting more visibly than the scale.

Weeks 7–8 (or however long the bottles last): the cumulative effect — sustained energy, improved cardio, leaner appearance, sharper cognition. The point isn't a single dramatic moment but a quiet shift in how your body runs day-to-day.

Yellow flags — reassess: rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week (deficit too aggressive; eat more), strength dropping noticeably (lean mass loss; bump protein), persistent GI past week 2, or sleep getting worse instead of better.
