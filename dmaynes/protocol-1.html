Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Always use a fresh insulin syringe for each draw — never reuse.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Week-by-Week
What to Expect Across the 12 Weeks
Tip 01 — Why 12 Weeks for Tesamorelin
The Body Comp Curve Doesn't Break Until Week 8–10

The Tesamorelin clinical trials — the ones that produced the 15–20% visceral fat reduction numbers everyone quotes — specifically ran 12 weeks. Not because that's a marketing-friendly round number, but because that's how long the body actually takes to remodel.

Weeks 1–4 — Priming: GH and IGF-1 levels climb. Sleep deepens. You may not see anything visible yet. Internally, the GH/IGF-1 axis is being amplified above your baseline pulse rhythm.

Weeks 4–8 — Mobilization: Visceral fat starts to mobilize. Skin quality begins to improve. Recovery and energy noticeably better. Subtle changes in midsection composition.

Weeks 8–12 — The Payoff Window: This is where the curve gets steepest. The body comp shifts that started in Week 4–6 compound and become visible. Stopping at Week 8 cuts off the slope right at the inflection point — you get the priming and a sliver of the mobilization, and almost none of the visible payoff.

Synergy bonus: Running Tesa after CJC/Ipa ends at Week 8 prevents the GH axis from collapsing. CJC/Ipa hands off to Tesa, and the GH layer stays continuous all 12 weeks instead of crashing at Week 8.

Tip 02 — First-Time Injecting
Start Slow on Day 1

All five compounds are SubQ — just under the skin, not into muscle. Short, thin insulin needle (29–31G), most people describe it as barely noticeable.

Day 1 approach: reconstitute and inject ONE compound to start. KLOW is a good first choice — small dose, daily use, builds the habit. Add SS-31 the next day if comfortable, CJC/Ipa the day after, Tesa pre-bed by end of Week 1. You'll be in the rhythm by start of Week 2.

Mild injection-site redness or a small bruise is normal and resolves quickly. Rotate sites daily (abdomen 4 quadrants, then outer thigh) and use a fresh needle every time.

Weeks 1–2
Foundation Phase

Don't expect dramatic results yet — the compounds are building up. Most first-time users notice improved sleep quality by end of Week 1 as CJC/Ipa + Tesa influence nocturnal recovery. Mild injection-site redness is normal.

Log how you're sleeping and your post-workout recovery time. This baseline matters for evaluating Week 3–4 changes.

Weeks 3–4
First Signals

Where most people notice the first meaningful shift. Post-workout soreness should resolve faster — what used to take 48 hours may now clear in 24. Energy steadier through the day. Joint and soft-tissue nags starting to ease.

CJC/Ipa + Tesa contribution to body recomp showing: lean muscle building, midsection starts to feel tighter, sleep depth increasing.

Weeks 5–6
Peak Recovery Window

The sweet spot for the recovery stack. Recovery at full capacity. Training sessions feel more productive, body bounces back noticeably faster. Muscle fullness and firmness improve. Sleep deeper and more restorative.

SS-31 vial finishes around end of Week 6 — that's expected. Recovery floor stays high because KLOW + CJC/Ipa + Tesa continue.

PT-141 is most effective during this phase — overall hormonal environment is dialed in.

Weeks 7–8
Transition Window

SS-31 is done; KLOW + CJC/Ipa + Tesa continue. You shouldn't notice a recovery drop — the GH layer compensates. Strength and body comp continue trending.

End of Week 8: KLOW and CJC/Ipa supplies exhaust. From Week 9 forward it's Tesamorelin solo to finish the body comp curve.

Weeks 9–10
Tesamorelin Solo — The Payoff Begins

Now it's just the pre-bed Tesa shot, M–F. This is the phase the whole 12 weeks was designed for. Visceral fat reduction starts compounding visibly. Midsection composition changes become noticeable. Skin quality continues improving.

Sleep should still be strong — Tesa carries the GH pulse on its own. If sleep dips, that's a sign the body is adapting; usually self-corrects by mid-Week 10.

Weeks 11–12
Consolidation & Lock-In

The steepest part of the body comp curve. Visceral fat, lean tissue, and skin quality changes from Weeks 4–10 visibly consolidate.

End-of-cycle questions: How much better is recovery vs. Week 1? Body comp shift from baseline? Sleep? Energy? These answers determine whether to run a maintenance cycle, repeat, or take 4–6 weeks off and reassess.

Execution Tips
Pairing · Timing · PRN Use
Tip 03 — AM Cluster
Stack KLOW + SS-31 + CJC/Ipa in One Window

All three AM compounds can go in the same morning shot window. KLOW + SS-31 don't strictly require fasted, but CJC/Ipa does (food blunts the GH pulse). Easiest approach: shoot all three before breakfast in the same 5-minute window, then wait 30–45 min before eating.

Rotate sites between the three compounds so each lands in a different abdomen quadrant.

Tip 04 — Pre-Bed Dosing (CJC/Ipa + Tesa)
Same Window, Both Need an Empty Stomach

Both the pre-bed CJC/Ipa shot and the pre-bed Tesamorelin shot ride the body's natural nocturnal GH pulse — that's when most muscle repair happens during sleep. Both require an empty stomach: 2+ hours after your last meal. If you ate at 8 PM, inject at 10 PM or later.

Inject both back-to-back in the same window, rotating sides so they land in different abdomen quadrants. Water and black coffee/tea are fine in the fasting window. Skip the dose if you snacked late.

In Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12) it's just Tesa — same rules apply.

Tip 05 — PT-141 First Use
Half-Dose, Plan the Timing

First time: 5 units (250 mcg) only. Nausea is the most common side effect at full dose — better to start light, see how you respond, then scale to 10u (500 mcg) on subsequent uses if 5u feels too mild.

Inject 30–60 minutes before the window you want. Peaks at 60–90 min, lasts 6–12 hours. Cap at one use per 24–48 hours.

Tip 06 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites and use proper SubQ technique
· Mild flushing or tingle with first 1–2 CJC/Ipa or Tesa shots — subsides
· Slight warmth or "fullness" feeling 30 min after CJC/Ipa or Tesa — normal GH response
· Mild water retention in first 2 weeks of Tesa — normal, resolves as the body adapts

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique issue or contamination
· Severe nausea with PT-141 (drop to half dose next time)
· Persistent headaches or sleep getting worse — reassess CJC/Ipa or Tesa timing
· Numbness/tingling in hands beyond Week 4 — can happen at higher GHRH exposure; flag to reassess dosing
