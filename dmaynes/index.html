❄ Refrigerated, Protected, One Vial at a Time

Dry vials: refrigerate at 36–46°F, protected from light. Never freeze.

After mixing: refrigerate and use within ~30 days. Reconstitute one vial at a time — open the next only when the current one runs dry.

Every shot: fresh single-use syringe, wipe the stopper and your skin with alcohol, rotate injection sites. For Wolverine, keep the shots grouped toward the area you're targeting.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Each Cycle
Tip 01 — Wolverine (New)
Inject Toward the Site · 20 Days Straight

One shot a day, 10u (1 mg), for 20 days in a row. Inject SubQ toward the area you're targeting — BPC-157 does its best repair work locally, so the closer to the injury/injection site, the better. Rotate the exact spot around the area day to day.

Two vials cover the sprint (each vial = 10 daily doses); the other two are backup or a second round. Mix one vial at a time. Miss a day? Resume the next day — don't double up.

Tip 02 — Injection Basics
SubQ, Short Needle, Barely Noticeable

Everything is SubQ with a short, thin insulin needle. Wash hands, wipe the stopper and skin with alcohol, pull to the right unit mark, pinch an inch of skin, insert at 45°, push slowly, drop the syringe in the sharps container. Rotate sites.

Draw marks (active): Wolverine 10u, Reta 10u, SS-31 10u, MOTS-C 20u. Glance at the line every shot.

Tip 03 — Reta Dose Ladder
Start Low, Titrate If Needed

Reta starts at 1 mg (10u) M/W/F — conservative entry to build GI tolerance. If response stalls after a few weeks and you're tolerating it well, we can step up to 1.5–2 mg. Message me before climbing. Hydrate well (3–4 L/day) — Reta blunts thirst.
