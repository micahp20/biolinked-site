Protocol Overview — How the Three Compounds Work Together
Retatrutide — The Fat-Loss Engine (Long Runner)
The compound that defines the cycle length. Triple-agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon) drives appetite suppression, fat oxidation, and energy expenditure all at once. The glucagon arm is what separates Reta from Tirzepatide on body comp outcomes — you get more fat loss per unit of appetite suppression. Starts at 1 mg M/W/F (conservative); can titrate up to 1.5–2 mg if response stalls. Runs ~20 weeks on the single vial.
SS-31 — The Mitochondrial Recovery Floor (Wks 1–6)
Binds cardiolipin on the inner mitochondrial membrane, stabilizing the electron transport chain. Translation: less oxidative stress from training, faster cellular recovery between sessions, more energy under deficit. M–F until the vial empties at ~Week 6 — the front-loaded window where you're stacking calories down and training hard simultaneously.
MOTS-C — The Mitochondrial Second Wave (Wks 7–8) · Free Gift
A mitochondrial-derived peptide that activates AMPK — the same pathway exercise activates. Drives fat oxidation, mitochondrial biogenesis, and insulin sensitivity. Run after SS-31 by design: SS-31 stabilizes the mitochondrial membranes through the deficit's steepest weeks; MOTS-C then turns the metabolism inside those membranes up a notch right when the body is most adapted to the deficit and most receptive to a metabolic nudge. M–F for ~2 weeks until the 10 mg vial empties.
Stack Synergy — Stabilize, Then Activate: The three compounds run in a deliberate sequence. Reta drives the body comp side across the whole cycle. SS-31 protects the mitochondria during the steepest part of the deficit (Wks 1–6) when training + calorie restriction stress the system the most. MOTS-C then picks up where SS-31 leaves off: instead of just protecting the engine, it tunes it — AMPK activation = fat oxidation + insulin sensitivity + biogenesis, the cellular signal of "trained / metabolically fit." This is the same pathway exercise activates, layered on top of an already adapted body. The result: a sharper recomp curve that doesn't just preserve recovery (SS-31) but amplifies metabolic activity (MOTS-C) right when fat loss should be compounding the most.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Reta active vial will be in the fridge for ~20 weeks — the convention with longer-cycle Reta is that potency holds well past the 30-day window when kept clean and dark. Rotate to a fresh draw site on the stopper each time and avoid contamination.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Reta Titration
Move the Dose Based on Response
Tip 01 — Reta Dose Ladder
Data-Driven, Not Calendar-Driven

Starting at 1 mg M/W/F is the conservative entry — right move for the first month to establish tolerance. After that, the dose is yours to move based on how the body is responding. The ladder:

  • 0.5 mg = 5 units — drop-back dose if GI issues, fat loss too fast, or energy crashes
  • 1.0 mg = 10 units — starter (Weeks 1–4 minimum)
  • 1.5 mg = 15 units — advance to this only if 1 mg has plateaued + tolerance is solid
  • 2.0 mg = 20 units — cap. Only if 1.5 has plateaued. No benefit going above this on a recomp cycle.

Move UP signals: GI tolerance is clean (no nausea/vomiting/persistent constipation), appetite control is effective but not extreme, fat loss has slowed or plateaued.

Move DOWN signals: persistent nausea past Week 2, vomiting at all, fat loss faster than ~2 lbs/wk sustained (lean tissue at risk), energy dropping or workouts feeling flat (under-eating).

Hold rule: if you're getting what you want at the current dose, hold — never advance for the sake of advancing.

Tip 02 — Run-Until-Empty Mindset
The Cycle Is Not Locked to a Week Count

SS-31 finishes at ~Week 6 when the vial empties M–F. Reta finishes whenever the 60 mg vial runs out — at flat 1 mg M/W/F that's ~Week 20. If you titrate up, you consume the vial faster.

Don't chase a date. The math determines the duration. When you have ~10 doses left, that's the signal to plan what's next: re-up on Reta, switch to a different fat-loss compound, or take a break and reassess body comp.

Week-by-Week
What to Expect
Weeks 1–2
Foundation · First Reta GI Window

SS-31 starts contributing recovery improvements within the first week. Reta at 1 mg M/W/F is the conservative entry — the first 1–2 weeks are the GI tolerance window. Mild nausea or appetite suppression is expected; usually clears by Week 2. If nausea is severe or persistent, drop to 0.5 mg (2.5 units) for a few doses before resuming the climb.

Log appetite, energy, and any GI signals. This baseline matters for whether to advance later.

Weeks 3–4
First Signals

Reta is now at therapeutic concentration — appetite control firm, fat loss starting to show. SS-31 contribution is steady recovery between sessions — what used to take 48 hours clearing in 24. Energy steadier through the day.

Don't get aggressive with the deficit. Reta does the appetite work for you — let it.

Weeks 5–6
SS-31 Closing Out · MOTS-C On Deck

SS-31 vial empties at the end of Week 6 — the membrane-protective work it did is locked in. The recovery baseline carries forward; you've adapted to the training-while-in-deficit pattern.

Reta concentration is solid. Fat loss curve should be at its steepest right around here. If it's slower than expected, consider the Week 5+ Reta bump to 1.5 mg.

Reconstitute the MOTS-C vial in Week 6 so it's ready to start the morning after SS-31 runs out.

Weeks 7–8
MOTS-C On · Mitochondrial Activation Window

MOTS-C takes the baton from SS-31. Same morning shot as SS-31 had — M–F, 1 mg / 20 units — but a completely different mechanism. Where SS-31 was protective (cardiolipin stabilization), MOTS-C is activating: AMPK upregulation, fat oxidation, mitochondrial biogenesis. The body is most receptive to this signal now — the membranes have been protected for six weeks, the deficit has driven adaptation, and Reta has primed the fat-loss machinery. MOTS-C nudges the metabolism up at exactly the right moment.

Expect: steadier energy through training, fat loss potentially accelerating (especially visceral), better post-workout recovery feel. The vial empties at end of Week 8 — 10 doses M–F across 2 weeks, exact match.

Weeks 9–12
Reta Solo · Body Comp Continues

Just Reta now. Fat loss continues at the dose you've settled on. This is the longest stretch of the cycle — predictable, steady. The only adjustments here are response-driven: hold, bump up, or back off.

If at any point during this stretch the body comp curve stalls for 2+ weeks, that's the time to bump dose (1.5 mg, then 2 mg cap). If energy or training quality drops, back off or add cals around training.

Weeks 13–20
Final Stretch · End-of-Cycle Decisions

Last third of the Reta vial. Track vial fill weekly — when you're down to ~10 doses, decide what's next.

End-of-cycle questions: How does body comp compare to Week 1? Is appetite control still effective? Is energy holding? These answers determine whether to re-up on Reta, switch compounds, or take 4–8 weeks off and reassess.

Execution Tips
Timing · Pairing · Monitoring
Tip 03 — Same Morning, Different Sites
Stack the AM Shots, Rotate Quadrants

On M/W/F you have two shots all the way through Week 8. Easiest approach: do them back-to-back in the same morning window, but rotate to different abdomen quadrants. Example: SS-31 (or MOTS-C, Wks 7–8) upper-left, Reta lower-right. Next M/W/F flip the sides. Never the same spot two days in a row.

None of the three compounds require fasted — just morning consistency. Doing them together builds the habit and keeps them on the same calendar event.

Tip 04 — Half-Life + Missed Doses
Different Rules per Compound

Reta (long half-life, ~6 days): miss by <24 hrs — take when you remember and continue M/W/F. Miss by >24 hrs — skip and resume next scheduled day. Don't double up.

SS-31 and MOTS-C (short half-life): if you miss a dose, just skip and resume next M–F day. No doubling.

Tip 05 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild nausea / appetite drop with first 1–2 Reta shots — subsides
· Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites and use proper SubQ technique
· Constipation on Reta — common; hydration + fiber addresses it
· Slightly disrupted sleep the first few SS-31 days — resolves

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique issue or contamination
· Severe nausea or vomiting past Week 2 of Reta — drop to 0.5 mg or pause
· Fat loss faster than ~2 lbs/wk sustained — drop Reta dose, lean tissue is at risk
· Persistent fatigue or workouts feeling flat — usually under-eating; add cals around training
