Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, One Vial at a Time

Dry vials: refrigerate at 36–46°F, protected from light. Never freeze.

After mixing: refrigerate and use within ~30 days. Reconstitute one vial at a time — open the next only when the current one runs dry.

Every shot: fresh single-use syringe, wipe the stopper and your skin with alcohol, rotate injection sites. For Wolverine, keep the shots grouped toward the area you're targeting.

Execution & Tips

Tip 01 — Wolverine (New)
Inject Toward the Site · 20 Days Straight

One shot a day, 10u (1 mg), for 20 days in a row. Inject SubQ toward the area you're targeting — BPC-157 does its best repair work locally, so the closer to the injury/injection site, the better. Rotate the exact spot around the area day to day so you're not hitting the same pinhole.

Two vials cover the sprint (each vial = 10 daily doses); the other two are your backup or a second round if the area needs more. Mix one vial at a time.

Miss a day? Take it when you remember or just resume the next day — don't double up. The goal is 20 total daily doses toward the site.

Tip 02 — Injection Basics
SubQ, Short Needle, Barely Noticeable

Everything here is SubQ (just under the skin) with a short, thin insulin needle. Wash hands, wipe the vial stopper and your skin with alcohol, pull the plunger to the right unit mark, pinch an inch of skin, insert at 45°, push slowly, and drop the syringe in the sharps container. Rotate sites so nothing stacks in one spot.

Draw marks: Wolverine 10u, Reta 10u, SS-31 10u, KLOW 10u, CJC/Ipa 10u, PT-141 10u (5u first use), MOTS-C 20u, Tesamorelin 20u. Glance at the line every shot.

Tip 03 — Reta Dose Ladder
Start Low, Titrate If Needed

Reta starts at 1 mg (10u) M/W/F — a conservative entry to build GI tolerance. If appetite/response stalls after a few weeks and you're tolerating it well, we can step up to 1.5–2 mg. Message me before climbing and we'll time it. Hydrate well (3–4 L/day) — Reta blunts thirst.

Tip 04 — Timing That Matters
Fasted GH Shots & PT-141 PRN

CJC/Ipamorelin & Tesamorelin are pre-bed and fasted (2+ hrs after your last meal) — food spikes insulin and blunts the GH pulse. If you ate late, skip that night rather than dose fed.

PT-141 is as-needed, 30–60 min before — peaks at 60–90 min, lasts 6–12 hrs. First use: half dose (5u) to check for nausea. Cap at one use per 24–48 hrs.

SS-31 & MOTS-C are AM. MOTS-C starts the day SS-31's vial empties — stabilize first, then activate.
