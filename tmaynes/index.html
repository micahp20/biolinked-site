Taylin's Ankle Rebuild
Two phases, ~7 weeks — repair the ankle hard, then heal it broad, before the season.

The goal: get the hurt ankle fully healed — not just quiet, but durable — before you're back cutting, sprinting, and playing college soccer. This is built in two phases that follow how tissue actually heals: Phase 1 repairs, Phase 2 calms and consolidates. You run them back to back, and both go in right at the ankle so the healing signal lands on the injury.

Phase 1 · Repair — Wolverine, Days 1–20. A short, aggressive front-load: Wolverine (BPC-157 + TB-500) at 1 mg, once daily for 20 straight days, injected SubQ at the injured ankle. A soccer ankle — rolled, sprained, or just beat up from cutting and running — is a mix of ligament/tendon damage and swelling. BPC-157 is the local repair driver (new blood vessels, ligament and tendon healing right where you inject); TB-500 pulls repair cells to the injury and knocks down inflammation across the joint. Two vials cover the 20-day sprint.

Phase 2 · Heal & Consolidate — KLOW, ~4 weeks. Once the two Wolverine vials are empty, you roll into KLOW at 4 mg (15u), Monday–Friday for ~4 weeks — also injected at the ankle. KLOW is a four-peptide blend: GHK-Cu + BPC-157 + TB-500 + KPV. It keeps the two core repair peptides going, then adds GHK-Cu (collagen and connective-tissue remodeling) and KPV (a potent anti-inflammatory) on top. Here's the synergy: Phase 1 floods the ankle with fast repair signal every day; Phase 2 broadens that to a full anti-inflammatory, collagen-building blend to mature the new tissue and shut down the lingering swelling. Wolverine builds it; KLOW strengthens and calms it.

The part that isn't the peptide: during these ~7 weeks, respect the ankle. Peptides heal tissue, but they can't out-repair a joint you keep re-tweaking. Ease off hard cutting and sprinting early, ice after activity, and as it settles in Phase 2 start easing controlled ankle loading (balance/mobility work) back in — that's what tells the new tissue how to organize. Smart rehab now buys you a healthy ankle for the season.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, One Vial at a Time

Dry vials (before mixing): keep in the fridge at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light.

After you mix a vial: refrigerate and use within its window. Only mix one vial at a time — Wolverine Vial 1, then Vial 2, then the KLOW vial when Phase 2 starts.

Mixing technique: wipe the vial top with alcohol, draw the water (1 mL for Wolverine, 3 mL for KLOW), and let it run slowly down the inside wall — don't blast the powder. Swirl gently; never shake hard. KLOW turns clear blue once mixed — that's the copper in GHK-Cu, totally normal. Wolverine stays colorless.

Every shot: fresh single-use syringe, wipe the stopper and your skin with alcohol, keep the shots grouped at the injured ankle.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Day 1
Tip 01 — The Daily Rhythm
One Shot a Day, Two Phases, ~7 Weeks

Phase 1 (Days 1–20): Wolverine 1 mg (10u), once daily, every day, at the ankle. Phase 2 (~4 weeks): KLOW 4 mg (15u), Monday–Friday, at the ankle. No gap between phases — start KLOW the day after the second Wolverine vial empties.

Pick a consistent time. Morning is the easy default — do it after you wake up so you never forget. For repair peptides the exact clock time doesn't matter much; consistency does. Use the calendar file on the Schedule tab.

Two different doses — don't mix them up. Wolverine is 10 units; KLOW is 15 units. When you switch phases, the number on the syringe changes. Glance at the line every time.

Tip 02 — Injection Walkthrough
SubQ at the Ankle — Step by Step

Both compounds are SubQ shots — just under the skin, into the soft tissue right around the injured ankle, with the smallest needle in clinical use. Easy and mostly painless.

Step-by-step (every shot):

1. Wash hands. Pull the mixed vial from the fridge.
2. Wipe the rubber stopper with an alcohol pad; let it dry.
3. Pull the needle cap, push through the stopper, and draw to the right mark — 10 units for Wolverine (Phase 1), 15 units for KLOW (Phase 2).
4. Find a soft spot right around the injured area of the ankle. Wipe it with alcohol, let it dry.
5. Pinch a bit of skin, insert at 45°, push the plunger slowly.
6. Pull the needle straight out. Drop the whole syringe in the sharps container.

Rotate the spot each day — front, sides, above, below the sore point — but keep every shot in the same general ankle zone. That's the point: local healing.

Tip 03 — Respect the Ankle
Don't Out-Train the Healing

The single biggest thing you control is giving the ankle a break while it heals. BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, and KPV build and calm tissue, but they can't keep up if you keep re-rolling or overloading the joint.

Phase 1: pull back on hard cutting, sprinting, and jumping. Keep movement light and pain-free. Ice 10–15 min after anything that aggravates it.

Phase 2: as it settles, ease controlled loading back in — balance work, ankle mobility, light footwork. That loading tells the new tissue how to organize. Ramp back methodically; don't jump straight to full-speed cutting.

Tip 04 — Honest Timeline
What to Expect Across the Rebuild

Days 1–7 (Phase 1 start): loading. Some feel the ankle quiet down and loosen in the first week; plenty don't feel much yet. The repair signal is building — don't judge it by day 3.

Days 8–20 (rest of Phase 1): this is where most people notice the repair — less ache, better range of motion, the joint feeling steadier. Message Micah around day 10.

Weeks 4–7 (Phase 2 · KLOW): the anti-inflammatory, collagen-building phase. Swelling keeps dropping, the joint gets more durable underneath, and this is when you ease controlled loading back in. By the end of the KLOW vial you should have a clear read on how the ankle holds up to real soccer movements.

After the rebuild: we reassess. Healing tissue keeps improving for weeks after the last dose. If it's most of the way there, carry the smart-loading discipline into the season; if a specific movement still nags, we decide whether a short maintenance touch-up or continued rehab finishes it.

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Miss a day? Take it when you remember that day if you can. If a whole day slips by, resume the next scheduled day — don't double up. Two shots at once doesn't heal faster.

Phase 1 shifts, it doesn't break: if you miss a Wolverine day, the 20 doses just stretch across a few extra calendar days. Phase 2 is weekdays only — miss a weekday KLOW, take it that evening or just catch the next weekday; skip weekends either way.

Consistency beats perfection. Getting the shot in most days is what matters. Don't let one missed day turn into three.

Tip 06 — Red Flags · When to Pause
Signals to Watch For (Text Micah First)

Pause and message Micah if any of these show up:

· Redness, warmth, swelling, or pus at an injection site that's getting worse — a possible infection, not normal healing
· A hard, painful lump at the ankle that doesn't settle in a day or two
· Any rash, hives, or trouble breathing after a shot — stop and treat as an allergic reaction
· Ankle pain sharply worse after starting (vs. the usual ache) — may mean you're overloading it; back off activity

Normal and fine: a brief sting during the shot, a little redness at the pinhole that fades in an hour, mild tenderness at the site.

Keep it clean: fresh syringe every time, alcohol-wipe the vial and skin, one vial mixed at a time. When in doubt, text first — don't push through something that feels off.
