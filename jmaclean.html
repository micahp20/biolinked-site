Featured Tips for Your Stack
Tip 01 — Collagen + Vitamin C Pre-Rehab (Keith Baar Protocol)
The Nutrition Adjunct That Actually Works for Tendon + Cartilage

This one is non-negotiable for the meniscus side of the protocol. The Keith Baar lab work at UC Davis demonstrated that 15 g of hydrolyzed collagen + 500 mg of vitamin C taken 60 minutes before loaded movement drives a measurable spike in collagen synthesis at the tendon / ligament / cartilage site being loaded.

How to use it:

  • 15 g hydrolyzed collagen (Vital Proteins or similar — mixed in water or coffee).
  • 500 mg vitamin C (any form — ascorbic acid is fine).
  • Take 60 min before any rehab session, stationary bike, light loaded mobility, or training. Repeat before every loading session, not just heavy ones.
  • Stationary bike + light isometrics = "loading" for the meniscus and counts.

Why this matters for the meniscus specifically: the collagen + C spike provides the raw substrate exactly when the loading signal is telling the cartilage to remodel. The peptide stack amplifies the signal; this nutrition adjunct provides the building blocks. Without it, you're sending the signal without the materials.

Tip 02 — AVOID NSAIDs During the Cycle
No Advil, No Aleve, No Ibuprofen · Use Tylenol For Pain

NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen, etc.) blunt the inflammation signal that the body uses to drive the entire healing cascade. Inflammation in moderation is the healing signal — not a problem to suppress. Suppressing it suppresses healing.

Substitute Tylenol (acetaminophen) for pain — it works through a different mechanism (central, not anti-inflammatory) and doesn't blunt the healing cascade. Same for ice: short bouts post-acute irritation are fine, but don't ice every day — you want the local inflammatory signal active for the BPC + Cartalax + GH pulse to compound with.

This is the single biggest avoidable mistake people make during a recovery cycle. The peptides do their best work in an inflammatory environment, not a suppressed one.

Tip 03 — Rehab + Training Approach
Appropriate Load = Faster Healing · Total Rest = Slower Healing

Don't take this stack as license to skip rehab. The meniscus needs appropriate load to remodel. Total rest = slower healing. The peptides amplify the rehab work; they don't replace it.

  • Stationary bike (low resistance, controlled tempo) — perfect early-cycle loading. Trains the joint capsule and circulates synovial fluid without high impact.
  • Controlled isometric work — wall sits, quad sets, hamstring isometrics. Builds tendon stiffness without joint loading.
  • Gradual loaded mobility — step-ups, lunges, eventually loaded squats as PT clears the progression.
  • SLU-PP-332 lets you train the rest of the body harder — upper body + non-affected leg work without overloading the knee. Use the SLU window for those sessions.
  • Listen to the knee. Soreness 24–48 hrs after a session is normal; sharp pain at the site is not. Back off if the latter shows up.
Tip 04 — Consider PRP at Week 3–4 (Optional)
PRP + This Stack = Synergistic

If you're considering a PRP (platelet-rich plasma) injection at the meniscus, Week 3–4 of this cycle is the ideal timing. By then BPC has been driving angiogenesis for 3–4 weeks, the vascular bed is more receptive, and the PRP growth factors land into a tissue environment that's been primed by the peptide stack.

This is optional — the protocol works without PRP. But if your ortho / sports med doc is offering it, mid-cycle is the synergy window. Skip if not on the table.

Tip 05 — Protein + Sleep Targets
1 g/lb Bodyweight · 8+ Hours Minimum

Two non-negotiables for the lean muscle goal:

  • Protein: 1 g per lb bodyweight, daily. Distribute across 4–5 meals, 40–50 g per meal. Post-workout whey within 60 min. Pre-bed: slow-digest protein (cottage cheese, casein) 1.5–2 hrs before the CJC/Ipa shot — NOT closer (insulin near the shot blunts the GH pulse).
  • Sleep: 8+ hrs minimum. This is where CJC/Ipa actually does its work. Skipping sleep means paying for the shot but not collecting the result.

Peptides build the capacity; nutrition + sleep build the actual outcome.

Cycle 2 — SS-31 + MOTS-C + NAD+ Synergy
What to Add After This Cycle · The Mitochondrial Triple

After this 8-week recovery + lean build cycle, the next logical layer is the deep mitochondrial reset. SS-31 + MOTS-C together attack cellular energy from two complementary angles, and they pair perfectly with NAD+ as a continuing baseline.

SS-31 (Elamipretide) — stabilizes cardiolipin at the inner mitochondrial membrane. Cardiolipin is what makes mitochondria actually produce ATP efficiently. When it's damaged (from age, training stress, inflammation), energy output drops even if you have plenty of NAD+. SS-31 fixes the structural side. Most users report "I feel my engine running again" — it's that direct.

MOTS-C — mitochondrial-derived peptide that activates AMPK, the master metabolic switch. Drives fat oxidation, glucose disposal, and exercise capacity. Works on the SIGNALING side of mitochondrial function.

Why they synergize:

  • NAD+ provides the fuel (substrate for sirtuins and mitochondrial enzymes)
  • SS-31 fixes the hardware (cardiolipin = membrane integrity = ATP production)
  • MOTS-C runs the software (AMPK signaling = metabolic efficiency)

Run all three together and you're hitting every layer of mitochondrial function at once. This is what powers next-level lean muscle gains, training capacity, and recovery — far beyond what NAD+ alone can do.
