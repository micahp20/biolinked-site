Tip 01 — The Three-Layer Logic
Why KLOW + Wolverine + NAD Together

The knee hasn't healed because the cellular repair machinery isn't firing — usually a combination of chronic low-grade inflammation blocking the signal, the body's normal age-related slowdown in tissue turnover, and not enough cellular energy to execute big repair work. This stack hits all three.

1. KLOW (M–F AM) — the systemic instruction set. Four peptides in one shot tell the whole body to repair: KPV calms inflammation, GHK-Cu signals collagen synthesis, TB-500 drives tissue regeneration, BPC handles angiogenesis. The whole body gets the message, every weekday for 8 weeks straight.

2. Wolverine (M–F PM, at knee, ~4 weeks) — the local intensive. BPC + TB-500 injected directly at the knee concentrates a much stronger repair signal exactly where the damage is. Different injection site, much higher local dose, same peptides as part of KLOW but pointed at the meniscus instead of distributed systemically. Front-loaded for the first 4 weeks — the "hit it hard while it's actively responsive" window.

3. NAD (M/W/F AM) — the cellular fuel. Peptides give the instructions. NAD makes sure the body has the energy to follow them. Mitochondrial ATP production, sirtuin activation, DNA repair — all run on NAD+. After 30, NAD levels drop ~1.5–2% per year, and the cellular machinery that powers tissue repair slows with it. Adding 50 mg M/W/F gives the energy floor a noticeable lift.

Why all three together: peptides alone heal the knee, but the timeline is the timeline of the body's available energy. Add NAD and the repair work happens faster because the cellular substrate isn't the bottleneck. Add Wolverine at the site and the local response is dramatically stronger than KLOW's systemic dose alone can deliver. Each layer makes the other two work harder.

Tip 02 — Week-by-Week Timeline
What to Expect from the 8-Week Cycle

Week 1: baseline learning curve — getting the AM (KLOW + NAD on M/W/F) and PM (Wolverine at knee) routine locked in. NAD flushing may be noticeable the first 2–3 shots; slow the plunger if so. Peptide effects are subtle the first 5–7 days. Don't push the knee through painful range of motion; let the compounds work.

Week 2: NAD energy lift starts showing up — cleaner daytime focus, less afternoon dip. The knee should start to feel noticeably less inflamed. Range of motion typically improves first — the joint moves easier before the deep ache fully resolves.

Weeks 3–4 (Wolverine still running): meaningful improvement lands. Pain at rest drops substantially. Specific movements that triggered sharp knee pain start feeling less reactive. Many clients describe this as "the knee is actually getting better" rather than just being less inflamed. NAD energy effect is steady now — the cleaner-running feeling is the new normal. Wolverine vials empty at end of Week 4.

Weeks 5–6 (KLOW + NAD alone, deeper remodeling): the systemic healing signal continues. Deeper meniscus and joint tissue repair is happening on its slower 4–6 week timeline. Knee may feel like progress has plateaued visually, but the deep tissue work is the main thing happening here. Don't stop just because the dramatic improvements slow down — this is the consolidation window.

Weeks 7–8 (final remodeling): the tissue locks in. Strength and stability return to the knee. Full range of motion typically restored by end of Week 8. By cycle end, most clients describe the knee as "functional again" or fully healed — no longer the limiting factor in daily life. NAD still running with shots to spare.

End of cycle: decision point. If improvement is complete, the remodeling continues for another 4–6 weeks on its own. NAD continues if you want to keep the energy floor lifted (the remaining vial covers another ~3–4 weeks at M/W/F). If there's still residual issue, another round of KLOW or BPC + TB maintenance is an option.

Tip 03 — NAD Injection Technique (Flushing Fix)
Slow the Plunger · That's the Whole Trick

NAD SubQ can cause warmth, flushing, or a brief "hot" sensation in the chest, neck, or face during or right after the shot. It's harmless — the body clearing the bolus — and usually passes in 1–5 minutes. The fix is technique:

Push the plunger slowly. Aim for 15–30 seconds to deliver the full 25 units. Not 3 seconds, not "as fast as KLOW." Slow push gives the tissue time to absorb the bolus gradually — less flushing, less sting.

If flushing is still uncomfortable: slow the push even more next time (30–45 seconds), or split it — deliver half, pause 10 seconds, deliver the rest. After 3–4 shots the body usually acclimates and the flushing fades.

Where to inject: SubQ thigh works well — rotate sides. Or a different abdomen quadrant than the one you used for KLOW that morning. Don't stack NAD and KLOW in the same spot.

Doesn't need to be fasted. NAD can go right after KLOW. Many clients run them back-to-back, two different sites, two different syringes, two minutes apart.

Tip 04 — Wolverine At-Knee Injection How-To
Where Exactly to Inject (Knee Anatomy)

The Wolverine shot goes SubQ into the soft tissue around the painful part of the knee — not into the joint itself. Same technique as a standard SubQ shot, just at the knee instead of the abdomen.

Find the spot: press around the knee and find where the pain centers. Meniscus pain typically centers along the joint line on the medial (inner) or lateral (outer) side. Inject SubQ ~1–2 inches from the worst point of pain — close enough that the local BPC + TB signal lands at the meniscus, not so close you're poking obviously inflamed tissue.

Technique: wipe the skin with alcohol. Pinch a small fold of skin. Insert needle at 45°. Push the plunger steadily (20 units = 0.20 mL takes a beat longer than the KLOW 10u shot). Withdraw, dispose in sharps container.

Rotate within the knee region. Don't inject the same exact spot two nights in a row — rotate within a 2–3 inch area around the painful zone so no single point takes consecutive shots. Mild soreness at the injection site for an hour or two is normal; persistent redness or swelling past 24 hrs means rotate further next time.

Tip 05 — Movement During the Cycle
Light Use, Not Forced Rest or Forced Stretching

Move the knee gently through its current pain-free range every day. No forced stretching, no aggressive PT exercises, no pushing through sharp pain. Light use tells the body that the tissue needs to remain functional — the repair stages rebuild for use, not just for rest.

What to avoid: deep squats, heavy lunges, running, jumping, jerky lateral movements. If a movement creates sharp pain, that's a "skip this for now" signal — not something to push through.

What's fine: walking, slow stationary biking (low resistance), swimming or water work (joint-friendly), normal daily activities (driving, stairs without rushing, standing for work). Warm-water exposure (showers, baths) helps circulation to the area.

Tip 06 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

KLOW (M–F AM): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across the cycle doesn't move the needle.

Wolverine (M–F PM at knee): if you miss a PM dose, take it AM the next day at the knee instead and resume PM the day after. The local dual-peptide healing signal benefits from consistency, but a one-day shift isn't a problem. Don't double up.

NAD (M/W/F AM): miss a scheduled day, skip it. Resume the next M/W/F day. 3×/week is generous — one missed dose doesn't move the cellular energy floor.

Tip 07 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold the next KLOW or Wolverine dose if any of the following:

· Significant injection-site reaction at the knee (redness, heat, swelling, persistent pain past 24 hours) — could indicate the spot is too close to inflamed tissue; pause Wolverine, rotate location next time
· New or worsening allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes)
· Sharp new pain in the knee that's clearly different from your baseline issue — could indicate the area is being aggravated; pause and reassess
· Any sustained worsening past Week 2 (instead of improvement) — flag for adjustment, not a temporary side effect

Hold the next NAD dose if:

· Severe flushing that doesn't resolve in 5–10 minutes despite slowing the plunger
· Chest tightness, racing heart, or anxiety-type symptoms post-injection (rare but reported in fast-pushed NAD)
· Persistent injection-site reaction past 24 hours

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through — we adjust the plan based on what's actually happening.
