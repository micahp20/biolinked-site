Already burning. Now stacking for energy and lean tissue.

Both run M–F alongside the existing Reta schedule. Two bottles each — about an eight-week run that lands the back half of Annie's Reta cycle.

Reconstitution
Both New Vials Use the Same 2 mL Recipe
🧪 How to Recon — MOTS-c & CJC / Ipamorelin

Both new vials reconstitute with 2 mL bacteriostatic water. Same procedure, same draw amount, just keep them clearly labeled so you don't mix them up at 6 AM or 10 PM.

Procedure: wipe both the BAC water vial and the peptide vial with an alcohol pad. Draw 2 mL of BAC water into a syringe. Inject slowly down the inside wall of the peptide vial — not directly onto the powder. Swirl gently to mix. Do not shake. Solution should be clear within a minute. Refrigerate immediately.

Concentration math:

MOTS-c: 20 mg ÷ 2 mL = 10 mg/mL → 1 mg dose = 0.1 mL = 10 units on a U-100 syringe.
CJC / Ipamorelin: 10 mg ÷ 2 mL = 5 mg/mL → 500 mcg dose = 0.1 mL = 10 units on a U-100 syringe.

Convenient overlap: the draw amount is the same 10 units on the syringe for all three compounds in this stack (Reta, MOTS-c, CJC/Ipa). The compound and the time of day are what change — not the volume.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted MOTS-c & CJC/Ipa: store refrigerated. Use within ~30 days of reconstitution. At the pace of this protocol (1 vial per ~4 weeks), each vial is empty before the stability window matters — mix one vial at a time, finish it, then mix the next.

Syringe discipline: fresh U-100 insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe stopper + injection site with alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. With three different shots on Mon / Wed / Fri AM and two on Tue / Thu AM plus a pre-bed shot every weekday, rotation matters — pick a side per shot and stagger.

Stack Notes
Execution · What to Watch For · How It Should Feel
Tip 01 — The CJC / Ipa Fasting Window
2 Hours Empty Before the Pre-Bed Shot

The whole reason CJC/Ipa is dosed pre-bed is to amplify the natural GH pulse that happens in your first sleep cycle. Food — especially carbs and fats — spikes blood glucose and blunts that pulse. If you eat within 2 hours of bedtime, you've largely neutralized the effect of the shot.

Practical rule: 2+ hours empty before the shot, then injection, then sleep within ~30–60 minutes. Water is fine. If you slip and eat too close to bed, skip that night's dose and resume the next evening — better to miss a shot than waste one.

Tip 02 — MOTS-c Timing
AM Fasted Is Ideal, But Don't Stress Perfection

Default: AM on an empty stomach, ideally before or at the start of training if you train in the morning. MOTS-c works through AMPK signaling, and that pathway is most responsive when glucose and insulin are low — which is the fasted state.

If your morning routine doesn't allow fasted shots, an AM shot with light food is still effective. The schedule consistency matters more than the perfect fast. Stack it with Reta on M/W/F — same draw amount, same syringe size, just two separate shots in sequence.

Tip 03 — What MOTS-c Feels Like
Subtle Energy · Faster Recovery · Steadier Output

MOTS-c is a quiet performer — you don't feel a "hit" the way you do with a stimulant. What you notice is that the typical mid-cut energy drop just doesn't show up. Daytime energy stays steadier, training intensity holds even as the deficit deepens, and recovery between sessions feels faster.

If you're paying attention, you'll also notice a slight improvement in cardio endurance and how warm you feel during workouts — both signals that mitochondrial output is up.

Tip 04 — What CJC / Ipa Feels Like
Deeper Sleep First. Recovery Next. Body Comp Last.

Week 1: sleep deepens. You may wake feeling more rested even if total hours are the same. Some people get vivid dreams in the first week — normal, fades.

Weeks 2–4: training recovery improves. DOMS shorter, joints feeling better, less of the bone-tired feeling from training through a deficit.

Weeks 4–8: the body comp benefit shows up. Through the Reta deficit, lean tissue holds (or modestly improves) instead of getting eaten along with the fat. Visible result: tighter look at the same scale weight.

Mild side effects to know about: occasional flushing or tingling for a few minutes after the shot in week 1 (Ipamorelin's MSH-like effect — fades). Slight hand puffiness in the AM is possible but uncommon at this dose — if it happens, it resolves on its own.

Tip 05 — Hydration
3 L/Day Minimum — Especially on Reta

Reta suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite. You will not feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated, and that's the most common reason people on a GLP-1 feel headachy, foggy, or constipated and blame "the peptides."

Target: 3 L/day minimum. Add electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day, ideally pre-training. Coffee doesn't count. MOTS-c and the GH axis both work better when you're well-hydrated, so this matters across the whole stack — not just for Reta.

Tip 06 — Protein Floor
CJC / Ipa Only Works If You Feed the Tissue

CJC/Ipa amplifies the GH pulse that drives lean-tissue preservation. But the pulse needs amino acids to act on — if protein intake is low, you're firing the signal into an empty room.

Target: ~0.8–1.0 g of protein per pound of goal bodyweight daily. Reta makes this harder because appetite drops — front-load protein early in the day before suppression peaks. A protein shake within an hour of waking is usually the most reliable way to hit the floor through a Reta cut.

Tip 07 — Signals & Yellow Flags
What "Working" Looks Like · What to Watch For

What "working" looks like across the stack: steady fat loss continuing under Reta, daytime energy holding (MOTS-c), deeper sleep and faster recovery (CJC/Ipa), waist shrinking while the mirror still looks tight in the shoulders/arms (lean-mass preserved).

Yellow flags — reassess if any of these show up: persistent vomiting or severe abdominal pain (Reta-related — pause and contact a clinician); strength dropping noticeably in the gym (lean-mass loss — lift protein, possibly back Reta down a notch); sleep getting worse instead of better while on CJC/Ipa (usually a fasting-window issue — tighten the 2-hour rule); persistent AM puffiness in the hands that doesn't resolve (lower CJC/Ipa frequency or pause).

Tip 08 — When the Bottles Run Out
~8 Weeks In, Decision Point

This add-on is built as "run till empty" — finish the 2 vials of each, no taper required. At ~8 weeks the MOTS-c and CJC/Ipa bottles will both be empty around the same time, which is also roughly when your Reta cycle is winding down.

Decision point at that mark: extend the GH axis support with another round of CJC/Ipa to bridge the post-Reta phase cleanly (recommended if you've felt the recovery and sleep benefit), or pause the stack and let the body run on its own native rhythm for a few weeks before the next cycle. Either is fine; we'll look at how the cycle has gone and decide together.
