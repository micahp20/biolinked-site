✨ Why 1 mg M/W/F at a Steady Dose

Reta has a long half-life, so once-weekly dosing is the standard pattern — but that creates a sharp peak in the 24–48 hours after the shot, when most people feel nausea and post-shot fatigue. Splitting the same weekly total into three smaller M/W/F doses smooths the curve. Lower peaks, fewer side effects, more even daytime energy, and steadier appetite suppression across the whole week.

Holding at 1 mg per shot the entire 10 weeks (3 mg/week) is intentional. It's a low-and-steady dose that gives reliable appetite control and weight loss without the GI side effects of higher Reta doses, and it leaves the metabolic and hormonal "headroom" your body needs for the muscle-building and fat-oxidation work the optional stack is designed to do. If you went higher on Reta, you'd risk eating into the lean-mass and energy-expenditure benefits the rest of the protocol exists to deliver.

Design Philosophy

CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin
Muscle Growth · Recovery · Lean Mass
A GHRH (CJC-1295) and GHRP (Ipamorelin) combo that drives natural pulsatile growth hormone release. The benefits stack: improved recovery from training, lean tissue preservation through caloric deficit, deeper sleep, modest fat loss synergy, and — over months — increased lean muscle in trained populations. This is the muscle growth layer of your protocol.
Synergy with Reta: GLP-1 weight loss inevitably includes some lean-mass loss, particularly if protein intake or resistance training is inadequate. CJC/Ipa preserves muscle through the deficit so what you lose is fat — not metabolically active tissue. If you're training during the program (and you should be), this is the highest-leverage add. Most useful from Week 1 onward; results compound the longer you're on it.
MOTS-c
Energy Expenditure · Mitochondrial Output
A mitochondrial-derived peptide that signals the body to build more mitochondria and run them more efficiently. The downstream effect is increased cellular energy production and increased energy expenditure — your cells burn through more substrate to do the same work, which compounds with Reta's caloric deficit. MOTS-c also improves insulin sensitivity in skeletal muscle, helping nutrients route toward muscle instead of fat storage.
Synergy with Reta: Reta cuts intake; MOTS-c raises output. The combination is the cleanest version of "eat less, burn more" available in the peptide space — both sides of the energy equation, working at the cellular level on opposite levers. This is the energy expenditure layer.
Tesamorelin
Visceral Fat · Targeted Body Comp
A GHRH analog specifically validated to reduce visceral adipose tissue — the deep belly fat around your organs. Reta drops total weight; Tesa shifts the composition of what you lose toward the visceral compartment. Mechanism: pulsatile GH release → IGF-1 → preferential lipolysis of visceral stores. Of all the stack additions, this is the one with the most direct, named clinical effect on the specific fat depot you're targeting.
Synergy with Reta: Reta gives the deficit; Tesa decides where it gets paid. If your goal includes "flatten the belly" and not just "lower the scale," Tesa is the layer that converts caloric deficit into visible midsection change. This is the visceral fat layer.
5-Amino-1MQ
Accelerated Fat Burn · NNMT Inhibition
An NNMT inhibitor — it blocks the enzyme that drains the methyl-donor and NAD+ pool inside fat cells, which keeps adipocytes locked into "store fat" mode. Inhibiting NNMT flips them toward burning stored lipid and resists fat-cell expansion. As a bonus side effect, it raises your endogenous NAD+ levels, which feeds back into mitochondrial output (and pairs naturally with MOTS-c).
Synergy with Reta: Reta creates the deficit; 5-Amino-1MQ accelerates the metabolic consequence. Together: bigger deficit + faster fat oxidation = faster scale movement, particularly during the maintenance phase when Reta plateaus tend to appear (typically Weeks 6–8). This is the accelerated fat burner layer.
Safety & Holds

⚠️ When to Hold a Dose

Hold the next dose if any of the following: persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to back — rule out pancreatitis), unexplained tachycardia or palpitations, severe injection-site reaction beyond mild redness, or any new symptom that feels meaningfully off.

Hydration + fiber: low-grade nausea and constipation are the two most common Reta side effects. Both respond well to aggressive hydration (~3L/day) and adding fiber (psyllium husk or whole foods). Address before they become a reason to hold a dose.

Protein floor: with the goal of preserving (and ideally building) muscle through this cut, hit at least 0.8–1.0 g of protein per pound of target body weight daily. Reta makes this harder because appetite drops — front-load protein early in the day before suppression peaks.

Storage & Handling

❄️ Refrigerated, Protected, Single-Use

Dry vial (before reconstitution): store refrigerated at 36–46 °F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Reconstituted vial: refrigerate immediately after mixing, always.

Syringe hygiene: fresh single-use syringe for every draw, no exceptions. Needle reuse is the most common cause of injection site reactions and infections.

Site rotation: rotate between lower abdomen (a fingerbreadth from the navel), outer thigh, and love handles. Never the same 1-inch spot two consecutive injections — sites need 48+ hours to recover. With M/W/F dosing, alternating sides each shot works cleanly.
