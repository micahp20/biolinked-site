Protocol Overview — How the Stack Works Together
Testosterone — The Anabolic Base (185 mg/wk · 1 shot)
Once-weekly 185 mg IM cypionate. Provides the muscle-protein-synthesis floor that everything else builds on. 2 vials cover ~27 weeks (~6 months) at this dose. Peptide stack layers on top without interference: Tesa works on a different axis (GHRH / pituitary), Reta on a different one again (gut hormones / CNS appetite). Three independent levers, none stepping on each other. Bloodwork cadence & targets are in the Notes & Tips tab.
Tesamorelin — The 4-Week GH Primer
Short focused run to wake up the GH/IGF-1 axis at the start of the cycle. Pre-bed timing rides the body's natural nocturnal GH pulse — deeper sleep, better recovery, lean-tissue preservation while Reta drives fat loss. Finishes around Week 4 by design; the work it does (sleep depth, recovery baseline) carries through the rest of the Reta run.
Retatrutide — The Fat-Loss Engine (Long Runner)
The compound that defines the cycle length. Starting low at 1 mg M/W/F to establish tolerance, then titrating up based on response, not calendar. With one vial active and one frozen, runway is yours to dictate: extend continuously, or run vial #1 + take a break + restart with vial #2 for a second push.
Stack Synergy: Test base + Tesa GH primer + Reta fat-loss engine = three different mechanisms working in parallel. Tesa front-loads the cycle (Wks 1–4) to lock in sleep / recovery / lean-tissue preservation while Reta is still ramping. By the time Tesa runs out, Reta has built up therapeutic concentration and takes over as the lead compound for the rest of the cycle. Test sits underneath the whole thing as the anabolic anchor.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Freeze the Backup Reta
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials — fridge: store at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. This applies to Tesa and the active Reta vial before recon.

Reta vial #2 — freezer: store the second Reta vial in the freezer (0°F or colder) in its dry/lyophilized state. Lyophilized peptides are stable in the freezer essentially indefinitely on the cycle timescale — protects potency for when you need it later. Do not freeze after reconstitution.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Reta active vial will be in the fridge for ~13–20 weeks — that's longer than the 30-day window. The convention with longer-cycle Reta is that potency holds well past 30 days at 4°C when kept clean and dark; rotate to a fresh draw site on the stopper each time and avoid contamination.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Test Bloodwork Protocol
When to Draw, What to Run, What to Watch
Bloodwork — Cadence
When to Pull Labs Across the Cycle

1. Baseline — before Shot 1. If you haven't already pulled a recent panel (within ~3 months), do one before starting. Gives us the "before" picture to read everything else against.

2. 6-week check. Six weeks after Shot 1 puts you at steady-state on the 185 mg/wk cypionate dose — the blood levels have stabilized and what you measure reflects what's actually happening. This is the most important draw of the cycle. If anything needs adjusting (dose, AI, hematocrit management), it gets adjusted here.

3. 12-week recheck (only if anything changed at 6 wks). If we adjusted dose or added/removed an AI at 6 weeks, repeat at 12 weeks to confirm the change landed where we wanted it. If 6-week numbers were on target, skip this and go to maintenance cadence.

4. Maintenance — every 3 to 6 months. Once you're stable, two-to-four labs per year is the rhythm. Closer to every 3 months in the first year, can stretch to every 6 months once a year of clean labs is on record.

5. Symptom-driven draws. If something feels off — fatigue back, mood shift, libido drop, gym performance tanks, big water retention, persistent headaches — don't wait for the calendar. Pull labs and we read them against the current dose.

Bloodwork — Timing
When in the Week to Draw

For once-weekly TRT, draw at TROUGH — Day 7, morning, immediately before your next scheduled shot. That gives the floor of the curve, which is the level you actually want to be therapeutic. Peak levels right after a shot will look fine on any reasonable dose — trough is the honest number.

Practical: if you inject every Monday AM, schedule the draw for Monday AM, then go inject right after. Fasted (12 hr) is preferred for the lipid + glucose panels that ride along. No training, no sauna for 24 hrs before — both can transiently move some markers.

If trough timing is impossible for the visit, mid-cycle (Day 3 or 4) is the backup — tell the lab when you drew relative to last shot so we read it correctly.

Bloodwork — Red Flags
When to Adjust the Dose or Add a Lever

Hct > 52%: donate a unit of blood (Red Cross, every 56 days for men). If it stays high after two donations, drop the dose to 150–170 mg/wk and recheck. This is the #1 long-term monitoring concern on TRT — not optional to track.

E2 > 50 + symptoms (nipple sensitivity, water retention, mood): conversation about a low-dose anastrozole (typically 0.25–0.5 mg twice weekly). E2 > 50 without symptoms doesn't necessarily need an AI — many men run high E2 fine.

E2 < 15: too low. Joints will hurt, libido will tank, mood drops. If on an AI, back off. If not, dose may need a small bump.

Total T trough > 1,200 ng/dL: consider dropping the weekly dose by 25–35 mg (to 150 mg/wk or 160 mg/wk). Higher isn't better past a certain point — just more side effect risk.

Total T trough < 500 ng/dL on 185 mg/wk: rare at this dose but possible (high SHBG, fast metabolizer). May need to split to twice-weekly (92.5 mg × 2) for better steady-state, or bump dose slightly.

Big HDL drop, big LDL jump: usually diet / body fat / training related, not the test itself. Check those first. Persistent lipid shift across multiple labs — consider it.

Bloodwork — Where to Get Labs
Order Direct or Through a Doc

Through your doctor / TRT clinic: easiest if you have a relationship. Insurance often covers most of the panel.

Direct-order (no doctor required): services like Marek Health, Discounted Labs, Quest's Quest Direct, or LabCorp's OnDemand let you order the full TRT panel out of pocket. Typical cost: $150–$300 for the full panel above. Convenient for the 6-week and maintenance checks if you're not in the clinic for them.

What to ask for: "Full TRT monitoring panel: Total T, Free T, Estradiol (sensitive / LC-MS), SHBG, CBC w/ diff, CMP, lipid panel, PSA, TSH." That phrasing gets you the right test codes at most labs.

Reta Titration
How to Move the Dose Up, Down, or Hold
Tip 01 — Reta Dose Ladder
Data-Driven, Not Calendar-Driven

Reta is the long runner here — the active vial alone is 13–20 weeks depending on how you titrate. The dose ladder gives you four steps to move between:

WindowDoseUnits (5u = 1 mg)WeeklyTrigger
Wks 1–41.0 mg5u3 mg/wkStarter — establish tolerance + baseline response
Wks 5–81.5 mg7.5u4.5 mg/wkAdvance if tolerance is solid + want more effect
Wks 9+2.0 mg (cap)10u6 mg/wkOnly if 1.5 has plateaued — final ceiling
Drop step0.5 mg2.5u1.5 mg/wkBack-off dose if GI issues / fat loss too fast

Move UP signals: GI tolerance is solid (no nausea / vomiting / persistent constipation), appetite control is effective but not extreme, fat loss has slowed or plateaued. Move DOWN signals: persistent nausea past Week 2, vomiting at all, fat loss faster than ~2 lbs/wk sustained, energy dropping or workouts feeling flat (likely under-eating).

The rule: never advance because of the calendar. Advance because the body is asking for it. Hold a dose for an extra 2–4 weeks if you're getting what you want at the current step.

Tip 02 — The Frozen Vial Strategy
Two Ways to Use Vial #2

Lyophilized Reta in the freezer (0°F or colder) stays viable on any cycle timescale you'd realistically use it on. Keep it sealed in its original packaging, in the original vial, dry — do not reconstitute until ready.

Strategy A — Continuous extension: when the active vial is empty, pull the frozen vial, let it come to fridge temp (don't shock it — transfer to fridge for 24 hrs before reconstitution), then reconstitute and continue. This gives you a single ~26–40 week continuous run.

Strategy B — Maintenance restart: finish vial #1, take 4–8 weeks off Reta (keep training and Tesa cycle behind you), then restart with vial #2 for a second push. Lets the body's metabolic adaptation reset before the second wave hits.

Which to pick: depends on what's happening at the end of vial #1. Still losing? Continue (Strategy A). Stalled or feel run-down? Take the break (Strategy B). The frozen vial gives you that choice — it's not committed.

Week-by-Week
What to Expect
Weeks 1–2
Foundation · First Reta GI Window

Tesa starts kicking sleep depth + recovery within the first week. Reta at 1 mg M/W/F is the conservative entry — the first 1–2 weeks are the GI tolerance window. Mild nausea or appetite suppression is expected; usually clears by Week 2. If nausea is severe or persistent, drop to 0.5 mg (2.5 units) for a few doses before resuming the climb.

Log appetite, energy, and any GI signals. This baseline matters for whether to advance at Week 5.

Weeks 3–4
Tesa Closing Out · Reta Building

Tesa runs out around Week 4 — the GH-axis work it did is locked in. You should be sleeping better, recovering faster, lean tissue protected. Reta concentration is now therapeutic — appetite control firm, fat loss starting to show.

End of Week 4 decision point: bump Reta to 1.5 mg (7.5 units) starting Week 5, or hold at 1 mg if you're getting what you need.

Weeks 5–8
Reta Mid-Cycle · Body Comp Curve

Tesa is done; Reta carries the cycle. Fat loss curve at its steepest here if dosing is right — expect 1–1.5 lbs/wk sustained at 1.5 mg dose. If faster than that, drop back to 1 mg (you're risking lean tissue).

Energy and workout quality matter here — if they drop, you're under-eating. Add 200–300 cal of protein/carbs around training rather than holding the deficit hard.

Weeks 9+
Cap Decision · Vial Runway

Bump to 2 mg only if: 1.5 mg has plateaued, appetite control has weakened, GI tolerance has been clean for 4+ weeks. Otherwise hold at 1.5 mg through to vial empty. 2 mg is the cap — no benefit going above that on a recomp cycle.

Track vial fill level weekly. When the active vial gets to ~10 doses remaining, decide on Strategy A (extend with frozen vial) or Strategy B (finish, break, restart later).

Execution Tips
Timing · Site Rotation · Monitoring
Tip 03 — Tesa Pre-Bed Window
Empty Stomach, 2+ Hrs After Last Meal

Tesa rides the body's natural nocturnal GH pulse — food blunts the pulse, so fasted is non-negotiable. If you ate at 8 PM, inject at 10 PM or later. Water, black coffee, and tea are fine in the fasted window. Skip the dose if you snacked late.

Same time each night — the body locks onto the rhythm.

Tip 04 — Reta Timing
Morning Is Easiest · Same Time Each Dose Day

Reta has a long half-life (~6 days), so the exact time of day doesn't matter much from a pharmacology standpoint. But for habit + GI predictability, morning dosing is the cleanest — any nausea spike happens during waking hours rather than ruining sleep.

Same time each M/W/F holds the rhythm. If you miss a dose by <24 hrs, take it when you remember and continue the schedule. Miss by >24 hrs — skip and resume next scheduled day.

Tip 05 — Site Rotation
Four Abdomen Quadrants, Cycle Through

SubQ shots go in the abdomen, 2+ inches from the navel. Four quadrants: upper-left, upper-right, lower-left, lower-right. Rotate every shot — never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. Same-quadrant repeat is fine if the spot is >1″ from the last shot.

Test IM goes in a different zone entirely (quad / glute / delt — Kyle's own rotation). No overlap with the peptide sites.

Tip 06 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild nausea / appetite drop with first 1–2 Reta shots — subsides
· Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites and use proper SubQ technique
· Slight warmth or "fullness" feeling 30 min after Tesa — normal GH response
· Mild water retention in first 2 weeks of Tesa — normal, resolves as the body adapts
· Constipation on Reta — common; hydration + fiber addresses it

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique issue or contamination
· Severe nausea or vomiting past Week 2 of Reta — drop to 0.5 mg or pause
· Fat loss faster than ~2 lbs/wk sustained — drop Reta dose, lean tissue is at risk
· Persistent fatigue or workouts feeling flat — usually under-eating; add cals around training
· Numbness/tingling in hands beyond Week 4 of Tesa — flag to reassess dosing
