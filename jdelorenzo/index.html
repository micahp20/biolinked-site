Protocol Overview — Why This Order Matters
1. Why Reta Failed Joe the First Time
Retatrutide works by suppressing appetite, forcing a calorie deficit, and accelerating fat oxidation. The catch: burning fat requires functional mitochondria — that's where fat actually gets converted into ATP. After 20 years of UPS shift work, chronic occupational stress, repetitive joint impact, and likely poor sleep, Joe's mitochondria were damaged: leaky, inefficient, hemorrhaging energy as ROS instead of producing clean ATP. When Reta hit, his cells couldn't keep up with the metabolic demand. He crashed, blamed Reta, and quit. Reta wasn't the problem. The cellular foundation wasn't ready.
2. SS-31 — Repair the Engine
SS-31 binds cardiolipin on the inner mitochondrial membrane, stabilizes the electron transport chain, and dramatically reduces ROS leakage from damaged mitochondria. Think of it as servicing the engine — fixing the broken parts so they stop producing damage. This is the foundation of Phase 1.
3. BPC-157 — Heal the Body
BPC-157 reduces systemic inflammation, accelerates gut-lining repair, and supports soft tissue and tendon recovery. For a UPS driver with 20 years of cumulative occupational damage, BPC-157 addresses the structural background load that's been stealing his energy and recovery capacity. Inflammation steals energy. Healed tissue gives it back.
4. Cartalax — Target the Damage
Cartalax is a Khavinson bioregulator peptide that works at the level of genetic expression in cartilage and connective tissue cells — telling them to behave younger. For Joe's bad back and knees, this is the targeted lever. Run as a 20-day straight course M–F, it specifically addresses the cartilage matrix and joint structure decades of UPS work have ground down.
5. MOTS-C — Optimize the Engine
Once Phase 1 has repaired the mitochondrial machinery, MOTS-C drives it. MOTS-C activates AMPK, mimics the effects of exercise at the cellular level, improves insulin sensitivity, and drives mitochondrial biogenesis — the creation of new mitochondria. Running MOTS-C in Phase 1 (before SS-31 has done its repair) would be like flooring the gas on a blown engine. In Phase 2, on a healed foundation, MOTS-C builds real, sustained energy.
6. Reta — This Time Done Right
Reta returns in Phase 2 — but only after 4 weeks of foundational repair. Start at half-dose (0.5 mg) for the first two weeks to confirm Joe's cells can handle the metabolic demand this time. If tolerated, scale to the full 1 mg Mon–Thu. With repaired mitochondria, an optimized energy system, and a less-inflamed terrain, Reta should now do what it's supposed to: clean fat loss without the energy crash.
7. Why This Order Matters — Repair → Optimize → Cut. This is the order the body actually responds to. Skipping the repair phase and going straight to the cut is what failed Joe the first time. By the time Reta re-enters the picture in week 5, Joe will already feel meaningfully better — less joint pain, more daily energy, better recovery. That's the win that builds buy-in for Phase 2 and makes the cut sustainable.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Reconstitute one vial of each compound at a time — don't start the 30-day clock on a second vial until the first is close to empty.

Syringe discipline: fresh U-100 insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe stopper + injection site with alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Protocol Notes
Execution · What to Watch For
Tip 01 — Critical
Reta Re-Introduction — Don't Rush It

Joe crashed on Reta last time because his mitochondria couldn't keep up. Phase 1 fixes that — but verify before pushing the dose.

Weeks 5–6: hold at 0.5 mg (4 units) Mon–Thu. Track energy, GI tolerance, and weekly weight. If energy is steady and GI is manageable, the cellular foundation has done its job.

Weeks 7+: step up to 1 mg (8 units) Mon–Thu only if Phase 2A was clean.

If energy crashes again at ANY point: do not push through. Drop the dose back to 0.5 mg or pause for a week. Phase 1 should have resolved this, but the body has the final say. Text Micah, reassess together.

Tip 02 — Fasted State
Mitochondrial Peptides Need a Truly Empty Stomach

SS-31 (Phase 1) and MOTS-C (Phase 2) are both AM fasted. Not casually fasted — actually empty. No food, no calories, no coffee with cream, no protein, no fruit, no gum. Black coffee and water are fine.

Wait at least 30–45 minutes after injection before eating. The peptides need a clean absorption window. Eating spikes insulin, which shifts cells out of the AMPK/repair state these compounds are designed to drive.

Reta is also AM fasted in Phase 2 — same rules. Stack them in the same morning cluster.

Tip 03 — AM vs PM Timing
Why Cartalax AM and BPC-157 PM

Cartalax in the AM — keeps joint and cartilage bioregulator signaling active through the workday, when the back and knees are getting the most load. Stacks in the same morning cluster as SS-31 (or MOTS-C in Phase 2).

BPC-157 in the PM — the gut and soft-tissue repair work BPC drives is most effective overnight when the body is in its repair cycle anyway. A pre-bed (or post-dinner) shot lets BPC ride along with the body's natural overnight repair window.

Tip 04 — Phase 1 Structure
Why 20 Days Straight, Not Continuous

Phase 1's three repair compounds (SS-31, BPC-157, Cartalax) run M–F straight for 20 weekdays — matching the supply exactly. Sat / Sun off both for natural rest and for site-rotation recovery.

Bioregulator peptides like Cartalax (and BPC-157 used this way) work by signaling cells to express certain regulatory genes. A focused 20-day course gives the receptors a strong, consistent signal without dragging into the open-ended dosing window where continuous use can blunt the response. Get the signal in, let the body integrate, then move to Phase 2.

Tip 05 — Signs Phase 1 Is Working
What to Watch For Across Weeks 1–4

Week 1: not much subjectively yet. Background repair work that doesn't always feel like anything. Maybe slightly less generalized inflammation by end of week.

Week 2: mornings start to feel different. Joint stiffness on the first steps out of bed less sharp. Energy a touch steadier. Sleep deeper.

Weeks 3–4: compounding effect. Back and knees feel measurably better. Recovery from work shifts noticeably faster. By end of Week 4, Joe should feel meaningfully better than baseline — less daily pain, more morning energy, better recovery. That's the win that makes Phase 2 sustainable.

If nothing has shifted by end of Week 3: reassess. Could be sleep, diet, hydration, or something else gating the response. Text Micah, talk it through.

Tip 06 — Reta GI Side Effects
Expect Some First 1–2 Weeks of Phase 2 — Should Be Lighter This Time

Mild nausea, occasional reflux, early satiety, possibly constipation in the first 1–2 weeks of Reta re-intro is normal. This should be less severe than Joe's first attempt — healed gut lining (BPC-157), reduced systemic inflammation, and the half-dose titration all work in his favor.

Hydration is the single biggest lever. 3–4 L water/day on Reta, with electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day. Reta suppresses thirst signaling — you'll feel less thirsty even when dehydrated.

Red flags — call a clinician: persistent vomiting, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to the back — pancreatitis risk), gallbladder symptoms.

Tip 07 — TRT Stays Steady
No Changes Needed to the Hormonal Foundation

Joe's TRT (~150–200 mg/wk, self-managed with his physician) is the hormonal floor under everything else. Don't change the dose or timing just because the peptides are running — they layer on top.

If there's any question about T levels mid-cycle, that's a conversation for Joe's physician, not something to adjust in response to peptide effects. The peptides aren't fighting TRT — they're working with it.

Tip 08 — Signs Phase 2 Is Working
What to Watch For Weeks 5–12

Weeks 5–6 (Reta titration): appetite drops noticeably (faster this time given Reta history). Energy holds — this is the key signal that Phase 1 did its job. If energy holds at 0.5 mg, Joe's foundation is ready.

Weeks 7–9: Reta at 1 mg drives the fat loss curve. Expect 1–2 lb/week with steady energy. MOTS-C running underneath keeps the metabolic output up while Reta drops intake.

Weeks 10–12: cumulative effect. Lighter on the scale, leaner in the mirror, joints and energy still tracking with the Phase 1 gains. By end of Week 12, Joe should be in a different place than he started — not just lighter, but functionally better.

Yellow flags — reassess: rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week (deficit too aggressive; eat more), energy dropping (drop Reta dose or pause), strength dropping noticeably in physical work (lean mass loss; protein floor 0.7–0.9 g/lb of target body weight).
