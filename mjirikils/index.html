Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately. Use within 30 days. Fresh insulin syringe for each draw — never reuse.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. During Phase 1 M–F use different quadrants for Cartalax AM and BPC PM (e.g., Cartalax AM upper-right, BPC PM upper-left or near-knee site-targeted). BPC site-targeting for the knee: see the Notes tab Tip 02 for full guidance — SubQ tissue ~2″ above the patella, NOT into the joint or muscle, starting Week 3 after the surgical incision is fully closed.

Tip 01 — First-Time Injector? Read This First
SubQ Injection 101 · You've Got This

You flagged never having injected before. That's completely normal — SubQ (subcutaneous) injections with insulin syringes are the easiest type of injection there is. 29–31G needles are thin — you'll barely feel them. Here's the routine:

  • Wash hands. Pull out a fresh syringe + alcohol pad.
  • Wipe the vial top with alcohol. Let it dry 5 sec.
  • Draw your dose to 10 units (the mark on the syringe).
  • Pick a spot on your abdomen, ~2″ from belly button. Wipe with alcohol.
  • Pinch a small fold of skin. Insert needle at 45–90° (perpendicular is fine for SubQ).
  • Push the plunger slowly (~5 sec). Pull needle out. Light pressure with the pad if any bleeding.
  • Drop syringe in sharps container. Done. ~60 seconds total.

Fear-management tips: (1) ice the spot 30 sec before the shot — numbs the surface, makes the needle invisible. (2) The first shot is the hardest. By shot #3 you'll wonder why you were worried. (3) The needle is shorter than most eyelashes — truly tiny.

Tip 02 — Site Targeting for the Knee (BPC)
Closer to the Surgical Site = Stronger Local Signal

BPC's local effect when injected near tissue is well-documented in the recovery literature. For your total knee replacement, you have two options for the PM BPC shot:

  • Option A — Standard abdomen: default SubQ shot, systemic effect everywhere including the knee. Easiest, no learning curve.
  • Option B — Site-targeted SubQ: inject SubQ into the soft tissue around the knee — about 2″ above the patella, into the loose subQ tissue (NOT into the joint, NOT into muscle). Strong local effect on top of systemic. Use this 3–4×/week, alternating with abdomen.

Important: the surgical incision site should be fully closed and not actively healing before site-targeting near the knee. If you're not sure, start with abdomen-only for the first 2 weeks (Wks 1–2 of the protocol), then add site-targeting starting Week 3. If anything looks/feels off about the surgical site, default back to abdomen and message BioLinked.

Tip 04 — Knee Recovery + Physical Therapy Synergy
Peptides Layer ON Top of Your PT, Not Around It

Total knee replacement recovery has two halves: mechanical (range of motion, strength, gait — that's your PT) and tissue (healing, inflammation control, cartilage remodeling — that's where BPC + Cartalax come in). They reinforce each other:

  • Better tissue healing = PT exercises hurt less, you can push further faster.
  • More PT range-of-motion work = the tissue being healed is being moved + remodeled into functional positions.
  • BPC site-targeting works with PT load: use BPC after PT sessions (when tissue is freshly loaded) for an enhanced healing signal.

Light exercise (1–2×/week) is fine with this protocol — just listen to your knee and your PT. Don't push through actual pain. Soreness 24–48 hrs after PT/exercise is normal; sharp pain in the surgical site is not.

Tip 05 — AM + PM Routine Logistics (Phase 1 M–F)
2 Shots a Day · ~2 Minutes Each · Sat/Sun Off

Phase 1 has two short injection windows on Mon–Fri only: AM (Cartalax) and PM (BPC). Total ~2–3 min/day. Weekends fully off.

AM (any time after waking):

  • Cartalax 10u abdomen quadrant A. Doesn't need fasted.
  • Take it with your morning routine (coffee, breakfast, whatever).

PM (any time in the evening; ideally a couple hrs before bed):

  • BPC 10u in standard abdomen quadrant B (different from AM), OR site-targeted SubQ near the knee (see Tip 02).
  • No food requirement.

Sat + Sun: no shots. Your knee continues healing on its own; the M–F signal carries through. Phase 2 (Wks 9–16): single GLOW shot M–F AM. Even simpler.

Tip 06 — Medication / Allergy Considerations
Sulfa Allergy + Lexapro + Female Hormones · All Compatible

Quick rundown on the safety side based on your intake:

  • Sulfa allergy: BPC-157, Cartalax, and GLOW Blend contain no sulfa compounds. Bacteriostatic water uses benzyl alcohol as the preservative (not a sulfa drug) and is also fine. You're clear.
  • Lexapro (escitalopram): no known interactions with the peptides in this stack. SSRIs work on serotonin reuptake; these peptides work on tissue-healing and longevity-signaling pathways — completely different mechanisms.
  • Female hormones: no conflicts with this stack. If anything, GLOW's anti-aging signal (skin, stamina) tends to complement hormone-replacement effects.
  • Alcohol (1–2 drinks/wk): doesn't interfere with the peptides. BPC actually has some protective effects on gut lining if anything.

If anything unusual comes up — especially in the first 1–2 weeks — flag it. See "Watch For" below.

Anything Below Warrants a Quick Check-In
