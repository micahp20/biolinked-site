Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry vials (before reconstitution): refrigerate at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Stable for many months sealed.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Reconstitute one vial at a time — don't pre-mix all 17 vials on Day 1. Mix Cycle 1 vials on Day 1, mix Cycle 2 vials when you start that course, mix PRN compounds (PT-141, Oxytocin, DSIP) the day before you plan to use them so they're ready.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. With 3–4 AM shots on Mon, use different quadrants for each.

If You Want to Build More Muscle
The Muscle-Gain Version of This Plan
The physiology up front. Building muscle after menopause is real — but the equation shifts. Estrogen used to do a lot of the anabolic heavy lifting; without it, you have to be more deliberate with protein + resistance training + recovery. The CJC/Ipa pre-bed shot in your protocol is already doing part of this job (restoring the overnight GH pulse that normally protects and builds lean tissue). You cannot meaningfully build muscle in a deficit past age 55, so muscle gain requires shifting to at least a small surplus during the training-focused window.
Recommendation 01 — The Non-Negotiable
Resistance Train 3–4×/Week (Heavy)

Without progressive resistance training, no amount of protein or peptides will add muscle. The signal to grow is mechanical tension — the meal plan and CJC/Ipa provide the raw materials, but the training triggers the assembly.

The prescription:

· 3–4 sessions per week, 45–60 min each
· Focus on compound lifts: squats, deadlifts (even light), rows, presses, hip thrusts, lunges, pulldowns
· 4–8 reps per set at a weight that's genuinely hard by rep 6–8 — not the "20 reps of pink dumbbells" pattern that's the default for women in this age bracket
· Add 5 lbs to the bar (or one dumbbell size up) every 2–3 weeks when the previous weight starts feeling easy — progressive overload is what builds muscle at 59
· Rest 90–120 sec between sets on the heavy lifts

If you don't already have a program: Stronglifts 5×5, Starting Strength, or an appropriately-scaled powerlifting-style routine. Any competent trainer at your gym can walk you through form on the compounds.

Recommendation 02 — The Calorie Shift
Add ~300–500 Cal on Training Days

You currently sit at ~1,240–1,380 cal — that's a fat-loss window. To build muscle you need to be at maintenance or slightly above on training days. The realistic target is ~1,600–1,800 cal on training days, keeping your current fat-loss window on rest days. This is called "calorie cycling" and it lets you keep the fat-loss trajectory while creating just enough surplus around workouts to actually build tissue.

The easiest additions (in priority order):

1. Post-workout: 1/2 to 1 cup white rice or ~4 oz sweet potato alongside your existing chicken lunch (Meal 3). Adds ~150–300 cal + fast carbs to shuttle protein into the muscle you just worked.
2. Bump the dinner potato from 3 oz to 5–6 oz on training days — adds ~75 cal, better recovery overnight.
3. Add a second whole egg to breakfast on training days — adds ~70 cal + more of the leucine/choline signal.
4. Optional: bump the peanut butter at Meal 4 from 1 tbsp to 2 tbsp on training days — ~95 more cal, easy fats.

Total added: ~400–500 cal on training days. On rest days, keep the plan as-is — that's your fat-loss window.

Recommendation 03 — The Supplement
Add Creatine Monohydrate · 5 g/day

Creatine is the single highest-ROI supplement for postmenopausal muscle gain. It's not a peptide, not a hormone, and it's been studied for 40+ years. The research on women past menopause specifically shows it materially improves strength gains, muscle mass retention, and cognitive function.

The protocol:

· 5 g creatine monohydrate, mixed into any liquid, once daily
· Timing doesn't matter (skip the "load with 20g/day for 5 days" myth — 5 g/day builds saturation over ~3 weeks with no bloating)
· Take it every day, including rest days — the effect is cumulative
· Cost: ~$15 for a 6-month supply. Any brand labeled "creatine monohydrate" (not any of the fancier variants like HCL or ethyl ester — monohydrate is what all the research is on)

What to expect: ~2–4 lbs on the scale in the first 2–3 weeks (that's intramuscular water — a good sign, not fat gain). Strength gains show up around week 3–4 — you'll get 1–2 more reps at the same weight, or move up a weight bracket sooner than you would have.

Optimum Nutrition Creatine Monohydrate on Amazon is the gold standard — any 5 lb tub lasts most people 6–9 months.

Recommendation 04 — Protein Distribution
Every Meal ≥ 30 g Protein

Your total daily protein is already excellent (~180–210 g). But at 59, the distribution matters more than the total. Postmenopausal women hit "anabolic resistance" — the muscle-building response per gram of protein is blunted compared to a 25-year-old. The fix is to hit a bigger threshold per meal so each meal actually triggers muscle protein synthesis.

Target: 30–40 g protein at every meal. Your plan already does this at Meals 2, 3, 4, and 5. Meal 1 (breakfast) is at 24 g — the muscle-gain version is 2 whole eggs + 5 egg whites (bumps it to 30 g) or adding a scoop of collagen/whey into your morning coffee (add 15 g protein, 0 g fat, ~60 cal).

Recommendation 05 — Sleep + Recovery
7–8 Hours of Sleep, Non-Negotiable

Muscle isn't built in the gym — it's built during sleep. The overnight GH pulse (which CJC/Ipa amplifies) fires in deep sleep, and that's when actual tissue synthesis happens. If sleep is broken, the entire assembly line stops.

The rule: 7–8 hours per night, lights out at a consistent time, dark and cool room.

Interaction with your protocol:

· CJC/Ipa pre-bed → wait 30–60 min → sleep. This IS the muscle-building window.
· The dinner potato is deliberately timed for sleep support (carbs help serotonin conversion).
· If sleep quality is a problem, message Micah — Cycle 4 has DSIP in it, which is specifically for deep-sleep restoration and would slot cleanly into this stack.

What to avoid: alcohol within 3 hrs of bed (destroys sleep architecture even at 1 drink), scrolling on your phone in bed (blue light suppresses melatonin), late caffeine (past 2 PM if you're sensitive).

Recommendation 06 — Track the Right Thing
Strength > Scale for Muscle-Gain Phase

In a muscle-gain phase the scale is the wrong metric. Muscle gained = fat lost + some scale movement (or none), so the scale is a bad signal.

Track instead:

· The bar — are the weights on your compound lifts going up every 2–3 weeks? If yes, muscle is being built.
· The waist — measure at the belly button once a week. A stable-or-slightly-shrinking waist alongside rising weights = recomp is working.
· The mirror — take a monthly photo in the same lighting, same time of day. Muscle changes show up in the mirror before they show up on the scale.
· Strength gains at 59 are the leading indicator of muscle gains — you can't get 20% stronger on the same bodyweight without adding tissue. If the strength curve is going up, the muscle is being built even if the scale doesn't move.

Expect the scale to sit flat or go up 3–5 lbs while the waist shrinks — that's the recomp you want.

Tip 05 — Hydration on Reta
3–4 L/Day · Not Optional

Reta suppresses thirst alongside appetite. You will not feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people get headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor gym performance and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit.

Target: 3–4 L/day. Big water bottle, top it off all day. Coffee doesn't count (mild diuretic).

Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day. LMNT packet, pinch of salt + lemon in water, or Liquid IV. Especially on training days. The cramping people blame on "the peptide" is almost always low sodium / magnesium.

Tip 06 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Reta (Monday once-weekly): miss Monday, take it within 24–48 hours (Tuesday or Wednesday). More than 48 hours: push the rest of the week's anchor day to whichever day you took it, then pick that day the following week. Don't double up.

5-Amino / KLOW (M–F AM): miss one morning, take it later that day if you can. If the whole day passes, skip and resume next day.

CJC/Ipa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it. Don't shift to AM the next day — the pre-sleep window is what makes it work. If you ate too close to bed, skip rather than dose — a fed CJC shot is wasted.

Kisspeptin (Mon/Thu): miss one, take it within 24 hours; otherwise skip to next scheduled day.

Selank (M/W/F): miss one, take it the next AM (or PRN whenever the stress edge shows up). Fast-acting effect within an hour so missing 1–2 doses doesn't disrupt the baseline.
