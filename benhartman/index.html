KLOW + CJC/Ipa Recovery + GH Pulse
Daytime systemic healing. Overnight GH pulse for sleep depth and lean recovery.

Two compounds, two windows, one 8-week cycle. KLOW AM fasted is the systemic healing layer — the four-peptide blend (GHK-Cu, BPC-157, TB-500, KPV) covering connective tissue repair, gut lining, immune modulation, and skin/collagen all in one shot. CJC-1295/Ipamorelin PM pre-bed is the GH-pulse layer — amplifies your natural overnight growth-hormone release, deepens slow-wave sleep, and drives the IGF-1 signal that protects lean mass and accelerates recovery from training or daily wear.

Why these two together: KLOW does its work during the active day — tissue-level signaling that needs metabolic activity to compound. CJC/Ipa fires during the deepest sleep stages when growth hormone is naturally pulsing — the body's built-in repair window. Putting healing signal in both windows compounds the recovery effect: cellular repair from KLOW during the day, GH-driven systemic recovery overnight. The two don't compete for receptor bandwidth or timing — AM healing peptide, PM GH-axis peptide.

8-week runway, M–F cadence, weekends off. Two small SubQ shots a day on insulin syringes — AM after waking (fasted), PM 1.5–2 hours after last food and within an hour of sleep. BAC water included as a bundle bonus ($20 value — 2 free bottles).

Tip 01 — Why This Combo Works
KLOW (AM) + CJC/Ipa (PM) — Day Repair + Night GH Pulse

The body runs two distinct repair pathways at two different times of day. During the active day, repair is driven by cellular-level signaling at sites of tissue activity — collagen synthesis, anti-inflammatory work, gut-barrier maintenance. During deep sleep, repair is driven by the growth-hormone pulse and downstream IGF-1 signaling — systemic recovery, lean-tissue protection, soft-tissue rebuild. This stack puts a targeted peptide signal in each window.

KLOW AM — the day signal. Four peptides in one shot covering four complementary mechanisms:

· GHK-Cu: drives copper-dependent collagen + elastin synthesis. Visible effects on skin quality and connective tissue over 6–8 weeks.
· BPC-157: angiogenesis (new blood vessel growth at damaged tissue) + gut lining + soft tissue repair.
· TB-500: cellular migration to injury sites + tissue regeneration. Especially relevant for any nagging aches or slow-healing minor injuries.
· KPV: the anti-inflammatory + immune leg. Downregulates NF-κB signaling, quiets the low-grade systemic inflammation that taxes everything else.

CJC/Ipa PM — the night signal. CJC-1295 (GHRH analog) signals the pituitary to release growth hormone; Ipamorelin (ghrelin-receptor agonist) amplifies the same pulse without spiking cortisol or prolactin. When the peptide pair fires during deep sleep on a fasted PM stomach, the GH pulse runs cleaner and longer than the body's own would. Downstream IGF-1 rises across the cycle. The result: deeper sleep, more lean tissue protected through the cycle, and faster recovery from training, daily wear, and the cumulative inflammation load.

The combined effect: at 8 weeks you've put a healing-and-recovery signal into the two windows the body uses to do repair work — AM cellular signaling and PM GH pulse. Visible skin and connective-tissue improvement (GHK), faster healing of small things you didn't realize you were carrying (BPC + TB), lower baseline inflammation (KPV), deeper sleep + better recovery + a noticeable energy lift (CJC/Ipa). Each compound addresses a distinct lever; none are redundant.

Tip 02 — Week-by-Week Timeline
What to Expect from the 8-Week Cycle

Weeks 1–2: learning curve on the injection routine — AM KLOW + PM CJC/Ipa. Sleep often deepens noticeably within the first 4–7 nights from CJC/Ipa (this is the most consistent early signal of the cycle). KLOW effects are subtle the first 2 weeks — the tissue-level work needs time to compound. Expect vivid dreams for the first 1–2 weeks of CJC/Ipa — that's the deeper sleep showing up. Mild morning hand puffiness can also appear briefly as the GH effect ramps; it settles by week 2–3.

Weeks 3–5: the KLOW signal compounds. Skin quality starts visibly improving (GHK-Cu signature), nagging aches or slow-healing minor stuff resolves faster, recovery between training sessions or active days gets noticeably better. CJC/Ipa is delivering nightly GH pulses by now — the lean-tissue / recovery effect is building. Energy holds steadier across the day because the baseline inflammation load is lower.

Weeks 6–8: the back-half landing. Visible skin/hair/connective-tissue improvement from KLOW is clear. CJC/Ipa has driven enough IGF-1 cumulative exposure to deliver real recovery benefits — faster bounce-back from training, deeper sleep architecture, lean tissue protected (or built, depending on training input). The full benefit lands across these weeks.

End of week 8: decision point. Both compounds run empty at the same time. Options: rotate to a different stack (e.g. Reta or 5-Amino-1MQ if body comp becomes the next focus), repeat the same KLOW + CJC/Ipa cycle for another 8 weeks, or take a 4-week break before the next cycle. We'll reassess based on what you noticed and what the next 8 weeks should target.

Tip 03 — AM Fasted + PM Fasted: The Timing That Actually Matters
When to Inject and Why It Matters

KLOW AM fasted: ideally within the first 30–60 min after waking, on an empty stomach. Water and black coffee are fine. Wait 30–45 min before eating. The fasted part is preference, not strict requirement — KLOW components don't need a low-insulin window the way CJC/Ipa does. If your morning is busy and you can't strictly fast, still take it AM with breakfast and you'll get 90%+ of the benefit. AM is the higher-priority cue than fasted.

CJC/Ipa PM fasted — the non-negotiable. Last food 1.5–2 hours before the shot. This one is strict. Food (especially carbs and protein) spikes insulin, and insulin shuts down the pituitary's GH release. The peptide will fire the signal, but the pituitary will reject it. A fed CJC/Ipa shot is essentially wasted — better to skip that night entirely than dose it on a full stomach.

The exact pre-bed routine: dinner → wait 1.5–2 hrs → brush teeth → inject CJC/Ipa → lights out within ~30 min. The GH pulse needs the deep-sleep window to amplify into the IGF-1 cascade you're actually after. Injecting and then staying up 2 more hours scrolling = the pulse fires into a still-awake brain instead of into the slow-wave-sleep window. Time it tight with bedtime.

Tip 04 — Two Different Syringe Draws — Don't Auto-Pilot
15u for KLOW, 10u for CJC/Ipa

Same insulin syringe, two different pull marks. Worth flagging because muscle memory wants to default to one number for everything.

· KLOW AM: pull to 15 units (0.15 mL). Delivers 4 mg of total blend per shot (2.5 mg GHK + 0.5 each of BPC / TB / KPV).
· CJC/Ipa PM: pull to 10 units (0.10 mL). Delivers 500 mcg of CJC+Ipa blend (250 mcg of each).

Glance at the mark before every shot. Pulling to 10u on KLOW under-doses you ~33% across all four peptides; pulling to 15u on CJC/Ipa over-doses you 50% and burns through the supply early. Both are easy mistakes for the first 1–2 weeks. After 2 weeks the routine locks in.

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

KLOW (M–F AM): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across a week doesn't move the needle — the cumulative signal is what drives the effect, and a couple skipped doses don't break it.

CJC/Ipa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it — do not try to take it AM the next day (the pulse only works in the pre-sleep window). Resume the next pre-bed window. If you ate too close to bed and the shot would be on a fed stomach, skip rather than dose — a fed CJC/Ipa shot is wasted.

Tip 06 — Red Flags · When to Pause
Signals to Watch For

Hold KLOW or CJC/Ipa if any of the following:

· Significant injection-site reaction (redness, swelling, persistent pain past 24 hours)
· New or worsening allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes)
· CJC/Ipa: persistent morning hand/face puffiness past week 3, joint pain that's new, or sustained tingling/numbness in hands — reduce dose to 250 mcg (5u) or pause and flag
· Sleep got worse after starting CJC/Ipa instead of deeper — should be deepening; if reversed, flag for review
· Headaches or unusual fatigue past week 2 — flag

Any of these, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own — we adjust the plan based on what's actually happening in your body.
