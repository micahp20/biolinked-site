Sonya's 4-Cycle Reset
Heal first · then burn · then desire · then build · 157 → 120

You're not doing one stack — you're doing four cycles in sequence. That's the right call. Trying to throw a dozen compounds at the problem at once would feel overwhelming and wouldn't deliver what you actually want. The order is deliberate: heal and start the recomp first, then go deeper on visceral fat loss, then bring libido back, then build the muscle that makes the new 120 lb body actually feel strong.

Cycle 1 — heal and recomp in parallel. The old approach to "heal first then fat loss" is too slow for the timeline you want. Cycle 1 stacks the healing layer (KLOW + BPC-157) AND the body-comp engine (Reta + MOTS) together — gut and tissue repair runs in the background while Reta drives appetite control and MOTS keeps daytime energy up through the deficit. KLOW (GHK + BPC + TB + KPV) AM fasted handles broad systemic healing; standalone BPC-157 PM concentrates the gut-repair signal during the overnight window. Reta tiers up or down based on how you're feeling. 8 weeks.

Cycle 2 — visceral fat targeting. Once Cycle 1 has dropped the first 10–15 lbs and the gut is calm, Cycle 2 shifts to Tesamorelin — the only peptide FDA-approved for visceral fat reduction. It drives the body's overnight GH pulse and goes specifically at the deep belly fat that makes the midsection feel "heavy" even after scale loss. Cagrilintide layers in evening appetite control (different receptor than Reta — no GI overhead). Slower scale movement than Cycle 1 but the body comp at the end is materially different.

Cycles 3 and 4 come after the fat-loss phases. Cycle 3 (Kisspeptin + Oxytocin) brings sexual health back online once weight is moving and you feel like yourself again. Cycle 4 (CJC/Ipa + 5-Amino-1MQ) builds the muscle on the lighter frame — this is where 120 lb becomes tight, not skinny-fat.

Selank and DSIP are as-needed across all four cycles. Selank is your "stress dial" for the high-cortisol days. DSIP is for the nights when sleep won't come on its own — not nightly, just when you need it. Both safe, neither habit-forming.

⚠ Critical Allergy Notes · Read Before Day 1
  • ChloraPrep allergy → NEVER use chlorhexidine-based skin prep. The supplies list calls for alcohol prep pads (70% IPA) only. Confirm any wipe says "isopropyl alcohol" or "IPA" — never "chlorhexidine gluconate" or "CHG."
  • Bactrim + most antibiotic allergies → if an injection site goes hot, red, or swollen for >24 hrs, do not reach for antibiotic ointment. Call your doctor or message us first — the right answer is usually rest, cool compress, and rotation, not a topical you might react to.
  • Magnesium sensitivity → the sleep tip in this protocol suggests magnesium glycinate only — not citrate, oxide, or sulfate. Glycinate is the gentlest form on the gut.
  • No artificial sweeteners, no carbonation → the electrolyte recommendation in Supplies is plain salt + lemon, not powder packets with stevia/sucralose/erythritol.
🍹️ The Alcohol Conversation · This Is the Single Biggest Decision in the Protocol

You self-reported daily / heavy alcohol use. That changes the whole protocol from "smooth recomp" into "a real safety conversation we need to have before Day 1."

  • Retatrutide + chronic alcohol carries documented pancreatitis risk. Every FDA-approved GLP-1 (Ozempic, Mounjaro, Zepbound) carries this warning in their prescribing labels. Reta inherits the same class warning. Daily drinking puts you in the population those warnings are written for.
  • The work won't even land at full effect. Every drink disrupts REM/deep sleep (fights DSIP), spikes next-morning cortisol (fights MOTS-c + Reta), inflames the gut (fights KLOW + BPC-157), and suppresses overnight GH (fights Tesamorelin + CJC/Ipa downstream). You'd be paying for a protocol fighting itself.
  • The realistic ask: commit to substantially reducing alcohol — ideally to 0–1 drinks/day with 3+ dry days/week — for the full duration of Cycles 1 and 2 (roughly the first 16–18 weeks). After that, you'll likely want less anyway.
  • If you can't commit to that: we should pull Retatrutide and Cagrilintide from the protocol before sending. We can rebuild Cycle 1 around MOTS + KLOW + BPC alone, which carries no pancreatitis risk. The fat loss is slower but the safety profile is right for where you actually are.
  • The hidden gift: Selank (your PRN stress tool) delivers the same calm/relief feeling a drink does, without the next-day cost. Many clients in your spot find Selank quietly takes over the role drinks were playing inside 2 weeks — and the daily drink slides naturally down on its own.

Bottom line: this isn't moralizing about drinking. It's a real medical risk that needs an answer before we ship the order. Reply with where you land on alcohol and we'll finalize from there.

The 4-Cycle Logic — Why This Order
The damage profile: 51, female, 157 → 120 goal, sleep 2/5, stress 5/5, daily alcohol, light exercise, never injected before, on levothyroxine. That's not a "one protocol fits all" client — it's a sequencing problem.

Why Heal + Recomp first (parallel, not sequential): The old approach was "fully heal first, then fat loss" — that's too slow for your timeline. Cycle 1 stacks the healing layer (KLOW + standalone BPC-157) AND the body-comp engine (Reta + MOTS) so both layers run together. KLOW (GHK + BPC + TB + KPV) AM fasted handles broad systemic healing — gut, skin, connective tissue. BPC-157 PM concentrates the gut-repair signal overnight. Reta (M/W/F, tier up/down) drives appetite control. MOTS keeps daytime energy steady through the deficit. The first ~10–15 lbs come off here while the gut and skin are getting fixed in parallel — the inflammation drops faster when fat starts moving, the fat moves faster when sleep and gut are catching up.

Why Visceral Fat Loss next (Cycle 2): After Cycle 1 has done the first wave, Cycle 2 shifts to Tesamorelin — the only peptide FDA-approved for visceral fat reduction. Different mechanism than Reta: Tesa works on GH/IGF-1 to specifically mobilize deep belly fat (the layer that makes 51-year-old women feel "heavy" even after scale loss). Cagri keeps the appetite layer covered without GI overhead. Slower scale movement than Cycle 1 but the body comp at the end is materially different — the midsection feels tighter, not just smaller. 157 → 120 across Cycles 1+2 in roughly 5–7 months at 1–1.5 lb/week is the realistic curve.

Why Sexual Health third: Libido follows body composition + sleep + stress. Drop weight + fix sleep first, and Cycle 3 has a foundation to amplify. Kisspeptin restarts the upstream signal, oxytocin restores the emotional connection layer. At 51 with intermittent estrogen, this is a meaningful shift — not "trying to feel 25" but actually feeling present and wanted again in your body. PT-141 was pulled from this protocol because its prescribing label cautions against use in patients with cardiovascular risk markers (atorvastatin) plus age + intermittent estrogen — the right call for you was to leave it out.

Why Muscle last: Building muscle on the lighter frame is what turns 120 from "skinny" into "tight." CJC/Ipa + 5-Amino-1MQ = the final polish. Your body composition at 120 with this added muscle layer is materially different from 120 without it — better metabolic floor, better posture, better next-decade trajectory.

The Selank + DSIP layer: these aren't part of the sequencing — they're tools you use as needed across all 4 cycles. Bad sleep night? DSIP. Stress day? Selank. They make the rest of the protocol resilient to the bad days everyone has.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Clean · Rotate Sites
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days of reconstitution. Only reconstitute one vial of each compound at a time — don't start the 30-day clock on a second vial until the first is nearly empty.

Site rotation (SubQ abdomen): divide your abdomen into 8 quadrants — upper/lower × left/right × near/far from belly button. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. With 2–3 shots a day, that's easy: just step to the next quadrant.

Technique notes (you've never injected): 90° angle to skin, pinch 1″ of skin, slow steady push, hold for 5 seconds before withdrawing. Whole thing takes 10 seconds once you have the rhythm.

Tip 02 · First Injection Day
You've Never Done This Before — Here's the 5-Minute Walkthrough

The fear is bigger than the reality. Insulin syringes are tiny — thinner than a hair. Most clients report shot #1 stung less than expected, and by shot #5 it's a non-event.

Your first shot setup:

  • Pick the time you'll do it daily and stick with it (consistency > perfection)
  • Wash hands. Alcohol-wipe the vial top, let it dry 5 seconds
  • Draw your BAC water (only for the first recon) or pull your dose
  • Alcohol-wipe a fresh abdomen spot (1″ from belly button or further), let it dry
  • Pinch 1″ of skin between thumb and finger
  • Insert at 90° (straight in), push slow, hold 5 sec, withdraw
  • Used needle into sharps container. Done.

Start with Cycle 1. KLOW (15u, AM fasted) is your first injection — it's a single shot covering four peptides, no precise timing pressure beyond "before breakfast." That's your warm-up day. By Day 2 you'll add MOTS (10u), then Reta on M/W/F (10u), then BPC at night (20u). Builds incrementally.

⚠ Tip 03 · The Alcohol Conversation Is the Whole Conversation
Real Pancreatitis Risk · Read Twice

Read the alcohol banner at the top of the Protocol tab first. This tip is the longer version of that conversation.

The medical part: Retatrutide inherits the entire GLP-1 class safety warning around pancreatitis. Daily/heavy alcohol use is in the same warning. Stacking the two is the single highest real-medical-risk decision in this whole protocol — bigger than first-time injection technique, bigger than the allergy profile. Not because anything will go wrong, but because the probability moves from near-zero to non-trivial, and we have to treat that seriously.

The effect-on-results part: even if pancreatitis never happens, every drink:

  • Disrupts REM and deep sleep — actively fights DSIP and the recovery you need on Reta + MOTS
  • Spikes next-morning cortisol — fights MOTS-c daytime energy and Reta's fat-loss work
  • Inflames the gut lining — fights KLOW and BPC-157's whole job
  • Suppresses GH overnight — fights CJC/Ipa in Cycle 4
  • Pushes daily appetite higher 24 hrs later — cancels out half of Reta's signal

The three honest paths from here:

  • Path A (best): commit to substantially less alcohol — 0–1 drinks/day with 3+ dry days/week through Cycles 1 and 2 (8–9 weeks). The fat loss curve is 2–3× steeper, the sleep effect is dramatic, and Selank quietly takes over the role drinks were playing inside 2 weeks.
  • Path B (acceptable): keep Retatrutide and Cagrilintide but cut alcohol to maximum 1–2 drinks per occasion, with at least 2 dry days/week. Still works, slower curve, side-effect risk dramatically lower than continuing daily heavy use.
  • Path C (the safe alternative if A/B aren't realistic): we pull Retatrutide and Cagrilintide from the order before sending. Cycle 2 runs MOTS-c only — energy + insulin sensitivity, zero pancreatitis risk, slower fat loss but matched to where you actually are. Protocol cost drops by $160; safety profile becomes appropriate for daily drinkers.

Tip 04 · Levothyroxine Timing
Don't Let Peptides Disrupt Your Thyroid Rhythm

You're on levothyroxine — that means your AM is already structured: take levo first thing, fasted, wait 30–60 min before food, coffee, or anything else.

MOTS-c (Cycle 2) and 5-Amino-1MQ (Cycle 4) are "AM fasted" peptides — they fit cleanly inside the same fasted window. The simple stack: take levo → wait 5 min → do your fasted SubQ shot(s) → continue your normal fasting wait → eat. No interference, same window.

What to watch for: if your TSH drifts up (re-test at end of Cycle 2), Reta's metabolic effect can sometimes shift thyroid demand. Your prescriber may want to adjust the levo dose. Ask for a TSH + Free T4 + Free T3 check around weeks 8–10 of the program.

Tip 06 · The Reta Side-Effect Playbook
Manage Nausea Without Quitting

Most clients get mild–moderate nausea on Reta shot days for the first 2–3 weeks. Tactics that actually work:

  • Smaller meals on shot day. The "I'm full" signal hits halfway through a normal portion — respect it.
  • Lower fat content on shot day. Fat slows gastric emptying, which is already slowed by Reta. Don't compound.
  • Hydrate aggressively. 80–100 oz water on shot day. Counterintuitively prevents nausea more than it provokes it.
  • Ginger tea or chewed fresh ginger. Cheap, no-side-effect, works for ~70% of clients.

If nausea is >mild after week 2, message us — the answer may be holding at 1 mg longer, or splitting the dose into 0.5 mg twice a week.

Tip 07 · When to Skip a Shot
Three Scenarios Where the Right Move is "Skip"
  • Active illness with fever: skip all peptides until 24 hrs fever-free. Don't add immune work on top of the immune work already happening.
  • Ate too close to a pre-bed shot: if it's been less than 2 hrs, skip the CJC/Ipa and resume next night.
  • Site is irritated: skip rather than inject into an angry site — rotate, rest the area 48 hrs, resume.

Missing one shot in any cycle is not a setback. Missing 3+ days of Reta in a row in Cycle 1 lets appetite snap back to baseline — not the end of the world, just resume next M/W/F and let it ramp back up. KLOW + BPC + MOTS are more forgiving on misses; just skip and resume.

⚠ Tip 09 · Why PT-141 Was Pulled
Atorvastatin + Intermittent Estrogen + Age 51 = Don't

The original draft of your protocol included PT-141 for Cycle 3. It was pulled after a safety re-read of your med list.

The reason: the FDA-approved version of PT-141 (Vyleesi, for HSDD in women) explicitly cautions against use in patients with cardiovascular disease or risk markers. Atorvastatin is a CV risk marker — not just a cholesterol med, it's prescribed because there's dyslipidemia that needs treatment. Stack on intermittent estrogen + age 51 and you trigger three of the four caution flags in the actual prescribing information.

PT-141 also transiently spikes blood pressure 5–15 mmHg systolic. That spike is fine for healthy clients; it's the wrong direction for someone already in the cardiovascular-watch zone.

What replaces it: nothing — Cycle 3 stays Kisspeptin (baseline desire, upstream restart) + Oxytocin (emotional/connection). Together they cover the libido goal cleanly. PT-141 would have been the on-demand acute booster, and you're better off without that risk profile.
