Daily Protocol
Micah's Daily Stack
Thyroid · HRT · Longevity · IF-aligned

The protocol is built around 5 time-blocks plus M/W/F HRT. The Wake-fasted block carries the thyroid pair (levo + lio) plus tadalafil and modafinil — all on an empty stomach so absorption isn't competed with. One hour buffer before the morning drink. Eating window opens at 12 PM with metformin + the daily multi/omega/D3K2; closes after the 7 PM second meal. Pre-bed stack handles GABA tone, recovery, and sleep architecture. HRT runs M/W/F (TRT + small-dose HCG).

The key fix in this version: levothyroxine + liothyronine are isolated from the multivitamin and psyllium husk. The previous TSH of 5.48 was almost certainly absorption interference — calcium, iron, and fiber bind T4 in the gut. Move the multi to the 12 PM meal, keep psyllium in the morning drink (post-thyroid hour), and the levo gets a clean window to absorb.

• Thyroid pair has 60 minutes of quiet — no food, no coffee, no other supplements.

⚠ Why TSH was 5.48
Absorption Interference, Not a Dose Problem

TSH at 5.48 mIU/L is clinically elevated — pituitary calling for more T4 because it isn't seeing enough in circulation. The instinct is "raise the levo dose." That would have worked, but it would have masked the real issue.

The actual problem was the order of operations on the morning stack. Levothyroxine was being taken alongside (or close to) the multivitamin and psyllium. Both wreck T4 absorption:

  • Multivitamin → iron + calcium chelate T4 in the gut. Studies show 30–50% reduction in T4 absorption when co-administered.
  • Psyllium → bulk fiber binds the pill, sweeps it out before it can absorb. A few grams of psyllium can drop T4 absorption by 25%+.
  • Coffee within 60 min → documented 30% reduction in T4 absorption. Less of a factor here but worth flagging.

The fix is purely positional: levo + lio first, alone, with water only. Sixty-minute buffer. Multi moves to the 12 PM meal. Psyllium stays in the morning drink (which is now post-thyroid hour). Same doses, properly absorbed → TSH should drop into the 1–2 range on the next draw.

Re-test target: 6–8 weeks after the schedule change. Don't redraw earlier — TSH lags T4 changes by ~6 weeks.

Track Next Panel
What to Order on the Re-Test
Thyroid panel (full)
TSH · Free T4 · Free T3 · Reverse T3 · TPO Ab

Full panel — not just TSH. TSH alone tells you the pituitary's read; the free hormones tell you what tissue actually sees. Reverse T3 rules out stress/illness shunting T4 into the inactive isomer. TPO antibodies screen for Hashimoto's (one-time test if not already done).

HRT panel
Total T · Free T · SHBG · Estradiol · LH · FSH

Trough draw (just before the next injection) gives the truest read on the M/W/F protocol. SHBG drives free T availability — track it. Estradiol (sensitive assay, not standard) — standard E2 reads inaccurate on TRT. LH/FSH track HPTA suppression and confirm HCG is doing its job.

Metabolic + longevity
Fasting Insulin · HbA1c · Lipid Panel · hs-CRP

Metformin's working — confirm it. Fasting insulin <6 µIU/mL + HbA1c <5.4% = optimal. hs-CRP <1 confirms low systemic inflammation. Standard lipid panel on TRT can shift; track every 6 months.
