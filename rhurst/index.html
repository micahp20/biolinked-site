Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry vials (before reconstitution): refrigerate at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. They're stable for many months sealed.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Most peptides are stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Reconstitute one vial at a time — don't pre-mix all 13 vials on Day 1. Open vial #1 of each compound, finish it, then reconstitute vial #2 when needed.

Reconstitution timing for Full Stack Day 1: Reta vial #1, Tesa vial #1, MOTS vial #1, KLOW vial #1, 5-Amino vial, DSIP vial #1, BPC vial #1. Around Wk 4 reconstitute BPC vial #2 (it runs out first). Around Wk 6–8 reconstitute MOTS #2, KLOW #2, DSIP #2, Tesa #2. Around Wk 13 reconstitute Tesa #3, Tesa #4 if running through.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Tip 01 — The Daily Rhythm
AM Block + PM Block, 5 Days a Week

AM (fasted, first thing after waking): MOTS + KLOW + 5-Amino every M–F. Add Reta on M / W / F only. Wait 30–45 min before eating — the fasted state is what makes MOTS + 5-Amino work best (low insulin = best AMPK + NNMT activation).

Saturday + Sunday: nothing. The 5-on / 2-off rhythm is intentional — gives receptors a reset window so the compounds don't lose sensitivity. Don't sneak weekend doses thinking it'll help; it actually backfires.

Time budget: AM block takes 4–5 min once you've done it twice. PM block takes 3–4 min. Total ~10 min/day for the routine.

Tip 03 — Injection Walkthrough (Refresher)
You've Done This Before — Here's the Refresher

The injection is short, thin, and SubQ. Just under the skin in the fatty layer of your abdomen. Insulin syringe, smallest needle in clinical use.

Step-by-step (every shot):

1. Wash hands. Pull vial out of fridge.
2. Wipe rubber stopper of the vial with an alcohol pad. Let it dry.
3. Pull needle cap off. Push needle through rubber stopper, pull plunger to the right unit mark: 10u for Reta / Tesa / 5-Amino / DSIP / BPC, 15u for MOTS / KLOW. Glance at the mark every shot — don't auto-pilot.
4. Wipe a spot on your abdomen (~2″ from navel) with alcohol pad. Let dry.
5. Pinch about an inch of skin between your fingers. Push needle in at 45° (or 90° if more padding). Push plunger slowly until empty.
6. Pull needle straight out. Don't massage. Drop the whole syringe in the sharps container.

What it feels like: brief sting for some, nothing for others. Most peptides at these volumes are essentially painless.

Multiple shots in one block: AM block has 3–4 shots back-to-back. Use different quadrants of your abdomen for each — upper-right, upper-left, lower-right, lower-left. Don't stack two shots in the same 1″ area.

Tip 05 — Hydration + Electrolytes
3–4 L Water/Day · Not Optional on Reta

Reta suppresses thirst alongside appetite. You will not feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people get headaches, constipation, fatigue, and brain fog and blame "the peptide" when it's actually just water deficit.

Target: 3–4 liters/day. Big water bottle, top it off all day. Coffee doesn't count (mild diuretic).

Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day. An LMNT packet, a pinch of salt + lemon in water, or a Liquid IV. On a calorie deficit + GLP-1 your electrolyte balance shifts — the headaches and muscle cramps that get blamed on "the peptide" are almost always low sodium or magnesium.

Constipation prevention: 25–30 g fiber/day from food (greens, berries, chia, or a fiber supplement). Reta slows GI motility — fiber + water keeps things moving.

Tip 06 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Reta (M/W/F): if you miss one, take it within 24 hours and resume the schedule. More than 24 hours: skip and pick up at the next scheduled day. Don't double up.

Tesa (M–F PM): miss one night, skip it. Don't shift to AM the next day — the pre-sleep window is what makes it work. Resume the next pre-bed window. If you ate too close to bed and the shot would be on a fed stomach, skip rather than dose — a fed Tesa shot is wasted.

MOTS / 5-Amino / KLOW (AM): miss one morning, take it later that day if you can (not strictly time-locked, but earlier is better). If the whole day passes, skip and resume the next day.

DSIP / BPC (PM): miss one night, skip. Don't double up.

Missing 2–3 days in a row: not a problem. Pick up the schedule on the next planned day. Don't try to "make up" missed doses — the body recalibrates within days.
