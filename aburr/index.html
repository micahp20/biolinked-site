Protocol Overview — How This Works
Retatrutide — The Single Compound
Reta hits three receptors at once — GLP-1 (appetite + satiety), GIP (insulin sensitivity), and glucagon (energy expenditure). Tirz hits the first two; Reta adds glucagon, which is why it's been showing meaningfully larger weight loss in trials. The added glucagon agonism is also part of why Reta tends to raise resting energy expenditure rather than blunt it the way pure GLP-1 mono-agonists can.
Why solo: One compound, one shot a week, no other variables — if you feel something, you know exactly what's causing it. Reta alone will move the weight. Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Single-Use

Dry vial (before reconstitution): store refrigerated at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light.

Reconstituted vial: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Stays usable for the full 11-week cycle when properly stored.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use syringe for every draw, no exceptions. Needle reuse is the most common cause of injection-site reactions.

Site rotation: rotate between lower abdomen (fingerbreadth from the navel), outer thigh, and love handles. Never the same 1-inch spot two weeks in a row. With once-weekly dosing, sites have a full week to recover — just alternate left/right each week.

What to Expect
Week-by-Week Across the 11 Weeks
Tip 01 — Critical
Don't Skip the Titration

Reta's side effects — mostly nausea, fatigue, and constipation — almost always come from jumping the dose too fast. Going straight to 3 mg in week one would make the first month miserable, and most people who quit Reta quit in that window.

The 1 → 2 → 3 mg ramp lets your gut and appetite system acclimate at each step. By the time you're at the 3 mg working dose in Week 4, your body's already used to Reta and it lands smoothly. Slow ramp = better tolerance = you actually finish the bottle.

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–3
Titration · Body Adjusts

Appetite suppression starts subtly in Week 1 at 1 mg, more noticeable when you bump to 2 mg in Week 2. The "food noise" — that low-grade, always-thinking-about-the-next-meal hum — should quiet down.

Mild nausea or fatigue on the day-of or day-after the shot is possible but should be manageable. Drink water aggressively (~3L/day) and front-load protein early in the day before suppression peaks.

Phase 2 — Weeks 4–7
Working Dose · Fat Loss Takes Hold

At 3 mg/week the fat-loss curve takes hold. Most people drop 1–3 lbs/week through this window. Eating naturally drops without you having to white-knuckle anything — you'll stop earlier, skip snacks, lose interest in alcohol calories.

Energy should be coming back as the weight starts moving. This is the right window to start adding light workouts — even just 20–30 min walks daily — so the body learns to move again.

Phase 3 — Weeks 8–11
Compound Effect · Decision Point

By now you should be seeing both the scale and the mirror tell the same story. Walking the golf course (no cart) becomes realistic.

By Week 11, decision point: extend with a second bottle, transition to a maintenance dose, or step away cleanly. We'll talk it through around Week 9 so you're not deciding under pressure at the end.

Execution Tips
Hydration · Protein · Holds
Tip 02 — Hydration & Fiber
Drink More Than You Think You Need

Low-grade nausea and constipation are the two most common Reta side effects. Both respond well to aggressive hydration (~3L/day, more if you're sweating on installs) and adding fiber (psyllium husk or whole foods). Address before they become a reason to hold a dose.

Tip 03 — Protein Floor
Front-Load Protein Early in the Day

Appetite drops on Reta, so it gets easy to under-eat protein and lose muscle along with the fat — bad outcome. Target 0.7–0.9 g of protein per pound of goal body weight daily, and front-load it early in the day before appetite suppression peaks.

Eggs at breakfast, jerky/cottage cheese for snacks, real meat at dinner. This is the single biggest lever for keeping the strength you need on the job.

Tip 04 — Alcohol
Reta Often Drops Alcohol Interest Naturally

Reta tends to drop interest in alcohol naturally, which is generally a bonus. But heavy drinking on the same day as a shot can amplify nausea — give yourself 24+ hours of buffer if you can.

Tip 05 — When to Hold a Dose
Safety & Red Flags

Hold the next dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis)
· Unexplained tachycardia or palpitations
· Severe injection-site reaction beyond mild redness
· Any new symptom that feels meaningfully off

When in doubt, skip the next shot, text me, and we'll reassess.

Optional Add-Ons
If You Want to Compound Results · After Week 4
Heads-Up
Run Reta Solo First — Then Consider Layering

Don't add anything in the first 4 weeks while you're still titrating up. One compound, one shot a week, no other variables — that way if you feel something, you know exactly what's causing it.

After Week 4, once you're at the working 3 mg dose and your body has settled, the five tools below each address a specific layer (sleep, energy, fat oxidation, visceral fat, exercise mimicry) you could add if you want to compound results. Recommendations only — not part of this charge, not required to make the protocol work. Reta alone will move the weight.

Add-On 01
CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin — Sleep · Recovery · Stress Resilience

A growth-hormone-releasing peptide combo that drives natural pulsatile GH release. The benefits people actually feel: much deeper sleep, faster recovery from physical work and exercise, modest fat-loss synergy, and protection of lean muscle through a calorie deficit.

Why it fits you: 50–60 hours/week of HVAC install work is hard physical labor on top of mental load — and your wife being GM means the stress doesn't switch off at home. CJC/Ipa is the single biggest lever for sleep quality and physical recovery in someone with that load. If you start adding workouts and walking the course in Weeks 4–6, this is the add that makes them feel sustainable instead of crushing. Pre-bed shot, 5 nights a week.

Add-On 02
MOTS-c — Daytime Energy · Cellular Burn

A mitochondrial-derived peptide that signals the body to build more mitochondria and run them more efficiently. The downstream effect is more cellular energy and increased energy expenditure — your cells burn through more fuel doing the same work, which compounds with Reta's caloric deficit.

Why it fits you: Reta cuts intake; MOTS-c raises output. Both sides of "eat less, burn more" working at once. The other thing people notice on MOTS-c is steadier daytime energy — fewer afternoon crashes — which matters when you're running installs all day. Morning shot, fasted, M–F.

Add-On 03
5-Amino-1MQ — Accelerated Fat Burn · NNMT Inhibition

An NNMT inhibitor — it blocks the enzyme that drains the methyl-donor and NAD+ pool inside fat cells, keeping them locked in "store fat" mode. Inhibiting NNMT flips those cells toward burning their stored lipid. Bonus: raises endogenous NAD+, which feeds back into mitochondrial output (pairs naturally with MOTS-c).

Why it fits you: Reta creates the calorie deficit; 5-Amino tells your fat cells to actively release the fuel they've been hoarding. The two compound together — bigger deficit + faster oxidation = the scale moves faster, especially through the Week 6–8 stretch where Reta plateaus tend to show up. Morning shot, fasted, M–F.

Add-On 04
Tesamorelin — Visceral Belly Fat · Targeted Composition

A GHRH analog clinically validated to reduce visceral adipose tissue — the deep belly fat around your organs, the kind that drives cardiovascular and metabolic risk. Mechanism: pulsatile GH release → IGF-1 → preferential lipolysis of visceral stores. Of all the add-on options, this is the one with the most direct, named clinical effect on the specific fat depot most men carry.

Why it fits you: Reta decides how much you lose; Tesa decides where it comes off. If the goal includes "flat midsection" and not just "lower scale" — and at 50–60 hours a week mostly on your feet but not really training, visceral fat is the likely accumulation pattern — Tesa is the layer that converts the caloric deficit into the look you actually want. Daily PM shot, 6–12 week run.

Add-On 05
SLU-PP-332 — Exercise Mimetic · Endurance Signaling

An ERR agonist — it triggers many of the same downstream cellular changes as endurance training (mitochondrial biogenesis, increased fat oxidation, improved muscle endurance) at the signaling level, without you having to actually do the workout. It's not a replacement for training, but it amplifies what training you are doing and partially extends the metabolic effects to off days.

Why it fits you: This is the most directly mapped-to-your-goal compound in the list. You want to walk the course instead of taking the cart, start adding workouts back in, and rebuild endurance. SLU makes those efforts pay off faster — the walks burn more, the workouts produce more adaptation, and the days you can't get to the gym still see some of the cellular benefit. Morning shot, daily.
