Design Philosophy

🧭 One Vial. One Shot a Week. Slow Ramp Up.

Reta is the right starter compound for someone in your situation: appetite-driven weight loss without having to overhaul your whole life around it. One injection a week, climbing from 1 mg in Week 1 up to 3 mg by Week 4. No daily routine to remember, no stack to manage, no second peptide to learn. Pick a calm day of the week, do the shot in the morning, refrigerate the vial, repeat.

Why titrate instead of going straight to 3 mg: most of Reta's side effects (nausea, fatigue, GI sluggishness) come from going too high too fast. Stepping up — 1 mg, then 2 mg for two weeks, then 3 mg — gives your gut and appetite system time to acclimate at each level. By the time you're at the working 3 mg dose in Week 4, your body's already used to Reta and the 3 mg lands smoothly. Slow ramp = better tolerance = you actually finish the bottle.

Weeks 1–3 (titration): appetite suppression starts subtly in Week 1 at 1 mg, more noticeable when you bump to 2 mg in Week 2. The "food noise" — that low-grade always-thinking-about-the-next-meal hum — should quiet down. Mild nausea or fatigue on the day-of or day-after the shot is possible but should be manageable. Weeks 8–11 (compound): by now you should be seeing both the scale and the mirror tell the same story. Walking the golf course (no cart) becomes realistic. By Week 11, decision point: extend with a second bottle, transition to a maintenance dose, or step away cleanly. We'll talk it through around Week 9 so you're not deciding under pressure at the end.

Safety & Holds

⚠️ When to Hold a Dose

Hold the next dose if any of the following: persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis), unexplained tachycardia or palpitations, severe injection-site reaction beyond mild redness, or any new symptom that feels meaningfully off. When in doubt, skip the next shot, text me, and we'll reassess.

Hydration + fiber: low-grade nausea and constipation are the two most common Reta side effects. Both respond well to aggressive hydration (~3L/day, more if you're sweating on installs) and adding fiber (psyllium husk or whole foods). Address before they become a reason to hold a dose.

Protein floor: appetite drops on Reta, so it gets easy to under-eat protein and lose muscle along with the fat — bad outcome. Target 0.7–0.9 g of protein per pound of goal body weight daily, and front-load it early in the day before the appetite suppression peaks. Eggs at breakfast, jerky/cottage cheese for snacks, real meat at dinner. This is the single biggest lever for keeping the strength you need on the job.

Alcohol: Reta tends to drop interest in alcohol naturally, which is generally a bonus. But heavy drinking on the same day as a shot can amplify nausea — give yourself 24+ hours of buffer if you can.

Storage & Handling

❄️ Refrigerated, Protected, Single-Use

Dry vial (before reconstitution): store refrigerated at 36–46 °F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Reconstituted vial: refrigerate immediately after mixing, always.

Syringe hygiene: fresh single-use syringe for every draw, no exceptions. Needle reuse is the most common cause of injection site reactions and infections.

Site rotation: rotate between lower abdomen (a fingerbreadth from the navel), outer thigh, and love handles. Never the same 1-inch spot two consecutive injections. With once-weekly dosing, sites have a full week to recover — just alternate left/right each week.
