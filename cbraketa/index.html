Caiden's Elbow Rebuild
Two phases, ~50 days — repair the elbow hard, then remodel it strong, before college ball.

The goal: get the bad elbow fully healed — not just quiet, but durable — before you report for college basketball. This is built in two phases that follow how tissue actually heals: Phase 1 repairs, Phase 2 remodels. You run them back to back, no gap in between.

Phase 1 · Repair — Wolverine, Days 1–20. A short, aggressive front-load: Wolverine (BPC-157 + TB-500) at a full 2 mg dose, once every day for 20 straight days. An elbow beat up from shooting and lifting is a mix of tendon irritation and inflammation in the surrounding soft tissue. BPC-157 is the local repair driver — it builds new blood vessels (angiogenesis) and speeds tendon/ligament healing right where you inject it. TB-500 works more broadly — it pulls repair cells to the injury and cuts inflammation across the whole area. Together they lay down fresh tissue and blood supply fast.

Phase 2 · Remodel — GHK-Cu, Days 21–50. Once the four Wolverine vials are empty, you roll straight into GHK-Cu, 10u (~1.67 mg) once daily for 30 straight days — one vial covers it. GHK-Cu is a copper peptide that stimulates collagen and glycosaminoglycan production and drives tissue remodeling. Here's the synergy: Phase 1 lays down new collagen fast, but early-stage repair collagen is disorganized and weak. GHK's job is to mature and re-organize that new tissue into properly aligned, load-bearing tendon — plus it keeps inflammation and oxidative stress down while the elbow rebuilds. BPC/TB build the scaffold; GHK turns the scaffold into strong tissue.

Why the order matters. Running GHK after Wolverine (not during) means it works on tissue that's already been repaired and is ready to remodel — that's when the collagen-maturation signal does the most good. Both phases inject SubQ right at the elbow so the whole ~50-day rebuild stays local to the joint.

The part that isn't the peptide: across both phases, back off whatever's aggravating the elbow, and once you're feeling better in Phase 2, start easing structured tendon loading back in (light eccentrics / isometrics). Peptides set the table — smart loading is what tells the new collagen how to organize. Ease off heavy pressing and max shooting volume early, ice after sessions, and let the rebuild lock in.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, One Vial at a Time

Dry vials (before mixing): keep them in the fridge at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. Sealed, they're stable for months.

After you mix a vial: put it back in the fridge right away. Only mix one vial at a time. Phase 1: open Wolverine Vial 1, finish it over 5 days, then mix Vial 2, and so on. Phase 2: mix the single GHK vial on Day 21 and run it the full 30 days — GHK stays stable reconstituted across that window in the fridge. A reconstituted vial doesn't last as long as a sealed one, so there's no upside to mixing ahead.

Mixing technique: wipe the vial top with an alcohol pad, draw the bacteriostatic water (2 mL for Wolverine, 3 mL for GHK), and let it run slowly down the inside wall of the vial — don't blast it straight onto the powder. Swirl gently to dissolve; never shake hard. GHK-Cu turns clear blue once mixed — that's the copper, totally normal. Wolverine stays colorless.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the vial stopper and your skin with an alcohol pad every time.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Day 1
Tip 01 — The Daily Rhythm
One Shot a Day, Two Phases, ~50 Days

This protocol is simple on purpose: one injection every single day, straight through. Days 1–20: Wolverine 2 mg (40u). Days 21–50: GHK-Cu (10u). No weekends off, no gap between phases, no AM/PM split. The whole thing takes about two minutes once you've got the routine.

Pick a consistent time. Morning is the easy default — do it right after you wake up so you never forget. But for repair peptides the exact clock time doesn't really matter; consistency does. Set a daily phone alarm or use the calendar file on the Schedule tab.

Empty one vial before opening the next. In Phase 1 each Wolverine vial is exactly 5 doses (Vial 1 = Days 1–5, Vial 2 = Days 6–10, etc.). In Phase 2 the single GHK vial holds all 30 doses — mix it Day 21 and run it to Day 50. When a vial runs dry, it's time to mix the next one.

Two different doses — don't mix them up. Wolverine is 40 units; GHK is 10 units. Once you switch phases on Day 21, the number on the syringe changes. Glance at the line every time.

Tip 02 — Injection Walkthrough
SubQ at the Elbow — Step by Step

This is a SubQ shot — just under the skin, into the soft tissue right next to the sore elbow. Insulin syringe, smallest needle in clinical use. It's genuinely easy and mostly painless.

Step-by-step (every shot):

1. Wash hands. Pull the mixed vial out of the fridge.
2. Wipe the rubber stopper with an alcohol pad. Let it dry.
3. Pull the needle cap off. Push the needle through the stopper and pull the plunger to the right mark — 40 units for Wolverine (Phase 1), 10 units for GHK (Phase 2). Glance at the line — don't auto-pilot.
4. Find a soft spot right next to the sore area on the elbow. Wipe it with an alcohol pad, let it dry.
5. Pinch a bit of skin, push the needle in at 45° (or 90° if there's enough padding), and press the plunger slowly until empty.
6. Pull the needle straight out. Drop the whole syringe in the sharps container.

Rotate the spot each day — front of the elbow, outside, just above, just below the sore point. Keep every shot in the same general elbow zone (that's the point — local repair), just not the same exact pinhole two days running.

What it feels like: a brief sting for some, nothing for others. At this small volume it's usually a non-event.

Tip 03 — Deload the Elbow
Don't Out-Train the Healing

The single biggest thing you control: giving the elbow a break while it heals. BPC-157 and TB-500 build and repair tissue, but they can't keep up if you re-injure the joint every day with heavy pressing and max shooting volume.

For these 20 days: pull back on the movements that light the elbow up. Keep shooting light and low-volume if you need to stay sharp, but drop the heavy triceps / pressing work and the grind-it-out gym sessions. Ice the elbow 10–15 min after any session that aggravates it.

This isn't forever. Two weeks of smart deloading now, stacked on daily Wolverine, is what gets you to campus with a healthy elbow instead of one that's still flaring in October. Train around it, not through it.

Tip 04 — Honest Timeline
What to Expect Across the Rebuild

Days 1–7 (Phase 1 start): loading. Some guys feel the elbow start to quiet down and loosen up in the first week; plenty don't feel much yet. Either way the repair signal is building — don't judge it by day 3.

Days 8–20 (rest of Phase 1): this is where most people notice the repair — less ache during and after activity, better range of motion, the joint feeling more "normal." Message Micah around day 10 with a status check. By day 20 the acute repair work is largely done.

Days 21–35 (Phase 2 start): GHK takes over. This phase is less about a dramatic day-to-day feeling and more about the tissue getting stronger and more durable underneath. The elbow should keep feeling steadier, and now's the time to start easing structured loading (light eccentrics / isometrics) back in — that's what tells the remodeling collagen how to line up.

Days 36–50 (rest of Phase 2): the remodel locks in. The goal here isn't just a pain-free elbow at rest — it's one that holds up to shooting and lifting volume again. Ramp your training back methodically; don't jump straight to max loads on day 50.

After day 50: we reassess. Remodeling tissue keeps improving for weeks after the last dose, so the elbow should continue to firm up. If it's fully there, you're set for camp. If a specific movement still nags, we decide whether a short maintenance touch-up or just continued rehab loading finishes the job.

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Miss a day? Take it as soon as you remember that day if you can. If a whole day slips by, just pick up the next day — don't double up to "catch up." Two shots at once doesn't heal faster, it just wastes a dose.

The schedule shifts, it doesn't break. If you miss a day here and there, the doses just stretch across a few extra calendar days — finish all 4 Wolverine vials before starting GHK, then finish the GHK vial. The goal is 20 Wolverine doses then 30 GHK doses into the elbow, whether that lands in exactly 50 days or 54.

Consistency beats perfection. Getting the shot in most days is what matters. Don't let one missed day turn into three.

Tip 06 — Red Flags · When to Pause
Signals to Watch For (Text Micah First)

Pause and message Micah if any of these show up:

· Redness, warmth, swelling, or pus at an injection site that's getting worse instead of better — that's a possible infection, not normal healing
· A hard, painful lump at the elbow that doesn't settle within a day or two
· Any rash, hives, or trouble breathing after a shot — stop and treat as an allergic reaction
· Elbow pain that's sharply worse after starting (vs. the usual ache) — may mean you're re-loading it too hard; deload harder

Normal and fine: a brief sting during the shot, a tiny bit of redness right at the pinhole that fades in an hour, mild tenderness at the site. That's expected.

Keep it clean. Fresh syringe every time, alcohol-wipe the vial and skin every time, one vial mixed at a time. Good technique is what keeps the injection sites boring — which is exactly what you want.

When in doubt, text first. Don't push through something that feels off, and don't restart on your own after a pause — we'll sort it out together.
