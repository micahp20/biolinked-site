Fat-loss anchor, mitochondrial recomp, daily skin signal, cellular energy — plus the overnight GH pulse to actually build muscle on top of the cut.

Four layers, one cycle, all AM. Reta once a week on Monday is the fat-loss anchor. MOTS-c five days a week is the mitochondrial recomp layer that pairs with the Reta-driven deficit. GHK-Cu daily is the skin + collagen layer running quietly in the background. NAD three days a week is the cellular energy floor that powers everything else. All four go in the same AM window, just at different sites — one efficient morning routine.

Reta — the appetite suppressor + direct fat oxidation. Triple-agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon). The glucagon arm is what makes Reta the strongest fat-loss compound out there — not just appetite suppression, direct fat oxidation. A 16-amino-acid mitochondrial-encoded peptide that improves insulin sensitivity, drives fat oxidation pathways, and supports lean muscle retention during a calorie deficit. When you're losing weight on Reta, the body normally loses some lean mass alongside fat. MOTS shifts that — the deficit pulls more from fat and less from muscle.

GHK-Cu — the skin + collagen background layer. Copper tripeptide. Drives collagen synthesis, supports skin elasticity, improves overall skin tone, supports hair density, accelerates wound healing. Daily AM at 10u (~1.67 mg) keeps the collagen signal steady. Especially valuable during a Reta-driven cut, where fast fat loss can leave skin looking less full — GHK keeps tone and elasticity in shape while the loss is happening.

NAD+ — the cellular fuel underneath all of it. Cellular substrate for mitochondrial ATP, sirtuin activation (longevity pathways), and DNA repair. After 30, NAD levels drop year over year and the cellular machinery that powers everything — energy, repair, recovery — slows. Tip 01 — The Morning Routine
How to Run All Four Compounds in 5 Minutes

The whole stack is AM — one routine, different sites. Set up once: alcohol pads out, sharps container ready, 4 syringes prepped (or fewer on lighter days). Inject in order, different site each time, dispose, done.

Monday morning (the heaviest day — 4 shots):

1. Reta first, fasted, into one abdomen quadrant (e.g., upper-left). 20u, push steady.
2. MOTS into a different abdomen quadrant (e.g., lower-right). 10u, push steady.
3. GHK into the third quadrant (e.g., upper-right) or upper thigh. 10u, push steady.
4. NAD into the fourth quadrant (e.g., lower-left) or other thigh. 25u, push slow over 15–30 seconds.

Wait ~30–45 minutes after the Reta shot before food. Coffee (black, no sugar) is fine in the gap. The other compounds don't strictly need fasted timing — they're piggybacking on the Reta fast.

Wed/Fri (3 shots): MOTS + GHK + NAD. Same rotation logic, three different sites.

Tue/Thu (2 shots): MOTS + GHK. Two sites.

Sat/Sun (1 shot): GHK only. Doesn't need to be fasted — can do right after waking, before breakfast or with it.

PM pre-bed M–F (1 shot): CJC/Ipa, fasted — last food 1.5–2 hrs prior — 10u into a different abdomen quadrant from your AM sites. Lights out within 30 min. Sat/Sun no PM shot (weekends off for receptor reset). This is the muscle-build lever — the empty-stomach window is non-negotiable, fed shots are wasted.

Site rotation matters. With this many shots per week, the same spot getting hit repeatedly will get sore. Rotate quadrants daily and consider using thighs and upper arms in addition to the abdomen. Keep the AM sites and the PM CJC/Ipa site in different quadrants.

Tip 02 — Reta Onboarding (Mondays)
What Mondays Will Feel Like

Week 1 Monday: first Reta shot. Appetite suppression starts within 3–5 days. Mild nausea or "full quickly" feeling Monday afternoon through Tuesday is normal — manageable with smaller meals and lower-fat content for ~24 hrs after the shot. Skip greasy / fried food that day. Hydrate aggressively.

Weeks 2–3: Monday-side GI effects ease as the body acclimates. Appetite control feels like baseline. Food noise quiets noticeably across the whole week, not just post-shot.

Weeks 4–6: fat loss curve takes hold. Aim for 1–1.5 lbs/wk — faster than 2 lbs/wk sustained means the deficit is too aggressive (or you're losing more water than fat). With MOTS in the stack, the loss should look like cleaner body comp, not just scale drop.

Weeks 7–8: the loss continues. Body recomp visible — less fat, lean mass holding well thanks to MOTS, skin tone holding thanks to GHK, energy holding thanks to NAD, new tissue going on thanks to the CJC/Ipa GH pulse. The 8-week cycle ends with a noticeably different body comp than weight-only loss would deliver.

Tip 03 — CJC/Ipa Fasted PM — The Non-Negotiable
Empty-Stomach Window Is the Whole Point

Food — especially carbs and protein — spikes insulin. Insulin shuts down the pituitary's GH release. A fed CJC/Ipa shot is essentially wasted — the peptide fires the signal but the pituitary won't release GH while insulin is elevated.

Exact pre-bed routine: dinner → wait 1.5–2 hours → brush teeth → inject CJC/Ipa → lights out within ~30 minutes. The GH pulse needs to fire into the slow-wave-sleep window, not into a still-awake brain.

Why this matters for your "build muscle" goal specifically. CJC/Ipa drives the overnight GH pulse, which drives IGF-1, which drives muscle protein synthesis during sleep. This is the actual muscle-building lever in the stack. If you waste the shot by injecting on a fed stomach, you've spent the dose but gotten ~10% of the benefit. Better to skip a night entirely than dose it fed.

What to expect: sleep deepens noticeably within the first 4–7 nights. Vivid dreams the first 1–2 weeks (deeper REM showing up). Mild morning hand/face puffiness may appear briefly as GH ramps; it settles by Wk 2–3. Muscle recovery between training sessions visibly better by Wk 3–4 — if you're training during the cycle, this is when the muscle-build piece starts showing up.

Tip 04 — NAD Slow-Push Technique
The Whole Trick Is the Plunger Speed

NAD SubQ can cause warmth, flushing, or a brief "hot" sensation in the chest, neck, or face during or right after the shot. It's harmless — the body clearing the bolus — and usually passes in 1–5 minutes. The fix is pure technique:

Push the plunger slowly. Aim for 15–30 seconds to deliver the full 25 units. Not 3 seconds, not "as fast as the other shots." Slow push gives the tissue time to absorb the bolus gradually — less flushing, less sting.

If flushing is still uncomfortable: slow the push even more next time (30–45 seconds), or split it — deliver half, pause 10 seconds, deliver the rest. After 3–4 shots the body usually acclimates and the flushing fades almost entirely.

Doesn't need to be fasted. NAD can go right after the other AM shots — different site, slow plunger, done.

Tip 05 — Hydration on Reta
The Thing Most People Underestimate

Target: 2.5–3 L of water per day. Reta suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite — you won't feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people end up with headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor energy and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit.

Add electrolytes once daily. Sodium, potassium, magnesium. LMNT, Liquid IV, or similar work fine.

Coffee doesn't count. Drink it on top of your water target, not instead of.

Tip 06 — GHK + Skin During a Cut
Why Daily GHK Matters More When You're Losing Fat

Fast fat loss can leave skin looking less full. When fat under the skin shrinks faster than skin can remodel, the surface can look slightly looser or less toned — especially on the face, arms, and abdomen. This is the cosmetic downside of aggressive fat loss that people complain about.

Daily GHK directly addresses this. The collagen synthesis signal runs continuously across the cycle, telling the skin to keep producing collagen and remodeling at pace with the fat loss. Result: tone and elasticity stay intact, the skin looks tighter and clearer, not deflated.

Visible effects: noticeable improvement in skin tone and texture by Week 3–4. Face responds first. Minor wounds (cuts, scrapes) heal noticeably faster — that's the same dermal repair signal in action. Hair density may improve subtly over the 8 weeks.

Where to inject: rotate across abdomen quadrants, thighs, upper arm back. Daily injection means the same spot will get sore if you don't rotate — spread the love across at least 5–6 different sites in rotation.

Tip 07 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

Reta (Mon AM): if you miss the Monday shot, take it within 2 days (so by Wednesday). If more than 2 days have passed, skip that week's dose entirely and resume next Monday. Don't double up.

MOTS (M–F): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across the cycle doesn't move the needle.

GHK (daily): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. The collagen signal is continuous and a missed day doesn't break the cycle.

NAD (M/W/F): miss a scheduled day, skip it. Resume the next M/W/F day. 3×/week is generous — one missed dose doesn't move the cellular energy floor.

Tip 08 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain or right-upper-quadrant pain after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit is too aggressive, eat more
· Lightheadedness or dizziness on standing — could be blood sugar or dehydration; hydrate, eat, monitor

Hold MOTS or GHK if: significant injection-site reaction (redness, heat, swelling past 24 hrs), or any allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes). Both are well-tolerated; individual reactions are rare but possible.

Hold NAD if: severe flushing that doesn't resolve in 5–10 minutes despite slowing the plunger, chest tightness, racing heart, or anxiety-type symptoms post-injection, persistent injection-site reaction past 24 hours.

Hold CJC/Ipa if: persistent morning hand/face puffiness past Wk 3, joint pain that's new, sustained tingling/numbness in hands — drop dose to 125/125 mcg (5u) or pause. Sleep getting worse instead of deeper — flag.

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own.
