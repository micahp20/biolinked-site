Sam's Recomp + Cognitive Edge
Strip the excess. Build the lean. Keep the brain locked in at 2 AM.

Three goals, one integrated stack. The target: 190 → 170 while protecting lean tissue, plus a direct cognitive enhancement layer for Boulder PD shift work. Three compounds, three independent levers that work in parallel without stepping on each other.

The fat-loss anchor is Retatrutide — a triple agonist (GLP-1 / GIP / glucagon) that drives appetite suppression and metabolic burn at a low-and-steady dose. The muscle-protection + sleep layer is CJC-1295/Ipamorelin pre-bed — amplifies your natural overnight GH pulse so what you lose in the deficit is fat, not lean tissue, and sleep gets visibly deeper. The cognitive enhancement layer is Semax — ACTH(4–10) analog that drives BDNF expression and tunes dopamine + serotonin balance for clean focus, recall, and motivation on the days your shift work demands it most.

Run-till-empty structure. No fixed week-count. The schedule runs M–F for Reta + CJC/Ipa, with Semax on M/W/F only. Weekends off. You finish the supplied bottles, and we reassess. Once these three are routine, layering MOTS-c, 5-Amino-1MQ, and IGF-1 LR3 in a future cycle is a simple add.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry vials (before reconstitution): refrigerate at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. They're stable for many months sealed.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Most peptides are stable for ~30 days reconstituted. Reconstitute one vial at a time and finish it before opening the next — don't pre-mix everything on day 1.

Syringe discipline: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe the rubber stopper and your injection site with an alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never the same 1″ spot two days in a row. On Reta days (M/W/F) you'll have two AM shots — use adjacent quadrants so neither gets stacked.

Execution & Tips

Tip 01 — First-Time Injector Walkthrough
You've Never Done This Before — Here's the 5-Minute Version

The injection itself is short, thin, and SubQ. Not into muscle, not into a vein. Just under the skin in the fatty layer of your abdomen. The needle is the smallest you'll find in clinical use — most people describe it as a quick pinch and that's it.

Step-by-step (every shot):

1. Wash hands. Grab the vial out of the fridge.
2. Wipe the rubber stopper of the vial with an alcohol pad. Let it dry.
3. Pull the needle cap off the syringe. Don't pull the plunger back first — just push the needle through the rubber stopper and pull the plunger back to the right unit mark: 10 units for Reta and CJC/Ipa, 20 units for Semax. Glance at the mark before every shot — don't auto-pilot.
4. Pull the needle out. Wipe a spot on your abdomen (~2″ from the navel) with an alcohol pad. Let it dry.
5. Pinch about an inch of skin between your fingers. Push the needle in at a 45° angle (or 90° if you have more padding). Push the plunger slowly until empty.
6. Pull the needle straight out. Don't massage. Drop the whole syringe into the sharps container.

What it feels like: a brief sting going in for some peptides, nothing at all for others. Reta and CJC/Ipa are essentially painless. Semax is similarly mild — the bigger 0.20 mL volume takes a beat longer to push but the sensation is the same.

Tip 02 — What to Expect Week-by-Week
The Honest Timeline

Weeks 1–2: appetite suppression from Reta kicks in. Some mild nausea on shot days, especially the first 2–3 doses. CJC/Ipa makes sleep noticeably deeper. Semax effect is faster on the cognitive side — most people feel clearer focus + a slight uptick in motivation within the first few doses, building over weeks as BDNF expression compounds. Don't expect to see scale movement yet.

Weeks 3–6: the fat-loss curve takes hold. 1–2 lbs/week is the healthy range — faster means too aggressive a deficit. You'll notice clothes fitting differently before the scale catches up. Semax cognitive lift is now well-established — the M/W/F cadence carries the focus across the week. This is the sweet-spot window of the cycle.

Weeks 6–8: the typical Reta plateau may show up around now. If the scale stalls for more than 10–14 days, check protein intake first (aim for ~170 g/day at goal weight); if that's locked in, hold dose and let the metabolic adaptation work itself out before considering a bump. A future cycle layering 5-Amino-1MQ would specifically target the plateau if it becomes a pattern.

Run-till-empty: with 2 vials of Reta at 3×/wk you've got ~10 weeks of Reta supply. CJC/Ipa hits its last dose around the same window. Semax at M/W/F (3×/wk) on 2 vials runs ~6–7 weeks — that's intentional, the BDNF effect is well-set by then. We reassess at the Reta + CJC end — another cycle, take a break, or shift the stack composition (MOTS-c, 5-Amino, IGF-1 LR3 are natural next layers).

Tip 03 — Shift Work + the Stack
Running This on Boulder PD Hours

Anchor the schedule to YOUR wake time, not the clock. "AM" means within the first hour after you wake up — whether that's 6 AM after a normal night or 4 PM after a graveyard shift. Pre-bed for CJC/Ipa is within an hour of when you actually go to sleep.

The 2+ hour fast before CJC/Ipa is the only hard rule. Food spikes insulin and blunts the GH pulse the peptide exists to amplify. If you eat too close to your sleep window, skip that night's dose and resume the next sleep cycle — better to miss one than waste one.

Reta nausea on demanding shifts: if your Mon/Wed/Fri Reta day lands on a high-physical or high-decision shift, the week 1–2 nausea risk can be an inconvenience. Two options: (1) shift Reta to your days off until you know how your body handles it, or (2) take the Reta shot at the end of your shift instead of pre-shift so the peak hits while you're at home.

Tip 04 — Hydration + Protein Floor
The Two Things That Actually Matter on a Cut

Hydration target: 3–4 L/day, every day. Reta suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite — you will not feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people end up with headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor workout performance and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit. Add electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day, ideally pre-training. Coffee doesn't count.

Protein target: ~170 g/day (1 g per lb of goal bodyweight). Non-negotiable for keeping muscle through the cut. Reta makes this harder because appetite drops — front-load protein early in the day before the suppression peaks. A protein shake within an hour of waking is the most reliable way to hit the floor through a Reta cut.

Tip 05 — Alcohol on the Stack
3–7 Drinks/Week Range — Honest Read

Nothing in the stack has a dangerous interaction with alcohol, but several compounds are working against what alcohol does. The honest framing: alcohol slows fat oxidation, disrupts the deep-sleep window CJC/Ipa is trying to amplify, and adds cognitive load the next morning. At the 3–7 drinks/week range you're not going to derail the protocol, but you'll get better results by clustering drinks (e.g. 3–5 on one weekend night) instead of spreading them across multiple weeknights.

Don't drink within 4 hours of a CJC/Ipa shot. Alcohol blunts GH release the same way food does — defeats the purpose.

Don't drink the night before a Reta shot. Compounds the nausea risk meaningfully.

Tip 06 — Red Flags · When to Pause
Signals to Watch For

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain, RUQ pain after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit is too aggressive, eat more

Hold CJC/Ipa if: persistent morning hand puffiness that doesn't resolve, joint pain that's new, sustained tingling/numbness in hands.

Other yellow flags — reassess: strength dropping noticeably in the gym (means lean mass is going — lift protein, consider dropping Reta to 2×/wk), persistent fatigue past week 3, sleep getting worse instead of better.

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own — we adjust the plan based on what's actually happening in your body.

Tip 07 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip One

Reta: if you miss a M/W/F dose, take it within 24 hours and resume the schedule. If more than 24 hours have passed, just skip it and pick up at the next scheduled day — don't double up.

CJC/Ipa: miss one night, skip it. Don't try to "make it up" by doubling the next day. Resume the next pre-bed window.

Semax: miss a M/W/F dose, take it the next morning if it's within 24 hours; otherwise skip and resume the next scheduled day. Don't double up — over-firing the receptor dulls the effect.
