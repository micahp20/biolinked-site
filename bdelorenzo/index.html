Becky's Lean Strong & Recharged
Already at a happy weight. Now: stronger, sharper, sleeping better — with a soft brake for the weekends.

Becky's situation is the one most "weight loss" protocols don't fit. She's at the body composition she wants. Hitting the scale isn't the goal — in fact she actively doesn't want to bulk OR drop weight. The goal is everything around the weight: lean muscle that adds shape without bulk, sleep that actually recharges, mental clarity that holds through the day, and a way to keep weekends from undoing what the week built.

This protocol is built in two phases over ~16 weeks. Phase 1 (Wks 1–8) lays the cellular foundation — SS-31 repairs the mitochondria for clean energy production, GHK-Cu drives recovery so training adapts faster. Phase 2 (Wks 9–16) flips into active mode — MOTS-C activates the now-healthy engine, 5-Amino-1MQ supports the body's lean-tissue-building pathways, and CJC/Ipa runs at night to preserve muscle and deepen sleep at the same time.

Throughout both phases, the PRN tools layer on as needed. Cagrilintide is the weekend appetite brake — one shot Friday or Saturday evening to take the edge off weekend indulgences without killing the social part. DSIP is a sleep tool when nights need extra help. Semax is mental-clarity-on-demand for the days that need it. The result by Wk 16: leaner-looking but not lighter, energy that doesn't crash in the afternoon, sleep that delivers, and a body comp that holds through weekends instead of swinging.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Fresh insulin syringe for each draw — never reuse.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. With AM + PM clusters, alternate AM (abdomen R) and PM (abdomen L) sites to maximize healing time per spot.

Tip 01 — The "Lean Without Bulk" Logic
Why This Protocol Doesn't Move the Scale

The scale won't change much. That's by design. Becky's already at the weight she wants. The protocol is built around recomposition — same body weight, different distribution. More lean, less fat, sharper shape.

The mechanism: MOTS-C activates the mitochondria → fat oxidation increases. 5-Amino-1MQ inhibits NNMT → the brake on lean-tissue building releases. CJC/Ipa pulses GH at night → muscle preservation kicks in while you sleep. Net result: body comp shifts toward lean without weight loss. The scale moves 1–2 lbs in either direction — not the metric to watch.

Track these instead: how clothes fit (especially around the hips and arms), morning energy levels (out of 10), sleep quality (Whoop / Oura HRV trend), and a weekly mirror photo. By Week 16 you'll see what the scale never showed you.

Tip 02 — The PRN Layer Is the Sustainability Layer
Cagri / DSIP / Semax — Use Them, Don't Forget Them

Most clients forget the PRN compounds exist after Week 2 and just run the scheduled ones. Don't do that. The PRN layer is what makes this protocol sustainable through real life.

Cagrilintide on Friday evenings is the single highest-leverage weekend habit. Most weight rebounds happen because Sunday-night-Becky has been hungry for two days. One Cagri shot Friday PM and that's gone — you still enjoy Saturday dinner, still have the wine, just don't end the weekend feeling bloated and 3 lbs up.

DSIP on travel nights or post-stressful days protects sleep when life would otherwise nuke it. Semax on big-presentation or post-poor-sleep mornings brings clarity back when caffeine alone doesn't cut it. These are tools. Use them. They're already paid for.

Tip 03 — The AM + PM Two-Cluster Rhythm (Phase 2)
5 Shots / Day Sounds Like a Lot. It's Actually Two 60-Second Routines.

Phase 2 has up to 3 AM shots + 1 PM shot on M/W/F days. Looks like a lot on paper. In practice:

AM (fasted, 60 seconds): wake → coffee/water → MOTS-C 15u abdomen R + (M/W/F only) 5-Amino 12u abdomen L → 30 min later, breakfast. That's it for the morning.

PM (before bed, 30 seconds): brush teeth → CJC/Ipa 10u abdomen R (opposite side from AM) → bed within 60 minutes. The GH pulse fires during your first deep-sleep cycle.

It becomes habit by end of Week 1. The biggest hassle is the first weekend — once you're past that, the rhythm sets.

Execution Tips
Daily Rhythm · What to Monitor
Tip 04 — Cagrilintide GI Side Effects
First Weekend Might Be a Little Off — That's Normal

First Cagri shot can produce mild nausea or early satiety for 24 hours. Take it Friday evening, not before a big Saturday event — you want the first-shot effects to wash out before the high-stakes meal.

Hydrate (2+ L) and add electrolytes the day of dosing. Eat smaller, more frequent meals if nausea hits — large meals on Cagri = guaranteed discomfort.

By the second or third weekend dose, tolerance builds and side effects fade. The appetite suppression effect, however, stays.

Tip 05 — CJC/Ipa Timing Matters More Than People Think
Empty Stomach + Within 60 Min of Bed

CJC/Ipa triggers a growth hormone pulse. That pulse needs low insulin to fire properly. Two non-negotiables for PM dosing:

1. No food in the 2 hours before injection. Big dinner at 8pm? Inject at 10pm or later, not 9pm.
2. Sleep within 60 minutes of injection. The first deep-sleep cycle is when GH pulses normally; CJC/Ipa amplifies that pulse. Stay up an extra 2 hours and you miss the synergy.

Gets easier once it becomes part of the wind-down routine — phones away → brush teeth → inject → bed.

Tip 06 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild injection-site redness — rotate sites and use clean technique
· Slightly more vivid dreams in Weeks 1–2 from CJC/Ipa — that's the deeper sleep
· Mild flushing 5–10 min after MOTS-C — normal AMPK-related response
· First-weekend Cagri nausea — subsides as tolerance builds
· Tingling in extremities first nights of CJC/Ipa — the GH pulse causing temporary fluid shifts. Goes away.

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique or contamination
· Severe nausea / vomiting past Week 2 of Cagri — drop dose
· Persistent fatigue past Week 3 of any phase — rare; flag it
· Sleep disruption (paradoxical) from CJC/Ipa — rare; may need to shift timing or skip occasional doses
