Tip 01 — Sema → Reta Transition · Why the Slow Ramp
Restarting GLP-1 After a Gap = Conservative Entry, Not Previous Dose

You were on Sema at 3.5 mg/wk before stopping. The instinct might be to restart Reta at a "matching" dose — but that almost always crashes with nausea. Coming off a GLP-1 for any meaningful gap re-sensitizes your GI system. Restarting at the previous dose hits a now-untrained gut and produces 2–3 weeks of unnecessary misery.

The escalation ladder:

  • Wks 1–2: 1 mg Mon + 1 mg Thu = 2 mg/wk. Gentle. Most people feel mild appetite suppression by Day 3–5; mild GI awareness in the first 24–48 hrs after each shot is normal.
  • Wks 3–4: 1.5 mg Mon + 1.5 mg Thu = 3 mg/wk. Moderate. By here you should feel steady appetite control through the whole week. If GI is still rough at the start of Wk 3, hold at 1 mg for another week before climbing.
  • Wks 5+ ongoing: 2 mg Mon + 2 mg Thu = 4 mg/wk. Maintenance cap. This is where the bulk of the body-comp shift happens — and where you stay until goal weight or protocol reassessment.

4 mg/wk is your cap. Clinical Reta studies go up to 12 mg+ for max weight loss, but pushing higher costs in GI side effects and isn't necessary for your goal. When you hit 115, we taper down 0.5 mg/wk — not push higher. Below the cap stays available if you ever want to push more aggressively in a future cycle.

Tip 02 — Thyroid Surgery · The Bridge Plan
Pause Reta 1 Week Before + 1 Week After Surgery

You'll likely be on synthroid (levothyroxine) after thyroidectomy. Reta and synthroid don't interact — they work on completely separate pathways. The peptide stays on track while your thyroid axis is being rebuilt pharmacologically.

What changes around the surgery:

  • 1 week BEFORE surgery: pause Reta. The GI effects can compound with anesthesia/surgery prep (which often includes fasting + meds that affect GI motility).
  • The surgery week: off Reta entirely. Focus on the procedure + immediate recovery.
  • 1 week AFTER surgery: still off Reta. Let your incision heal and your synthroid titration start.
  • Resume Reta on the next regular Monday after that 2-week pause. At whatever tier dose you were on when you paused.

Synthroid timing: levothyroxine is usually taken first thing AM, 30–60 min before food/coffee. Take it 30+ min BEFORE your Reta shot on Mon and Thu — that protects the synthroid absorption window. Reta doesn't have an absorption-window issue (subcutaneous, not oral), so it's flexible.

Tell your surgeon you're using a peptide-based GLP-1 (don't need to use the brand name — "GLP-1 receptor agonist" is what they'll understand). They may have a specific pre-op fasting protocol. Follow theirs over the general 1-week pause if they're stricter.

Watch for: increased fatigue or weight regain in the immediate post-op weeks is mostly the thyroid axis adjusting — not the Reta. Synthroid takes 4–6 weeks to find the right dose. Be patient with both processes simultaneously.

Tip 03 — Cellular Energy Add-Ons (Recommendations Only — Not in Your Bundle)
Three Compounds for Thyroid-Affected Energy Drag

You mentioned bad energy — that's the thyroid talking. Even on the right synthroid dose, the cellular-energy layer often takes longer to recover than the bloodwork suggests. The peptide world has three compounds that target that specific layer. Not in this bundle — recommendations only — but worth knowing about if energy stays low through Wks 4–6 of the Reta cycle (after the GLP-1 acclimation):

  • NAD+ — nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide. Coenzyme that's central to mitochondrial ATP production. Why it helps: thyroid disorders deplete NAD+ pools faster than normal metabolism; supplementing restores the substrate your mitochondria need to actually produce energy. Subcutaneous injection (not oral NMN/NR — very different bioavailability). Most users feel a daytime clarity + energy lift within 1–2 weeks. Approx cost: $80–150/vial depending on size + supplier.
  • 5-Amino-1MQ — NNMT (nicotinamide N-methyltransferase) inhibitor. Translation: blocks the enzyme that uses up your NAD+ in adipose tissue, so more NAD+ stays available for energy production. Why it helps: drives metabolic flexibility (your body switches more easily between burning glucose and fat for fuel — thyroid disorders impair this). Oral compound (not injection), which makes it the lowest-friction add-on. Approx cost: $60–90/month supply.
  • MOTS-C — mitochondrial-derived peptide that activates AMPK (the metabolic master switch). Why it helps: AMPK activation drives fat oxidation, mitochondrial biogenesis, and exercise capacity. For someone with thyroid-related metabolic drag, MOTS-C stacks well with Reta's glucagon arm — both push energy expenditure but through different mechanisms. Approx cost: $60–100/vial; typical cycle uses 2 vials.

Most-recommended starting order if you want to add one:

  • 5-Amino-1MQ first — oral, lowest friction, no injection. Try for 4 weeks; if energy improves, you have your answer cheaply.
  • NAD+ second — if 5-Amino-1MQ alone isn't enough, add NAD+ subQ. The two stack well (5-Amino preserves the NAD+ pool you're supplementing).
  • MOTS-C third — if you want the AMPK/mitochondrial biogenesis layer on top. Best added in Cycle 2 or after the thyroid axis is settled with synthroid.

None of these conflict with Reta or synthroid. Message me when you want to layer one in — we'll order it standalone outside the Reta bundle.

Tip 04 — Alcohol on Reta Days
Skip or Minimize Sun + Wed Nights

You mentioned 3–7 drinks/week. Reta + alcohol increases the risk of nausea, GI discomfort, and dehydration — especially in the 24–48 hrs after a shot. Most people who get sick on Reta did so after drinking the night before a dose.

Practical rule: minimize alcohol Sunday night and Wednesday night (the nights before your Mon + Thu shots). Save drinks for Fri or Sat when you have a buffer day to recover before the next shot. Bonus: alcohol within 4 hrs of bed cuts deep sleep — and your sleep score is already 3/5. Pulling alcohol away from shot-prep nights gives you sleep depth AND smoother Reta tolerance for the price of two boring weeknights.

Tip 05 — The Ramp Logic Recap
Why Mon + Thu (Not Once-Weekly)

Reta's half-life is ~6 days, so the standard clinical pattern is once-weekly dosing. That pattern creates a sharp peak in the 24–48 hours after the shot — nausea, fatigue, "blah" feeling. Twice-weekly Mon + Thu splits the same weekly mg total into two smaller doses, smoothing the curve.

Result: lower peaks, fewer side effects, steadier appetite control through the whole week instead of front-loaded into Mon–Wed. For someone managing thyroid-related energy variability, the steadier curve matters — you don't want post-shot fatigue stacked on top of thyroid fatigue.

Tip 07 — Normal Effects (No Action Needed)
Expected on Reta Acclimation + Maintenance
  • Mild appetite suppression starting Wk 1 — the signal you want.
  • Mild nausea or GI awareness 24–48 hrs after each shot in Wks 1–2 — the re-tolerance window. Settles by Wk 3.

  • Cleaner daytime energy starting around Wk 4–5 — the glucagon-arm effect landing on top of the appetite control.

Tip 08 — Watch For (Message BioLinked)
Anything Below Warrants a Quick Check-In
  • Severe nausea, vomiting, or appetite loss that doesn't settle within 72 hrs of a tier climb — hold the current tier or step back, then flag.
  • Injection site hot, swollen, or red >24 hrs — possible technique or contamination.
  • Energy got worse on Reta than baseline — should be the same or better by Wk 4–5; flag if it's worse, especially given the thyroid context.
  • Sustained abdominal pain (especially upper-right or upper-mid abdomen) — pause and contact your physician (rare with Reta but worth ruling out pancreatitis/gallbladder).
  • Any allergic reaction symptoms (rash, itching, throat tightness) — stop and seek medical attention immediately.
  • Heart rate consistently elevated at rest — uncommon at 2 mg dosing but worth flagging.
  • Significant weight regain in the 2–4 weeks post-thyroidectomy — usually the thyroid axis adjusting, but flag so we can decide if dose needs to bump or hold.
