David's Mito Reset + Cut
Repair the engine first. Then the 10–15 lbs come off without the energy crash.

You're 45, recently off TRT, and want to drop 10–15 lbs. On paper that's a simple problem. In reality, coming off TRT exposes something most people don't think about — testosterone was quietly supporting your mitochondrial function. Without TRT in the picture, the metabolic floor drops. That's why guys coming off T often describe weight loss feeling "harder than it used to be" and energy that crashes in the afternoon.

The piece nobody talks about — mitochondria and weight loss. Fat is stored chemical energy. To burn it, your cells need functioning mitochondria (the energy-producing machinery inside every cell). When the mitochondria are damaged, slow, or inefficient — which happens with aging, post-TRT, chronic stress, poor sleep — weight loss becomes harder and energy depletes faster. Diet alone hits a wall. Cardio alone hits a wall. You can be in a deficit and still not see the scale move. That's mitochondrial dysfunction blocking the fat-burning pathway.

The fix is sequential, not parallel. You don't want to push a damaged engine harder — you want to repair it first, then run it. Phase 1 (SS-31 + Humanin) repairs and protects the mitochondria at the membrane and cell level. Phase 2 (MOTS-C + Retatrutide) then activates the now-healthy engine: MOTS-C drives metabolic activity (AMPK signaling), Reta drives the calorie deficit (appetite + fat oxidation). Repair, then activate, then cut. That's the whole logic.

Featured Tips for Your Situation
Tip 01 — Mitochondria + Weight Loss + Coming Off TRT
Why Weight Loss Got Harder Once You Stopped TRT

Most guys coming off TRT notice something within a few months — the body composition that was easy to maintain on testosterone suddenly feels harder. Weight loss attempts hit a wall. Energy crashes in the afternoon. Workouts feel flat. The reason isn't willpower or "metabolism slowing with age." It's mitochondrial.

Testosterone has a direct supportive effect on mitochondrial function — it upregulates the genes that produce mitochondrial proteins, improves the efficiency of the electron transport chain, and supports the cellular processes that convert stored fat into usable energy. When you come off T, that scaffolding goes away. The mitochondria are still there, but they're operating without the support they were used to.

How this affects weight loss specifically:

  • Fat = stored chemical energy. To burn it, you need functioning mitochondria. Slow mitochondria = slow fat burning, regardless of how good your diet is.
  • Energy depletes faster. Less efficient ATP production = afternoon crashes, gym sessions that feel harder than they should, and the "tired but wired" feeling of being in a deficit.
  • Plateaus hit early. Your body senses the metabolic stress and downregulates further to conserve energy. Cuts that should produce 1–2 lbs/week start producing nothing.

The protocol fix: repair the mitochondria before driving the deficit. SS-31 + Humanin in Phase 1 directly address the cellular machinery — 20 days of daily dosing (including weekends), which is the intensive-course format the research supports for mitochondrial peptides. By Day 20, you're heading into Phase 2 with mitochondria that are functioning closer to where they were on T. Then MOTS-C activates them and Reta does the fat-loss work — without the energy crash that most cuts produce.

Tip 02 — Phase Sequencing
Why Reta Doesn't Start in Week 1

Most people would buy Reta and start it Day 1. We're not doing that for you. The reason: Reta drives a calorie deficit, and calorie deficits stress mitochondria. Stressing already-stressed mitochondria (post-TRT) makes everything worse — more energy crashes, more plateaus, worse adherence.

The sequence: repair (Days 1–20) → activate (Wks 4+) → cut (Wks 4+). By the time Reta kicks in, you've spent 20 days of daily intensive repair on the engine. MOTS-C activates that repaired engine in Phase 2. Reta then drives the deficit on top of a metabolic system that can handle the deficit without falling apart.

You'll still lose the 10–15 lbs — possibly faster than a Reta-only approach — just with stable energy the whole way instead of the gas-out crash most cuts produce.

Tip 03 — Level-Down Protocol at Goal
Don't Overshoot. Drop the Dose When You're There.

10–15 lbs is your target. Reta will keep producing fat loss past that target if you let it. Most people don't level down in time and end up underweight or losing muscle they didn't want to lose.

The protocol:

  • ~3 lbs from goal: drop Reta from M/W/F back to Mon + Thu only (cuts weekly dose 33%)
  • At goal: drop Reta dose from 1 mg to 0.5 mg (5 units / 0.05 mL instead of 10 units / 0.10 mL) OR drop frequency to once weekly (Mon only)
  • 1–2 lbs past goal: stop Reta entirely. MOTS-C can continue if you want metabolic support.

The point isn't to keep losing weight forever. The point is to land at your target, lock it in, and maintain. Reta is a tool, not a lifestyle — use it for the cut, then back off cleanly.

Tip 04 — Reta GI Side Effects
What to Expect During the Ramp-Up

Reta in the first 1–2 weeks (Wks 4–5) is when GI side effects are most likely — nausea, slow gastric emptying, occasional constipation. The Mon + Thu titration is specifically designed to minimize this.

If nausea hits hard:

  • Drop to once weekly (Mon only) for an additional 2 weeks before resuming Mon + Thu
  • OR drop dose to 0.5 mg (5 units / 0.05 mL) instead of 1 mg (10 units / 0.10 mL) until tolerance builds
  • Eat smaller meals more frequently — large meals on Reta = guaranteed nausea
  • Hydrate aggressively (3+ L/day) and add electrolytes

By Week 9–10 most GI effects fade as tolerance builds. The bump to M/W/F should feel manageable.

Tip 05 — AM Cluster Logistics
Phase 1 vs. Phase 2 Morning Routine

Phase 1 mornings (Days 1–20, every day): SS-31 + Humanin together, 5 min. Same window, rotate to different abdomen quadrants. Doesn't have to be fasted — can be before or after breakfast. Easiest right after you wake up. Weekends included — the 20-day intensive needs daily continuity.

Phase 2 mornings (Wks 4+): MOTS-C fasted only — first thing AM, water/black coffee only, wait 30–45 min before eating. On Reta days (Mon + Thu in Phase 2a, M/W/F in Phase 2b), Reta can go in the same window — doesn't need to be fasted, just convenient to cluster the shots.

Tip 06 — Diet During the Cut
Reta Does the Hard Part — You Just Have to Eat Smart

Reta will kill your appetite. The trap most guys fall into: they eat too little and end up under-fueling, which crashes energy AND costs muscle. Hit these baselines:

  • Protein: 0.8–1 g per lb of bodyweight, daily. Non-negotiable for preserving muscle on a GLP-1.
  • Total calories: aim for a ~500 kcal/day deficit, not more. Reta will make it feel easy — resist the urge to push the deficit harder.
  • Hydration: 3+ L/day. Reta + dehydration = GI misery.
  • Training: 3–4 lift sessions/week to retain muscle. Cardio optional.
Tip 07 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild injection-site redness or small bruising — rotate sites and use clean technique
· Improved sleep depth + slightly more vivid dreams Wks 1–2
· Mild flushing 5–10 min after MOTS-C — normal AMPK-related vascular response
· Reta GI effects in the first 1–2 weeks of Phase 2a — subsides as tolerance builds
· Appetite gone within 3–5 days of starting Reta — that's the compound working

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique or contamination
· Severe nausea or vomiting past Week 2 of Reta — drop to 0.5 mg or pause and contact us
· Weight loss faster than 2 lbs/wk sustained — level down to protect lean tissue
· Persistent fatigue past Week 3 of Phase 1 — rare but worth flagging; may need to adjust dose timing
