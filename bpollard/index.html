Design Philosophy

🎯 Three Goals · Seven Tools · One 8-Week Window

The protocol is built around your three stated goals — and each compound is selected because it's the cleanest tool for one specific job, with no overlap.

Fat loss (the primary goal): Reta is the appetite + caloric deficit engine. Cagri (amylin analog) extends satiety so the deficit holds through the afternoon — this is the "CagriSema" pairing now in late-stage trials. MOTS-c lifts the metabolic floor so cellular energy expenditure stays high through the cut. SLU-PP-332 is an exercise mimetic that pushes fat oxidation up without requiring more gym time — useful for someone training "moderate 3–4x/week" with a packed life.

Cognitive enhancement: Selank is a clean nootropic / anxiolytic — it takes the cortisol edge off without sedating you. Built specifically for the high-pressure sales role: PM dosing PRN, used when the pressure spikes.

Longevity & anti-aging: SS-31 is the mitochondrial protector — it stabilizes the inner mitochondrial membrane, particularly relevant for cardiovascular tissue (you're already on lisinopril) and for the oxidative damage alcohol has been running up at the cellular level for years. DSIP is the sleep architecture rebuilder — alcohol crushes deep sleep, and deep sleep is where most of the body's repair, hormonal regulation, and longevity machinery actually runs. Fix the sleep, and a huge percentage of "anti-aging" takes care of itself.

Safety, Holds & Personal Notes

⚠️ When to Hold a Dose

Hold Reta or Cagri if any of the following: persistent nausea/vomiting beyond 24 hours, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to back — rule out pancreatitis), severe constipation lasting >3 days, severe injection-site reaction beyond mild redness.

Hold the entire AM stack if you wake up feeling actively unwell, fluish, or with unexplained tachycardia. Skipping a day costs nothing; pushing through a real signal can.

💧 Brandon-Specific — Lifestyle Pairing

Alcohol + Reta: the GLP-1 + alcohol combination dramatically increases nausea risk in the 24 hours after a shot. Plan injection days (M/W/F) to be your lighter drink days. Heavy drinking the night before a Reta shot is the single biggest avoidable trigger for "I feel terrible" the next morning.

Hydration: 3L/day minimum. Reta + Cagri both push toward dehydration; alcohol compounds it; lisinopril compounds it again. This is non-negotiable, and it'll also help the BP your medication is managing.

Protein floor: hit at least 0.8 g of protein per pound of goal weight (200 lbs × 0.8 = 160 g/day). Reta crushes appetite, which is the whole point — but it makes hitting protein harder. Front-load: protein-heavy breakfast and lunch before suppression peaks. Test + protein + the stack = lean tissue protected through the cut.

Lisinopril check: as you lose weight, your BP will likely come down. Have your physician check it around Week 4 and Week 8 — you may end up dose-reducing or coming off it entirely. That's a long-term win worth tracking.

Lipo shot: you can keep it running through this protocol — no interaction concerns. It's complementary to the rest of the fat-loss stack.

Storage & Handling

❄️ Refrigerated, Protected, Single-Use

Dry vials (before reconstitution): store refrigerated at 36–46 °F. Never freeze. Protect from light. All seven peptides are temperature-sensitive — the fridge is non-negotiable.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Use within 30 days. Open one vial of each compound at a time — don't start the 30-day clock on a second vial until the first is close to empty.

Syringe hygiene: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every draw. Needle reuse is the most common cause of injection site reactions and infections.

Site rotation: rotate SubQ between four abdominal quadrants (upper-L, upper-R, lower-L, lower-R), outer thigh, and love handles. Never the same 1-inch spot two consecutive injections — sites need 48+ hours to recover. With 4–5 AM shots/day, give each compound its own quadrant per session to keep tracking simple.
