Cumulative stack effect compounds: cleaner energy from SS-31 + MOTS-c, deeper sleep from DSIP, sharper afternoons from Selank. Sales work: you should feel a clear difference in how you handle pressure compared to Week 1 — that's the cognitive layer paying off.

Watch for: Reta plateaus around Weeks 6–8 are common — if the scale flattens, hold the dose, don't escalate; the stack's other compounds will keep working. Re-check protein intake (target ≥ 0.8 g/lb of goal weight).

Final week of cycle. Total expected fat loss: 22–30 lbs depending on starting point and adherence.

  • (1) Extend — continue the full stack for another 4–8 weeks if you haven't hit 200.

Most clients in your situation want option 1 or 2. We'll talk in Week 7 to figure out which.

Storage & Handling
Refrigerated · Single-Use · Rotate Sites
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry vials (before reconstitution): store refrigerated at 36–46°F. Never freeze. Protect from light. All seven peptides are temperature-sensitive — the fridge is non-negotiable.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing. Use within 30 days. Open one vial of each compound at a time — don't start the 30-day clock on a second vial until the first is close to empty.

Syringe hygiene: fresh single-use insulin syringe for every draw. Needle reuse is the most common cause of injection site reactions and infections.

Site rotation: rotate SubQ between four abdominal quadrants (upper-L, upper-R, lower-L, lower-R), outer thigh, and love handles. Never the same 1-inch spot two consecutive injections — sites need 48+ hours to recover. With 4–5 AM shots/day, give each compound its own quadrant per session to keep tracking simple.

Tip 02 — Lifestyle Pairing
Brandon-Specific · Make the Stack Work

Alcohol + Reta: the GLP-1 + alcohol combination dramatically increases nausea risk in the 24 hours after a shot. Plan injection days (M/W/F) to be your lighter drink days. Heavy drinking the night before a Reta shot is the single biggest avoidable trigger for "I feel terrible" the next morning.

Hydration: 3 L/day minimum. Reta + Cagri both push toward dehydration; alcohol compounds it; lisinopril compounds it again. This is non-negotiable, and it'll also help the BP your medication is managing.

Protein floor: hit at least 0.8 g of protein per pound of goal weight (200 lbs × 0.8 = 160 g/day). Reta crushes appetite, which is the whole point — but it makes hitting protein harder. Front-load: protein-heavy breakfast and lunch before suppression peaks. Test + protein + the stack = lean tissue protected through the cut.

Lisinopril check: as you lose weight, your BP will likely come down. Have your physician check it around Week 4 and Week 8 — you may end up dose-reducing or coming off it entirely. That's a long-term win worth tracking.

Lipo shot: you can keep it running through this protocol — no interaction concerns. It's complementary to the rest of the fat-loss stack.

Tip 03 — AM Cluster Logistics
~5 Shots Peak Day · ~5 Minutes

M/W/F mornings have up to 5 shots (Reta, MOTS-c, SS-31, SLU-PP-332 in the AM block, plus DSIP at night). Looks intimidating; in practice it's a single 5-minute routine. All five are SubQ insulin syringes — same technique, same site rotation, different vials and draws.

Cluster them: alcohol the vial tops + skin in one go, draw and inject in order, drop everything in the sharps container. Give each compound its own quadrant per session (upper-L, upper-R, lower-L, lower-R, outer thigh) so you can keep tracking simple and sites can recover.

Tip 04 — When to Hold a Dose
Skipping a Day Costs Nothing

Hold Reta or Cagri if any of the following: persistent nausea / vomiting beyond 24 hours, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to back — rule out pancreatitis), severe constipation lasting > 3 days, severe injection-site reaction beyond mild redness.

Hold the entire AM stack if you wake up feeling actively unwell, fluish, or with unexplained tachycardia. Skipping a day costs nothing; pushing through a real signal can.

Continue working with your physician throughout this protocol — particularly given your current Lisinopril (blood pressure) and Test prescriptions. Discontinue and seek medical attention if adverse reactions occur.

Tip 05 — What to Expect · First Two Weeks
Common Side Effects · The Ramp Is Built to Minimize Them

Common Reta + Cagri side effects: nausea, decreased appetite, fatigue, occasional injection-site irritation. The cycle is built to ramp gradually to minimize these — full-dose Reta at 1 mg is already conservative.

Mild nausea on Reta shot days for the first 1–2 weeks is normal. Hydrate aggressively. Front-load protein early in the day — appetite drops fastest in the second half. Slightly looser stools are common — they normalize by end of Week 2.
