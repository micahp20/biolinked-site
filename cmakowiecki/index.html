Tip 01 — The 12-Week Tesa Rule
Why Tesa Doesn't "Work" Until Week 8–12

Tesa is a slow build. The full 12 weeks is non-negotiable. Tesamorelin is a GHRH analog — it signals the pituitary to release the body's own growth hormone in a natural pulsatile pattern. The visceral fat reduction, lean mass support, and body comp changes are downstream of a slow IGF-1 ramp that takes weeks to register.

What the timeline actually looks like:

· Weeks 1–2: sleep often gets noticeably deeper. Mild tingling in fingers/toes is common — resolves on its own. No body comp changes yet.
· Weeks 3–6: IGF-1 ramps. You might notice improved recovery from workouts, slightly better skin quality, more vivid dreams. Visceral fat hasn't moved yet — this is normal.
· Weeks 7–10: visceral fat starts shifting. Waist measurement may tighten 0.5–1 inch. Lean mass holds well during the Reta-driven calorie deficit. Recovery and sleep stay strong.
· Weeks 11–12: body comp changes are visible. The "tighter" look in the midsection that pure scale-weight loss doesn't deliver.

You're already running Tesa from another source — that's exactly the right move. This $45 BioLinked vial slots in as one ~2-week piece of that full 12-week run. The only thing that matters is no gaps in the M–F dosing across the full 12 weeks. Stopping and restarting resets the IGF-1 ramp and you lose the runway.

Don't evaluate Tesa at Week 4. Don't evaluate at Week 6. The honest assessment window is Weeks 10–12.

Tip 02 — Reta Onboarding
What the First Few Weeks of Reta Look Like

Week 1: appetite suppression starts within 3–5 days. Mild nausea or "full quickly" feeling on shot days (Mon/Wed/Fri) is normal — manageable with smaller meals and lower fat content for 24 hrs after the shot. Skip greasy / fried food the day after a dose day. Hydrate aggressively.

Week 2–3: initial GI side effects settle. Appetite control feels like baseline. Food noise quiets noticeably. Some mild fatigue is normal as the body acclimates to the deficit.

Weeks 4–6: fat loss curve takes hold. Aim for 1–1.5 lbs/wk — faster than 2 lbs/wk sustained means the deficit is too aggressive (or you're losing more water than fat).

Past Week 6: the 20 mg Reta vial will be running low. Reach out when you're 2 weeks from empty so the next vial is queued up — don't let Reta gap out mid-Tesa cycle.

Tip 03 — Stacking the Two Shots on M/W/F
How to Run Tesa + Reta on the Same Morning

Order: Tesa first (fasted), then Reta a few minutes after. Doesn't matter beyond that — the order is just for routine consistency.

Two different injection sites: don't stack both shots in the same spot. Tesa in one abdominal quadrant, Reta in the opposite. Rotate quadrants daily so no single site gets hit twice in a week.

Timing: both shots before eating. Wait ~30–45 minutes after the Tesa shot before food, then eat normally. Reta doesn't care about fasted vs fed — the fasted timing is only for Tesa's GH pulse.

Coffee: black coffee before the shots is fine. No sugar, no cream, no protein shakes — anything that spikes insulin blunts the Tesa-driven GH pulse.

Tip 04 — Hydration on Reta
The Thing Most People Underestimate

Target: 2.5–3 L of water per day. Reta suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite — you won't feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated. That's how people end up with headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor energy and blame "the peptide" when it's actually water deficit.

Add electrolytes once daily. Sodium, potassium, magnesium. LMNT, Liquid IV, or similar work fine.

Coffee doesn't count. Drink it on top of your water target, not instead of.

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

Reta (M/W/F): if you miss a shot day, take it within 2 days of the missed day and resume the original schedule. If more than 2 days have passed, skip that dose and resume next scheduled day. Don't double up.

Tesa (M–F): miss one day, skip it — don't double up the next morning. Two missed days in a row across the 12-week run is fine; more than that and the cycle's continuity starts to slip. Stay consistent.

Tip 06 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold the next Reta dose if any of the following:

· Persistent nausea or vomiting that doesn't resolve within 24 hours
· Severe abdominal pain, especially radiating to the back — rule out pancreatitis
· Gallbladder-area pain or RUQ pain after fatty meals
· Rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week sustained — deficit is too aggressive, eat more
· Lightheadedness or dizziness on standing — could be blood sugar or dehydration; hydrate, eat, monitor

Hold the next Tesa dose if:

· Persistent fluid retention / puffy face or hands beyond Week 2
· Sustained tingling or numbness in fingers/toes past Week 3 (mild tingling Week 1–2 is normal)
· Carpal tunnel-type symptoms — wrist pain, hand weakness
· Joint pain or stiffness that wasn't there before

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through and don't restart on your own.
