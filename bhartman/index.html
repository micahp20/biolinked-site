Tip 01 — The Gut-Healing Science
Why BPC-157 + KLOW Together Is the Right Combo

The gut lining is a single cell thick. That one layer of enterocytes separates your gut contents (food, microbes, microbial products) from your bloodstream. When that layer breaks down — from stress, NSAIDs, alcohol, gut dysbiosis, autoimmune patterns, surgery, or just chronic inflammation — you get "leaky gut": bacterial endotoxins, undigested food particles, and microbial signals cross into systemic circulation. The downstream effects are everything from food sensitivities and brain fog to autoimmune flares and chronic systemic inflammation.

BPC-157 is the most-studied peptide for rebuilding that layer. Three documented mechanisms in the GI tract specifically:

1. Mucosal regeneration. BPC accelerates the natural turnover of intestinal epithelial cells — the cells that line your gut. Faster turnover = damaged cells get replaced more quickly = the barrier rebuilds itself.
2. Angiogenesis at sites of GI damage. BPC drives new blood vessel growth wherever there's tissue injury. More blood flow = more oxygen and nutrients delivered to healing tissue = repair happens faster.
3. Anti-ulcer and anti-erosion effects. Strongest peptide data for stomach, small intestine, and colon ulcer healing in research models. Used in clinical contexts for IBD, ulcers, and post-surgical anastomosis repair.

KLOW's role in the gut-healing picture: KPV (the K in KLOW) is a dedicated anti-inflammatory leg that works partly through NF-κB downregulation in the gut, supporting barrier function from a different angle. GHK-Cu and TB-500 round out the tissue-repair context. Together with BPC, you've got the angiogenesis layer + the cellular regeneration layer + the anti-inflammatory layer all pointed at the same target.

The combined effect: faster gut-lining repair than either alone, lower systemic inflammation, better tolerance for foods that were problematic before, and over the 8-week window measurable improvement in chronic GI symptoms if any are present.

Tip 02 — Week-by-Week Timeline
What to Expect from the Gut-Healing Cycle

Weeks 1–2: baseline learning curve — getting the AM and PM injection routine locked in. KLOW effects are subtle early on; BPC may produce a slight feeling of improved digestion or reduced post-meal bloating in the first 1–2 weeks. Some people notice nothing at all yet — that's normal too.

Weeks 3–5: the gut-repair signal compounds. If there are existing GI symptoms (bloating, IBS-pattern issues, food sensitivities, post-meal discomfort), they should start trending better here. Skin and connective tissue benefits from KLOW also start showing — clearer skin, better joint comfort, faster recovery from any minor injuries.

Weeks 6–8: the deeper benefits land. Gut-barrier integrity improves on the cellular level — tighter junctions, less endotoxin translocation, lower systemic inflammation. Foods that were problematic may become tolerable again. Energy holds steadier because the inflammation tax on the body is lower.

End of week 8: decision point. Run another KLOW vial cycle or rotate to a different healing approach. BPC standalone can typically be continued at lower dose (e.g. 3×/week) as ongoing maintenance.

Tip 03 — AM vs PM Timing
Why KLOW Is AM and BPC Is PM

KLOW AM: the blend includes GHK-Cu which has some mild stimulatory effect on cellular activity. AM dosing puts that signal in the body when you want energy and tissue activity high. Doesn't strongly prefer AM but PM dosing for KLOW can occasionally interfere with sleep onset for sensitive people.

BPC PM: the body's repair pathways are most active during sleep — growth hormone pulses, cellular regeneration, gut motility resets. Dosing BPC pre-bed puts the repair signal in the body right when those overnight pathways are most receptive. Specifically for gut work, the gut does most of its own repair overnight while the digestive system is quieter.

Practical: KLOW with morning routine (coffee, etc.), BPC within the last 2 hours before sleep. Food doesn't matter much for either — both tolerate full or empty stomach.

Tip 04 — Worth Considering for the Headaches
SS-31 & Cerebrolysin — The Migraine / Chronic Headache Layer

You mentioned headaches as something you're dealing with — this stack as built doesn't directly address that, but two peptides target the underlying biology of chronic headaches and migraines specifically. Worth raising as something to consider adding now or in your next cycle.

SS-31 (Elamipretide) — the mitochondrial layer. Modern migraine research has converged on mitochondrial dysfunction as one of the core upstream mechanisms. When neurons can't generate energy efficiently — particularly in the cortex and brainstem — you get the cortical excitability that triggers migraines. SS-31 targets the inner mitochondrial membrane specifically, restoring electron transport efficiency and reducing reactive oxygen species. Multiple clinical trials in mitochondrial disease populations show improvements in headache burden. For chronic headaches with a metabolic / fatigue component, this is the most mechanistically targeted peptide available.

Cerebrolysin — the neurotrophic / neuroinflammation layer. A mixture of neurotrophic peptide fragments that mimic BDNF, NGF, and GDNF. Used clinically in Europe since the 1970s for stroke recovery, TBI, and cognitive decline. The relevant angle for chronic headaches: Cerebrolysin reduces neuroinflammation and supports neural repair pathways that get chronically activated in migraine and tension-type headache patterns. Effective for patterns where the headaches feel tied to stress, mental fatigue, or post-illness fog.

How they'd layer on: SS-31 is a typical M–F AM SubQ peptide, similar workflow to KLOW. Cerebrolysin runs as an intensive (M–F for ~30 days at 10 mg/day, then reassess). Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

KLOW (M–F): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across a week doesn't move the needle.

BPC-157 (daily PM): if you miss a PM dose, you can take it AM the next day instead and resume PM the day after. The gut-healing signal benefits from consistency, but a one-day shift isn't a problem. Missing 2–3 days in a row is when the compounding effect on gut-lining repair starts to thin out — try to keep it daily.

Tip 06 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold KLOW or BPC if any of the following:

· Significant injection-site reaction (redness, swelling, persistent pain past 24 hours)
· New or worsening allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes) — both KLOW and BPC are well-tolerated, but individual reactions can happen
· Any worsening of GI symptoms past week 2 — if BPC is making things worse instead of better, that's a flag, not a temporary side effect

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through — we adjust the plan based on what's actually happening.
