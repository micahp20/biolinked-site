Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) HGH: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze. Never shake.

Reconstituted HGH: most stable for ~3–4 weeks in the fridge; potency degrades after that. At ~7 weeks per bottle (the rate this protocol uses), the back half of each bottle is past peak stability — see the HGH Stability tip in Notes & Tips for how to manage this.

Reta: store dry-vial refrigerated. Once reconstituted, use within 30 days.

Syringe discipline: fresh U-100 insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse. Wipe stopper + injection site with alcohol pad every time.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Execution · Tier-Down · What to Watch For
Tip 01 — The Reta Tier-Down Protocol (Critical)
Phase Down As Bodyweight Stabilizes

GLP-1s are easy to over-rely on. Once Chris hits or approaches 205, the body's metabolic adaptation is what needs to take over — not the drug. Tier the dose down as you close in on goal weight to preserve lean mass and let the metabolism re-adapt to maintenance.

Tip 02 — HGH Timing
AM Fasted, or Pre-Bed on Off Days

Default: AM on an empty stomach, ideally 30–45 min before food. HGH triggers the IGF-1 cascade; insulin blunts it, so any food (especially carbs) within an hour of the shot dampens the effect.

On training rest days, a pre-bed shot is fine and may actually deepen sleep slightly — same fasted rule, 2+ hrs after the last meal. Pick one slot per day and stay consistent.

Tip 03 — HGH Stability (Heads Up)
Pace the Bottles — You're Stretching the Window

Reconstituted HGH is most stable for the first 3–4 weeks in the fridge. After that, potency degrades. Each vial in this protocol covers ~7 weeks (36 IU ÷ 5 IU/wk), which means the back half of each bottle is past peak stability.

What this means in practice:

1. Use bacteriostatic water for reconstitution (not sterile water) — the benzyl alcohol preservative is what makes the longer storage window possible.
2. Keep it cold. Back of the fridge, not the door. Never leave at room temp.
3. Don't shake. Ever. Roll or swirl gently to mix. Shaking shears the protein.
4. Expect a small dropoff in week 5–7 of each vial — subjective: slightly less of the deep-sleep effect, slightly less recovery boost. Not catastrophic, but real. You can shorten this by going to 2 vials at a time with shorter recon-to-empty windows (reconstitute one, use it within 4 weeks, then the next) if you want maximum potency.

Tip 04 — Reta Onboarding
No Titration Needed, But Expect Some GI

Starting at 1 mg M/W/F is fine for an athletic 215 lb male — no need for the slow titration that smaller users or first-time GLP-1 patients require. Body mass + metabolic robustness handle this dose cleanly.

Expect mild GI in weeks 1–2: some nausea, occasional reflux, early satiety, possibly constipation. These usually settle by week 3. Eat smaller, slower meals, lower fat content early on. If nausea is persistent or severe past week 2, drop to 1 mg twice/week briefly and ramp back up.

Red flags (call a clinician): persistent vomiting, severe abdominal pain (especially radiating to the back — pancreatitis risk), gallbladder symptoms.

Tip 05 — Hydration
3–4 L Water/Day on Reta — Non-Negotiable

Reta suppresses thirst signaling alongside appetite. You will not feel thirsty even when you're dehydrated — that's how people end up with headaches, constipation, fatigue, and poor performance, then blame "the peptide" when it's just water deficit.

Target: 3–4 L/day across shifts and training. Add electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) once a day, ideally pre-training or mid-shift. Coffee doesn't count.

Tip 06 — Sleep Hygiene
HGH Pays Off Most During Deep Sleep

The biggest dividends from HGH show up during slow-wave sleep — that's when the GH/IGF-1 axis does most of its recovery work. Protect the first 3 hours of sleep aggressively.

Tactics for shift workers: blackout curtains, cold room (~65°F), no screens 30 min before bed, magnesium glycinate 400 mg before lights out, and protect a consistent 7–8 hr window whenever the schedule allows. Stacking HGH on top of bad sleep is a partial waste — the compound multiplies the recovery you give it room to do.

Tip 07 — Signals
Signs the Protocol Is Working

Weeks 1–2: appetite drops noticeably (Reta kicking in). Some GI noise. HGH effects subtle at first — maybe deeper sleep, maybe a hand-tingling/water retention sensation in the mornings (normal, resolves).

Weeks 3–6: steady weight loss, ~1–2 lbs/week. Recovery from training feels faster. Skin clearer. Sleep deeper. The mirror starts moving even when the scale plateaus a bit.

Months 2–4: bodyweight approaching 205–208. Time to think about Phase 2 of the tier-down. Lean tissue holding (waist shrinking but shoulders/arms staying full). Joint feeling better than it has in years.

Months 5–7: goal weight held with reduced Reta dose. HGH still doing recovery work. The recomp is real — not just lighter, but denser.

Yellow flags — reassess: rapid weight loss above 3 lbs/week (means too aggressive a deficit; eat more), strength dropping noticeably in the gym (lean mass loss; consider tier-down + more protein), persistent fatigue, or sleep getting worse instead of better.

Tip 08 — The CJC/Ipamorelin Bridge-Off (Phase 2)
What to Expect After HGH Ends

Once HGH supply ends at week 16, start CJC/Ipamorelin the following Monday (week 17). M–F, pre-bed, on an empty stomach (2+ hrs after last meal). 500 mcg = 10 units per shot.

Why this matters: 6–7 months of exogenous HGH partially suppresses the pituitary's own GH output. If you stop HGH cold with nothing in its place, you'll feel a recovery and sleep dip for 2–4 weeks while your axis wakes back up. CJC/Ipa amplifies the natural GH pulses your pituitary can still make and lengthens the release window — bridging you cleanly back to native production.

Expectations across the bridge:

Weeks 1–2 (of bridge-off): sleep stays good or improves — the pre-bed shot often deepens the first sleep cycle. Recovery from training holds.
Weeks 3–5: any HGH-related water retention (if you had any) clears. Body composition holds; you're not adding fat back.
Weeks 6–8: CJC/Ipa starts to taper in effect — your own GH axis should be doing more of the work by now. By the end of week 23 you can stop the bridge cleanly.

The fasting window is non-negotiable. Food — especially carbs and fats — spikes blood glucose and blunts the GH pulse this peptide is designed to amplify. If you ate within 2 hours of bedtime, skip the dose and resume the next night.

Yellow flags: If recovery drops noticeably or sleep falls apart in weeks 1–2 of the bridge-off, that usually means you need to be stricter on the fasting window or the pre-bed timing. Adjust before reaching for anything else.
