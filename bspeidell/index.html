What to Expect — Week by Week
Weeks 1–2
Initial phase. You may feel more tired than usual as your body begins clearing accumulated oxidative stress. Sleep may deepen noticeably. Some users notice mood improvement by end of week 2 as NAD+ begins restoring dopamine pathways. Mild flushing with NAD+ is normal — slow your injection speed.
Weeks 3–4
Cognitive clarity starts emerging. Working memory, verbal fluency, and focus begin sharpening — critical for high-pressure performance. Physical energy improves as the mitochondrial compounds take full hold. This is when most clients first notice a meaningful shift in how they feel under pressure.
Weeks 5–6
Peak protocol window. Most clients report their best cognitive performance during this phase. Anxiety under pressure should be noticeably lower. You may also notice improved sleep architecture, better resilience to stress, and less reliance on stimulants to perform at your best.
Weeks 7–8
Consolidation phase. The neurological gains from weeks 3–6 begin to stabilize. Results should feel natural and integrated. End-of-cycle check: How is sleep? Focus? Stress tolerance? This determines whether a repeat cycle or maintenance protocol makes sense going forward.
Storage & Handling

Before reconstitution (dry powder)

Store all vials in the refrigerator at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep away from light and moisture. Do not freeze. Shelf life is typically 12–24 months unopened.

After reconstitution

All reconstituted vials must be refrigerated. Use within 30 days (NAD+ within 14 days for best potency). Never freeze a reconstituted peptide. Always use a fresh insulin syringe for each draw.

Bacteriostatic water (BAC water)

Use sterile BAC water for all five injectable compounds in this protocol. BAC water's benzyl alcohol preservative extends shelf life of reconstituted peptides. Do not substitute plain sterile water.

Syringes & injection sites

Use 29–31 gauge insulin syringes (0.5 mL or 1 mL). Never reuse a syringe. Dispose in a sharps container. Rotate injection sites daily — abdomen, outer thigh, and glute all work well.

What to Monitor
Normal: Mild flushing or warmth with NAD+ — slow injection speed to 30–60 seconds. Resolves after week 1–2.
Normal: Small injection site redness or minor bruising — rotate sites daily and use proper SubQ technique.
Normal: Vivid dreams or slightly disrupted sleep in week 1 as your nervous system begins recalibrating.
Monitor: Persistent headaches beyond week 2 — reduce Semax dose by half and contact BioLinked Solutions.
Monitor: Worsening anxiety or significant mood swings — pause Semax, continue Selank, reach out to your consultant.
Monitor: Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — may indicate technique issue or contamination. Do not reuse that vial.
