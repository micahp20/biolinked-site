Two-block architecture. The Sequential Block runs three compounds in order — FOX04-DRI (senolytic flush, ~5 days) → Cerebrolysin (neurogenic repair, 4 weeks) → Cortagen (cortical regulatory regeneration, 4 weeks). The Concurrent Block layers 5 metabolic + recovery peptides on top for 8 weeks (MOTS-c, Humanin, CJC/Ipa, Melanotan I, GHK-Cu). Total cycle 9 weeks.

Concurrent block starts Day 1 of Cerebrolysin (Mon of Week 2). FOX04 runs solo for the first 5 days so the senolytic flush isn't competing with growth-signaling peptides for cellular bandwidth — clean senolytic phase, then full stack from Week 2 forward. Cerebrolysin and Cortagen hand off cleanly at the Wk 5/6 boundary; the concurrent block runs the full Wks 2–9 span, ending exactly when Cortagen does.

Same protocol, same dosing, both of you. Two people, identical math — just rotate injection sites independently and don't share vials/syringes. Supply tally below covers per person; double everything for the couple's total.

Protocol Overview — How the Two Blocks Work Together
Sequential Block — Senolytic → Neurogenic → Regenerative
The three sequential compounds run in clinical order, not arbitrary order. FOX04-DRI clears senescent cells first (5 days, 3 EOD doses) so the regenerative phase isn't fighting inflammatory zombie-cell signaling. Cerebrolysin drives the actual regrowth (6 weeks, M–F) by mimicking the brain's own neurotrophic factors. Cortagen locks the gains in (4 weeks, M–F) by regulating how the new neural tissue organizes itself. Skipping any stage compromises the next.
Concurrent Block — Metabolic + Recovery Support Layer
Five peptides running alongside the sequential block (Days 6–62, ~Wks 2–9). MOTS-c + Humanin upgrade mitochondrial output so the cellular ATP demand of the regenerative work has the energy to meet it. CJC/Ipa drives nightly GH pulses that amplify the tissue-repair signal. Melanotan I handles dermal pigmentation + minor mood/libido lift. GHK-Cu drives systemic recovery + skin/connective tissue + hair quality. Concurrent block ends 2 weeks before the sequential block does, leaving the last 2 weeks for Cortagen alone to finalize neural regulation.
Why the two blocks together produce more than the sum of parts: The sequential block does the upstream work — clear the deadwood, regrow the neural infrastructure, regulate the regrowth. The concurrent block makes sure the cells have resource bandwidth for that work to actually happen: clean ATP (MOTS-c + Humanin), nightly GH pulses (CJC/Ipa), reduced inflammation (GHK-Cu), and cosmetic/skin support (Melanotan I + GHK-Cu). A protocol of this depth running on a depleted system would stall by Week 4. Running on an upgraded metabolic + recovery layer, the brain + body have what they need to fully respond.

End-state by Week 11: Cleared senescent cell burden, regrown + organized neural tissue (cognitive sharpness, mood stability, energy floor), upgraded mitochondrial output, improved skin/hair/joint quality, and (notably for the couple) a hormonal + recovery layer that compounds across both partners simultaneously.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Labeled · Don't Cross-Contaminate
❄ Refrigerated, Labeled, Two Sets

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze. Cerebrolysin specifically: do not ship at room temp for >48 hrs.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Fresh syringe for each draw — never reuse.

Two-person labeling: tape "M" or "C" on every vial top with a sharpie at recon time. Each person draws only from their own vial. Never share vials — cross-contamination risk + ruins the dose math.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Cerebrolysin IM rotates between deltoids + vastus lateralis. With 5–6 shots/day during Wks 2–9, plan rotations the night before to avoid stacking sites.

Tip 01 — cpauldino Baseline Labs (Strong Recommendation)
Pre-Protocol Draw Before Day 1 If No Recent Panel

mpauldino has an established lab cadence from previous protocols. cpauldino should have a baseline draw before Day 1 if there's no recent panel — different physiology means different risks, particularly with Melanotan (skin/mole response) and GHK-Cu (copper status / hormonal interplay).

Recommended pre-protocol panel for cpauldino:

  • CBC + CMP (baseline cell counts, kidney/liver/electrolytes)
  • hs-CRP (inflammation baseline before FOX04 senolytic)
  • Sensitive E2 + FSH + LH (hormonal baseline)
  • TSH + Free T4 + Free T3 (thyroid baseline)
  • IGF-1 (baseline before CJC/Ipa GH amplification)
  • Ferritin + B12 + Vitamin D (energy + recovery base)
  • Lipid panel

If results show anything outside reference range, flag before starting. Particularly: low ferritin → fatigue will dominate the experience; hypothyroid → MOTS-c response will be muted; elevated CRP → may want to wait until inflammation settles before FOX04.

Tip 02 — Red Flags · Stop and Call Physician
Both of You · Treat These as Immediate Stops

Stop the protocol and contact a physician for any of:

  • Fever or severe malaise >72 hrs after FOX04 dose — normal senolytic flush is 24–48 hrs; longer = abnormal
  • Chest pain, palpitations, or sustained resting HR >15 bpm above baseline — could be Cerebrolysin or fluid shift response
  • New mole changes or rapid darkening (Melanotan side effect) — dermatology referral, same day
  • Severe headache or vision changes (Cerebrolysin / CJC GH-related)
  • Persistent jaundice, RUQ pain, or dark urine (hepatic stress)
  • Unexplained rash or swelling (Cerebrolysin or Cortagen hypersensitivity)
  • Mood/cognitive shifts beyond mild brain fog (Cerebrolysin can occasionally trigger dysphoria or insomnia — flag if persistent)

For mpauldino-specific TRT / hormone interactions: hormonal panel mid-cycle (Wk 8) is the existing cadence — flag any new symptoms vs. prior baselines.

Tip 03 — FOX04 Flush Window (Days 1–7)
Don't Schedule the Week of Senolytic

The first week of this protocol — FOX04 EOD on Days 1, 3, 5 — produces predictable systemic effects. Mild flu-like malaise, low-grade fever, fatigue, possibly mild GI for 24–48 hrs post-dose is the senolytic flush. It's the body clearing dying cells. Not side effects, not a failure — signal.

Do not schedule big work, training, travel, or social events during Days 1–7. Hydrate 3+ L/day, light easily-digested food, no caffeine reliance, no training. Sleep more than usual.

By Day 8–10, the flush resolves and the body resets cleaner than before. Then Day 12 (start of Cerebrolysin) the regenerative phase begins on a clean baseline.

Tip 07 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

  • Flu-like malaise Days 1–7 from FOX04 — resolves by Day 8
  • Mild flushing 5–10 min after MOTS-c (AMPK vascular response)
  • Vivid dreams first 1–2 weeks of CJC/Ipa — deeper sleep showing up
  • Mild tingling extremities first nights of CJC/Ipa — GH fluid shift
  • Even tan developing by Wk 4–5 from Melanotan + sun exposure
  • Improved energy + cognitive clarity by mid-Wk 3 from Cerebrolysin

Monitor (contact BioLinked / physician):

  • Any red-flag symptom in Tip 02 — immediate stop
  • Persistent insomnia past Wk 2 of CJC/Ipa — may shift timing earlier
  • Mood dysphoria past Wk 2 of Cerebrolysin — rare but documented; may reduce dose
  • Any mole that changes shape, color, or starts growing — dermatology, immediate
  • Joint pain or new inflammation despite GHK-Cu — may flag autoimmune flare
