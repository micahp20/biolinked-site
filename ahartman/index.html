Protocol Overview — How This Stack Works Together
KLOW — The Systemic Foundation
Kpv + GHK-Cu + BPC-157 + TB-500 blend that creates a systemic anti-inflammatory and repair environment — reduces gut inflammation, heals connective tissue, supports skin and vascular health, lowers oxidative load. KLOW sets the terrain. You do not upregulate mitochondrial activity inside an inflamed system, so KLOW runs all 8 weeks as the backbone underneath everything else.
SS-31 (Elamipretide) — Repair the Engine
Mitochondrial-targeted peptide that binds cardiolipin on the inner mitochondrial membrane. Stabilizes the electron transport chain and dramatically reduces ROS leakage from damaged mitochondria. Servicing the engine — fixing the broken, leaky machinery so it stops producing oxidative damage. Runs weeks 1–4 only; finishes before MOTS-C begins.
MOTS-C — Optimize the Engine
Mitochondrial-derived peptide that activates AMPK, mimics the metabolic effects of exercise, improves insulin sensitivity, and drives mitochondrial biogenesis — the creation of new mitochondria. Tuning the engine for performance and building more of them, once the existing ones are running clean. Runs weeks 5–8 only.
Retatrutide — The Metabolic Amplifier
Triple agonist (GLP-1 / GIP / glucagon) that drives fat loss, improves insulin sensitivity, and reduces visceral adiposity. Operates from a different angle than the mitochondrial peptides but feeds the same downstream result: less inflammation (complements KLOW), sharper insulin signaling (amplifies MOTS-C and SS-31). Better terrain for mitochondrial work. Client-managed; runs throughout.
5-Amino-1MQ — The NAD+ Lever
NNMT inhibitor that blocks the enzyme depleting NAD+, raising intracellular NAD+ availability — the critical cofactor for mitochondrial energy production. While MOTS-C and SS-31 fix and optimize the machinery, 5-Amino raises the fuel currency that machinery runs on. Pre-workout: sharpens performance, supports lean mass retention during fat loss.
Stack Synergy — Why SS-31 Before MOTS-C: Running MOTS-C on damaged, ROS-leaking mitochondria is like flooring the gas pedal on a car with a blown head gasket — you amplify output, but you also amplify the damage. The sequencing is intentional: SS-31 first establishes mitochondrial integrity by repairing the existing fleet. MOTS-C then drives those healthy mitochondria harder and stimulates the creation of new ones. The order respects the repair → optimize hierarchy. KLOW runs underneath the entire cycle so neither peptide is fighting an inflamed systemic environment. Reta and 5-Amino layer in around this core sequence — Reta as the metabolic backdrop, 5-Amino as the on-demand performance lever.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days. Reconstitute one vial of each compound at a time — don't start the 30-day clock on a second vial until the first is close to empty.

Syringe discipline: fresh U-100 insulin syringe for every shot. Never reuse.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Protocol Notes
Execution · Mindset · Signals
Tip 01 — Fasted State
Mitochondrial Peptides Need a Truly Empty Stomach

KLOW, SS-31, and MOTS-C are all dosed in the AM fasted cluster — not casually fasted, but actually empty. No food, no calories, no coffee with cream, no protein, no fruit, no gum. Black coffee and water are fine; everything else breaks the state.

Wait at least 30–45 minutes after injection before eating. The peptides need a clean absorption window without competing signals from incoming glucose, amino acids, or fat.

Tip 02 — Why It Matters
Why Carbs/Calories Blunt the Effect

Eating — especially carbs — spikes insulin. Insulin shifts the cell into storage and synthesis mode, which is the opposite of the AMPK-activated, autophagy-promoting, oxidative-repair state these mitochondrial peptides are designed to drive.

SS-31 working on cardiolipin and MOTS-C activating AMPK both happen most cleanly in a low-insulin environment. Inject fasted, stay fasted ~30 minutes, then eat. Skip this and you're paying for a blunted version of the effect.

Tip 03 — Sequencing
Why Phasing Matters — Don't Stack SS-31 + MOTS-C

It's tempting to think "more is better" and run both mitochondrial peptides at once. Don't. SS-31 stabilizes and repairs existing mitochondria; MOTS-C drives them harder and triggers the creation of new ones. Doing both simultaneously means MOTS-C is forcing demand on a fleet that's still being repaired — you blunt SS-31's repair window and amplify oxidative load.

The phased structure (4 weeks repair → 4 weeks optimize) is the whole point of this stack. Resist the urge to overlap.

Tip 04 — Signals
Signs the Protocol Is Working

Weeks 1–2: not much subjectively yet. KLOW + SS-31 are doing background repair work that doesn't always feel like anything — you may notice less generalized inflammation (joints, gut, skin).

Weeks 3–4 (end of Phase 1): deeper sleep, more stable energy through the day, fewer afternoon crashes. Recovery from workouts noticeably faster. Skin clearer.

Weeks 5–6 (early Phase 2): MOTS-C kicks in — expect a noticeable lift in workout output, better endurance, faster glucose disposal after meals. Body composition starts shifting more visibly here.

Weeks 7–8: the cumulative effect — sustained energy, improved cardio capacity, leaner appearance, sharper cognition. The point is not a single dramatic moment but a quiet shift in how your body runs day-to-day.

Red flags — pause and reassess: persistent headaches, sustained fatigue (vs. the brief week 1–2 adjustment), GI distress that doesn't resolve, or unusual heart rate changes. Mitochondrial peptides are well-tolerated, but listen to signal over plan.

Tip 05 — Storage
Storage & Handling
