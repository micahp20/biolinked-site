This is the compound that addresses the fat cells directly, while the other four compounds address your appetite, mitochondria, insulin sensitivity, and GH axis. Dosed 3 days per week in the morning.

Storage & Handling

Before reconstitution (dry powder)

Store all vials in the refrigerator at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep away from light and moisture. Do not freeze. Shelf life is typically 12–24 months unopened. Label each vial clearly — Retatrutide, SLU-PP-332, MOTS-C, Tesamorelin, and 5-Amino-1MQ.

After reconstitution

All reconstituted vials must be refrigerated. Use all compounds within 30 days of reconstitution. Never freeze. Reconstitute only what you'll use within 30 days — if you stagger reconstitution, you extend effective shelf life of your supply.

Bacteriostatic (BAC) water

Use sterile BAC water for all five compounds. BAC water's benzyl alcohol preservative extends shelf life to 30 days. Do not substitute plain sterile water — it does not preserve reconstituted peptides and they'll break down faster.

Syringes & injection sites

Use 29–31 gauge insulin syringes (0.5 mL or 1 mL barrel). Never reuse a syringe. Dispose in a sharps container. Rotate injection sites every day — abdomen, outer thigh, upper arm. Morning stack (SLU + MOTS + 5-Amino-1MQ + Retatrutide on dose days) can all go abdomen; Tesamorelin PM use a different site.

What to Monitor
Normal: Mild nausea with Retatrutide in weeks 1–2 — take first thing in the morning and eat a small meal within 30 min. Avoid heavy or fatty food on injection days.
Normal: Significant appetite reduction — this is the desired effect of Retatrutide. Focus on hitting protein target (0.7–1g per lb goal bodyweight) even when not hungry.
Normal: Deeper sleep and vivid dreams in week 1 from Tesamorelin — this is the GH pulse doing its job. Quality of rest improves over time.
Normal: Slight injection site redness or bruising — rotate sites daily. Use short 29–31G pins and inject slowly at 45°.
Monitor: Persistent nausea/vomiting beyond week 2 with Retatrutide — hold at 10 units, do not increase. Take with food. Contact BioLinked if severe.
Monitor: Joint swelling, carpal-tunnel-like symptoms, or water retention — may be GH axis response to Tesamorelin. Reduce to 3×/week and monitor.
Monitor: Extreme fatigue or blood sugar lows — possible interaction of Retatrutide + metabolic peptides. Eat small meal with morning stack. Report if ongoing.
Monitor: Elevated heart rate or palpitations — uncommon but possible with aggressive fat loss stacks. Reduce or pause and reach out to BioLinked.
