Tip 01 — Why The Aggressive Build (BPC + TB + 8 Weeks)
The Science Behind "Better and Longer"

The reason the shoulder hasn't healed isn't lack of damage repair capacity — it's a stuck signal. Soft-tissue injuries in adults heal in three stages: inflammatory (the body sends repair signals to the damaged area), proliferative (new cells and blood vessels form), and remodeling (tissue rebuilds and strengthens). When a shoulder injury becomes chronic, it usually means one of those stages has stalled — most commonly chronic low-grade inflammation that's blocking the proliferative stage from kicking in, plus age-related slowdown in tissue turnover.

Why this aggressive build is "better" than a single-peptide approach. The original Matt protocol used BPC-only at the shoulder for ~10 days. That hits one mechanism (angiogenesis + anti-inflammatory) for a short window. This build runs the Wolverine Stack (BPC + TB-500 blended) at the shoulder for a full 4 weeks. Two different mechanisms, same target:

1. BPC-157 at the site — angiogenesis + anti-inflammatory. Drives new blood vessel growth at the injury (more oxygen and nutrients delivered to healing tissue) and calms the chronic inflammation that's been blocking repair.
2. TB-500 at the site — cell migration + actin assembly + anti-scarring. Thymosin Beta-4 fragment that drives cell migration to the injury, supports the actin scaffolding needed for tissue regeneration, and reduces fibrotic scar tissue formation. Different pathway from BPC; complementary effect.
3. KLOW systemic context — whole-body healing. KPV (anti-inflammatory + NF-κB downregulation), GHK-Cu (collagen synthesis), TB-500 (tissue regeneration), low-dose BPC. Runs M–F AM for the full 8 weeks — gives the body a continuous signal to repair across every tissue, not just the shoulder.

Why this build is "longer" — 2 KLOW vials = 8 weeks. The 4–6 week mark is when most clients feel "noticeably better." But soft-tissue remodeling (the third repair stage) doesn't fully consolidate until ~6–8 weeks. The original 4-week cycle stopped right when the body was ready to lock in the gains. The 8-week cycle gives the tissue the runway to actually finish remodeling — the difference between "feels good for a while then comes back" and "actually healed."

The combined effect: the stuck inflammatory signal gets calmed, the dual-mechanism at-site stack drives faster tissue repair with less scar formation, and the longer systemic runway lets the remodeling fully consolidate. This is the stack to run when the shoulder needs to actually get healed, not just feel better.

Tip 02 — Week-by-Week Timeline
What to Expect from the 8-Week Cycle

Week 1: baseline learning curve — getting the AM (KLOW abdomen) and PM (Wolverine at shoulder) injection routine locked in. Effects are subtle the first 5–7 days. Don't push through painful range of motion; let the compounds work.

Week 2: the shoulder should start to feel noticeably less inflamed. Range of motion typically improves first — the joint moves easier before the deep ache fully resolves. This is the inflammation-clearing window.

Weeks 3–4 (Wolverine still running): meaningful improvement lands. Pain at rest drops substantially. Specific movements that triggered sharp pain start feeling less reactive. Most clients describe this as "the shoulder is actually getting better" rather than just being inflamed. Wolverine vials empty at end of Week 4.

Weeks 5–6 (KLOW alone, deeper remodeling): the systemic healing signal continues. Deeper tendon and ligament repair is happening on its slower 4–6 week timeline. Shoulder may feel like progress has plateaued visually, but the deep tissue work is the main thing happening here. Don't stop just because the dramatic improvements slow down — this is the consolidation window.

Weeks 7–8 (final remodeling): the tissue locks in. Strength returns to the shoulder. Full range of motion typically restored by end of Week 8. By cycle end, most clients describe the shoulder as "functional again" or fully healed — no longer the limiting factor in daily life.

End of cycle: decision point. If improvement is complete, no further protocol needed — the remodeling continues for another 4–6 weeks post-cycle on its own. If there's still residual issue, BPC + TB ongoing at 3×/wk maintenance or another KLOW cycle is an option.

Tip 03 — Wolverine At-Site Injection How-To
Where Exactly to Inject (Shoulder Anatomy)

The Wolverine shot goes SubQ into the soft tissue surrounding the painful part of your shoulder — not into muscle, not into the joint itself. Same technique as a standard SubQ shot, just at the shoulder instead of the abdomen.

Find the spot: press around the shoulder area and find where the pain centers. Most rotator cuff pain centers around the front of the deltoid (where the cuff tendons insert) or the posterior shoulder (above the shoulder blade ridge). Inject SubQ ~1–2 inches from the worst point of pain — close enough that the local BPC + TB signal lands, not so close you're hitting an obviously inflamed spot.

Technique: wipe the skin with alcohol. Pinch a small fold of skin. Insert the needle at 45°. Push the plunger steadily (20 units = 0.20 mL takes a beat longer than the KLOW 15u shot). Withdraw, dispose in sharps container.

Rotate within the shoulder region. Don't inject the same exact spot two nights in a row — rotate within a 2–3 inch area around the painful zone so no single point takes consecutive shots. Mild soreness at the injection site for an hour or two is normal; persistent redness or swelling past 24 hrs means rotate further next time.

Tip 04 — Movement During the Cycle
Light Use, Not Forced Rest or Forced Stretching

Move the shoulder gently through its current pain-free range every day. No forced stretching, no aggressive PT exercises, no pushing through pain. Light use signals to the body that the tissue needs to remain functional — tells the repair stages to rebuild for use, not just for rest.

What to avoid: heavy overhead pressing, weighted carries, sudden jerky movements. If a movement creates sharp pain, that's a "skip this for now" signal — not something to push through.

What's fine: normal daily activities (driving, working at a desk, light groceries), slow range-of-motion work (slow circles, gentle reaching), warm-water exposure (showers, baths — the warmth helps circulation to the area).

Tip 05 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

KLOW (M–F): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across the cycle doesn't move the needle.

Wolverine (M–F PM at shoulder): if you miss a PM dose, take it AM the next day at the shoulder instead and resume PM the day after. The local dual-peptide healing signal benefits from consistency, but a one-day shift isn't a problem. Don't double up.

Tip 06 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold KLOW or Wolverine if any of the following:

· Significant injection-site reaction at the shoulder (redness, heat, swelling, persistent pain past 24 hours) — could indicate the spot is too close to inflamed tissue; pause Wolverine, rotate location next time
· New or worsening allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes) — KLOW and Wolverine are both well-tolerated, but individual reactions can happen
· Sharp new pain in the shoulder that's clearly different from your baseline issue — could indicate the area is being aggravated; pause and reassess
· Any sustained worsening past Week 2 (instead of improvement) — flag for adjustment, not a temporary side effect

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through — we adjust the plan based on what's actually happening.
