Lindsay's Mito Repair + Recomp
Repair the engine first. Activate it. Then drive the cut on a metabolism that can actually keep up.

Two-phase design built around mitochondrial health. Phase 1 (Days 1–20) runs SS-31 alone to repair the inner mitochondrial membrane — the place where ATP gets made. Phase 2 (Wks 5–12) layers in MOTS-C (activates the now-healthy mitochondria via AMPK signaling), Retatrutide (the fat-loss anchor), and GHK-Cu (recovery + connective tissue support).

Why repair first. Most "fat loss" protocols just push the deficit harder and ignore why people stall, crash, or rebound. If the mitochondria can't oxidize fat efficiently, even an aggressive deficit produces slow results + bad energy. SS-31 fixes the cellular machinery before Reta drives the deficit. By the time the cut starts in Phase 2, your cells are ready to actually use the energy you're freeing up.

Bonus PRN tool: Cagrilintide included free as a weekend appetite brake. One shot Friday or Saturday evening dampens hunger for 24–48 hours — precision lever for the weekends where most fat-loss progress gets undone. Not on the schedule, use as needed.

Featured Tips for Your Stack
Tip 01 — Why SS-31 Runs Alone First
The Repair-Before-Activate Logic

Most fat-loss protocols just push the deficit harder. This one starts by fixing the cellular machinery that has to handle the deficit. SS-31 stabilizes the inner mitochondrial membrane — specifically the cardiolipin, which is where the electron transport chain (your ATP factory) lives. When mitochondria are damaged or inefficient, even an aggressive deficit produces slow results + bad energy.

4 weeks of SS-31 alone = the repair signal compounds without other peptides competing for cellular bandwidth. By Week 5 when Phase 2 layers in MOTS-C + Reta + GHK, your cells are primed to actually use the energy you're freeing up. The result: cleaner energy, less crash, less plateau.

Tip 02 — The Weekend Brake (Free Cagri Bonus)
When and How to Use Cagrilintide

Cagri is in your stack for one specific job: protect the weekly deficit Reta is building from getting undone by weekend splurges. Most fat-loss progress disappears on Saturday + Sunday because people are hungrier (Sunday-night-Lindsay has been in a deficit all week) and social calendars produce calorie spikes.

How to use it:

  • Take it Friday or Saturday evening — NOT before the meal. Before the weekend.
  • 500 mcg = 20 units (0.20 mL). One shot.
  • Effects: dampens hunger 24–48 hrs. You still enjoy the meals — you just don't overshoot.
  • Use as needed, not every weekend. 10 doses in the vial = ~10 weekend events.

The first shot can produce mild nausea or early satiety for 24 hrs. Use Friday evening the first time, not before a big Saturday event — lets first-shot effects wash before the high-stakes meal.

Tip 03 — Phase 2 AM Cluster Logistics
3 Shots on Mon/Thu · 2 Shots Tue/Wed/Fri · ~5 Minutes Each

Phase 2 heaviest days (Mon + Thu) hit 3 morning shots: MOTS-C + GHK-Cu + Reta. In practice it's one routine:

  • Wake, water/black coffee. No food yet.
  • MOTS-C first — 10u abdomen upper-right. Fasted-dependent.
  • GHK-Cu — 10u abdomen lower-right. Not fasted.
  • Reta (Mon/Thu only) — 10u abdomen upper-left. Not fasted.
  • Wait 30–45 min, then breakfast.

Tue/Wed/Fri = 2 shots (MOTS-C + GHK only). Sat/Sun off. By Week 6 it's habit.

Weeks 1–2 · Phase 1 Start
Subtle Energy Lift · No Scale Movement Yet

SS-31 only, M–F. No scale movement — this isn't a weight loss phase. What you might notice: slightly more stable energy through the day, mild improvement in sleep depth, less "afternoon crash" feeling. Mild injection-site redness is normal.

Weeks 3–4 · Phase 1 Wraps
Mito Repair Compounds · Foundation Set

By end of Week 4 the mitochondrial repair is locked in. Energy is more consistent. SS-31 vial is empty around Day 20. Phase 2 starts Monday of Week 5.

Weeks 5–6 · Phase 2 Starts
MOTS-C Activates, Reta Ramps

MOTS-C starts AM fasted M–F. Reta begins Mon + Thu. GHK-Cu joins the AM cluster M–F. Expect: noticeable energy bump from MOTS-C (AMPK activation), appetite suppression from Reta within the first week, mild GI adjustment.

Weight starts dropping by end of Week 5 — expect 1–1.5 lbs/wk through these weeks.

Weeks 7–10 · Cut Accelerates
Body Comp Shift Becomes Visible

Fat loss in this stretch: 1–1.5 lbs/wk steady. Energy stays solid thanks to the Phase 1 foundation + MOTS-C support. GHK-Cu keeps recovery + connective tissue layer working through the deficit. Most clients see the body comp shift in the mirror around Week 8.

GHK-Cu vial empties end of Week 10 (~30 doses at M–F).

Weeks 11–12 · Final Stretch
MOTS-C + Reta Carry the Close

GHK-Cu done. MOTS-C + Reta continue at the same M–F + Mon/Thu cadence. End of Week 12: Phase 2 wraps. MOTS-C has ~20-dose buffer (could extend ~4 wks if desired). Reta has 14-dose buffer (could extend 7 weeks at Mon+Thu).

End-of-cycle questions: body comp shift vs Week 1? Energy compared to start? How close to goal weight? These shape whether to take 4–8 weeks off, run a maintenance dose, or restart with a different focus.

Tip 04 — Normal Effects
No Action Needed

  • Mild GI effects first 1–2 weeks of Reta (nausea, early fullness) — subsides as tolerance builds
  • Mild flushing 5–10 min after MOTS-C — normal AMPK vascular response
  • Slightly more vivid dreams in Phase 2 — deeper sleep showing up
  • First-weekend Cagri mild nausea — subsides as tolerance builds
Tip 05 — Watch For
Message BioLinked If Any Of These Happen
  • Injection site hot, swollen, or red >24 hrs — technique or contamination
  • Severe Reta GI past Wk 2 — drop to 0.5 mg (5u) or pause
  • Rapid weight loss >3 lbs/wk sustained — deficit too aggressive, eat more
  • Energy dropping despite Phase 1 + MOTS-C — flag for review
  • Cagri severe nausea / vomiting — drop dose to 250 mcg or pause
