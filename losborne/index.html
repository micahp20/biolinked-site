Lydia's 2-Cycle Reset
Lose fat, build lean muscle, and rebalance your hormones — organized into two simple cycles.

Your goal, in your words: lose weight to improve your overall health and wellness. At 52, going from 200 toward 150 is very doable — and doing it the right way means losing fat while protecting muscle, and supporting the hormonal shifts that make weight loss harder after menopause. So instead of one big pile of shots, everything is split into two cycles you can run together or add one at a time.

Cycle 1 — Fat Loss + Muscle + Energy is your daily engine. Retatrutide drives the fat loss (and helps your blood sugar — your HbA1c is in the prediabetic range). Tesamorelin raises your own growth hormone to preferentially burn visceral belly fat while protecting lean muscle, and it deepens sleep — a direct hit on your 2/5 sleep. MOTS-c lifts cellular energy and fat-burning so the deficit doesn't leave you drained.

Important — because you're already on Semaglutide / Tirzepatide. Retatrutide is the same class of medication (a GLP-1 agonist). You should not run both at once — the plan is to transition off the Sema/Tirz and onto Reta, starting Reta low and ramping up slowly. Running two GLP-1s together stacks the side effects (nausea, GI upset) and isn't safe, especially with your blood-sugar, GI, and kidney/liver history. Details are in the Reta card and the Notes tab — message us so we time the switch right.

Cycle 2 — Hormonal Balance (Kisspeptin + Oxytocin + PT-141) supports hormones, mood, and libido from the top of the axis — the piece that helps with the hormonal side of midlife weight gain, stress, and well-being. Two goals, two cycles, one page.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, One Vial at a Time

Dry vials: refrigerate at 36–46°F, protected from light. Never freeze.

After mixing: refrigerate and use within ~30 days. Reconstitute one vial at a time — open the next only when the current one runs dry. This matters most for the Tesamorelin (6 vials) and Kisspeptin (4 vials).

Reconstitution amounts: Reta 3 mL, Tesamorelin 2 mL, MOTS 3 mL, Kisspeptin 1 mL, Oxytocin 10 mL, PT-141 1 mL. Add the water slowly down the vial wall; swirl, don't shake.

Every shot: fresh single-use syringe, wipe the stopper and your skin with alcohol, rotate injection sites.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Day 1
Read This First — You're On Semaglutide / Tirzepatide
One GLP-1 at a Time — We Switch, We Don't Stack

Retatrutide is the same class of medication as your Semaglutide / Tirzepatide (a GLP-1). You should not run both at the same time — doubling up stacks the nausea and GI side effects and isn't safe. The plan is a clean switch: come off the Sema/Tirz, then start Reta low. Message us so we time the changeover to your last dose.

The rules:

· Start at 10u (~1.33 mg) once a week — a deliberately low start — and message us after each of your first few Monday doses.
· Monday + Tuesday: small, low-fat meals, stop at ~80% full, hydrate hard (2–3 L/day).
· Watch your blood sugar. You're on a glucose medication and your HbA1c is 5.9% — as the fat comes off your numbers will improve, so we may need to adjust. If you feel shaky, sweaty, or lightheaded, treat it and message us.
· If nausea or vomiting shows up — hold the next dose, don't push through. We'd rather drop to 5u or pause than have you sick.

Your other conditions: with your GI/gut, autoimmune, and kidney/liver history, go slow and keep us posted — and flag all of this plus your current meds (Famotidine, etc.) with your provider before starting.

Tip 01 — The Daily Rhythm
AM Block + One PM Pre-Bed Shot

AM (fasted): MOTS + Kisspeptin every weekday, plus Oxytocin on M/W/F and Reta on Monday. Wait 30–45 min before eating.

PM (pre-bed, fasted 2+ hrs): Tesamorelin every weekday — the empty-stomach window is what makes the growth-hormone pulse (and your deeper sleep) work.

As needed: PT-141 30–60 min before intimacy — start at a half dose (5u) the first time.

Tip 02 — Injection Refresher
SubQ, Small Needle — You've Done This

All SubQ, short insulin needle. Wash hands, wipe the stopper and skin with alcohol, pull to the right unit mark, pinch an inch of skin, insert at 45°, push slowly, drop the syringe in the sharps container. Rotate sites.

Draw marks: Reta 10u, Tesamorelin 20u, MOTS 10u, Kisspeptin 10u, Oxytocin 10u, PT-141 10u (5u first use). Glance at the line every shot.

Tip 03 — Week-by-Week
What to Expect

Weeks 1–2: Tesamorelin deepens sleep within the first week or two (big for your 2/5). Reta is intentionally gentle after the switch — go slow, watch tolerance, don't expect much scale movement yet. Kisspeptin/Oxytocin start smoothing mood.

Weeks 3–6: fat loss picks up as Reta settles in (assuming it's tolerated), energy from MOTS is established, and Tesamorelin works on the visceral belly fat while protecting muscle. Appetite is lower and steadier.

Weeks 6–12: the real recomposition window — leaner while holding muscle from the GH pulse. This is where the 200→150 progress starts to show. Reorder MOTS and Tesamorelin as they empty.

Ongoing: a run-till-empty protocol. We titrate Reta up only as tolerated, and recheck your HbA1c + CBC around week 12 to see the progress in the labs.

Tip 04 — Sleep, Stress & Hormones
Your 2/5 Sleep and 3/5 Stress Are Part of the Plan

Sleep is where the fat-loss and muscle-protection work actually happens — so fixing the 2/5 is a real lever, not a side quest. Tesamorelin deepens sleep nightly via the GH pulse. Keep the Vitamin D and Fish Oil you already take; adding magnesium glycinate in the evening stacks well here.

For the 3/5 stress and the hormonal side of midlife: Oxytocin (M/W/F) lowers the stress response and lifts mood without sedation, and Kisspeptin supports your hormones from the top of the axis. Between better sleep and these two, both the stress and the hormonal symptoms should ease over the first several weeks.

Tip 05 — Red Flags
When to Pause & Message Us

Hold Reta if: vomiting, persistent nausea >24 hrs, or severe abdominal pain radiating to the back — especially important given your GI history and the switch from Sema/Tirz.

Low blood sugar: shaky, sweaty, lightheaded, or confused — treat with fast carbs and message us; we may need to adjust with your prescriber since you're on a glucose medication.

Injection sites: a little redness at the pinhole is normal; spreading redness, warmth, swelling, or worsening pus = possible infection — stop that site and message me.

PT-141 first use: half dose (5u), and expect possible mild nausea/flushing — that's normal and passes.

Anything that feels off, message first — don't push through, and don't restart on your own.
