John's Joint Repair + Recovery
Repair what the years took. Recover faster than they did. Be ready when the new baby and the next pickup game both need you at full speed.

You're 34, 6'0", 200 lbs, master electrician, with a baby on the way. Years of pickup basketball put real miles on the knees and connective tissue. The electrician job adds daily physical load on top — stairs, ladders, awkward positions, hauling gear. None of it shows up as injury, but it shows up as residual: knees that ache before workouts, recovery that takes a day longer than it used to, sleep that doesn't fully restore.

The protocol does three jobs in parallel. Tissue + joint repair via TB-500 + GHK-Cu — the two peptides with the strongest evidence for soft-tissue, tendon, ligament, and connective-tissue regeneration. GH-axis recovery via CJC-1295 w/ DAC + Ipamorelin — nightly GH pulse for deep sleep, muscle preservation, and the cellular repair signal your body uses to put itself back together overnight. Cellular energy via NAD+ — restores the mitochondrial substrate that gets depleted with chronic physical load, long hours, and (incoming) sleep deprivation.

The new-baby timing is part of the design. Sleep is about to take a hit. CJC w/ DAC + Ipa amplify the limited sleep you do get (deeper slow-wave, more recovery per hour). NAD+ keeps cellular energy production stable through the rough nights. 2 vials of each compound means this isn't a short-cycle "validation" run — it's a real cycle. Each compound runs at the same per-dose level it's set to, and stops when its supply naturally exhausts. Compounds taper off in sequence over ~24 weeks: TB-500 wraps the loading layer first (Wk 5), Ipa + NAD+ carry the GH-axis + cellular energy through Wks 12–13, CJC w/ DAC keeps the GHRH baseline going to Wk 20, and GHK-Cu finishes the recovery layer alone through Wk 24. By the time the protocol fully wraps, you'll have ~6 months of accumulated repair work in the tank.

Why NAD+ for your situation: Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide — the essential coenzyme for mitochondrial ATP production. Chronically depleted by: long physical workdays, training load, alcohol, and (incoming) sleep deprivation from a new baby. Less the "pre-workout pump" effect, more the "you have full bandwidth at 4 PM after a long workday" effect.

The smaller 25 mg per-shot volume is usually well-tolerated; if it's still uncomfortable, split into two 0.075 mL (7.5u) shots on opposite sides.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately. Use within 30 days. Fresh insulin syringe for each draw — never reuse.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row. On stacked-shot days (especially Mon = GHK AM + TB-500 + NAD+ PM + Ipa pre-bed), use a different quadrant for each.

Featured Tips for Your Situation
Tip 01 — The Knee Question
What TB-500 + GHK-Cu Can and Can't Do

What they're good for: chronic soft-tissue inflammation, slower-than-it-used-to-be recovery, mild tendon/ligament wear, residual joint aches from cumulative load. Your knees, exactly. By the end of Week 5, expect noticeably reduced morning stiffness, faster recovery after pickup games, and the "warming up takes 10 minutes instead of 25" effect.

What they're NOT for: acute structural injuries (torn meniscus, ACL tear, full ligament rupture). If anything in the knee feels structurally unstable — clicking, locking, giving way — that's an orthopedist visit, not a peptide protocol. Peptides accelerate the body's own repair signal; they don't rebuild missing tissue.

Tip 02 — New Baby Timing
Why This Protocol Is Designed Around Sleep Loss

The CJC w/ DAC + Ipa pairing was chosen specifically because new-baby sleep is going to take a hit. You can't out-sleep a newborn. What you can do is amplify the limited sleep you get — deeper slow-wave per hour, more GH per pulse, more recovery per night.

NAD+ is the same logic on the cellular-energy side. When sleep is short, the mitochondria get hammered and ATP production drops — this is the "I'm tired even after coffee" feeling that hits 3 weeks into broken sleep. NAD+ keeps the substrate full so you have bandwidth left at the end of a long workday.

Practical: protect the PM pre-bed Ipa window when the baby comes. If you can't get the 30-min pre-bed shot in, skip that day — don't try to backload it later. One missed dose per week is statistical noise; eight missed in two weeks isn't.

Tip 04 — Why 2 Vials of CJC w/ DAC Matters
You're Set Up For the Real Cycle — Not Just Validation

The pharmacology context: CJC w/ DAC has a long half-life (~6–8 days) by design — that's the whole point of the DAC modification. But that long half-life means it takes 3–4 weeks of weekly dosing to reach steady-state plasma levels. Until you're at steady state, the GHRH baseline isn't actually elevated — you're still climbing the curve. Real benefit lives in weeks 5–16+, when you're at steady state AND the GHRH baseline has had time to compound with daily Ipa pulses.

What 2 vials means for your run: at 1 mg / week, 2 vials = 20 doses = 20 weeks of weekly dosing. Weeks 1–4 are still climbing the curve, but you have 16 more weeks at steady state after that — the exact duration the research recommends for the meaningful body comp + recovery shifts to compound. This isn't a "starting to notice it" cycle. This is the real one.

The full picture across all 5 compounds:

  • TB-500 2 vials = full standard 5-week loading window. Standard loading protocols want 4–6 weeks at 2–5 mg twice weekly — you land squarely in that range.
  • CJC w/ DAC 2 vials = 20 weeks. First 4 wks climbing to steady state, then 16 weeks of compounded effect. This is where the body comp shifts and sleep architecture changes show up clearly (not just "starting to feel it").
  • Ipa 2 vials = 12 weeks paired daily M–F. Carries the nightly GH pulse through the prime CJC steady-state window.
  • NAD+ 2 vials = ~13 weeks at 25 mg M/W/F. Cellular energy substrate stays elevated through the heaviest workload period.
  • GHK-Cu 2 vials = 24 weeks. The longest tail — carries the recovery + connective tissue layer the full 6 months.

Bottom line: the protocol's supply is now sized for the recommended duration of each compound — nothing in the stack is artificially cut short. By Wk 24 you'll have ~6 months of accumulated repair work, exactly the timeline the research framework wants. Plan the next cycle 8–12 weeks after Wk 24 (~Wk 32–36) so the gains have time to settle before re-loading.
