Personalized Protocol
John's Joint Repair + Recovery
Repair what the years took. Recover faster than they did. Be ready when the new baby and the next pickup game both need you at full speed.

You're 34, 6'0", 200 lbs, master electrician, with a baby on the way. Years of pickup basketball put real miles on the knees and connective tissue. The electrician job adds daily physical load on top — stairs, ladders, awkward positions, hauling gear. None of it shows up as injury, but it shows up as residual: knees that ache before workouts, recovery that takes a day longer than it used to, sleep that doesn't fully restore.

The protocol does three jobs in parallel. Tissue + joint repair via TB-500 + GHK-Cu — the two peptides with the strongest evidence for soft-tissue, tendon, ligament, and connective-tissue regeneration. GH-axis recovery via CJC-1295 w/ DAC + Ipamorelin — nightly GH pulse for deep sleep, muscle preservation, and the cellular repair signal your body uses to put itself back together overnight. Cellular energy via NAD+ — restores the mitochondrial substrate that gets depleted with chronic physical load, long hours, and (incoming) sleep deprivation.

The new-baby timing is part of the design. Sleep is about to take a hit. CJC w/ DAC + Ipa amplify the limited sleep you do get (deeper slow-wave, more recovery per hour). NAD+ keeps cellular energy production stable through the rough nights. Goal end-state at Week 5: knees quieter, recovery faster, body comp tighter, sleep deeper — and a foundation that holds through the first months of fatherhood without you breaking down.

Why TB-500 for your situation: Thymosin Beta-4 analog — the most-studied peptide for soft-tissue repair, ligament/tendon regeneration, and wound healing. For the knees specifically: drives actin polymerization (cell movement to injury sites), accelerates revascularization of damaged tissue, and reduces inflammation in chronically loaded joints. Years of basketball don't tear ligaments — they leave them chronically inflamed and slower to repair. TB-500 reverses that profile.
Why a 2.5-week loading window: standard TB-500 protocols recommend 4–6 weeks of loading for max effect. A 10 mg vial gives one solid loading burst at 2 mg twice weekly. Future cycles (every 3–6 months) can run 3–4 vials for a full 4-week load. This first cycle is a focused first push.

Drives systemic recovery + tissue repair via a different mechanism than TB-500 — collagen synthesis, modulation of inflammatory cytokines, support of skin/connective tissue/hair. While TB-500 targets the soft-tissue injury sites, GHK-Cu works on the system-wide recovery + inflammation layer. Daily AM dosing maintains the steady signal. The two together cover both targeted (TB-500) and broad (GHK-Cu) repair work.

Why CJC w/ DAC vs the non-DAC version: DAC (drug affinity complex) extends the half-life to ~6–8 days. One shot a week instead of daily. That's the right pattern for John — new baby + work schedule means a daily PM peptide ritual is hard to keep. One Sunday-night shot anchors the whole week's GH-axis signal. Pairs with daily Ipa (next compound) for the consistent nightly pulse.
Why Sunday night: establishes the steady-state plasma level by Monday training. Some clients prefer Friday (so the peak lands on weekend rest); either works. Stick to whichever day once you pick.

Together they produce a deeper, more consistent GH release during sleep than either alone. The downstream effects map directly to John's goals: deeper slow-wave sleep (the new-baby's nemesis), faster overnight recovery, muscle preservation while body comp tightens, improved skin / hair / connective tissue.
Empty stomach matters: GH pulse needs low insulin to fire properly. No food 2+ hrs before injection. Big dinner at 7? Inject at 9pm or later. The 30-min pre-bed window is non-negotiable.
NAD+ · 500 mg Cellular Energy 1 vial / cycle
  • Dose50 mg → pull syringe to 30 units (0.30 mL)
  • ScheduleMon + Thu PM (2×/wk) · 5 weeks
  • Supply500 mg / 50 mg per dose = 10 doses · 2/wk × 5 wks = exact

Why NAD+ for your situation: Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide — the essential coenzyme for mitochondrial ATP production. Chronically depleted by: long physical workdays, training load, alcohol, and (incoming) sleep deprivation from a new baby. Direct injection restores the cellular substrate that supports energy, focus, and recovery at the source. Less the "pre-workout pump" effect, more the "you have full bandwidth at 4 PM after a long workday" effect.
The push-slow rule: NAD+ injection produces a localized sting / warmth / mild flushing for some clients. Pushing slowly (30–60 sec for 0.30 mL) minimizes this. If it's still uncomfortable, split into two 0.15 mL shots on opposite sides — same as the Cerebrolysin split-dose pattern.

Read Before Day 1
Featured Tips for Your Situation
Tip 01 — The Knee Question
What TB-500 + GHK-Cu Can and Can't Do

What they're good for: chronic soft-tissue inflammation, slower-than-it-used-to-be recovery, mild tendon/ligament wear, residual joint aches from cumulative load. Your knees, exactly. By the end of Week 5, expect noticeably reduced morning stiffness, faster recovery after pickup games, and the "warming up takes 10 minutes instead of 25" effect.

What they're NOT for: acute structural injuries (torn meniscus, ACL tear, full ligament rupture). If anything in the knee feels structurally unstable — clicking, locking, giving way — that's an orthopedist visit, not a peptide protocol. Peptides accelerate the body's own repair signal; they don't rebuild missing tissue.

Tip 02 — New Baby Timing
Why This Protocol Is Designed Around Sleep Loss

The CJC w/ DAC + Ipa pairing was chosen specifically because new-baby sleep is going to take a hit. You can't out-sleep a newborn. What you can do is amplify the limited sleep you get — deeper slow-wave per hour, more GH per pulse, more recovery per night.

NAD+ is the same logic on the cellular-energy side. When sleep is short, the mitochondria get hammered and ATP production drops — this is the "I'm tired even after coffee" feeling that hits 3 weeks into broken sleep. NAD+ keeps the substrate full so you have bandwidth left at the end of a long workday.

Practical: protect the PM pre-bed Ipa window when the baby comes. If you can't get the 30-min pre-bed shot in, skip that day — don't try to backload it later. One missed dose per week is statistical noise; eight missed in two weeks isn't.

Tip 03 — The Heavy Days (Mon + Thu)
4 Shots Sounds Like A Lot — Here's the Routine

Mon and Thu hit 4 shots: GHK-Cu AM, TB-500 + NAD+ in the evening, Ipa pre-bed. In practice it's three windows of activity totaling ~5 minutes:

  • AM (60 sec): GHK-Cu, abdomen, doesn't need fasted. Pair with coffee.
  • Evening 7–8 PM (2 min): TB-500 first (lateral thigh near a knee if targeting), then NAD+ — push slow over 30–60 sec. Rotate sites from morning GHK.
  • Pre-bed 30 min before lights out (60 sec): Ipa, abdomen. Empty stomach 2+ hrs.

Tue/Wed/Fri are way lighter — 2 shots total (GHK AM + Ipa pre-bed). Most days are easy; Mon + Thu are the "do all the things" days.

Week-by-Week
What to Expect Across the 5 Weeks
Week 1
Build the Routine · Subtle Effects

First week is about locking the rhythm. Possible early effects: slightly deeper sleep depth from CJC/Ipa, slight tingling extremities the first nights (GH-related fluid shift — goes away), possible mild localized warmth at NAD+ sites for 30 min post-injection.

Don't expect joint relief yet — TB-500 + GHK-Cu compound over time. Week 1 is foundation.

Weeks 2–3
First Knee Shift · Sleep Deepens

By end of Week 2 most clients notice the first joint shift — less morning stiffness, faster warm-up before workouts. Sleep is noticeably deeper from the compounding CJC/Ipa effect.

TB-500 wraps end of Week 2.5 (5 doses done). Effects compound for 2–3 weeks after the last dose — you'll get peak TB-500 benefit Wk 3–4 even though the dosing is done.

Weeks 4–5
Recovery Window Locks In

This is the visible window. Knees should be the quietest they've been in months. Recovery from pickup games or hard workdays faster than baseline. Body comp likely tighter from CJC/Ipa muscle preservation + better recovery between sessions.

End of cycle questions: How are the knees vs Day 1? Sleep depth vs baseline? Energy through long workdays? Answers shape what the next cycle (in 3–6 months) looks like — might be heavier TB-500 loading if knees still need work, or a different focus if the joints are dialed.

Message Micah If Any Of These Happen
  • Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique or contamination
