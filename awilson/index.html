KLOW + Wolverine
Systemic healing in the morning, dual-mechanism local repair at night. 8-week recovery cycle.

Two layers, one cycle. KLOW M–F AM fasted gives the body a daily systemic healing signal across every tissue for the full 8 weeks. Wolverine Stack M–F PM concentrates a dual-mechanism local repair signal at the site of injury for the first ~4 weeks (until both vials empty). After Wolverine wraps, KLOW continues solo through Week 8 to consolidate the gains while the deep tissue remodels.

Why KLOW is the systemic anchor. KLOW pre-blends four peptides — GHK-Cu (collagen synthesis + antioxidant), BPC-157 (angiogenesis + soft tissue), TB-500 (tissue regeneration + actin), KPV (anti-inflammatory + immune). Four mechanisms in one shot, every weekday morning for the full 8 weeks. This is the layer that gives the body a continuous "rebuild" signal across the entire body.

Why Wolverine is the at-site intensive. Wolverine pre-blends BPC-157 + TB-500 in one vial — the two strongest soft-tissue healing peptides hitting from complementary angles. Injected SubQ at the area you want to heal, the combined signal compounds on itself: BPC drives new blood vessel growth at the injury, TB-500 drives cell migration + tissue regeneration + anti-scarring. Two vials at M–F PM cover ~4 weeks of intensive local repair — the front-loaded "hit it hard" window.

Run it 8 weeks or until empty. The vials are paced to hit the 8-week mark cleanly: KLOW finishes exactly at end of Week 8 at M–F; Wolverine finishes around end of Week 4. No fixed end date beyond "the bottles run out."

Featured Tips
Tip 01 — The Two-Layer Logic
Why KLOW + Wolverine Together

KLOW gives the body a system-wide repair instruction every morning. Four peptides covering four mechanisms simultaneously — KPV calms chronic inflammation, GHK-Cu activates collagen synthesis, TB-500 drives tissue regeneration, BPC drives angiogenesis (new blood vessel growth to deliver oxygen and nutrients to healing tissue). The whole body gets the message, every weekday for 8 weeks straight.

Wolverine concentrates that signal at the area you actually want healed. BPC-157 + TB-500 injected SubQ at the target site for the first 4 weeks. Different mechanisms, same target = faster healing with cleaner tissue rebuild. After 4 weeks, the at-site work is done; KLOW continues the systemic context for another 4 weeks while the deep tissue remodels.

Why the order matters: AM fasted KLOW gives the body the systemic instruction with the cleanest signal (insulin and blood sugar at floor levels). PM Wolverine puts the at-site repair signal in the body right when overnight repair pathways are most active. AM + PM bookends each weekday for 4 weeks; then KLOW solo for the tail.

Tip 02 — Week-by-Week Timeline
What to Expect from the 8-Week Cycle

Week 1: learning the routine. Effects are subtle the first 5–7 days as the body acclimates. Don't push through painful range of motion; let the compounds work.

Week 2: the area should start to feel noticeably less inflamed. Range of motion typically improves first — movement feels easier before the deeper ache fully resolves. This is the inflammation-clearing window.

Weeks 3–4 (Wolverine still running): meaningful improvement lands. Pain at rest drops substantially. Movements that triggered sharp pain start feeling less reactive. Most clients describe this as "the area is actually getting better" rather than just being less inflamed. Wolverine vials empty at end of Week 4.

Weeks 5–6 (KLOW alone, deeper remodeling): the systemic healing signal continues. Deeper tissue repair is happening on its slower 4–6 week timeline. The area may feel like progress has plateaued visually, but the deep tissue work is the main thing happening here. Don't stop just because the dramatic improvements slow down — this is the consolidation window.

Weeks 7–8 (final remodeling): the tissue locks in. Strength and stability return. By cycle end, most clients describe the area as "functional again" or fully healed.

Tip 03 — Movement During the Cycle
Light Use, Not Forced Rest or Forced Stretching

Move the area gently through its current pain-free range every day. No forced stretching, no aggressive PT exercises, no pushing through sharp pain. Light use signals to the body that the tissue needs to remain functional — tells the repair stages to rebuild for use, not just for rest.

What to avoid: heavy loading, jerky sudden movements, anything that creates sharp pain. If a movement creates sharp pain, that's a "skip this for now" signal — not something to push through.

What's fine: normal daily activities, slow range-of-motion work, warm-water exposure (showers, baths — the warmth helps circulation to the area).

Tip 04 — Missed Doses
What to Do If You Skip

KLOW (M–F AM): miss one day, skip it. Don't double up. Resume the next scheduled day. Missing 1–2 doses across the cycle doesn't move the needle.

Wolverine (M–F PM): if you miss a PM dose, take it AM the next day at the site instead and resume PM the day after. The local healing signal benefits from consistency, but a one-day shift isn't a problem. Don't double up.

Tip 05 — Red Flags
When to Pause

Hold the next KLOW or Wolverine dose if any of the following:

· Significant injection-site reaction (redness, heat, swelling, persistent pain past 24 hours)
· New or worsening allergic-type symptoms (hives, throat tightness, breathing changes)
· Sharp new pain at the target area that's clearly different from your baseline issue — could indicate the area is being aggravated; pause and reassess
· Any sustained worsening past Week 2 (instead of improvement) — flag for adjustment, not a temporary side effect

Any of these symptoms, text Micah first. Don't push through — we adjust the plan based on what's actually happening.
