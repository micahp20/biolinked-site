Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, One Vial at a Time

Dry vials: refrigerate at 36–46°F, protected from light. Never freeze.

After mixing: refrigerate and use within ~30 days. Reconstitute one vial at a time. GHK-Cu turns clear blue once mixed (the copper) — normal.

Reconstitution amounts: Reta 2 mL, GHK-Cu 3 mL. Add the water slowly down the vial wall; swirl gently, don't shake.

Every shot: fresh single-use syringe, wipe the stopper and your skin with alcohol, rotate injection sites.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Day 1
Tip 01 — The Morning Routine
One AM Block, 5 Days a Week

Everything is a morning shot, fasted. Every weekday: GHK-Cu (10u). Add Reta (10u) on Monday. Wait 30–45 min before eating. Weekends off.

Draw marks: Reta 10u, GHK-Cu 10u. Glance at the line every time so you don't overshoot.

Tip 02 — Reta: Start Low, Titrate Up
The Fat-Loss Dial

You're running Reta at 2 mg (10u) once weekly — a solid working dose. Some mild fullness or nausea the first Monday or two is normal; eat lighter, lower-fat meals that day and hydrate hard (Reta blunts thirst). Message us after the first couple of doses and we can ease off or step up as tolerated — that's how you find the sweet spot without triggering side effects.

Tip 03 — Week-by-Week
What to Expect

Weeks 1–2: Reta is gentle at the start — a little appetite drop, but don't expect big scale moves yet. GHK-Cu is quietly working on skin and tissue.

Weeks 3–5: fat loss picks up as Reta titrates up; GHK-Cu's skin/collagen work starts showing (tone, firmness).

Weeks 6–8+: the leaning-out window — and because GHK-Cu is running the whole time, the loss firms rather than sags. Reorder GHK as it empties; Reta runs long. Run till the bottles are empty, then reassess.

Tip 04 — Red Flags
When to Pause & Message Us

Hold Reta if: persistent nausea/vomiting past 24 hrs, or severe abdominal pain radiating to the back. Don't push through — we drop the dose or pause.

Injection sites: a little redness at the pinhole is normal; spreading redness, warmth, swelling, or pus that's worsening = possible infection — stop that site and message us.

Anything that feels off, message first — don't push through or restart on your own.
