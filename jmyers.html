🎯 Why This Stack vs. Another Round of Sema

Sema is a single-receptor (GLP-1) agonist. Once your body acclimates and you hit a stall, increasing the dose generally produces more GI side effects without further weight loss. Retatrutide is a tri-agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon), and the glucagon component is exactly the lever Sema doesn't have — it directly raises basal metabolic rate, mobilizes hepatic fat, and improves glucose disposal. Combined with the GH pulse from CJC/Ipa + Tesamorelin and the NNMT inhibition from 5-Amino-1MQ, you're now hitting fat loss from four independent pathways instead of one. That's how a stall ends.

💡 Protocol Design Philosophy — Recomp, Not Just Loss

You don't want to lose 10 lbs of muscle and water — you want to lose 10 lbs of fat while maintaining (or building) lean tissue. That's a different target, and it requires a different stack. Reta + 5-Amino drive the fat-loss vector. CJC/Ipa + Tesamorelin protect and grow lean tissue through the GH/IGF-1 axis. SLU-PP-332 amplifies the workouts you're already doing. Your existing HRT-T base and 5g creatine are perfectly aligned with this — they keep your muscle protein synthesis machinery primed throughout. The result: at week 8, the scale shows 138, but the mirror and the gym show more lean tissue than where you started.

Why it's in your protocol
5-Amino-1MQ is one of the most underrated compounds for women in their 40s trying to drop the last layer of stubborn fat. Its mechanism is unique: it inhibits NNMT (nicotinamide N-methyltransferase), an enzyme that becomes overexpressed in adipose tissue with age and that progressively "locks" fat into storage by depleting cellular NAD+ and methyl group availability. By blocking NNMT, 5-Amino-1MQ does two things simultaneously: (1) it makes adipose tissue actively release fatty acids into circulation for oxidation, and (2) it raises intracellular NAD+, which improves mitochondrial energy output in muscle tissue. The net effect is exactly what you want — fat comes off, muscle gets more energy. This is why 5-Amino-1MQ is showing up in protocols specifically built for women over 40 who hit fat-loss plateaus. Run it AM, fasted, alongside Tesamorelin.

Storage & Handling

Dry powder vials (before reconstitution)

Store all lyophilized vials refrigerated at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep away from light. Do not freeze. Shelf life is 12–24 months unopened. Only reconstitute what you plan to use within 30 days.

After reconstitution

All reconstituted vials must be refrigerated immediately and used within 30 days. Never freeze a reconstituted peptide — this destroys the protein structure. Use a fresh syringe for every draw.

Bacteriostatic water (BAC water)

Use sterile BAC water for all 5 compounds. The benzyl alcohol preservative allows safe multi-draw use over 30 days. Do not substitute plain sterile water. With 5 compounds (one of which uses 5 mL recon), order at least 6 BAC water vials — see your supplies checklist for the McGuff link.

Syringes & injection technique

29–31 gauge insulin syringes (0.5 mL or 1 mL barrel). Never reuse — one syringe per injection, always. Dispose in sharps container immediately. Rotate injection sites: abdomen is preferred for all SubQ injections. Pinch skin, 45° angle, slow injection. Wipe vial stopper and skin with alcohol prep pad before every use.
