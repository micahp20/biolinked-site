Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze. Tesamorelin is reasonably stable but lyophilized peptides degrade faster at room temperature.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after recon. Stable for ~30 days — well within your usage window (each vial covers 10 doses = ~2 weeks of Phase 1). Don't pre-recon all 5 vials — reconstitute one at a time as needed so the last vial isn't sitting reconstituted for 10 weeks.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen, ~1″ from belly button. Rotate between 4 quadrants. Never inject the same 1″ spot two times in a row. Cycle: Mon upper-right → Tue upper-left → Wed lower-right → Thu lower-left → Fri back to upper-right (different spot within the quadrant).

Tip 01 — Why Tesamorelin (vs. Direct GH or Sermorelin)
The GHRH Analog with the Strongest Clinical Visceral-Fat Signal

Tesamorelin sits in a specific clinical lane: a GHRH analog with a long enough half-life and strong enough receptor affinity to drive a measurable pulse-pattern GH release, with the most well-documented visceral adipose tissue reduction data of any GHRH-class compound. That's the differentiator.

  • vs. Direct HGH: HGH floods the system with exogenous GH — works, but causes water retention, joint stiffness, carpal tunnel, and insulin resistance over time. Tesa drives your own pituitary to release GH in physiologic pulse patterns — same benefits, none of the puffiness.
  • vs. Sermorelin: Sermorelin is shorter-acting and gentler — great as an entry-level GHRH layer but you need higher frequency and the clinical effect on visceral fat is smaller. Tesa is more clinically validated for body-comp shifts.
  • vs. CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin: CJC/Ipa is a GHRH + GHRP combo that fires bigger pulses but tends to be evening-dosed for sleep+GH. Tesa is the morning-dose body-comp specialist — complementary, not competitive.

For you specifically: the 12-week Tesa run targets the visceral fat layer and protects lean mass while you're in any caloric deficit. Sleep depth + recovery improvements typically show up by Wk 4–5 as the GH pulses get larger.

Tip 02 — AM Fasted Protocol (Non-Negotiable for Tesa)
A Fed Shot Is a Wasted Shot

Tesamorelin's GH-pulse effect is blunted by carbohydrate-driven insulin spikes. If you take it after breakfast (or with milk-coffee, or with a banana, or anything caloric), insulin shuts down the GH release before it gets started. You get maybe 30% of the effect.

  • The window: first thing on waking. Before food. Before any caloric drink.
  • Allowed during the wait: plain water, black coffee, plain tea (no milk, no sugar, no honey, no oat milk). Anything caloric resets the fasted state.
  • Wait time: 30–45 minutes between shot and first meal.
  • Routine that works: wake → bathroom → shot → black coffee → ~30 min of light activity (shower, walk, prep) → breakfast. The wait window doubles as your morning routine.

This window matters every single shot, every week. Skipping it doesn't ruin the cycle, but it does waste that day's dose. Build the morning routine around it from Day 1.

Tip 03 — The Built-In Taper Logic
Why 5 Vials, Why This Shape

The 5-vial bundle is sized to deliver an exact taper-down rather than a hard stop. Here's the math:

  • Phase 1 (Wks 1–8): 1 mg M–F = 5 doses/week × 8 weeks = 40 doses = 4 vials
  • Phase 2a (Wks 9–10): 1 mg M / W / F = 3 doses/week × 2 weeks = 6 doses
  • Phase 2b (Wks 11–12): 1 mg M / Th = 2 doses/week × 2 weeks = 4 doses
  • Total: 40 + 6 + 4 = 50 doses = 5 vials × 10 doses each = exact fit

Why this design: GH-axis compounds reward steady-state signaling. Hard-stopping after 8 weeks of full M–F tends to produce a 2–3 week post-cycle slump as endogenous GHRH re-establishes baseline. Stepping down through Phase 2 keeps the receptors gently stimulated while your own GHRH output ramps back up — smoother exit, better gain retention.

Tip 04 — What to Expect, Week by Week
Subjective Markers Across the 12-Week Cycle
  • Wks 1–2: mostly nothing felt subjectively. Maybe slightly deeper sleep. Mild injection-site tenderness as you learn the rotation. GH pulses are landing but the cumulative effect is small.
  • Wks 3–4: sleep depth noticeably improves — first cue that the cycle is working. Some users report mild bloating or fluid shift in the first 7–14 days as cell-water rebalances. Visceral fat hasn't visibly changed yet.
  • Wks 5–6: body-comp shifts start to show. Belt feels a notch looser even if scale weight hasn't moved much — visceral fat reduction shows up in waist circumference before scale weight. Recovery between training sessions noticeably faster.
  • Wks 7–8: end of Phase 1. Most of the visible change is now in the mirror — lean look, less abdominal fullness. Sleep is at its deepest. This is when most people first say "yeah, this is doing something."
  • Wks 9–10: Phase 2a taper. You'll feel almost no difference vs Phase 1 — the M/W/F frequency is enough to maintain the gains. This is the cleanest part of the cycle.
  • Wks 11–12: Phase 2b. M/Th. Last hand-off. Gains hold. By end of Wk 12, vials are empty.
  • Post-cycle (Wks 13–20): body-comp gains typically hold 4–8 weeks. Sleep depth tapers gradually back toward baseline over 2–3 weeks. Reassess at Wk 14 for whether to repeat, layer in something different, or maintain with diet/training alone.

Tip 05 — Normal Effects (No Action Needed)
Expected on Tesamorelin
  • Mild injection-site tenderness or small bruising — especially Wk 1 as you learn the rotation. Settles within 2–3 weeks.
  • Vivid dreams — cue that deep sleep is increasing. This is a feature, not a side effect.
  • Mild fluid retention / "fuller" look first 1–2 weeks — cell water rebalancing as GH levels stabilize. Resolves naturally.
  • Slight increase in appetite mid-cycle — GH increases lean tissue demand. Eat protein. Don't fight it.
  • Cleaner energy through the day starting Wk 4–5 — the signal you want.
Tip 06 — Watch For (Message BioLinked)
Anything Below Warrants a Quick Check-In
  • Persistent joint stiffness or carpal-tunnel-like wrist tingling — rare at 1 mg but flag if it shows up; we may step the dose down.
  • Significant water retention beyond mild first-week shift (e.g. puffy face, ankle swelling) — reduce dose to 0.5 mg or pause and flag.
  • Injection site hot, red, or swollen >24 hrs — possible technique or contamination issue.
  • Sustained elevated fasting glucose >110 (if you check) — rare at 1 mg, but GH does affect insulin sensitivity at higher doses. Worth flagging.
  • Any allergic reaction (rash, itching, throat tightness) — very rare but stop and seek medical attention immediately.
  • No subjective response by Wk 6 — sleep should be deeper, recovery faster. If nothing, message me — we'll evaluate technique, fasted window, and timing.
