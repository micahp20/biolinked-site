Khavinson Protocol
Context, Timing, and What to Expect
Tip 01 — The Khavinson Pairing
Why Epitalon + Pinealon Run Together for 20 Days

The Khavinson protocol pairs the upstream signal (Epitalon, pineal-targeting) with downstream tissue protection (Pinealon, brain-protective). Run them together for 20 consecutive days — this is the dose-response window established in the original Russian clinical literature. Bioregulator effects accumulate across the cycle, then plateau; you don't redose constantly to get the benefit, and 20 days hits the sweet spot before diminishing returns.

Why AM/PM split: Pinealon supports daytime cognitive resilience (AM aligns with the activity window). Epitalon resets the pineal/melatonin signal (PM aligns with the body's own melatonin onset ~1 hour before sleep). Two doses per day, opposite halves of the body's internal clock.

What to expect: deeper sleep onset by Day 4–7. More consolidated REM. Subjective shift in morning restedness. Cognitive sharpness should be noticeable by mid-cycle. Some report a subtle "circadian reset" feel by Day 10–14.

Tip 02 — MT-1 First Use
Half-Dose the First Few Times

The first 2–3 doses commonly produce mild flushing, nausea, and reduced appetite for ~30 minutes. This is normal — melanocortin receptors are getting their first signal. Symptoms fade quickly and disappear entirely once the body adapts (usually by dose 3–4).

Inject in the evening (you sleep through any mild side effects) and start with 5 units (250 mcg) — this is the standard maintenance dose, not a starter. If side effects are intense, consider splitting the first dose to 2.5 units for the very first injection only, then ramp to 5 units. Most don't need to.

Tanning effect: MT-1 builds eumelanin — you should notice a slight tan deepening within 4–6 weeks of consistent dosing, even without significant sun exposure. This is the protective layer doing its job before peak UV season.

Execution Tips
Timing · Pairing · Monitoring
Tip 03 — Reconstitution Cadence
One Vial at a Time, As Each Empties

Each Epitalon and Pinealon vial holds 5 doses at 2 mg. Reconstitute vial #1 of each on Day 1; recon vial #2 of each around Day 6 as #1 finishes; same pattern for #3 (Day 11) and #4 (Day 16). This keeps every dose fresh and minimizes time in the fridge for any single vial.

MT-1: recon vial #1 on Day 1. It covers ~40 doses (~20 weeks Mon/Thu). Don't recon vial #2 until vial #1 is empty — lyophilized vials in the freezer-cold dry state last much longer than reconstituted ones.

Tip 04 — DSIP Usage Rules
PRN, Not Nightly — Use When Sleep Is Disrupted

DSIP modulates the brain's delta-wave generators — deep sleep enhancement without sedation. Use it as needed, not nightly. The right moments: post-travel (jet lag), high-stress weeks, disrupted nights you'd otherwise toss and turn through.

Dose: 10 units (500 mcg) approximately 1 hour before bed. Most users fall asleep faster and wake up more rested the following morning. Cap at one use per night.

Since DSIP touches circadian rhythm and Epitalon also resets melatonin signaling, don't use DSIP during the 20-day Khavinson cycle unless sleep is genuinely disrupted — the Khavinson pair is doing the sleep work in that window. After the 20 days end, DSIP is yours for whatever the year throws at you.

Tip 05 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Vivid dreams the first 3–5 nights of Epitalon — pineal/melatonin shift, fades
· Mild flushing or nausea with first 1–2 MT-1 doses — subsides
· Mild injection-site redness — rotate quadrants and use proper SubQ technique
· Subtle appetite changes during MT-1 ramp — normalizes within a week

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique issue or contamination
· Severe nausea or persistent flushing past dose 3–4 of MT-1 — consider lower starting dose
· Sleep getting worse during Khavinson cycle — rare; flag to reassess timing
· Sudden mole or skin change during MT-1 — flag any new or changing pigmented lesions to a dermatologist as a baseline precaution

Why a 2-Week GH Layer Pairs With Reta
Tip — Tesa + Reta Pairing
Different Mechanisms, Same Recomp Direction

Why add Tesa as a 2-week layer: Reta and Tesa hit body comp from completely different angles. Reta works through the metabolic axis — appetite suppression (GLP-1), glucose handling (GIP), and fat oxidation / energy expenditure (glucagon). Tesa works through the GH/IGF-1 axis — amplifying the body's natural growth hormone pulse from the pituitary. Different receptors, different mechanisms, no interaction. The result is two complementary recomp signals running at the start of the cycle.

Why pre-bed fasted is mandatory: Tesa amplifies the body's nocturnal GH pulse — the natural GH surge that happens during deep sleep and drives overnight tissue repair, fat mobilization, and skin quality. Food in the system blunts that pulse (insulin and IGF-1 dampen GH release). Empty stomach for 2+ hours before the shot. Water, black coffee, tea are fine in the fasted window.

Why only 2 weeks (not the full 12-week Tesa cycle): a full clinical Tesa cycle is 12 weeks at 2 mg/day — that's a different protocol with different goals (visceral fat reduction as the primary outcome). Here, Tesa is a bolt-on at 1 mg/day for 10 doses: enough to prime the GH axis at the start of the cycle while Reta titrates, but short enough that it doesn't dominate the cycle structure. Think of it as front-loading the GH side — you get some of the early Tesa benefits (deeper sleep, faster recovery, mild GH pulse amplification) without committing to a full GHRH cycle.

What to expect: noticeably deeper sleep starting Night 3–5. Slight water retention in Wk 1 (normal, resolves as the body adapts). Mild tingling in hands occasionally (normal GH effect, fades). By end of Wk 2 when the vial empties, the GH axis stays primed for ~1–2 weeks of carry-over before drifting back to baseline.

Retatrutide Notes
Titration, Tolerance, and the Ramp at Week 4
Tip 04 — Why Mon + Thu for 3 Weeks First
Tolerance Window Before Full Cadence

Retatrutide is a powerful triple-agonist — GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon hitting at the same time can produce GI side effects (nausea, appetite suppression, slowed digestion) more aggressively than Tirzepatide alone. Mon and Thu for the first 3 weeks gives the GI system time to adapt to the signaling before stepping to 3 doses per week.

If tolerance is clean by end of Week 3: step to M/W/F at the start of Week 4. If nausea is still present or persistent: hold another week at Mon/Thu before stepping. There's no rush.

Long half-life (~6 days) means the active drug is in your system continuously across both cadences — the difference between 2 doses/week and 3 doses/week is the steady-state concentration, not whether the drug is "on."

Tip 05 — Run-Until-Empty + Reevaluate
~11 Weeks at the Math, Then Reassess

30 mg vial ÷ 1 mg per dose = 30 doses total.

Weeks 1–3 at Mon + Thu = 6 doses. Remaining 24 doses at M/W/F = 8 weeks. Total cycle: ~11 weeks. The vial empties around Week 11 and the natural reevaluation window opens.

End-of-cycle questions: how is body comp vs. Week 1? Appetite control still effective? Energy holding? GI tolerance still clean? These determine whether to re-up on Reta (continue), switch to a different metabolic compound, or take a 4–8 week off and reassess.

Tip 06 — Site Rotation
Different Quadrants, Same Morning

Reta is AM SubQ abdomen. Tesa is PM SubQ abdomen — different window, different quadrant. On Wks 1–2 when both are running, rotate so AM and PM land in different quadrants each day (e.g. Reta upper-left AM, Tesa lower-right PM). Wk 3+ is Reta only.

Tip 07 — What to Monitor
Normal vs. Watch For

Normal (no action):

· Mild nausea / appetite suppression with first 1–2 Reta shots — subsides
· Constipation on Reta (GLP-1 slows gut motility) — hydration + fiber addresses it
· Mild injection-site redness — rotate sites and use proper SubQ technique

Monitor (contact BioLinked):

· Injection site hot, swollen, or red beyond 24 hours — technique issue or contamination
· Severe nausea or vomiting past Week 3 of Reta — pause titration and reassess
· Fat loss faster than ~2 lbs/wk sustained — back off Reta dose or add cals around training
