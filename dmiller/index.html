Debbie's NAD+ Energy Protocol
One compound, low and slow — a gentle NAD+ start aimed at daytime energy support.

The idea: keep it simple. A single compound — NAD+ — started at a low dose and stepped up gently, three mornings a week, with energy as the focus.

What NAD+ is. NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme your cells use in mitochondrial energy metabolism and cellular repair. Its levels are known to decline with age. Restoring NAD+ is widely studied and commonly reported to support energy and recovery — but these are reported and still-under-study effects, not guaranteed outcomes. Think of this as a gentle trial: we start low, go slow, and let how you feel guide each step.

The rhythm: one small morning shot on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, stepping the dose up week by week only if you're feeling good on it. Weekends off. One vial runs roughly 5–6 weeks.

Storage & Handling
Keep It Cold · Keep It Clean
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Handled Clean

Dry vial: refrigerate at 36–46°F, protected from light. Never freeze.

After mixing: add 5 mL of bacteriostatic water slowly down the vial wall, swirl gently — don't shake. Refrigerate and use within ~30 days. Since one vial runs 5–6 weeks, it comfortably stays within that window.

Every shot: fresh single-use syringe, wipe the stopper and your skin with alcohol, and rotate injection sites.

Execution & Tips
Read Before Day 1
Tip 01 — The Routine
One AM Shot, Mon / Wed / Fri

NAD+ is a single morning shot on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. Take it first thing. Weekends off.

Draw mark: Week 1 12.5u, Week 2 25u, Week 3 onward 37.5u — glance at the syringe line every time so you draw the right amount. Push slowly over 60–90 seconds; a brief warm flush is normal.

Tip 02 — Step Up By Feel
Start Low, Go Slow, Hold If Needed

The whole point of the weekly step-up is to let your body set the pace. Move from 25 → 50 → 75 mg a week at a time, only when the current dose is sitting well. If a week feels like too much — tired, off, or just not right — hold at the dose you're on and don't climb. Holding longer is a perfectly good outcome, not a setback. Message us anytime and we'll help you decide.

Tip 04 — Safety & Red Flags
When to Pause & Message Us

Clear it with your physician first. A quick confirmation that NAD+ is fine alongside your health and any medications — especially worth doing at 67.

Stop escalating — or pause — if any dose leaves you feeling unwell. Don't push up to the next step until things settle. A brief warm flush or mild pressure while pushing is normal and passes; anything beyond that, hold and message us.

Injection sites: a little redness at the pinhole is normal. Spreading redness, warmth, swelling, or worsening soreness can mean an irritated or infected site — stop that spot and message us.

Missed a day? No need to double up — just take your next scheduled M/W/F shot as usual.

Anything that feels off, message first — don't push through or change the plan on your own.
