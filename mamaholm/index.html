Personalized Longevity Program
Mama Holm
Live Forever Young. Three sequential cycles, never more than two shots a day.

This program is built around a single idea: aging well is a slow, patient project, and the body responds best when you give it time to rebuild one layer at a time. Instead of stacking six or seven compounds at once, this protocol runs as three focused cycles spanning roughly six months, with no more than two injections on any given day. Each cycle targets a different layer of how the body ages — the first restores the foundation, the second reawakens the cellular engines that slow down with time, and the third reaches into the deeper machinery of longevity itself.

The peptides chosen here are long-acting by design. They work gradually, the way real healing works. Nothing flips overnight, and that's the point. What unfolds over weeks and months is steadier energy, better recovery, clearer sleep, and a body that handles its daily work a little more easily than it did the month before. Forever Young isn't a slogan — it's the quiet, compounding result of giving your cells exactly what they need, one careful step at a time.

Why sequencing instead of stacking: running each pair of peptides in a dedicated window gives a clean readout of what each compound is actually doing. If skin, energy, sleep, or recovery shift during Cycle 1, we know it's GLOW + SS-31. If something changes during Cycle 2, we know it's MOTS-c + Humanin. If something shifts during the Cycle 3 pulse, we know it's Epitalon + NAD+. The sequencing also gives your body small, scheduled recovery windows between cycles — brief wash-outs where receptors and signaling pathways reset before the next pair begins.

Pineal peptide · telomerase support · endogenous melatonin. Epitalon is a short synthetic tetrapeptide (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) modeled on a natural peptide from the pineal gland. Best known for two effects: supporting telomerase activity (the enzyme that maintains the protective caps on chromosomes that shorten with age) and supporting endogenous melatonin production (which declines substantially with age and drives many sleep-related aging effects).

Significant Russian gerontology research spanning decades; Western RCT data is limited. Included on mechanism and a long safety record at these doses.

Coenzyme for mitochondrial energy, sirtuin activity, and DNA repair. NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is not a peptide — it's a coenzyme central to almost every energy and repair process in the cell. It's what mitochondria use to move electrons through the electron transport chain, it fuels the sirtuins (a longevity-associated enzyme family), and it's required for PARP-driven DNA repair. NAD+ levels decline sharply with age — by 50% or more between young adulthood and 70 — tracking directly with many hallmarks of aging.
Why low-dose daily instead of high-dose IV infusions: high-dose NAD+ infusions are notorious for causing intense flushing, nausea, and chest tightness. At 70, tolerability is a real consideration. Breaking the dose into 20 mg daily subcutaneous shots captures most of the biological benefit while avoiding the acute side effects — and produces a steadier NAD+ level rather than a spike-and-crash. Evidence level: NAD+ decline and its role in aging is well-established; optimal dosing route and timing for exogenous NAD+ in humans remains an active research question. Low-dose daily subcutaneous is a tolerability-first approach.
⚠ Tolerability — watch for these. Even at low doses, NAD+ can produce mild facial flushing, warmth, or transient blood pressure drop (dizziness on standing up too fast) in the 20–30 minutes after injection. Inject sitting down. Stand up slowly the first several shots. If flushing or dizziness is notable, inject more slowly (over 30–60 seconds) — that alone resolves it for most people. If chest tightness, persistent dizziness, or anything beyond mild flushing — stop and contact Dr. DiLorenzo or your physician.

Protocol Overview — Why This Sequence Works

Lays the groundwork. GLOW (three repair peptides together — GHK-Cu, BPC-157, TB-500) rebuilds skin, connective tissue, and gut lining while reducing chronic background inflammation. Cycle 2 · MOTS-c + Humanin (Weeks 9–16, after brief wash-out)
Builds on the cleaner foundation. With mitochondria functioning better, MOTS-c tells the body to produce more of them and Humanin protects them from age-related damage — this is mitochondrial biogenesis, the single most well-studied longevity mechanism in mammalian biology. Same M–F AM rhythm, weekends off.
Cycle 3 · Epitalon + NAD+ (20-Day Pulse, after brief wash-out)
Caps the program at the deepest layer with a short, focused 20-consecutive-day pulse — not an 8-week run. Epitalon supports telomerase and melatonin (evening dose); low-dose daily NAD+ restores the coenzyme cells use to run every energy and repair pathway (morning dose). The biggest effects of this cycle consolidate after dosing ends — not during.

Kept on the shelf across all three cycles for rough-sleep nights, high-stress weeks, or jet lag. Not part of the sequential program — a strategic tool, not a habit. Pre-bed only.

Storage & Handling
Refrigerated · Protected from Light · Rotated
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Never freeze. Protect from light.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing, always. Use within 30 days of adding BAC water. Fresh single-use insulin syringe for every single draw — no exceptions. Needle reuse is the single most common cause of infection and injection site reactions.

Injection site rotation: rotate between lower abdomen (fingerbreadth away from navel), outer thigh, and love handles. Never inject the same 1-inch spot two days in a row — sites need 48+ hours to recover. At 2 shots per day, alternate left and right sides morning and evening.

Reconstitution timing for Cycle 3: reconstitute one vial of Epitalon and one vial of NAD+ at the start of the pulse. The second bottle of each stays dry (do not reconstitute) — 20 doses fits within one reconstituted vial of each, and we don't need to start the 30-day clock on the second bottles.

Featured Tips
Tip 01 — Design Philosophy
Two Shots a Day. Three Cycles Instead of a Stack.

Most longevity protocols stack six or seven compounds at once. We are not doing that. You asked for no more than two injections in any single day — and that constraint actually produces a better program, not a compromised one.

By running each pair of peptides in a dedicated window, we get a clean readout of what each compound is actually doing. If skin, energy, sleep, or recovery shift during Cycle 1, we know it's GLOW + SS-31. If something changes during Cycle 2, we know it's MOTS-c + Humanin. If something shifts during the Cycle 3 pulse, we know it's Epitalon + NAD+. Stacking everything at once would smear those signals together and make it impossible to tell which peptide is producing which effect.

The sequencing also gives your body small, scheduled recovery windows between cycles — brief wash-outs where receptors and signaling pathways reset before the next pair begins.

Tip 02 — Realistic Expectations
These Peptides Work Gradually — Not Overnight

Anti-aging peptides do not produce the immediate, obvious response of a stimulant. They work at the cellular level — on mitochondria, telomeres, signaling pathways, and the enzymes that drive repair and autophagy — and that work unfolds over weeks and months, not hours or days.

What to expect realistically: subtle early signals in the first 1–2 weeks of each cycle (slightly better sleep, cleaner morning energy, small shifts in skin tone or digestive comfort), more noticeable changes by weeks 3–5, and meaningful consolidated effects by the end of each 8-week window. The full program — all three cycles over six months — is where compounding benefits become evident. If you are expecting dramatic week-1 transformation, please recalibrate now. What we are building is durable, layered improvement in how your cells do their job.

The research base for these compounds varies: some (GHK-Cu in GLOW, the general NAD+ biology) have substantial mechanistic and clinical evidence; others (Humanin, Epitalon) have strong mechanistic support but more preliminary human trial data. Where evidence is mechanism-based rather than RCT-proven, the protocol tab says so directly.

Tip 03 — Medical Oversight
Reviewed With Dr. DiLorenzo · Worth Re-Flagging Before Cycle 3

This full protocol — every compound, dose, and sequence — has been reviewed with Dr. DiLorenzo before being handed to you. That review is a floor, not a ceiling. Please continue working with your own primary physician throughout the program, especially for anything related to cardiac function, blood pressure, hormonal medication, or oncology history.

NAD+: can cause transient blood pressure drop. If you're on antihypertensives or have had episodes of orthostatic hypotension, please flag this to your physician before Cycle 3.

Epitalon and cancer history: Epitalon's proposed mechanism involves telomerase activity, which is an oncology-relevant pathway (cancer cells often up-regulate telomerase). The clinical safety record is long and reassuring, but if you have any personal or family history of cancer, confirm with your physician before beginning Cycle 3 specifically. We can pause or omit Cycle 3 without affecting Cycles 1 and 2.

Stop any cycle and seek medical attention if you experience unexpected adverse reactions, persistent dizziness, chest symptoms, or anything that doesn't feel right. You are the most important monitor in this program.

Daily Execution
Rhythm Tips
Tip 04 — Two-Shot Daily Ritual
AM Preferred · ~5 Minutes Apart · Rotate Sites

For Cycles 1 and 2 (and the NAD+ shot of Cycle 3), AM is the default — energy-related effects land in daylight hours and sleep stays clean. Space the two shots about 5 minutes apart so you're not stacking two injections at the exact same site or moment. Always rotate between left and right abdomen.

The Cycle 3 Epitalon dose is the one exception: evening, 30–60 minutes before bed. The melatonin-pathway support lands when the body is preparing for sleep.

Tip 05 — Wash-Outs Between Cycles
Receptor Reset · Don't Skip Them

Roughly 1 week between Cycle 1 and Cycle 2, and another between Cycle 2 and Cycle 3. The wash-out isn't dead time — receptors and signaling pathways reset before the next pair begins. Skipping or shortening the wash-out compresses the program but blunts the signal of the next cycle. The calendar download bakes the wash-outs in automatically.

Tip 06 — Reconstitute One Vial at a Time
Especially for Cycle 3

Reconstituted peptides last ~30 days refrigerated. For Cycles 1 and 2, the math works out so each vial gets used inside its 30-day window. For Cycle 3, you only need 20 doses of each compound — one reconstituted vial of Epitalon and one of NAD+ is enough. Leave the second of each dry. If you reconstitute both up front, you've started the clock on a vial you don't need to use.
