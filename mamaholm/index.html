Design Philosophy

🧭 Two Shots a Day. Three Cycles Instead of a Stack.

Most longevity protocols stack six or seven compounds at once. We are not doing that. You asked for no more than two injections in any single day — and that constraint actually produces a better program, not a compromised one.

By running each pair of peptides in a dedicated window, we get a clean readout of what each compound is actually doing. If skin, energy, sleep, or recovery shift during Cycle 1, we know it's KLOW + SS-31. If something changes during Cycle 2, we know it's MOTS-c + Humanin. If something shifts during the Cycle 3 pulse, we know it's Epitalon + NAD+. Stacking everything at once would smear those signals together and make it impossible to tell which peptide is producing which effect.

The sequencing also gives your body small, scheduled recovery windows between cycles — brief wash-outs where receptors and signaling pathways reset before the next pair begins.

Realistic Expectations

⏳ These Peptides Work Gradually — Not Overnight

Anti-aging peptides do not produce the immediate, obvious response of a stimulant. They work at the cellular level — on mitochondria, telomeres, signaling pathways, and the enzymes that drive repair and autophagy — and that work unfolds over weeks and months, not hours or days.

What to expect realistically: subtle early signals in the first 1–2 weeks of each cycle (slightly better sleep, cleaner morning energy, small shifts in skin tone or digestive comfort), more noticeable changes by weeks 3–5, and meaningful consolidated effects by the end of each 8-week window. The full program — all three cycles over six months — is where compounding benefits become evident. If you are expecting dramatic week-1 transformation, please recalibrate now. What we are building is durable, layered improvement in how your cells do their job.

The research base for these compounds varies: some (like GHK-Cu in KLOW and the general NAD+ biology) have substantial mechanistic and clinical evidence; others (like Humanin and Epitalon) have strong mechanistic support but more preliminary human trial data. Where evidence is mechanism-based rather than RCT-proven, the compound cards below say so directly.

SS-31 (Elamipretide)
Mitochondrial-Targeted Antioxidant · Cardiolipin Stabilizer
$90

Why it's in your protocol
SS-31 (also called elamipretide) is one of the most targeted mitochondrial peptides we have. It localizes to the inner mitochondrial membrane and binds cardiolipin, a phospholipid that anchors the electron transport chain — the machinery that actually produces ATP (cellular energy). In aging cells, cardiolipin oxidizes and destabilizes, which is a core mechanism of age-related fatigue and declining cellular energy. SS-31 stabilizes cardiolipin, which improves electron transport efficiency and reduces reactive oxygen species leaking out of the mitochondria.
Translation: it makes tired mitochondria work better before we ask the body to build new ones in Cycle 2. Running it alongside KLOW in Cycle 1 means we clean up the existing factory before expanding production.
Evidence level: substantial clinical trial data in primary mitochondrial disease and age-related conditions; mechanism well-established.

Evidence level: preclinical data is strong; human trials are early but promising. Mechanism is well-characterized.

Why it's in your protocol
Epitalon is a short synthetic tetrapeptide (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) modeled on a natural peptide from the pineal gland. It's best known for two effects: supporting telomerase activity (the enzyme that maintains telomeres — the protective caps on chromosomes that shorten with each cell division and age) and supporting endogenous melatonin production (which declines substantially with age and drives many sleep-related aging effects).
It's dosed as a pulse, not continuously. The original Russian research used 10–20 consecutive days followed by months off. We're using the 20-day protocol. Pulsing is both traditional and pharmacologically sensible here — telomerase effects don't require or benefit from constant stimulation.
Evidence level: preliminary. Significant Russian gerontology research (V. Khavinson and colleagues) spanning decades suggests longevity benefits, but Western RCT data is limited. Included on mechanism and a long safety record at these doses.

NAD+
Coenzyme · Mitochondrial Energy · Sirtuin Activity · DNA Repair
$200
2 vials · 1000mg each
Why it's in your protocol
NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is not a peptide — it's a coenzyme central to almost every energy and repair process in the cell. It's the molecule mitochondria use to move electrons through the electron transport chain; it fuels the sirtuins (a family of longevity-associated enzymes); and it's required for PARP-driven DNA repair. NAD+ levels decline sharply with age — by 50% or more between young adulthood and 70 — and this decline tracks directly with many hallmarks of aging.
Why low-dose daily instead of high-dose IV infusions: high-dose NAD+ infusions are notorious for causing intense flushing, nausea, and chest tightness. At 70, tolerability is a real consideration. Breaking the dose into 20mg daily subcutaneous shots captures most of the biological benefit while avoiding the acute side effects. It also produces a steadier NAD+ level rather than a spike-and-crash.
Evidence level: the biology of NAD+ decline and its role in aging is well-established; the optimal dosing route and timing for exogenous NAD+ in humans remains an active research question. Low-dose daily subcutaneous is a reasonable, tolerability-first approach.

🫀 Tolerability — Watch For These

Even at low doses, NAD+ can produce mild facial flushing, warmth, or transient blood pressure drop (dizziness on standing up too fast) in the 20–30 minutes after injection. Inject sitting down; stand up slowly the first several shots. If flushing or dizziness is notable, inject even more slowly (over 30–60 seconds) — that alone resolves it for most people. If you experience chest tightness, persistent dizziness, or anything beyond mild flushing, stop and contact Dr. DiLorenzo or your physician.

Storage & Handling

❄️ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: store refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Never freeze. Protect from light. Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after mixing, always. Use within 30 days of adding BAC water. Insulin syringes: fresh single-use syringe for every single draw — no exceptions. Needle reuse is the single most common cause of infection and injection site reactions.

Injection site rotation: rotate between lower abdomen (fingerbreadth away from navel), outer thigh, and love handles. Never inject the same 1-inch spot two days in a row — sites need 48+ hours to recover. At 2 shots per day, alternate left and right sides morning and evening.

Reconstitution timing: for Cycle 3, reconstitute one vial of Epitalon and one vial of NAD+ at the start. The second bottle of each stays dry (do not reconstitute) — 20 doses fits within one reconstituted vial of each, and we don't need to start the 30-day clock on the second bottles.
