Kay's Skin & Glow Stack
Three lanes, one endpoint — visible skin quality, clean recovery, sleep-driven repair.

This stack runs three compounds in parallel — each one in its own lane, all pointed at the same outcome: visible skin quality, cleaner recovery, and better sleep-driven repair. Nothing redundant, nothing that competes for the same pathway.

The day-night rhythm is intentional. Mornings are for skin and antioxidant defense — GLOW (GHK-Cu + BPC-157 + TB-500) and Glutathione both run AM fasted, when uptake is best and the day's oxidative load hasn't started yet. Evenings are for the GH pulse — CJC-1295/Ipamorelin pre-bed rides the body's natural nocturnal growth-hormone wave, where overnight cellular repair, slow-wave sleep, and lean recovery all converge.

The schedule is identical weeks 1–8. No ramps, no titration. GLOW + CJC/Ipa Mon–Fri, Glutathione Mon & Fri only, Sat/Sun full rest. The vials land cleanly at the end of the cycle — no waste, no shortage. The full skin/recovery arc usually becomes visible weeks 5–8.

Storage & Handling
Refrigerated · Protected from Light
❄ Refrigerated, Protected, Rotated

Dry (lyophilized) vials: refrigerated at 36–46°F before reconstitution. Protect from light. Never freeze.

Reconstituted vials: refrigerate immediately after adding BAC water. Use within 30 days of reconstitution. Glutathione is especially oxidation-sensitive — keep it in the darkest, coldest part of your fridge. Discard if the glutathione solution turns deeply orange or cloudy.

Syringe discipline: fresh insulin syringe for every single draw — no exceptions. Piercing a rubber stopper dulls the needle tip and increases site reactions.

Site rotation: SubQ abdomen rotates between 4 quadrants (upper left/right, lower left/right). Never inject the same 1″ spot two days in a row.

Tip 01 — The Stack Logic
Three Compounds, Three Lanes, One Endpoint

GLOW drives direct dermal and connective-tissue rebuild — GHK-Cu copper peptide for collagen, BPC-157 + TB-500 for tissue repair. Glutathione is the body's master intracellular antioxidant — quenches the oxidative stress that ages skin, supports liver detox, and noticeably brightens tone over weeks. CJC-1295/Ipamorelin taken pre-bed amplifies the natural nocturnal growth-hormone pulse — the same pulse that drives overnight cellular repair, deeper slow-wave sleep, and leaner body composition.

Morning peptides for skin and antioxidant defense, evening GH pulse for repair — a clean day-night rhythm. Each compound covers a distinct mechanism, and none of them compete for the same pathway.

Tip 02 — What to Expect
Week-by-Week Arc

Weeks 1–2: sleep deepens (CJC/Ipa — vivid dreams are normal, a hallmark of better slow-wave / REM architecture). Mild flushing or head-tingling 5–10 min post-CJC injection is normal (histamine-mediated, harmless). Any pre-existing minor skin inflammation often starts to calm.

Weeks 2–3: skin hydration and tone changes start to show (GLOW). Mornings feel cleaner — the cumulative antioxidant load is doing work.

Weeks 4–5: brightness from glutathione becomes visible — evening of tone, less reactive skin to sun exposure. Body composition improvements from the GH pulse start to register (recovery, lean tissue).

Weeks 5–8: collagen-driven texture and firmness improvements from GLOW are at their most visible. The full skin/recovery arc is in place.

Tip 03 — AM Fasted vs PM Pre-Bed
Why the Timing Matters

AM fasted (GLOW + Glutathione on Mon/Fri): peptide uptake is highest on an empty stomach. Glutathione specifically is at its most effective before the day's oxidative load begins — you're starting the day with a full antioxidant tank instead of refilling a depleted one. Dose within the first hour after waking, wait 30–45 minutes, then eat normally.

PM pre-bed (CJC/Ipa, empty stomach): the GH pulse needs low insulin to fire. Carbs or fats within the hour blunt the pulse. Dose 30 min before bed, ideally 2+ hours after your last meal. The peptide rides the body's natural nocturnal GH wave — you're amplifying a pulse that was going to happen anyway.

Tip 04 — Stay Hydrated on Glutathione Days
Mon & Fri: Water + Vitamin C

Glutathione is a potent detox driver. On dosing days (Mon & Fri), drink 16–20 oz of water in the hour after injection to support Phase II liver clearance.

Pair with 500 mg vitamin C (oral) earlier in the morning — vitamin C recycles used glutathione back to its active form, noticeably extending its effect. The two are synergistic at the molecular level.

Tip 05 — Weekends Off Are Non-Negotiable
Sat & Sun = Full Rest, All 8 Weeks

No injections Saturday or Sunday across all 8 weeks. This isn't a "rest if you remember" thing — it's structural.

The weekend gap prevents receptor desensitization (especially on the GH pulse side — the ghrelin receptor blunts if hit 7 days a week), and gives glutathione's recycling pool time to cycle through. Push through the weekends and you'll get diminishing returns by weeks 4–5. Take them off and the cycle keeps compounding cleanly.

Tip 06 — Red Flags · Stop & Reassess
Normal vs Watch For

Normal:

  • Mild flushing / head-tingling for 5–10 min after CJC/Ipa — histamine-mediated, harmless
  • Vivid dreams — deeper sleep architecture showing up
  • Yellow tint in reconstituted glutathione — expected
  • Mild injection-site redness that clears within 12 hrs

Watch for (pause and message us):

  • Injection site hot / swollen / red >24 hrs — technique or contamination
  • Glutathione solution turning deeply orange or cloudy — oxidized, discard the vial
  • Persistent insomnia or sleep disruption from CJC/Ipa — rare, but worth flagging
  • If you're on thyroid, cortisol, or hormone-related medication, coordinate timing with your physician — glutathione and CJC/Ipa both touch hormonal/metabolic pathways
